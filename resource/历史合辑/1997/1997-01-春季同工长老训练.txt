第一篇　使徒们生活的最高点─活出新耶路撒冷（一）
大纲|Outline|对照-听抄-目录
壹　使徒们生活的惟一目标乃是新耶路撒冷─神永远经纶终极的目标：
一　作得胜者，以神经纶的目标新耶路撒冷为他们生活惟一并终极的目标。
二　新耶路撒冷之三一的生活，是他们正常的生活：
１　活出在宝座上作生命源头的父─启二二1，约五26。
２　活出作生命和生命供应的子，就是生命树─启二二2，约十四6。
３　活出作经过过程并终极完成之三一神全备供应的灵，就是生命水─启二二1，腓一19。
４　使他们凭神的增长而在神圣生命上长大，以建造基督的身体，并终极完成生命的城，作三一神终极的扩展、扩大和彰显─西二19，弗四16。
贰　看见新耶路撒冷是圣经中最大、终极的表号，表征经过过程之三一神与蒙祂重生、变化、荣化之三部分选民的生机构成，以及宇宙、扩大之神人二性的合并─启二一2，9～10：
一　借着住在三一神─他们的殿─里面，并借着祂住在他们─祂的帐幕─里面，而过一种与三一神合并的生活─启二一3，22，加二20，林后十二2，约十五5。
二　爱主，与祂有最亲近、最密切的交通，而作羔羊之妻的构成成分─彼前一8，林前二9，林后五14～15，约壹四19。　
叁　使徒们渐渐成为神，而成为神完整救恩具体表现的新耶路撒冷，使他们在生命、性情并外表上成为神，但无分于神的神格：
一　借着神法理的救赎─启二二14上。　
二　借着神生机的救恩─罗五10：
１　重生，成为神的种类，神的儿女─约一12～13，启二一7，二二14下。
２　圣别，成为像圣城一样的圣─帖前五23，弗五26。
３　更新，成为像新耶路撒冷一样的新─林后四16，弗四23。
４　变化，以三一神为金、银（珍珠）、宝石所构成─林前三12，林后三18，罗十二2。
５　模成神长子的形像，有新耶路撒冷显出的样子─罗八28～29。
６　得荣，完全充满新耶路撒冷的荣耀─罗八30，腓三21。
三　借着神加强七倍的生机救恩，使他们得着七倍的加强，而胜过召会的堕落，使基督的身体得以建造，以终极完成新耶路撒冷─启一4，三1，四5，五6。
肆　使徒们生活在基督宇宙身体的相调中，而被带进基督身体的实际，以终极完成新耶路撒冷─林前十二14，十17，利二4～5。

GENERAL SUBJECT
THE NEW JERUSALEM—THE HIGHEST POINT OF THE APOSTLES' LIVING AND WORK
Message One
The Highest Point of the Apostles' Living—-to Live Out the New Jerusalem (1)
纲目|对照-听抄-目录
I.　The unique goal of the apostles' living being the New Jerusalem—the ultimate goal of God's eternal economy:
A.　Being overcomers who take the goal of God's economy, the New Jerusalem, as the unique and ultimate goal of their living.
B.　The triune living of the New Jerusalem being their daily living:
1.　Living out the Father as the source of life on the throne—Rev. 22:1; John 5:26.
2.　Living out the Son as the life and life supply, the tree of life—Rev. 22:2; John 14:6.
3.　Living out the Spirit as the bountiful supply of the processed and consummated Triune God, the water of life—Rev. 22:1; Phil. 1:19.
4.　For their growth in the divine life by the growth of God to build up the Body of Christ and consummate the city of life for the ultimate expansion, enlargement, and expression of the Triune God—Col. 2:19; Eph. 4:16.
II.　Seeing the New Jerusalem as the greatest and ultimate sign in the Scriptures, signifying the organic constitution and universal, enlarged, divine-human incorporation of the processed Triune God with His regenerated, transformed, and glorified tripartite elect—Rev. 21:2, 9-10:
A.　Living a life of incorporation with the Triune God by their abiding in Him as their temple and by His abiding in them as His tabernacle—Rev. 21:3, 22; Gal. 2:20; 2 Cor. 12:2; John 15:5.
B.　Loving the Lord in the closest and most intimate fellowship with Him as constituents of the wife of the Lamb— 1 Pet. 1:8; 1 Cor. 2:9; 2 Cor. 5:14-15; 1 John 4:19.
III.　The apostles being deified to become the New Jerusalem, which is the embodiment of God's complete salvation, for them to become God in life, nature, and appearance but not in the Godhead:
A.　Through God's judicial redemption—Rev. 22:14a.
B.　Through God's organic salvation—Rom. 5:10:
1.　Regeneration to become God's species, God's children—John 1:12-13; Rev. 21:7; 22:14b.
2.　Sanctification to be as holy as the holy city—1 Thes. 5:23; Eph. 5:26.
3.　Renewing to be as new as the New Jerusalem—2 Cor. 4:16; Eph. 4:23.
4.　Transformation to be constituted with the Triune God as gold, silver (pearl), and precious stones—1 Cor. 3:12; 2 Cor. 3:18; Rom. 12:2.
5.　Conformation to the image of the firstborn Son of God to have the appearance of the New Jerusalem— Rom. 8:28-29.
6.　Glorification to be completely permeated with the glory of the New Jerusalem—Rom. 8:30; Phil. 3:21.
C.　Through God's sevenfold intensified organic salvation for them to be intensified sevenfold to overcome the degradation of the church that the Body may be built up to consummate the New Jerusalem—Rev. 1:4; 3:1; 4:5; 5:6.
IV.　The apostles living in the blending of the universal Body of Christ to be ushered into the reality of the Body of Christ to consummate the New Jerusalem—1 Cor. 12:24; 10:17; Lev. 2:4-5.

BROTHER LEE'S FELLOWSHIP ON APRIL 6, 1997
The co-workers must see that we should only do one work, which is to make God's chosen people regenerated ones, sanctified ones, renewed ones—-the new man—transformed ones, conformed ones—those conformed to the image of the firstborn Son of God—and glorified ones. All those who will be in the New Jerusalem are this kind of people.

Specifically, God proceeds step by step to make a chosen one of God a person who is regenerated, sanctified, renewed, transformed, conformed to the image of the firstborn Son of God, and even glorified by God. In this way, we go up level by level until we reach the highest point, where we become the same. There is no more flesh and no more natural being. All are in the spirit. All are the kingdom of the heavens, and all are beings of the New Jerusalem. This is the highest point. As we go step by step, ascending level by level, eventually we reach the highest point, where there is no more flesh and no more natural being, and all are in the spirit. That is the New Jerusalem. I believe the blending brothers will do a good job, speaking according to my speaking. Then, all the elders and co-workers will see how they should carry out their work, what work they should do, and what goal they should attain. They should no longer do their own work.They should only do the work of the New Jerusalem.

You need to get into these messages gradually. The vision is so high. You need to get into them point by point. I hope the Christians outside of the Lord's recovery can also see this. I hope all the co-workers in the whole world can see this.

The title of this series of messages is "The New Jerusalem—The Highest Point of the Apostles' Living and Work." The first three messages in this series are on the highest point of the apostles' living, which is to live out the New Jerusalem. Then in the last three messages we will see that the highest point of the apostles' work is to work out the New Jerusalem. We must get into the reality of these messages so that we would live the New Jerusalem and that our work would be the work which is toward the building up of the New Jerusalem. We hope and believe that after receiving these messages, we will treasure the above series title to the uttermost. We need to be impressed very much with it and with the Lord's burden for these messages which He put in our Brother Lee. The New Jerusalem is the highest point of the apostles' living and work. If we consider this word, we can see that there is light in it. Who among us could have composed such a title? We need to be brought to this highest point of the apostles' living, where our whole living is the living out of the New Jerusalem. Our whole career, our whole life, is to do two things: to live out the New Jerusalem and to work out the New Jerusalem.
Although the subject of this conference is the apostles' living and work, we have to realize that the apostles represent all the New Testament saints. In the New Jerusalem, the twelve gates of the city are inscribed with the names of the twelve tribes, which represent the Old Testament saints, whereas the twelve foundations have the names of the twelve apostles, who represent all of us, the New Testament saints. Therefore, the apostles' living should be our living, and their work should be our work.
THE UNIQUE GOAL OF THE APOSTLES' LIVING BEING THE NEW JERUSALEM—THE ULTIMATE GOAL OF GOD'S ETERNAL ECONOMY
The goal of the apostles' living is the New Jerusalem. In the last international co-workers' and elders' conference, Brother Lee pointed out to us the goal of the apostles' teaching. The apostles' teaching, which is the entire speaking of God in the New, Testament, is the teaching of God's New Testament economy concerning the full ministry of Christ in His three divine and mystical stages: the stage of incarnation, the stage of inclusion, and the stage of intensification. In the third stage of Christ's ministry, the stage of intensification, the Lord's burden is to intensify His organic salvation, to produce the overcomers, and to consummate the New Jerusalem. This is the contents of the apostles' teaching. From this we see that the goal of the apostles' teaching is the New Jerusalem.
The unique goal of the apostles' living is the New Jerusalem—the ultimate goal of God's eternal economy. Their goal should be our goal. The goal of the elders and co-workers must be the New Jerusalem, and they should bring all the saints to have this same goal. We should not have any other goal. Actually, the New Jerusalem is not only the apostles' goal but God's goal. The Bible begins with the Triune God, and it ends with the New Jerusalem. As the final item of the Bible, the New Jerusalem is surely God's goal. This goal is a great corporate God. This great corporate God, who is actually the incorporate God, has been incorporated with His chosen, redeemed, regenerated, transformed, and glorified tripartite elect. This is God's desire; this is His "hobby"; this is His goal, what He is working on. What He is doing now is consummating the New Jerusalem. Since this is God's goal, it should also be our goal. We should not have any other goal.
The degradation of the church is mainly due to the fact that nearly all the Christian workers have been distracted to take many things other than the New Jerusalem as their goal. We may consider that the degradation of the church is due to Satan, sin, the world, the flesh, and the self, and no doubt all these negative things do degrade the church. However, the main reason for the degradation of the church is that nearly all the Christian workers have been distracted to take many things other than the New Jerusalem as their goal. The reason division comes in is that many do not have the New Jerusalem as their goal. We should have no other goal.
We have seen that the goal of the apostles' teaching is the New Jerusalem. First John 1:3 speaks about the fellowship of the apostles. The goal of this fellowship is also the New Jerusalem. Note 3 on this verse in the Recovery Version points out that fellowship not only refers to the flow of the divine life in and among the believers, but it also implies the putting away of private interests and joining with the apostles and the Triune God for the Triune God's unique purpose and goal, which is the New Jerusalem. I hope we would have a fresh consecration to the Lord and tell Him, "Lord, I like to put away all my private interests. I do not like to have any other goal but the New Jerusalem as my unique goal."
Being Overcomers Who Take the Goal of God's Economy, the New Jerusalem, as the Unique and Ultimate Goal of Their Living
The overcomers are those who take the New Jerusalem as their goal. If we want to be overcomers, we need to overcome the negative things, but even more we have to overcome the positive things which replace the New Jerusalem as our goal. Although the local churches are precious to us, they are not the goal of God's economy. Praise the Lord for the local churches, but our local church should not be our goal. Our goal is the New Jerusalem. When we first wake up in the morning, we should say, "Lord Jesus, I love You," but another good thing to say is, "My goal is the New Jerusalem." To say this will calibrate us. When someone asks us what our career goal is, we should say, "My career goal is the New Jerusalem." We need to be impressed with this. The local churches are not the goal; they are the procedure to reach the goal.
We can see from the Scriptures that the overcomers have the New Jerusalem as their goal. In Revelation 3:12 the Lord said to the church in Philadelphia, "He who overcomes, him I will make a pillar in the temple of My God, and he shall by no means go out anymore, and I will write upon him the name of My God and the name of the city of My God, the New Jerusalem, which descends out of heaven from My God, and My new name." The fact that the name of God is written on the overcomers means that they have become God in life and nature but not in the Godhead. In the same way, the name of the New Jerusalem being written on the overcomers means that they have become the New Jerusalem. Actually, the New Jerusalem is composed of the over comers. On a small scale the New Jerusalem is in the millennial kingdom, which is all the overcomers as His overcoming bride. God's goal is His bride. Eventually, after that wedding day of a thousand years, He will have a wife for eternity. The constituents of that wife are the over comers. Hence, the New Jerusalem is the consummate goal of the overcomers. .
The Lord's recovery is to build up Zion. What is Zion? Zion is the overcomers. The Lord is raising up overcomers among the churches just as Zion is raised up in the center of Jerusalem. Zion is the very spot where God is. It is the enlarged Holy of Holies, the very spot where God dwells. On the one hand, Zion is the Holy of Holies. On the other hand, we see that the New Jerusalem is also the Holy of Holies. The whole New Jerusalem is a cube twelve thousand stadia in height, breadth, and length (Rev. 21:16 and note 4). It is a twelve-thousand- stadia Holy of Holies. Eventually, all the overcomers, which are Zion, become the enlarged Holy of Holies, which is the New Jerusalem. The Lord's recovery is to build up Zion, and Zion is the New Jerusalem. We need to have this as our goal.
The goal of our living must be the New Jerusalem. Everything that is ascribed to the New Jerusalem must be our personal, individual, and corporate experience. Every detail of the New Jerusalem must first be our personal experience. Our brother pointed out that every believer is a "little New Jerusalem." We have the tree of life, the throne, the river of life, and the golden street in us. We are short of experience, but we need to pursue everything that is ascribed to the New Jerusalem so that it becomes our personal experience to make us a little New Jerusalem. Moreover, our personal experience is for the building up of the Body of Christ. As the Body of Christ, our goal is to consummate the New Jerusalem in a corporate way. Therefore, wherever we are, our church life should be a miniature of the New Jerusalem.
We need to pray-read, study, and recite all the items of the outline of this message, so that they get into our being and we can prophesy these items, be constituted with them, and become them to consummate the New Jerusalem and bring the Lord back.
The Triune Living of the New Jerusalem Being Their Daily Living The triune living of the New Jerusalem is the daily living of the apostles. Oh, may our daily living be such a triune living! To have a triune living means that we have to know Christ, experience Christ, enjoy Christ, gain Christ, and win Christ. We have to confess and be desperate before the Lord. Most of our living is not a triune living but a "-une" living, a living of one, that is, of the self. We do not want this kind of living. We need to pray, "lord, may my living be a triune living, and may all the saints in the recovery have a triune living." We see this triune living in the New Jerusalem.
Living Out the Father as the Source of Life on the Throne
First, we need to live out the Father as the source of life on the throne (Rev. 22:1; John 5:26). The picture of the triune living is quite marvelous. In Revelation 22:1 there is the throne of God and of the Lamb, and in the ministry we have been helped to see that this throne is not a love seat. A love seat has room for two persons, but there is only one throne, and only One is sitting upon that throne. Revelation 21:23 tells us that Christ as the Lamb is the lamp, and that God is within Him as the light. The One who is sitting upon the throne is a coinhering One, who is God in the Lamb. In Revelation 4:3 we see that the One sitting on the throne has the appearance of jasper stone and sardius. Sardius is red, and red signifies redemption. The Lamb is for our redemption. The green jasper God is within the red sardius Lamb. The God of life full of glory is within the redeeming Lamb. In the One sitting on the throne we can see God's complete salvation. With God in the Lamb there is judicial redemption and organic salvation. Furthermore, the river of water of life flows out of the Father God embodied in the Lamb on the throne (22:1). This should be the source of our triune living.
We need to take the Father as our source in everything. When we speak of the Father in this way, we refer to the Father with His redeeming element and with the element of His divine authority. God the Father with His redeeming element and with the element of His divine authority must be our unique source. The source of our family life, marriage life, business life, church life, daily life, and everything related to us must be the Father with His redeeming element and with the element of divine authority. The Lord Jesus lived in this way. He was the first God-man as the model for the many God-men. He did not live by His own life. Rather, He rejected His natural life, His created life, His human life, and He lived by another life. He lived by the divine life of His Father, and He did everything by taking the Father as His unique source. He was under the restriction, the control, and the ruling of the life of the Father. He did not do anything apart from being under the ruling of the Father's life. This is a pattern for us. To reign in life is to be under the ruling of the divine life. In Matthew 8:8-9, the centurion said to the Lord Jesus, "Lord, I am not fit for You to enter under my roof; but only speak a word, and my servant will be healed. For I also am a man under authority, having soldiers under me. And I say to this one, Go, and he goes; and to another, Come, and he comes; and to my slave, Do this, and he does it." The centurion had a great revelation that Jesus was a man under authority. The Lord said of him, "With no one in Israel have I found such great faith" (v. 10). We also need such a revelation that the One in us is a man under authority. What kind of man is in our spirit? He is a man under the authority of the Father's life, a man who takes the Father as His source and everything.
Christ did not speak His own word, and He did not do His own work (John 8:29; 12:49; 14:10). Likewise, we should not speak our own word. It is a glory not to speak our own word. It is a glory to speak the same thing, to speak what our Father has released through Brother Nee and Brother Lee. We should not want to speak anything else. The Lord spoke the Father's word, He did the Father's will, and He did not seek His own glory but the Father's glory. He did not do anything out of Himself. He was perfect, but He rejected that perfect human life and did everything by the Father. Everything that came out of Him had the Father as His source. He said, "The Son can do nothing from Himself except what He sees the Father doing, for whatever that One does, these things the Son also does in like manner" (John 5:19). May we be the same. May the Lord bring us into this kind of living.
Ephesians 4:6 says, "One God and Father of all, who is over all and through all and in all." When we take the Father as our source, this verse becomes our reality. It means that the Father is over us in a practical way; that is, we are under the ruling and control of His life. In such a condition, He has a way to be through us. On some streets there is a sign that says, "Not a through street." We must confess that many times there is such a sign in our being. The reason why there is no thoroughfare in us is that we do not allow the Father to be over us. In everything and in every detail we need to take Him as our source and be under His ruling and reigning within us. Then He will have a way to be through us and in us to fill us with His life. If we want to live out the Father as the source of life on the throne, a good practice is to set aside a definite time every day just to remain in the fellowship of the divine life with the Father. Many saints have been revived by this practice.
Living Out the Son as the Life and Life Supply, the Tree of Life
To have the triune living of the New Jerusalem in our daily life, we must also live out the Son as the life and life supply, the tree of life (Rev. 22:2; John 14:6). We live out the Father as the source of life on the throne in the fellowship of the divine life, and we live out the Son as the life and life supply, the tree of life, for His magnification. We need to receive the bountiful supply of the Spirit of Jesus Christ to eat Christ as the tree of life in a daily way for His magnification (Phil. 1:19-20). In the ministry we have had such a pattern among us, a pattern of one living an overcoming life in this way. Brother Lee kept a sign on the desk in his study, which said, "In nothing be put to shame." To live with this view is to be an overcomer. This is the way we need to be. To not magnify Him is to be put to shame. To not be put to shame is to magnify Him in all our environments and in all our circumstances. To do that, we need to eat Him. "He who eats Me shall live because of Me" (John 6:57). Eating Christ, that is, enjoying Christ as our life supply, should be the primary matter in the church life.
Living Out the Spirit as the Bountiful Supply of the Processed and Consummated Triune God, the Water of Life
We must also live out the Spirit as the bountiful supply of the processed and consummated Triune God, the water of life (Rev. 22:1; Phil. 1:19). This is for the supply of the holy city. The way to consummate the New Jerusalem is to eat the overcoming Lion-lamb as the tree of life, to drink the Spirit as the river of water of life every day, and to take the Triune God as the essence, element, and way to build up the New Jerusalem.
For Their Growth in the Divine Life by the Growth of God to Build Up the Body of Christ and Consummate the City of Life for the Ultimate Expansion, Enlargement, and Expression of the Triune God
All the foregoing items are for our growth in the divine life by the growth of God to build up the Body of Christ and consummate the city of life for the ultimate expansion, enlargement, and expression of the Triune God (Col. 2:19; Eph. 4:16). We need much prayer, study, and reciting so that we can get this heavenly vocabulary into us. Our living out the Father as the source of life in the fellowship of the divine life, living out the Son as the life and life supply, the tree of life, for His magnification, and living out the Spirit as the bountiful supply of the processed and consummated Triune God to be our all-inclusive nourishing supply to fill us and saturate us with all that God is, is for our growth in life, and our growth in life is the increase of God within us. Again we all have to confess that we are short of God. We need more God. We need to pray, "God, I want You to increase in me." This growth in life is the building up of the Body of Christ and consummates the New Jerusalem.
SEEING THE NEW JERUSALEM AS THE GREATEST AND ULTIMATE SIGN IN THE SCRIPTURES, SIGNIFYING THE ORGANIC CONSTITUTION AND UNIVERSAL, ENLARGED, DIVINE-HUMAN INCORPORATION OF THE PROCESSED TRIUNE GOD WITH HIS REGENERATED, TRANSFORMED, AND GLORIFIED TRIPARTITE ELECT
The highest point of the apostles' living is in seeing the New Jerusalem as the greatest and ultimate sign in the Scriptures, signifying the organic constitution and universal, enlarged, divine-human incorporation of the processed Triune God with His regenerated, transformed, and glorified tripartite elect (Rev. 21:2, 9-10). The first verse of the book of Revelation is the key to the whole book because it tells us that the Lord made known this revelation to John by signs (1:1). Signs are symbols with spiritual significance. The greatest symbol with the greatest spiritual significance is the New Jerusalem. There are millions of believers who do not see this great sign. We need to see this sign and become its reality, we need to propagate this great truth, and we need to get into this precious experience. In eternity, the only positive thing left in the universe will be the New Jerusalem. Everything else will go to the lake of fire. Why should we have another goal? Every other goal will go to the lake of fire. It is not logical to have another goal. The only thing that remains is God's ultimate goal, and His ultimate goal is this divine-human, universal incorporation of Himself as the processed and consummated Triune God with His forgiven, chosen, redeemed, regenerated, sanctified, renewed, transformed, conformed, and glorified tripartite elect. That will be all that is left, so it must be our career.
Living a Life of Incorporation with the Triune God by Their Abiding in Him as Their Temple and by His Abiding in Them as His Tabernacle
The apostles live a life of incorporation with the Triune God by their abiding in Him as their temple and by His abiding in them as His tabernacle (Rev. 21:3, 22; Gal. 2:20; 2 Cor. 12:2; John 15:5). First we need to see the sign of the New Jerusalem, and then by seeing it we are brought into such a living. What we see about the New Jerusalem is that it is a mutual abode. We are the tabernacle of God in whom God dwells, and He is the temple of God in whom we dwell. This is not just something for the future; we need to be in this mutual abiding day by day. This is why the Lord told us, "Abide in Me, and I in you" (John 15:4), and "If anyone loves Me, he will keep My word, and My Father will love him, and we will come to Him and make an abode with Him" (14:23). We need to be those who are loving the Lord to the uttermost and telling the Lord that we love Him. Day by day we should say, "Lord, renew my love for You today. I want my love for You to be fresh and new today, not stale and old. I want to love You to the uttermost to be in this mutual abiding."
Loving the Lord in the Closest and Most Intimate Fellowship with Him as the Constituents of the Wife of the Lamb
Such a living is in loving the Lord in the closest and most intimate fellowship with Him as the constituents of the wife of the Lamb (1 Pet. 1:8; 1 Cor. 2:9; 2 Cor. 5:14-15; 1 John 4:19). Another thing we should see about the New Jerusalem is that from one angle, it is the human wife of the redeeming God, and from another angle, it is the divine husband of God's redeemed elect. It is a wife-husband in which the husband and the wife coinhere. There has never been a husband and wife like this. For a husband to coinhere with his wife is to be absolutely one with her. For us to be in this relationship, we need to love the Lord in the closest and most intimate fellowship.
First Corinthians 2:9 says, "Things which eye has not seen and ear has not heard and which have not come up in man's heart; things which God has prepared for those who love Him." We want to be those who love Him, and this means we have to set our entire being absolutely on Him. We need to be occupied by Him, be lost in Him, and love Him with our whole being. Verse 10 tells us what happens when we love Him: "But to us God has revealed them through the Spirit, for the Spirit searches all things, even the depths of God." The word searches means active research. When we love the Lord in the closest and most intimate fellowship, the Spirit begins to do active research in us. The Spirit of God explores the depths of God concerning Christ and shows them to us in our spirit for our realization and participation. This experience is actually the experience of all the items of the New Jerusalem. May all of these points become our reality. We want to be those who are the lovers of the Lord so that we may be the constituents of the wife of the Lamb and practically live out the New Jerusalem. — E. M.
THE APOSTLES BEING DEIFIED TO BECOME THE NEW JERUSALEM, WHICH IS THE EMBODIMENT OF GOD'S COMPLETE SALVATION
In living out the New Jerusalem the apostles are deified to become the New Jerusalem, which is the embodiment of God's complete salvation, for them to become God in life, nature, and appearance but not in the Godhead. The New Jerusalem is the embodiment of God's complete salvation. Whenever we are in the process of God's complete salvation, we are joining the apostles to live out the New Jerusalem. The marvelous New Jerusalem mainly signifies the high peak of the truth, which is that God became man to make man God. The details of this city reveal and express this highest truth in God's eternal economy. The New Jerusalem is not a material, lifeless, physical city, but rather, a marvelous constitution of the Triune God with His elect in a universal incorporation of divinity with humanity. In every aspect of this constitution and this incorporation, there is the manifestation of God becoming man to make man God. We become God in life, nature, and appearance but not in the Godhead. The entire New Jerusalem is a testimony, an expression, and an exhibition that God became man to make man God. What a miracle! What a mystery! For us to live out the New Jerusalem in reality and practicality is to remain constantly in the process of God's complete salvation. Every moment we remain in this process, we are a practical participant in the marvelous New Jerusalem.
Through God's Judicial Redemption
The embodiment of God's complete salvation is first worked out through God's judicial redemption (Rev. 22:14a). This is the first aspect of God's complete salvation, and it must not be ignored or overlooked. Before we came into the Lord's recovery, many of us had no realization of the preciousness of the Lord's dear blood, of His all-inclusive judicial redemption. Today we more fully appreciate Christ's full ministry, which includes His precious judicial redemption accomplished through His death. Today we stand with boldness, not because of anything we have or can do, but because we have received this marvelous gift of righteousness. We may now sing, "I stand upon His merit; /I know no other stand" (Hymns, #206). We can be constituents of the New Jerusalem every day and live it out by passing through the enjoyment of God's judicial redemption. What a privilege today to wake up in the morning and lay our hands on the unique, beautiful head of our all-inclusive judicial Redeemer. He is our sin offering for what we are. He is our trespass offering for all the mistakes we make each day. He is also our burnt offering for what we never could be—satisfaction to God and absoluteness for God. As the first item of our complete salvation, this is very crucial. Every day we live out the New Jerusalem by enjoying His cleansing and His forgiveness. Judicial redemption includes justification, being approved according to the standard of God's righteousness that we may be as righteous as He is. It also includes reconciliation to God and positional sanctification to our dear God, whom we love. This is impossible apart from our marvelous judicial Redeemer. Revelation 22:14 says, "Blessed are those who wash their robes that they may have right to the tree of life and may enter by the gates into the city." God's judicial redemption qualifies and positions the believers to enjoy His organic salvation.
Through God's Organic Salvation
God's complete salvation is further worked out through God's organic salvation (Rom. 5:10). Through the opening of the revelation of God's organic salvation in recent years, our salvation has been renewed. Our salvation is not old and stale. It is wonderful that we were saved many years ago, but we must continue to be saved. If we continue to be saved, we will all be joyful. Every day and every hour we can be saved. Romans 5:10 says, "For if we, being enemies, were reconciled to God through the death of His Son, much more we will be saved in His life, having been reconciled."
Our burden is to live out the New Jerusalem in the present age by enjoying the organic salvation of our God. God's organic salvation is in a number of stages, so we have much salvation to enjoy. Because the New Jerusalem is the embodiment of God's complete salvation, to be constituents of the New Jerusalem and to join the apostles in being deified to live out the New Jerusalem, we must be those who constantly remain in the enjoyment of God's organic salvation. Our Brother Lee was a testimony to us of one who was constantly in the organic salvation, enjoying God's judicial redemption and being saved moment by moment in His organic salvation as a constituent of the New Jerusalem, living out the New Jerusalem. We want to be such persons. According to our earnest expectation and hope, we want to be put to shame in nothing, but with all boldness, as always, even now, we want Christ to be magnified in our body, whether through life or through death (Phil. 1:20). This was Paul's salvation (v. 19). We appreciate God's judicial redemption through His death, but much more we shall be saved in His life. The Lord's burden in these days as the age draws to a close is that day by day and hour by hour we would be persons freshly enjoying and constantly remaining in the process of all the stages of His organic salvation.
Regeneration to Become God's Species, God's Children
God's organic salvation includes regeneration to become God's species, God's children (John 1:12-13; Rev. 21:7; 22:14b). John 1:12 says, "But as many as received Him, to them He gave the authority to become children of God, to those who believe into His name." According to verse 13, these are children of God because they are born of God. We are here as God's species. To live out the New Jerusalem every day we must enjoy being children, not of our natural parents, but of God. We must enjoy the new, flowing life we have and live according to it.
When another life came into us, we experienced regeneration, but according to Titus 3:5 we also must have the washing of regeneration. This life is flowing, and every day we can be in the enjoyment of this flowing life. To be a person living out the New Jerusalem is to be a person drinking and enjoying the flowing of this life. This drinking and enjoying is "into eternal life" (John 4:14), which is the New Jerusalem. The phrase into eternal life refers to the New Jerusalem as the totality of the eternal life. When we experienced regeneration, we were born again. Another life came in, and it floods us until we are full of Him. Regeneration is the beginning of a flood. Our God as the eternal life has entered into us, and He is not only in us but also flowing in us to bring us through all the stages of His organic salvation until we become Him in life, nature, and appearance but not in the Godhead. What a precious thing it is ^o remain in the enjoyment of regeneration with its washing! We want to be those who are always drinking of this river of water of life.
Sanctification to Be as Holy as the Holy City
God's organic salvation includes our sanctification to be as holy as the holy city (1 Thess. 5:23; Eph. 5:26). First Thessalonians 5:23 says, "And the God of peace Himself sanctify you wholly, and may your spirit and soul and body be preserved complete, without blame, at the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ." We must be saturated and permeated by Him with His holy nature. To live out the New Jerusalem every day we have to preserve our spirit from deadness, always rejoicing in the Lord. We also have to preserve our soul from pollution by a thorough confession of all the negative matters which enter into our soul. We also have to preserve our body from all defilement by exercising concerning what we look at, what we listen to, what we speak, and what we touch. Such a preservation is to live out the New Jerusalem. Through this, His divine and holy nature is saturating us, sanctifying us, to make us as holy as the holy city.
Ephesians 5:26 says, "That He might sanctify her, cleansing her by the washing of the water in the word." In the Lord's recovery we are in the "bathtub" of God's word. As we read and pray-read the crystallization outlines and the life-studies, we are brought into the word. In the Lord's recovery we have been blessed to be brought deeply into the word and the washing of the water in the word that we may become as holy as the holy city. Ephesians 1:4 says we were chosen to be holy. To be holy means not only to be sanctified, separated to God, but also to be different, distinct from anything that is common, as God Himself is. Only God Himself is separate, different, distinct, and not common, but we were chosen to be the same as God. This is not a small matter. Every day we enjoy this One imparting His holy nature into our being. Second Peter 1:4 tells us that we are partakers of the divine nature through the precious promises. This means that we may become as holy as the holy city. Man becomes God first in life by regeneration and second in nature by His holy nature.
Renewing to Be as New as the New Jerusalem
God's organic salvation includes our renewing to be as new as the New Jerusalem (2 Cor. 4:16; Eph. 4:23). Often our co-workers or fellow students ask us what is new with us. We agree with Solomon that there is nothing new under the sun (Eccl. 1:9). Even if something seems to be new, it is old. The only newness in the entire universe is Christ. Anything without Christ is old, but everything with Christ is new. As those who exercise our mingled spirit, we are being constantly renewed in the spirit of our mind (Eph. 4:23). In this way, the newness of Christ as the Spirit with our spirit spreads into our whole being to make us new.
Transformation to Be Constituted with the Triune God as Gold, Silver (Pearl), and Precious Stones
God's organic salvation also includes transformation to be constituted with the Triune God as gold, silver (or pearl in the New Jerusalem), and precious stones (1 Cor. 3:12; 2 Cor. 3:18; Rom. 12:2). To live out the New Jerusalem is to enjoy living according to the Father's nature. Every day we partake of the Father's golden nature. We also enjoy this marvelous Christ who is the pearl and who is duplicating Himself to make us the many pearls so that many more could enter into this holy organic constitution. We become pearls by remaining in our permanent residence, the sweet death of Christ. When we are dead, all our problems are over; if you kick a dead man, he will not be bothered. The death of Christ is sweet because with this death is the death-overcoming and life-secreting resurrection to constitute us to be the pearls. We also become the precious stones by enjoying the Triune God every day as the life-giving Spirit that He might moment by moment and day by day change us from clay to stone, as Simon was changed to be Cephas (John 1:42), good for God's building, the New Jerusalem.
Conformation to the Image of the Firstborn Son of God to Have the Appearance of the New Jerusalem
God's organic salvation also includes conformation to the image of the firstborn Son of God to have the appearance of the New Jerusalem (Rom. 8:28-29). In Revelation 4:3 the One on the throne has the appearance of jasper, and in 21:11 the entire New Jerusalem has the same appearance. By His organic salvation the Lord is bringing us into the same appearance as His own. In Acts 3:1-5 as Peter and John were enjoying God's organic salvation on their way to the temple, they told the beggar, "Look at us!" What the beggar saw were persons similar in appearance to the living God. Likewise, in Acts 6:15 Stephen's face appeared as the face of an angel because he also was enjoying God's organic salvation and was being conformed to the image of the firstborn Son; that is, he was being deified.
Glorification to Be Completely Permeated with the Glory of the New Jerusalem
God's organic salvation includes glorification to be completely permeated with the glory of the New Jerusalem (Rom. 8:30; Phil. 3:21). Stanza 7 of hymn #501 in Hymns says, "Thy Spirit will me saturate, / Every part will God permeate." As Stephen was being stoned, he was being deified in God's organic salvation (Acts 7:55-59). Acts 7:55-56 says, "But being full of the Holy Spirit, he looked intently into heaven and saw the glory of God and Jesus standing at the right hand of God; and he said, Behold, I see the heavens opened up and the Son of Man standing at the right hand of God." At that time he was enjoying a sweet foretaste of glorification. He was becoming the duplication of the glorious God whom he was beholding. We also with unveiled face are beholding and reflecting as a mirror the glory of the Lord day by day and hour by hour that we may be transformed into the same image from glory to glory, even as from the Lord Spirit (2 Cor. 3:18).
Through God's Sevenfold Intensified Organic Salvation for Them to Be Intensified Sevenfold to Overcome the Degradation of the Church That the Body May Be Built Up to Consummate the New Jerusalem
The apostles are deified to become the New Jerusalem through God's sevenfold intensified organic salvation for them to be intensified sevenfold to overcome the degradation of the church that the Body may be built up to consummate the New Jerusalem (Rev. 1:4; 3:1; 4:5; 5:6). This stage of God's complete salvation is necessary because of the degradation of the church. Christ needs our cooperation in this dark, degraded age. Degradation is all around us, even in us. Every day we should pray, "Lord, don't let me remain in darkness and self-deceit." There is so much degradation, but thank the Lord that He is intensified sevenfold to carry out His organic salvation in an intensified way to make us God in life, nature, and appearance but not in the Godhead.
THE APOSTLES LIVING IN THE BLENDING OF THE UNIVERSAL BODY OF CHRIST TO BE USHERED INTO THE REALITY OF THE BODY OF CHRIST TO CONSUMMATE THE NEW JERUSALEM
The apostles live in the blending of the universal Body of Christ to be ushered into the reality of the Body of Christ to consummate the New Jerusalem (1 Cor. 12:24; 10:17; Lev. 2:4-5). When we gather as the churches, we have the blending. We gather as the Body, not caring for regions, localities, districts, or countries. We care for the unique Body of Christ to consummate the New Jerusalem. Because of this the Lord pours Himself out and moves within us as the sevenfold intensified Spirit. He is the unique Spirit in His existence, but those who love Him with the first love and those who care uniquely for the Body of Christ enjoy Him as the intensified Spirit. If we do not care for the Body, we do not enjoy the sevenfold Spirit. We hope to become more lost in the Body. We may still be individual lumps. We must have more blending that we may experience the Lord in a sevenfold intensified way so that we could become Him in life, nature, and appearance but not in the Godhead.
Not only do the apostles constantly remain in the process of God's organic salvation, but they live in the blending to live out the New Jerusalem. We need to live in the blending. To live in the blending is to live where there is the adjusting, the tempering, the harmonizing, and the mingling. We live in the blending by doing everything through the cross, by the Spirit, to dispense Christ for the sake of the Body. Whatever we do and say, we must dispense Christ into others for the building up, not of a work or locality, but of the Body of Christ. Blending is so precious. It is to go through the cross, to go by the Spirit, and to dispense Christ into others for the building up of the Body to consummate the New Jerusalem. For this we need fellowship, and in this fellowship we who are the many grains are ground properly in the divine life through the cross by the Spirit to become one loaf.
God has blended the Body together (1 Cor. 12:24). This blending is like that of bread. First Corinthians 10:17 says we are one bread, one Body, and in the type in Leviticus 2 the blending is the mingling of the fine flour with the oil, producing a cake that is so delicious to God and edible to man. The Body of Christ is a loaf, and our ultimate consummation, the New Jerusalem, is a cake. This cake is the mingling of the Triune God with His redeemed, regenerated, transformed, and glorified elect as the fine flour. These two have been mingled together, even to the point of incorporation, coinhering each other. To live out the New Jerusalem is to remain in the living of the blending. Blending is the way of the New Jerusalem.
Colossians 3:11 tells us that the new man is just Christ. Ultimately, the New Jerusalem is just Christ, the Son of Man as the upright standing ladder (John 1:51), a blending of divinity with humanity. The New Jerusalem is the giant meal offering formed from the blending of God with all of His people, and this cake is just Christ. In his letter of fellowship with thanks written on March 22, Brother Lee told us that he was our "fellow partaker in experiencing and gaining Christ." Our living is the living of Christ, as Paul said, "For to me, to live is Christ" (Phil. 1:21). The New Jerusalem ultimately is such a blending, which is Christ blended with all of His believers. The Bible begins with God, but the Bible ends with God expanded. Christ is the meal offering, but by the end of the Bible He is the expanded meal offering. Every day we enjoy this Christ as our food supply, and we are those who enjoy living in the blending for the sake of the testimony of the universal Body of Christ. We are being ushered into the reality of the Body; that is, we are living in the resurrection life of Christ to consummate the New Jerusalem. Hallelujah for such a living! — D. T.

BROTHER LEE'S FELLOWSHIP ON APRIL 6, 1997
The following is Brother Lee's fellowship given on April 6, 1997:
The co-workers must see that we should only do one work, which is to make God's chosen people regenerated ones, sanctified ones, renewed ones—-the new man—transformed ones, conformed ones—those conformed to the image of the firstborn Son of God—and glorified ones. All those who will be in the New Jerusalem are this kind of people.
Specifically, God proceeds step by step to make a chosen one of God a person who is regenerated, sanctified, renewed, transformed, conformed to the image of the firstborn Son of God, and even glorified by God. In this way, we go up level by level until we reach the highest point, where we become the same. There is no more flesh and no more natural being. All are in the spirit. All are the kingdom of the heavens, and all are beings of the New Jerusalem. This is the highest point. If you understand this, you can explain it to the brothers and sisters, and you can ask them to speak the same.
This way of expression is very high and sophisticated. It is a continuation of what we have been speaking before, yet it is a going further. This is the highest point. As we go step by step, ascending level by level, eventually we reach the highest point, where there is no more flesh and no more natural being, and all are in the spirit. That is the New Jerusalem. I believe the blending brothers will do a good job, speaking according to my speaking. Then, all the elders and co-workers will see how they should carry out their work, what work they should do, and what goal they should attain. They should no longer do their own work.They should only do the work of the New Jerusalem.
You need to get into these messages gradually. The vision is so high. You need to get into them point by point. I hope the Christians outside of the Lord's recovery can also see this. I hope all the co-workers in the whole world can see this.
GOING UP TO REACH THE HIGHEST POINT
From the above fellowship, we can see that all the co-workers and elders should have only one work. There may be different regions and languages in the work, but we have only one work. The New Testament shows us that although there are different apostles, they all do the same work, and that one work is the work of the New Jerusalem. In order to have a part in the one work, we must go on "step by step, ascending level by level" until we reach that highest point, which is the New Jerusalem. These steps begin by our being chosen and predestinated by God. However, God's intention does not stop there. His intention is to produce persons through His organic salvation who are not just good, godly, moral, or even spiritual, but "beings of the New Jerusalem." God is making New Jerusalem persons, those who are regenerated, sanctified, renewed, transformed, conformed to the image of the firstborn Son of God, and glorified. All these precious truths as the high peak of the divine revelation have been opened to us year by year in the Lord's recovery as the culmination of all of the ministry of Brother Nee and Brother Lee.
THE APOSTLES LIVING OUT THE REALITY OF THE GOLD BASE OF THE NEW JERUSALEM FOR ITS BUILDING
The base of the New Jerusalem is a great mountain of solid gold. This is a real vision and a revelation. In the Bible, gold signifies the divine nature of God. The fact that the New Jerusalem is a mountain of gold means that it is fully constituted with God's divine nature. In regeneration we partake of God's divinity, which means that we share in His divine nature (2 Pet. 1:4). That is the beginning of our partaking of God's divine nature, but it is only the beginning; the process does not stop with regeneration. The divine nature grows and develops through the stage of sanctification, which is to be sanctified in our nature. It also passes through the stage of renewing, which is the renewal of our mind. Following this, it passes through the stage of transformation, which is to be transformed in our whole being. This process continues until we become glorified. To be glorified means that God's divinity breaks out of our entire being, and we express the Triune God completely in our tripartite being. Hallelujah for such a wonderful process!
This process does not merely make us individual, spiritual persons, but it makes us the constituents, the members, the "beings of the New Jerusalem." This is to go on "step by step, ascending level by level." In the Lord's recovery we do not talk about how to behave in a proper way as a godly or moral person. Neither do we merely talk about how to be spiritual. Rather, we talk about how to become God, how to partake of His divine nature. We go on in this way step by step and level by level until we reach the highest point. At the highest point we become the same. There is no other way that we can become the same except by becoming "beings of the New Jerusalem." In these days we are very much exercised that we be in one accord, and we have received many messages about the blending. However, the only way that we can be the same is if we are the same in God's organic salvation, that is, if we are regenerated, sanctified, renewed, transformed, conformed, and glorified. If we are different, it is because we have the flesh, the natural being, and we are not in the spirit. If we have no more flesh, no more natural being, and we are all in the spirit, then we are all "the kingdom of the heavens," and we are all "beings of the New Jerusalem." This truly is the highest point.
This truth is what Paul, John, and Peter spoke in all their Epistles. In the New Testament, the ultimate goal is just to reach this highest point, which is the New Jerusalem. If we understand this, we can explain it to the brothers and sisters, and we can ask them to speak the same. Let us speak the same thing. If we all speak the New Jerusalem, we will be speaking the same thing. This is actually a continuation of what our brother spoke before. However, sometimes we are like the two disciples on the way to Emmaus (Luke 24:13-35); the Lord spoke much to them, but their understanding was dull. These things are a continuation of what has been spoken in the ministry since 1994. Brother Lee's burden, vision, utterance, speaking, conferences, and messages all lead to this one thing. Everything in the New Testament has Christ as the center, the Body of Christ as the line, and the New Jerusalem as the goal. All the high-peak truths concerning God's organic salvation, blending, and even incorporation lead to this one goal, which is the New Jerusalem. This goal is the apostles' living and work.
The present ministry is a continuation, but it is also a further development. This kind of speaking is very high and very sophisticated. Instead of preaching the low gospel to unbelievers and speaking the low things to new believers, we should speak to them about the New Jerusalem. We can use the New Jerusalem to preach the gospel, to nourish the new ones, and to take care of all the new believers. May we all strive to get into these messages. As we go on step by step, ascending level by level, eventually we will reach this highest point where there is no more flesh, no more natural being, and all are in the spirit.
The base of the entire New Jerusalem is a golden mountain. There may be a certain amount of gold in our living and work today, but it needs to increase more and more until it becomes a mountain. Beginning from regeneration, a little more of God's divinity is added into us. Renewing adds a little more of God's divinity into our mind. Sanctification adds more of God's divinity into our nature, and transformation adds more of God's divinity into our whole being. This process goes on and on until we reach the highest peak. The New Jerusalem is a mountain with the highest peak, where there is the throne. Out of this throne is the street, and in the middle of the street is a river of life that supplies and nourishes.
Partaking of and Being Constituted with the Divine Nature of God, the Divine Element of the New Jerusalem
The apostles partake of and are constituted with the divine nature of God, the divine element of the New Jerusalem (Rev. 21:18b; 2 Pet. 1:4). Nothing other than God's divine nature will make it to the New Jerusalem. It does not matter how good our old nature is; it will never make it to the New Jerusalem. Therefore, there is the need of the various steps of God's organic salvation. As we experience the various steps of God's organic salvation, we share in the divine nature of God, and as we share in this divine nature and partake of it more and more, we have more and more of the divine element of the New Jerusalem in us.
Doing Everything according to the Divine Nature of God, Taking the Divine Nature of God as Their Pathway, to Be under the Ruling of God's Golden Administration
The apostles do everything according to the divine nature of God, taking the divine nature of God as their pathway, to be under the ruling of God's golden administration (Rev. 21:21; 22:1). The result of being constituted with and partaking of the ,divine nature of God is that we partake of and are brought under the ruling of God's golden administration. The throne and the street are not apart from the base. Rather, the throne is part of the base. The great mountain ascends until at the top it is the throne with the street where the river is flowing. The growth in the organic salvation and our participation in and constitution with the divine nature of God culminates in God's golden administration. The experience of God's organic salvation equals our reigning in life, and the reigning in life is the highest point.
The Divine Life Flowing in the Divine Nature Being the Unique Way for Their Daily Life
The divine life flowing in the divine nature is the unique way for the apostles' daily life. This is not a doctrine; it is something that we experience day by day. Paul said, "Let no corrupt word proceed out of your mouth, but only that which is good for building up, according to the need, that it may give grace to those who hear" (Eph. 4:29). We should not let any corrupt word come out of our mouth in our daily life and especially in our family life. We do not let a corrupt word come out of our mouth not because we are moral but because we are children of God. It is because we are children of the light (Eph. 5:8) that we act according to this divine nature, and this divine nature will not let any corrupt word come out of our mouth. God gives us a spouse to test us day by day to see whether we are living in the divine nature. It is very easy to not live in the divine nature. Christians today have a very low standard. Many say that they act according to God's Word, but there is something more than that. We must act not merely according to the black and white letter of God's Word but according to God's nature. If we act according to God's divine nature, no corrupt word will come out of our mouth.
Practicing the Divine Fellowship Based upon the Golden Nature within Them
The apostles practice the divine fellowship based upon the golden nature within them (1 John 1:3). As we grow in this organic salvation to become the regenerated, sanctified, renewed, and transformed ones, we partake of the one divine fellowship. This fellowship has nothing to do with the natural realm. Because the apostle John was daily in this fellowship, he wrote his Epistle to bring the believers into the same fellowship. This fellowship is not a fellowship of like-mindedness or a fellowship of like-flesh. It is a fellowship in the Father and in His Son Jesus Christ. In other words, it is a fellowship in the divine nature. If we see this wonderful revelation, it will govern and limit us. It will kill all the activities that come out of the natural being and the flesh. May we all be such persons practicing the divine fellowship.
THE APOSTLES LIVING OUT THE REALITY OF THE PEARL GATES OF THE NEW JERUSALEM FOR ITS ENTRY
Pearls Signifying the Issue of Christ's Secretion in Two Aspects: His Redeeming and Life-releasing Death and His Life-dispensing Resurrection
Pearls in the New Jerusalem signify the issue of Christ's secretion in two aspects: His redeeming and life-releasing death and His life-dispensing resurrection (Rev. 21:21a; John 12:24; 1 Cor. 15:45b). Christ's death not only has a judicial aspect; there is a life-releasing aspect to His death. On the cross Christ was pierced in His side, and out came blood and water (John 19:34). Blood takes care of the judicial requirement, while water, signifying life, takes care of the organic need. Christ's death was not merely a judicial dealing and settlement; it was a secretion in which something issued forth. That issuing forth is the life-releasing element of His death. Pearls are produced when sand enters into an oyster. The sand irritates the oyster, and the oyster secretes something out of its own life, producing a pearl. This is a picture of our experience in Christ. As we hide in Christ, sometimes even irritating Him, something issues forth. Such a secretion is the life-releasing death which produces the pearls for the gates of the New Jerusalem.
Daily Experiencing the Death of Christ Subjectively by the Power of Christ's Resurrection That They May Be Conformed to the Death of Christ
The apostles daily experience the death of Christ subjectively by the power of Christ's resurrection that they may be conformed to the death of Christ (Phil. 3:10; 1 Cor. 15:31b; Rom. 8:13; Gal. 5:24-25). The book of Philippians shows that Paul was a person who daily experienced the death of Christ and the,, resurrection of Christ. His experience of the death of Christ was carried out in the power of Christ's resurrection (3:10). We can only experience Christ's death in the power of His resurrection, and we can only experience resurrection in the life-giving Spirit. Stanza 2 of #481 in Hymns says, "'Tis not hard to die with Christ / When His risen life we know." We can only know the death of Christ as we know His risen power, that is, the resurrection power. It is in His resurrection power that daily we can abide in the death of Christ. The way we talk to our spouse is a test of our experience of the death of Christ. To abide in the death of Christ is to not speak rashly when we are irritated and to say, "Lord, forgive me for how I speak to my wife." One who apologizes to his wife for his speaking is a testimony of living in the death of Christ by the power of His resurrection.
Daily Experiencing the Resurrection of Christ Subjectively by the Bountiful Supply of the Spirit of Jesus Christ That They May Be Conformed to the Image of the Firstborn Son of God
The apostles also daily experience the resurrection of Christ subjectively by the bountiful supply of the Spirit of Jesus Christ that they may be conformed to the image of the firstborn Son of God (Phil. 1:19; Rom. 8:29). The life-giving Spirit is the reality of resurrection. Without the life-giving Spirit, resurrection is merely a theory with no reality. The only time we can experience resurrection is when we are in the life-giving Spirit. In the life-giving Spirit as the reality of resurrection we experience the resurrection of Christ. Through His death we are conformed to the death of Christ, and by His resurrection we are conformed to the image of the firstborn Son of God.
Being Made Pearls to Be the Gates for Repentant Sinners' Entry into the New Jerusalem as the Ultimate Consummation of God's Eternal Economy
The apostles are made pearls to be the gates for repentant sinners' entry into the New Jerusalem as the ultimate consummation of God's eternal economy (Rev. 21:12b-13). The pearls become a wonderful entry. We should always appreciate this entry. This entry was established by Christ in His death and resurrection. This entry is universal because it faces all four sides. It involves the Triune God because each side has three gates, and it is widely open to invite and usher people into the consummation of God's eternal economy. This is not just salvation to heaven. This is the entry that Christ has afforded us into the New Jerusalem. We should preach this entry wherever we are. —A. Y.
THE APOSTLES LIVING OUT THE REALITY OF THE WALL OF THE NEW JERUSALEM
To live out the reality of the wall of the New Jerusalem is a matter in the divine and mystical realm. We have to be in the divine and mystical realm to see these matters. This wall is a stone wall; it is not something merely created but something that has gone through a transformation.
Its Wall Being of Jasper, a Precious Stone Signifying the Appearance of God, Indicating That the Entire City Bears the Appearance of God in His Glory, Having the Glory of God
The wall of the New Jerusalem is of jasper, a precious stone signifying the appearance of God, indicating that the entire city bears the appearance of God in His glory, having the glory of God (Rev. 4:3a; 21:11, 18a). In Revelation 4:3 jasper refers to God Himself, but in 21:11 it refers to the entire New Jerusalem. Because the wall is of jasper, jasper is what you see when you come to the New Jerusalem. Jasper is a dark green stone, signifying life in its richness. It is not created that way but becomes that way through a process. For the apostles to live out this wall means that they are fully in the riches of God's life. Since the apostles are the representatives of all the churches, the churches must live the same way the apostles live. To have the appearance, the testimony, of jasper is to have the very appearance of God. God is jasper, the city is jasper, and the wall is jasper. This simply means that the wall is the same as God in appearance.
Being under the Continuous Transforming Work of the Transforming Spirit to Be Living and Precious Stones for God's Building
The apostles are under the continuous transforming work of the transforming Spirit to be living and precious stones for God's building (1 Pet. 2:4-5). We are stones for God's building. The apostles were first clay, but they were transformed into stones. To have the living out of the wall of the New Jerusalem means that we are going through a continuous transformation to have an appearance which is no longer of clay but of jasper, rich with the divine life. Revelation 4:3 indicates that the appearance of God is also like sardius, a deep red stone, which signifies judicial redemption. Jasper and sardius go together. We experience not only judicial redemption but all the aspects of His organic salvation. Together they cause us to become jasper as the appearance of God, the reality of the wall.
Through this transformation in the divine nature, all of our different concepts and views are swallowed up. By this transforming work we are not only blended, but we are becoming so one with the Lord that we have His very appearance. This appearance is a mark that marks us out from the rest of fallen humanity. We are going through a process of transformation all the time. Everything that happens to us and every situation and problem we have are working out a transformation in us to change our appearance.
The whole city has the same appearance. There is only one city, and there is only one wall, but the wall is on four sides. It is not one kind of stone on one side and another kind of stone on the other side. All of its sides have the same appearance. We are in the process of becoming the New Jerusalem by living out all the aspects of all the items in it. We are becoming the wall, and the wall has an appearance. That appearance does a lot for us. It protects us and separates us from all the worldly things. It makes us appear different and brings us more and more into the expression of the Triune God. The process of transformation is by the continuous work of the Spirit.
Expressing the Triune God Embodied in Christ in Their Living, Bearing His Appearance
The apostles express the Triune God embodied in Christ in their living, bearing His appearance (Phil. l:20-21a). All of God's work is to bring us into the experience of living out this wall, which is the experience of the Triune God. In Philippians 1:20 Paul desired that in nothing he would be put to shame, whether through life or through death. Thank the Lord that among us we have seen a pattern of one who appeared as a jasper stone; we were with him and watched the appearance of God being worked out in him. May we all be like this. We would not be satisfied merely with our redemption and a certain measure of renewing. We desire a continuous transformation until we all have the appearance of jasper and have a living that expresses God.
Giving God the Glory in All Things and Doing All to the Glory of God
The apostles lived out the reality of the wall by giving God the glory in all things and doing all to the glory of God (Eph. 3:21; 1 Cor. 10:31; 6:20; 1 Pet. 4:11).
The Jasper Wall Being for the Separation and Protection of the New Jerusalem
The jasper wall is for the separation and protection of the New Jerusalem. When we live this kind of life, we are living out the Father, the Son, and the Spirit, and we are experiencing the transforming work of the Triune God. As this appearance becomes more and more evident in us, we are separated from the world, and we are protected. In one sense the wall is a separation from everything worldly, but in another sense it is a real protection. The more the appearance of this wall is worked out in our living, the more we are protected from all the evil things of the world. There is no room for the enemy to come in to damage us. Rather, whatever happens to us is for the working out of more of this jasper expression. The divine expression is our separation, and this separation is our protection and our preservation. To live out the wall of the New Jerusalem means that we are being transformed to have an expression that really protects us and separates us.
Being Separated unto God from All Things Other Than God Positionally That They May Be Saturated with God Dispositionally to Be Holy as God is Holy
The apostles are separated unto God from all things other than God positionally that they may be saturated with God dispositionally to be holy as God is holy (1 Pet. 1:15-16; 2 Cor. 6:14—7:1; 1 Thes. 5:23). When the expression of God separates us, we also begin to experience the saturation. Perhaps our greatest need is to deal with our disposition. Our disposition is the greatest and most frustrating problem. The source of rebellions is usually the dispositions of the individuals involved. We have all been separated positionally, but the extent to which we have been saturated is the extent to which we are dispositionally dealt with. Our disposition,, is composed of all kinds of cultural differences plus what we were born with. Our disposition, however, does not have to be a hindrance. If we live out the reality of the wall of the New Jerusalem, our disposition will be changed. The biggest problem that causes the most difficulties concerning our oneness, our one accord, the blending, and the Lord's progress is our natural disposition. Therefore, we really need the transforming work, and we need the saturating life to fill us every day, to make us holy as God is holy. If we are this kind of person, living out the reality of all the aspects of the New Jerusalem, surely there will be no problem. We will be one and the same. We have to open ourselves all the time that we would not cling to something that merely satisfies our way, and we must learn always to be not only delivered and protected but also saturated, that we may become holy as God is holy.
Protecting the Interests of the Riches of God's Divinity on the Earth and the Attainments of His Consummation
The wall of the New Jerusalem is for protecting the interests of the riches of God's divinity on the earth and the attainments of His consummation (1 Cor. 15:12; 1 John 2:22). Without the Lord's recovery there would be little protection for the interests of the riches of God's divinity on the earth. Many of the riches of God's divinity are not even known except in the Lord's recovery, where so many of the riches have been brought to us. Unless these riches produce in us the reality of the wall of the New Jerusalem, there will be no protection for them. They will be torn down in many ways and treated as our own amusement or gain as if they were nothing. By our being the wall of the New Jerusalem and our living out this wall, there is a real protection for all the riches of God's divinity that have been given to us. That in the Lord's recovery we live in the spirit and are one with the Lord and that we have the work of the Triune God going on within us and blending us together as one are a real protection for the riches that have been dispensed into His Body. We must speak the pure truth from the Word to protect the interests of God's divinity.
The wall of the city also functions to protect the attainments of Christ's consummation. God in Christ consummated so many things. He consummated incarnation, death, the resurrection, and ascension. There is the need of the wall of the New Jerusalem to protect the attainments of His consummation. We must also speak these things.
The Jasper Wall Being Measured "according to the Measure of a Man, That Is, of an Angel," Signifying That the Wall of the City Is Not Natural but in Resurrection
That the jasper wall is measured "according to the measure of a man, that is, of an angel," signifies mat the wall of the city is not natural but in resurrection, since it is in resurrection that man will be like the angels (Rev. 21:17; Matt. 22:30). Everything related to the living out of the reality of the wall is something in resurrection, not in our natural man or our natural way.
Living in Resurrection by Rejecting Their Natural Life and Living by the Divine Life
The apostles live in resurrection by rejecting their natural life and living by the divine life. We must be divinely human, and our living must be "no longer I...but...Christ" (Gal. 2:20). It must be God's own attributes lived out in our human virtues for the Lord to have a living that expresses Him.
Living a Crucified Life for the Manifestation of the Resurrection Life by the Power of the Treasure in Their Earthen Vessels
The apostles live a crucified life for the manifestation of the resurrection life by the power of the treasure in their earthen vessels (2 Cor. 4:7-12). It is one thing to claim, "I am crucified with Christ," but it is another matter to always be delivered unto death. Because the vessel for the treasure is earthen, it is always being delivered unto death. Second Corinthians 4:7-12 says, "But we have this treasure in earthen vessels that the excellency of the power may be of God and not out of us. We are pressed on every side but not constricted; unable to find a way out but not utterly without a way out; persecuted but not abandoned; cast down but not destroyed; always bearing about in the body the putting to death of Jesus that the life of Jesus also may be manifested in our body. For we who are alive are always being delivered unto death for Jesus' sake that the life of Jesus also may be manifested in our mortal flesh. So then death operates in us, but life in you." The goal of this experience is that while death is operating in us, life is operating in someone else. This comes about by the treasure that we have in this earthen vessel and by our always bearing about in the body the putting to death of Jesus that the life of Jesus may be made manifest. Verse 11 speaks of "we who are alive." When we bear about the death of Jesus in our body, we are living, and when we are alive, we are always being delivered unto death for Jesus' sake that the life of Jesus might be manifested in our mortal flesh. If death is operating in us, life will be operating in someone else. This delivers us from our self.
THE APOSTLES LIVING OUT THE REALITY OF THE FOUNDATIONS OF THE NEW JERUSALEM
The Foundations of the Holy City Being the Twelve Apostles of the Lamb
The apostles live out the reality of the foundations of the New Jerusalem. The foundations of the holy city are the twelve apostles of the Lamb (Rev. 21:14). In the New Jerusalem the Old Testament prophets are over. The New Testament apostles are the foundation of the city.
The Revelation of the Mystery of Christ Which the Apostles Received Being the Foundation upon Which the Church Is Built, and the Apostles Representing the Grace of the New Testament and Living and Having Their Being by the Grace of God
The revelation of the mystery of Christ which the apostles received is the foundation upon which the church is built (Eph. 2:20; 3:5-6; Matt. 16:18). All the apostles were created as clay, but they were regenerated and transformed into precious stones. This makes them the real foundation for the building up of the New Jerusalem. The apostles represent the grace of the New Testament and live and have their being by the grace of God (1 Pet. 4:10; 1 Cor. 15:10; John 1:16).
The Foundations Being in the Appearance of a Rainbow Signifying That the Entire City Is Built upon the Faithfulness of God
The foundations of the New Jerusalem are in the appearance of a rainbow, signifying that the entire city is built upon the faithfulness of God (Rev. 21:19-20; Gen. 9:8-17). All the points concerning the living out of the wall and the foundation indicate the need for the real blending so that we all may have" the same expression and manifestation. There is not one manifestation for one region and another manifestation for another region. We all are doing one work, and we are going one way. We are expressing one jasper stone outwardly because we are filled with the gold inwardly. The more we are filled with the gold inwardly, the more we express the jasper outwardly.
Receiving Mercy of the Lord to Be Faithful
The apostles receive mercy of the Lord to be faithful. This is how the apostle Paul spoke of himself. Even in giving his opinion he said he was, "one who has been shown mercy by the Lord to be faithful" (1 Cor. 7:25).
Being One with the Faithful God to Be Faithful Stewards of God's Eternal Economy
The apostles are also one with the faithful God to be faithful stewards of God's eternal economy (1 Cor. 4:2). We need the Lord's mercy to be faithful to God's intention, God's purpose. We are in the stage in the Lord's recovery in which so much has been opened up that it seems there is no major truth left to be opened. All the pieces of the "jigsaw puzzle" are in place. We are in a very crucial time and a very marvelous situation. We are living out all the aspects of the New Jerusalem. In the Lord's recovery the church should really be the New Jerusalem. To the extent that we live in the reality of all these items, God is expressed. To the extent that we are saturated with Him, His glory is expressed. Our greatest needs are a fresh consecration and much blending together that the Lord may have a way to bring us into one accord, so much in harmony with His purpose and His goal, that we may fully realize that there is nothing else to do, no place else to go, and no other goal to look for. There is no other ambition to have and no other goal for which to strive but to gain Christ and reach the goal of the New Jerusalem. May the Lord be with us that we may be those faithful to God, faithful stewards for God's eternal economy. May the Lord bless us and have His way. God's eternal economy has the New Jerusalem as the goal. Everything we do and all of our daily living is to bring about in us this kind of oneness with the Lord, oneness with His purpose, and oneness with the goal of the New Jerusalem. Anything other than this is a distraction. — E B.

The Highest Point of the Apostles' Living—-to Live Out the New Jerusalem (3)

IX.　The apostles living out the reality of the throne of the New Jerusalem:
A.　Honoring God on the throne as their unique source of supply and blessirg -Rom. 11:33-36.
B.　Subjecting themsch ts to the headship of the redeeming God in everything that they may continually enjoy His divine dispensing in everything—Rev. 22:1.

第三篇　使徒们生活的最高点─活出新耶路撒冷（三）
The Highest Point of the Apostles' Living—-to Live Out the New Jerusalem (3)

（本文为英文听抄扫描所得，未经校对）
The general subject of these messages is the "New Jerusalem—The Highest Point of the Apostles' Living and Work." This kind of speaking is mysterious and difficult to explain. The best explanation is Brother Lee's fellowship given on April 6, 1997. This fellowship shows how strong his burden was on the living out of the New Jerusalem. We should memorize this fellowship because in it we can see the highest point of our living and work.
THE APOSTLES LIVING OUT THE REALITY OF THE THRONE OF THE NEW JERUSALEM
The apostles live out the reality of the throne of the New Jerusalem. Revelation 22:1-2 says, "And he showed me a river of water of life, bright as crystal, proceeding out of the throne of God and of the Lamb in the middle of its street. And on this side and on that side of the river was the tree of life, producing twelve fruits, yielding its fruit each month; and the leaves of the tree are for the healing of the nations."
Honoring God on the Throne as Their Unique Source of Supply and Blessing
The apostles honor God on the throne as their unique source of supply and blessing (Rom. 11:33-36). All of us who have been chosen by God and regenerated are "little New Jerusalems," and the church life today is the miniature New Jerusalem. The kind of person we should be, the condition we should have, and the condition the church life should have are all seen in the New Jerusalem. In the New Jerusalem, the center is the throne, and that throne is the throne of God and of the Lamb. Do not think that God is God, the Lamb is the Lamb, and these two sit on two thrones. God and the Lamb are one. God is the God who has an eternal purpose and economy, and the Lamb is the
One who redeemed us and who is carrying out God's eternal economy. The throne is the throne of God and the Lamb, that is, the throne of the redeeming God who has redeemed us back to Himself. Out of this throne there is a golden street, and in the middle of the golden street is the river of water of life. Growing on the two sides of this river is the tree of life, who is Christ, the true vine. The branches of this vine tree, the tree of life, spread over both sides of the river. Everyone who wants to eat of the tree can come to it. The street signifies the divine nature of God the Father, the river is the all-inclusive life-giving Spirit, and the tree is Christ. Here we see the Triune God flowing out of the throne.
We honor the Triune God on the throne as our unique source of supply and blessing. In our daily life, we have many different needs. Sometimes we feel we need strength. Other times we feel we need wisdom, light, and many other things. However, we should realize that we only have one unique source of supply. When we sense that we have a need, we must not go to our natural man or any other source to look for the supply. We only have one source of supply—the Triune God flowing out of His throne.
Subjecting Themselves to the Headship of the Redeeming God in Everything That They May Continually Enjoy His Divine Dispensing in Everything
The apostles subject themselves to the headship of the redeeming God in everything that they may continually enjoy His divine dispensing in everything (Rev. 22:1). Since the Triune God is our unique supply, we constantly enjoy Him. We need to realize that this supply comes out of the throne. Therefore, we must subject ourselves to the headship of the Triune God. Many Christians may think that the throne and the enjoyment of God are two separate, even mutually exclusive, things. They may suppose that if we want to enjoy the Lord, it is better to forget about the throne, and that because the throne is frightening, it is difficult to have the enjoyment of the throne. However, the real divine enjoyment comes from the throne. This is our experience. The more we subject ourselves to the throne and put ourselves under the authority of the Triune God, the more we sense the flow, the supply, and the enjoyment of God. When we refuse to obey Him, we remain dry within; there is no water, no river, and no tree of life to enjoy. The picture of the New Jerusalem is very experiential. If we submit ourselves to this throne, to the headship of the redeeming God, we will have His constant infusion and dispensing to enjoy Him continuously.
Being under the Ruling of the Divine Life to Reign in Life with Grace over All Things unto Eternal Life
The apostles are under the ruling of the divine life to reign in life with grace over all things unto eternal life (Rom. 5:17, 21). To reign in life is simply to put ourselves under the ruling of the divine life. It is not that we have the authority in ourselves to reign, but that we are under the ruling of the divine life. If we are not being ruled by the divine life, we are not able to reign in life. Therefore, to be under the ruling of the divine life equals reigning in life.
Continually Coming Forward to the Throne of Grace to Receive Mercy and Find Grace for Timely Help
The apostles continually come forward to the throne of grace to receive mercy and find grace for timely help (Heb. 4:16). In the whole universe there is only one throne. To the universe it is the throne of authority, but to the believers, it is the throne of grace. Hebrews 4:16 tells us that we can constantly come to the throne of grace. When we come to the throne of grace, we receive mercy. Even if our condition is not worthy to receive God's grace, His mercy still can reach us. We can come to Him as we are at any time or place to receive mercy and enjoy His grace for our timely help.
Living a Daily Life of the New Jerusalem by Enthroning the Triune God in the Center of Their Being to Enjoy the Spirit as the River of Water of Life and Christ as the Tree of Life with the Father in His Divine, Golden Nature for the Divine Fellowship
The apostles live a daily life of the New Jerusalem by enthroning the Triune God in the center of their being to enjoy the Spirit as the river of water of life and Christ as the tree of life with the Father in His divine, golden nature for the divine fellowship (Rev. 22:1-2). If we put all these five points together, the picture of New Jerusalem will be clear to us. At the center of the New Jerusalem there is the throne of God and the Lamb, that is, the throne of the redeeming God. Coming out of this throne is the golden street, the river of water of life, and the tree of life, all signifying the Triune God. Whenever we subject ourselves to the headship of the Triune God, we drink the river and we eat the tree. However, when we disregard the throne, the river stops. We can no longer find the river in us, and we do not have anything to eat. How crucial it is for us to be under the ruling of the divine life! Since the throne is at the center of the New Jerusalem and we are the "little New Jerusalems," this throne should be in the center of our being. In our daily life, family life, and church life, when we go to work or when we go to school, the throne must be the center in our being. It is no problem for the sisters to go shopping; the problem comes when they shop apart from the throne. Often when the sisters enter the department store, they leave the throne outside the store. Whenever we leave the throne outside the door, there is no more river of life, and we do not sense or enjoy the tree of life.
Sometimes we try to keep the throne in our being, but either consciously or unconsciously, we force the Triune God, the redeeming God, off the throne, and we put ourselves on the throne. Once we put ourselves on the throne, the throne is no longer the throne of God and of the Lamb; it becomes our throne. Then the river stops, and the tree does not grow anymore; we dry up and even starve to death spiritually. Often when we sense the dryness and spiritual starvation, we try to look for excuses. We may say that we are busy, we have too many things to do, we do not get enough sleep, or we are bearing the problems in the church. We should not say this. The main reason we sense dryness or spiritual starvation is that either consciously or unconsciously we have put ourselves on the throne. Once the throne is no longer the throne of the redeeming God, there is no river and no tree. However, when we enthrone the Triune God in the center of our being, the river immediately flows and the tree immediately grows, and we can drink and eat.
When we drink the river of water of life and eat the tree of life, we have something to minister to people. We can minister something of the Triune God, something of Christ, to other people. This is our real service. If we cannot minister the Triune God to people, regardless how busy we are and regardless how many things we think we have done or are doing for God, they are all vanity of vanities. In one sense, God does not need us to do so many things for Him. The one thing God needs is for us to eat and drink of Him. God is almighty; He can do everything for us, but there is one thing He cannot do: He cannot eat Himself for us, and He cannot drink Himself for us. We have to do the eating and drinking.
Whenever we enthrone the Triune God in the center of our being to honor and subject ourselves to His headship, right away the river flows, the tree grows, and we have something to ,drink and eat. Then we have something real, something from the Triune God, that we can minister to other people. Chapter thirty-eight of God's New Testament Economy has much to say concerning the throne of God.
THE APOSTLES LIVING OUT THE REALITY OF THE TEMPLE OF THE NEW JERUSALEM
The Inner Temple Being the Lord God the Almighty and the Lamb
The apostles live out the reality of the temple of the New Jerusalem, and the inner temple is the Lord God the Almighty and the Lamb. Revelation 21:22 says, "And I saw no temple in it, for the Lord God the Almighty and the Lamb are its temple." The Lord God the Almighty and the Lamb are the temple of the New Jerusalem.
Taking the Redeeming God as Their Dwelling Place, Their Palace, in Which They as the Co-kings Dwell with Their Father-king as the Divine, Royal Family
The apostles take the redeeming God as their dwelling place, their palace, in which they as the co-kings dwell with their Father-king as the divine, royal family (John 15:5; Rev. 22:5b; cf. Psa. 90:1). The temple is the place where God dwells, and the palace is the place where the king and the royal family dwell. Solomon built the temple for God and separately built a palace for himself. At that time the temple and the palace were two separate buildings, but in the New Jerusalem the temple and the palace are one. Here God is our dwelling place, and we are God's dwelling place. We have a mutual abode, which is both a temple for God as well as the palace where all of the royal family live as the co-kings with God. In the whole world there is no such royal family of which all the members are co-kings, but in God's family, the royal family, we are all the co-kings with God.
We must appreciate the sequence here. First we need to see the throne, and then we can be the co-kings. To be a co-king with Christ does not mean that God has given us authority to do whatever we want. Only when we subject ourselves to the headship of the Triune God, putting ourselves under the ruling of the divine life, will His authority flow out to us. This is the only way we can be the co-kings.
Abiding in Christ, the Redeeming God, to Be One with His Inward Parts
The apostles abide in Christ, the redeeming God, to be one with His inward parts. In Philippians 1:8 Paul said, "For God is my witness how I long after you all in the inward parts of Christ Jesus." The phrase inward parts refers to His inward feeling, His inward love and affection. This indicates our abiding in the Lord to be one with Him.
The Three Dimensions of the City Being Equal Indicating That the City as a Whole Will Be the Holy of Holies, the Inner Temple
The three dimensions of the city are equal, indicating that the city as a whole is the Holy of Holies, the inner temple. The length, breadth, and height of the New Jerusalem are equal, making it a cube, like the Holy of Holies (Rev. 21:16 and note 4).
Living in Their Spirit as the Reality of the Holy of Holies and Eating Christ as the Hidden Manna in the Holy of Holies to Be Their Hidden Supply in Their Spirit for Them to Be Incorporated into the New Jerusalem
The apostles live in their spirit as the reality of the Holy of Holies (Rom. 8:4; Heb. 10:22), and they eat Christ as the hidden manna in the Holy of Holies to be their hidden supply in their spirit for them to be incorporated into the New Jerusalem (Rev. 2:17). Our God has an eternal purpose, which is to gain a universal, divine-human incorporation of God and man. To be an incorporation means that we are completely one with God. Not only are we one with God in life and nature; we are also one with God in His person. For many years we have seen that God's eternal purpose is to have a corporate man to be His corporate expression. This is true, but we must see that the way to be God's corporate expression is to be totally incorporated into this universal incorporation. This is the way we can express Him. It is not that God is there, and we are here trying to express Him apart from Him.
The incorporation with the Triune God has two aspects. The first is concerning the Triune God's being. The universal incorporation starts with the Triune God. In John 14:10 the Lord Jesus said, "Do you not believe that I am in the Father and the Father is in Me? The words that I say to you I do not speak from Myself, but the Father who abides in Me does His works." The Father and the. Son coinhere in each other. When we speak concerning other things, we can give illustrations for them, but it is difficult to illustrate coinherence. However, this is the way the Triune God is one. The Lord did not say, "The words that I say to you, to these the Father agrees." That is not enough. Rather, the Son's speaking was the Father's work. The Son is in the Father, and the Father is in the Son. By these two "ins" the Son and the Father work together. They are completely one. In verse 20 the Lord said to His disciples, "In that day you will know that I am in My Father, and you in Me, and I in you." We are in the Lord, and the Lord is in us. We are incorporated into the divine incorporation. God's eternal purpose is fulfilled by this universal incorporation, which is the New Jerusalem.
In Revelation 2:17 the Lord promised the hidden manna to the overcomers in the church in Pergamos. The hidden manna is the manna which was preserved in the golden pot, the golden pot was inside the ark, and the ark was in the Holy of Holies. Manna signifies Christ. When manna comes down from heaven, it is open manna, but when it is put inside the golden pot, it is hidden. Gold refers to the nature of God the Father, so the golden pot signifies the Father. The golden pot was put inside the ark, which was made of acacia wood overlaid with gold, signifying the mingling of divinity and humanity in Christ. This picture is fulfilled in John 14:11, which says that "I am in the Father and the Father is in Me." The hidden manna in the golden pot signifies that the Son is in the Father, and the golden pot in the ark signifies that the Father is in the Son. This is the divine incorporation. Moreover, the ark is in the Holy of Holies, signifying that the divine incorporation is in our human spirit.
We have to eat the hidden manna. The more we eat the hidden manna, the more we will be incorporated into this divine incorporation. This is the apostles' living. They lived out the reality of the throne, and they lived out the reality of the inner temple, which is the Holy of Holies, our human spirit. The revelation of the divine universal incorporation is very high, but we must take the practical way of eating Jesus as the hidden manna. The more we eat the hidden manna, the more we are incorporated into the divine incorporation. This is the apostles' living. We need to learn to honor the Lord's headship, and we need practically to set apart a time every day to go to the Lord, have fellowship with Him, and eat and drink Him. Brother Lee once said that if the elders and co-workers would spend a time with the Lord in the morning, the condition of the whole recovery would be revolutionized. We may think that the co-workers and elders should spend one hour in the morning with the Lord. Actually, a short time is sufficient. However, many of us may not even spend five or ten minutes to pray to the Lord. May the Lord be merciful to us. Even though the revelation is so high, we need to apply it in a practical way. Practically speaking, we need to live under the ruling of the divine life and put ourselves under the throne; that is, we need to enthrone the Triune God in the center of our being so that we can eat and drink of Him. Then practically every day we have to set apart a definite time to go to the Lord, pray to Him, and fellowship with Him. If we will do this, the condition of the Lord's recovery will be revolutionized.
We need to remember the general subject of these messages: "The New Jerusalem—The Highest Point of the Apostles' Living and Work." Apparently we are reviewing the various aspects of the New Jerusalem, but in actuality we are emphasizing that the highest point of the apostles' living and work is the New Jerusalem. We have seen the interpretation and application of the New Jerusalem, but we may have received the revelation of the New Jerusalem as something by itself alone. However, the title of this series of messages is enlightening. The New Jerusalem is not a separate revelation in the Bible, as many of us have taken it to be. Rather, the New Jerusalem should be to us, as it was to the apostles, the highest point of our living and work. Beneath the highest point are many lower points. The apostles' living and work have many different aspects, yet among these many aspects there is a highest point. Although Christians appreciate the many aspects of the apostles' living and work, few have realized that there is a highest point, which is the ultimate point, the most supreme point. Based upon all the lower levels of life and work as the foundation, the crystallization of the highest point of the apostles' living and work is the New Jerusalem. They lived for the New Jerusalem, and they worked for the New Jerusalem. Apparently they displayed many wonderful virtues and served diligently in the churches among the believers, but the highest point of all their living and their work is the New Jerusalem, which is the ultimate goal of God's eternal economy. As we go through all the points in these messages, we must not lose sight of their general subject. Otherwise, we will become lost in the details. This is the crystallization of the apostles' living and work. They lived and worked for the New Jerusalem. — B. C.
THE APOSTLES LIVING OUT THE REALITY OF THE LIGHT OF THE NEW JERUSALEM
The apostles live out the reality of the light of the New Jerusalem. The New Jerusalem is a city, even a mountain, of light. The entire New Jerusalem is a universal lampstand upholding God as light with Christ the Lamb as the lamp. As the lampstand, it is the diffuser shining forth the Triune God in the whole universe. Whenever we touch the New Jerusalem, we touch the realm of light. Light is the nature of God's expression. It is God Himself expressed in life and righteousness.
In opposition to light, darkness is the expression of Satan. Due to Satan's rebellion, at the beginning of Genesis the whole earth became empty and void, and darkness was upon the face of the earth (Gen. 1:2). What Satan brought in was darkness. Darkness is a characteristic of the work of Satan. Then God came in to restore the heaven and the earth to bring in life. The first thing He did to bring in the old creation was to speak, "Let there be light" (v. 3). To restore order for the old creation, the first thing that God did was to "turn on the switch." Before that time, due to Satan's rebellion, the entire creation was in darkness, and in this "dark room" all the "bugs and creeping things" were rampant, crawling everywhere. It was as if God came into the room and "turned on the switch," and all the creeping things fled. That was for the bringing in of the old creation. At the end of the divine revelation, after thousands of years of God's working in the old creation and Christ's coming to bring in the new creation, there is a city and a mountain of light. Revelation 21:23 says that in that city there is no need of the sun or of the moon to enlighten it because God in the Lamb as the lamp is shining over it. They are the enlightenment, enlightening the entire city twenty-four hours a day to the point that there is no night, no darkness, in the New Jerusalem. In other words, there is no trace of any element of the satanic work. This means that the fall is altogether eradicated. The New Jerusalem as such a diffuser, such a bearer of the divine light, stands for the full completion of God's eternal economy. There at last God has an entire mountain of light as the totality of His chosen people. Through God's working from regeneration through sanctification, renewing, transformation, conformation, and eventually glorification, they become a people of light. The New Jerusalem people are a people of light. With them there is no darkness.
The apostles live out the reality of the light of the New Jerusalem. In the New Testament, Paul, Peter, and John all spoke about light. In Romans 13:12 Paul said, "Let us therefore cast off the works of darkness, and let us put on the weapons of light." In Ephesians 5:8-9 he says, "For you were once darkness but are now light in the Lord; walk as children of light (for the fruit of the light consists in all goodness and righteousness and truth)." Peter said that God "has called you out of darkness into His marvelous light" (1 Pet. 2:9). He describes our salvation as a transfer out of darkness into light. Darkness is of the kingdom of Satan, and light is of the kingdom of God. The apostle John said that we "walk in the light as He is in the light" (1 John 1:7). The apostles all spoke the same thing because they lived this way. They realized that their living should be a living in the light, which is the reality of the Triune God who is light.
Walking in God as the Divine Light in Christ the Lamb as the Lamp to Be under His Continual Illumination
The apostles walk in God as the divine light in Christ the Lamb as the lamp to be under His continual illumination (Rev. 21:23; 22:5; John 8:12; 1 John 1:5-7; cf. Psa. 119:130). In the Old Testament it was fearful to come into God's light. God's light was too intense. Paul said that God dwells in unapproachable light (1 Tim. 6:16). In that sense, we cannot approach God in His shining. However, Revelation 21 tells us that the divine shining, the divine light of God, is in Christ the Lamb as the lamp. Now in the New Testament, we can come boldly to God to come under His shining to enjoy His light in a free way, even in a bold way, because this shining is not just out of God, but it is also in Christ the Lamb as the lamp.
First John 1:5-7 tells us that when we walk in the light, we have fellowship with God. To be sure, the light in this fellowship brings us much exposure. We are exposed, and many things are brought to light. Our attitude, temperament, and wrongdoings are all exposed under this light through the divine fellowship. However, the apostle John continues to tell us that when we are exposed by the divine light, the blood of Jesus Christ His Son cleanses us (v. 7). In the New Testament age we are not afraid of the light because under the exposing of the light we have the blood of Jesus Christ, God's Son, to cleanse us. The shining of God as the divine light in the New Jerusalem is not the shining of God directly; rather, it is the shining of God in Christ the Lamb as the lamp. Therefore, we are joyful, willing, and happy to receive the light because once we are exposed, the blood of Christ the redeeming Lamb cleanses us from all our sins.
It is by this shining that the apostles live under His continual illumination. Sometimes when we need guidance from the Lord, we come to Him and ask for His enlightenment. Likewise, when we read the Word, we ask for His enlightenment to gain some understanding. This is one aspect of enlightenment. The apostles, however, come to the Lord for enlightenment not merely for certain activities. They live under the constant illumination. This shining is not just an instance of light given to us to show us what we should do or how to understand a part of the Scriptures. Rather, the light in the New Jerusalem is a realm. As we are enjoying the Triune God, we are in another realm. We are in the divine and mystical realm, and this is a realm of light. It is not merely that we need guidance or help in a particular matter. Rather, our whole living must be under constant illumination. In these days, the Lord's recovery as a whole is really in the light. Through the ministry of the Word, the Lord has brought His recovery into the broad daylight, as under the midday sun. The shining is here. Whenever we come to the Word in the crystallized way, the shining is so bright and clear. This is the shining of the day.
In the last two thousand years there may never have been a time like today. The ministry has been crystallized and brought to the high peak. Even the highest point of the apostles' living and work has been crystallized and presented to us. What else can be higher or brighter than this? How blessed we are; we are indeed in the light. Psalm 119:130 says, "The entrance of Your word gives light." The recovery today is so much in the light because of the Word. The ministry has been so crystallized, the picture is so clear, and all the pieces of the jigsaw puzzle have been put together. Now we see a complete presentation of God's eternal economy in such a crystallized way. Today through this ministry the whole recovery has been brought under this bright light. It is so good to be here. We are not merely talking about these things; we are also enjoying them. We are enjoying the light in His recovery through the opening of His Word.
Being Diffusers of the Divine Light, Shining as Luminaries in the World, to Let the Light of God Shine before All Men for His Glory
The apostles are diffusers the divine light, shining as luminaries in the world, to let the light or God shine before all men for His glory (Rev. 21:24a; Phil. 2:15; Matt. 5:15-16; Eph. 5:8-9, 14; Rev. 1:20). By the apostles' walking in God as the divine light, they become diffusers. The more we walk in God as the divine light in Christ the Lamb as the lamp, the more we become light bearers, diffusers. Philippians 2:15 says that we shine as luminaries in the world. This means we are the light bearers, like the moon. The moon does not shine; it simply reflects the light of the sun. In ourselves, we do not shine, but by our walking in the divine light we become the diffusers. God is the light, and the Lamb is the lamp, yet this lamp shining the light needs a diffuser. This diffuser is the church, the Body of Christ. Our job as the Lord's recovery at the end of this age is to diffuse the divine light of His crystallized word. Brother Lee spoke the word, and we also speak the word like tape recorders. We listen and receive the speaking, and then we go back to our respective places to speak the same thing. We should be thousands of tape recorders speaking the same thing, diffusing this light to this dark earth. Day by day we are pray-reading, studying, reciting, and prophesying the word, being saturated and permeated with the divine light of God in Christ as the Lamb. Then we become the diffusers. This is why in Revelation the local churches are typified by the lampstands. Every local church is a lampstand to shine forth the divine light, and ultimately the entire New Jerusalem is just a universal lampstand, shining forth the wonderful God as His expression.
THE APOSTLES LIVING OUT THE REALITY OF THE PROCESSED AND CONSUMMATED TRIUNE GOD AS THE SUPPLY OF THE NEW JERUSALEM
The apostles live out the reality of the processed and consummated Triune God as the supply of the New Jerusalem. The New Jerusalem is not only a mountain of light; it is also a mountain of rich supply. What a storehouse of exhaustless supply! Here our supply is not physical; our supply is the processed and consummated Triune God as the Father, the Son, and the Spirit to be our eternal supply. The New Jerusalem is not Mount Sinai, giving us the law of commandments to keep and demanding us to be a certain way. The New Jerusalem is a golden mountain, full of the divine nature. The standard is so high, yet at the same time the holy city is a city of bountiful supply. The sign of the New Jerusalem not only shows us that the divine nature of God is high, holy, wonderful, and absolute; it also shows us that our processed Triune God is the supplying One. He is the One supplying us with all that we need to live out this divine life.
Drinking the One Spirit as the Crystal-bright River of Water of Life for Their Satisfaction to Purify Them and Make Them Transparent as Crystal, Having No Dimness or Opaqueness
The apostles drink the one Spirit as the crystal-bright river of water of life for their satisfaction to purify them and make them transparent as crystal, having no dimness or opaqueness (Rev. 22:1; 1 Cor. 12:13). The supply of the city is the river of water of life. Hallelujah for this river! This river is a crystal-bright river. All the New Jerusalem people are not good law keepers, but they are good drinkers. To live the reality of the processed and consummated Triune God as the supply of the New Jerusalem means that we live as the enjoyers. The apostles are wonderful enjoyers. Paul was a great drinker. When he was in prison, he knew how to drink. Outwardly, he was in stocks, bound and restricted, yet inwardly he was drinking. He was drinking through the saints' prayer and through the bountiful supply of the Spirit of Jesus Christ (Phil. 1:19). He was richly nourished and satisfied, supplied by the river of water of life. This is how he could live a life for the Lord on the earth, and this is how he carried out his work for the Lord on the earth. He was drinking of the river of water of life.
The river of water of life has the characteristic of being crystal- bright. It is not just for our satisfaction to quench our thirst; it is for purifying us whenever we drink of it. Water is the best purifying agent. This is even true medically. It is good to drink much water every day. Water cleanses and purifies us. In the New Jerusalem there is not just a cup of water; there is a crystal-bright river of living water. No mineral water or sparkling water is like the crystal-bright water of the New Jerusalem with its purifying effect. The more we drink of this river, the more our impure motives, mixture, flesh, self, and natural man are purged and purified.
Moreover, the river of water of life makes us transparent. To pray-read, study, and recite with the brothers makes us transparent.
Sometimes when we are not transparent, we look at our hymn book or Bible as if hiding behind a shield. However, as we pray-read, study, recite, and prophesy, we are drinking the Spirit, and as we are drinking the Spirit and speaking one to another, we become more transparent. We are not political and have nothing to hide. We can see each other eye to eye. Sometimes when we come across a brother, we cannot look him in the eye. This certainly means that we are opaque. There is something we have hidden within ourself. Either we have been offended by him, or we do not like something about him. When we PSRP the word, eye to eye with the brothers, to exercise the spirit and drink of
the Spirit, all the opaqueness is stripped off. We all become transparent. The river of life purifies us and makes us transparent. The entire New Jerusalem is a transparent city. The gold of the New Jerusalem is transparent gold; it is not opaque. Nothing is hidden, and there is no mixture. Rather, it is crystal clear.
Eating Christ as the Tree of Life for Their Nourishment to Live as Men of Life Who Live in the Principle of the Tree of Life
The apostles eat Christ as the tree of life for their nourishment to live as men of life who live in the principle of the tree of life (Rev. 22:2; John 6:57; Rom. 8:10, 6, 11). Another element of the supply in the New Jerusalem is the tree of life. Actually, the tree of life is the main element of supply in the city. There is the river to quench our thirst, but there is also the tree of life, signifying Christ as the embodiment of the Triune God to be our life supply. In Genesis 2 God gave the tree of life to the created man that he would partake of God's divine life, yet because of the fall, man was kept away from that tree. The pathway to the tree of life was shut by God's righteousness, holiness, and glory. The flaming sword guarded the way to the tree of life until Christ came to be the redeeming Lamb, fulfilling the requirements of God's righteousness, holiness, and glory. He removed the flaming sword, and through the accomplishment of His judicial redemption He opened the way to the tree of life once again. Now we can all come boldly to partake of the tree of life.
In the middle of the river and on both sides of the river the tree of life is growing. This tree of life is like a vine tree, growing on both sides of the river. Whenever we drink the flowing river, we partake of the supply from the tree of life. This is to nourish us and to impart, dispense, the divine life of God into us to constitute us men of life. Paul was such a person; he lived that way. He was not only a good drinker, but he was also a good eater. He was eating this tree of life all the time. He told us that our spirit became life at the time of our regeneration, but he also charged us that after regeneration we have to set our mind on the spirit that our mind would become life and that even our mortal body would also become life (Rom. 8:10, 6, 11). By daily partaking of and enjoying the tree of life, we become men of life in our entire being. The New Jerusalem people are people of life, not of the law or letter, by enjoying Christ as the tree of life.
Living Continually in the Presence of God, Seeing the Face of God, to Be Supplied with the Riches of God
The apostles live continually in the presence of God, seeing the face of God, to be supplied with the riches of God (Rev. 22:4; 2 Cor. 2:10; 4:6-7). Revelation 22:4 says, "And they will see His face, and His name will be on their forehead." Not only did the apostles drink the river of life and eat the tree of life, but they continually lived to see God's face. Second Corinthians 4:6-7 says, "Because the God who said, Out of darkness light shall shine, is the One who shined in our hearts to illuminate the knowledge of the glory of God in the face of Jesus Christ. But we have this treasure in earthen vessels that the excellency of the power may be of God and not out of us." Here God is the light who shines out of darkness into our heart to illuminate the knowledge of the glory of God in the face of Jesus Christ. God's face is His shining, His presence. As we are drinking the river and eating the tree, we behold Him and see His face. What God is, is expressed in the face of Jesus Christ. By our being illuminated by Him, the very indwelling Christ who is the treasure is infused into our being to be our constant supply. Our supply is not merely outward. Our supply is from the indwelling Christ, who is the hidden treasure within us, who is infused into us by our beholding His face. The glory of God is shining in the face of Christ. This is why we have to spend five or ten minutes a day to fellowship with the Lord before His face, to allow His face to shine into us and to infuse the very Christ to be the hidden treasure in us. Sometimes we are busy as we labor, and we may miss our time with the Lord. To have a definite time to be in the Lord's presence helps us tremendously. As we spend time in His face to allow Him to shine on us, we begin to experience this hidden treasure who is our constant supply. Let us all practice this. Let us not be so busy that we cannot give the Lord five or ten minutes, in His presence to fellowship with Him.
For the Building Up of the Body of Christ by the Growth in Life to Consummate the New Jerusalem
All the foregoing items are for the building up of the Body of Christ by the growth in life to consummate the New Jerusalem (Eph. 4:16). As we are receiving the supply from the river of water of life and from the tree of life, receiving His infusion through the shining of His face, we are growing. We receive the nourishment for the growth, and this growth is for the building up of the Body of Christ. As we grow, we also minister the Triune God as the supply to others that they also may grow. The eventual issue is the building up of the Body of Christ. Hallelujah for the reality of the light of the New Jerusalem and the processed and consummated Triune God as the supply of the New Jerusalem! Both of these are not for ourselves. They are for the building up of the Body of Christ. The apostles practiced and lived out these realities as the high point of their living and work. Today may we also be the ones receiving mercy to live in the same way, that being in the light and receiving the constant supply for the building up of His Body would be the high point of our living. — J. L.

第四篇　使徒们工作的最高点─作出新耶路撒冷（一）
The Highest Point of the Apostles' Work— To Work Out the New Jerusalem (1)
纲目|Outline|对照-目录
（本文为英文听抄扫描所得，未经校对）
The first three messages in this series concern the highest point of the apostles' living, which is to live out the New Jerusalem. Beginning from this message we will consider the highest point of the apostles' work, which is to work out the New Jerusalem. From God's point of view, living and work are one. Hymns, #910 says, "The overflow of life is work, / The work should be our living!" We may also say, "The work should be our being." Our being in action is our work.
This message is governed by the crucial word of fellowship which Brother Lee gave on April 6, 1997, which is reproduced at the start of message two of this series. According to that word, the co-workers bear the responsibility to see that they must only do one work, and they should no longer do their own work. According to Brother Lee's uplifted character as manifested in his ministry, he did not waste words or exaggerate. Therefore, when he said the elders and co-workers, "should no longer do their own work," he implied that to some extent at least some of us may be doing our own work, and our own work in the context of the Lord's recovery may not be the one work, the work of the New Jerusalem. Under the Lord's grace, may we, especially the co-workers and the elders, compare ourselves not with each other but with the divine revelation, the New Jerusalem, the highest point of the apostles' work. When Brother Lee gave us the line for this conference, "The New Jerusalem—The Highest Point of the Apostles' Living and Work," his aspiration was that we would settle for nothing less than the highest point. Among Christians today very few care for what God wants. So many work and serve, but who cares for the desire of God's heart? The ministry in the Lord's recovery has shown us what God wants, what is on God's heart. It is good enough that we just care for this, live this, work this, become this, and help others become this so that the good pleasure of God can be satisfied and His heart's desire can be fulfilled.
In the first three messages we touched our living, which involves our person. In this message we touch our work, which may be even more sensitive. It is clearly revealed in the Word and lucidly expounded in the ministry that the day is coming when all the workers of God will face God's objective final exam." The issue, the produce, of our work will be there, and the five will prove every man's work concerning not how successful, big, or prominent it is, but of what sort it is (1 Cor. 3:13). If we build with wood, grass, and stubble, an entire lifetime of effort will go up in smoke, and nothing of our labor will enter the New Jerusalem. However, if by the Lord's grace we together work the work of God, that is, work the work of the New Jerusalem, that work when tested by fire will remain, and the issue of our lifetime of labor will stand for eternity as part of the New Jerusalem.
THE UNIQUE GOAL OF THE APOSTLES' WORK BEING THE NEW JERUSALEM—THE ULTIMATE GOAL OF GOD'S ECONOMY
According to the Entire Revelation of the New Testament, the Unique Goal of the Christian Work Should Be the New Jerusalem
The unique goal of the apostles' work is the New Jerusalem—the ultimate goal of God's economy. According to the entire revelation of the New Testament, the unique goal of the Christian work should be the New Jerusalem. May the Lord shine His light on our goals. Outwardly we may be gentlemen, and we may be quite congenial and get along together, but if there is a difference of goal, eventually there will be discord. How marvelous it would be if all over the earth there were a company of co-workers having the same goal, no one having a private interest, no one doing a work within the work, but every one having God's goal as his or her goal. May we pray for this, and may we believe in this.
The Degradation of the Church Is Mainly Due to the Fact That Nearly All Christian Workers Are Distracted to Take Something Other Than the New Jerusalem as Their Goal
The degradation of the church is mainly due to the fact that nearly all Christian workers are distracted to take something other than the New Jerusalem as their goal. It takes much insight and discernment to realize that the degradation of the church is mainly due to Christian workers being distracted, aiming at something other than the New Jerusalem as their goal. May we be saved from distraction now rather than having to be saved from distraction later, and may the Lord grant us mercy to give up all the distracting goals and uniquely aim at the New Jerusalem.
The New Jerusalem Is the Ultimate Goal of God's Eternal Economy
God Has Only One Ultimate Goal—the New Jerusalem
The Bible Begins with One Single God, and the Bible Ends with a Great, Incorporate God— the New Jerusalem as God's Enlargement, Expansion, and Expression
The New Jerusalem is the ultimate goal of God's eternal economy. God has only one ultimate goal—the New Jerusalem. There is one God, one Christ, one Spirit, one way of salvation, one redemption, one economy, one Body, and one goal. Our God is single and our God is absolute. From eternity to eternity, He has one goal. The Bible begins with one single God and the Bible ends with a great, incorporate God—the New Jerusalem as God's enlargement, expansion, and expression. God becomes not merely corporate, but incorporate. His eternal Godhead never suffers change or is altered in any way by the processes of His economy. However, God in His economy is being enlarged, expanded, and expressed. The New Jerusalem will be the ultimate consummation of the incorporate God. This is what is on God's heart. This means that eventually we all need to be satisfied with being one small part of a jasper city. The reward will be in the millennial kingdom, but for eternity we will all be the same. You will be you, and I will be me, but we will all be the same. We will all be God in life, nature, and appearance. That is what we are here for, and our work is to work this out.
We Must Take the Goal of God's Eternal Economy— the New Jerusalem—as Our Unique and Ultimate Goal
As God's Co-workers We Should Do the Work of God
We must take the goal of God's eternal economy—the New Jerusalem—as our unique and ultimate goal. As God's co-workers we should do the work of God (1 Cor. 3:9; 2 Cor. 6:1; 1 Cor. 15:58; 16:10). In the ministry in the Lord's recovery, especially since the time of the resumption of Watchman Nee's ministry, a major theme has been the central work of God. Creation was a work, but it was a preliminary work, a necessary work, not the central work of God. The central work of God is to work Himself in Christ into our being to make us His corporate expression. We are not the only ones carrying out this work; we believe that God Himself is working even as we work. We are not the worker; we are God's coworkers. We are workers together with God. If we are to work together with God, surely we need to do the work of God, and to do the work of God, we need to see what God is doing. God is not doing a great and glorious outward work in this present age. Rather, our God is a God who hides Himself. He is working inside out from our being. He is doing an intrinsic work, an organic work, that eventually will have a glorious consummation.
We need to see these matters and prepare ourselves to make a turn to work not merely in an outward way but with God in an inward way. Paul did this. He indicated this in Galatians 4:19 as he was fighting to rescue the Galatians from religious distraction. He said, "My children, with whom I travail again in birth until Christ is formed in you." This is a subjective, organic, and intrinsic work. This work involves the inner being of the apostle in travail again until Christ is formed in them. Probably there are few among us that know what it is like to travail, but in the days to come the co-workers need to do something more than an outward, objective work with outward, visible, measurable results. We must be one with God to labor with Him as He endeavors to work Himself into the being of the saints.
The ministry in the Lord's recovery has used a number of expressions concerning the central work of God. One is that God's purpose is to work Himself into man. Apart from this purpose the Christian life is meaningless. God's intention is to make Himself our inward element, and the material with which God works is Himself. In 2 Samuel 7 David desired to build a house for God, but God revealed that He, not David, would build His house. God is endeavoring to build Himself in Christ into our being. Do we see this and pray for this? We should spend a little time to say, "Lord, I understand somewhat what You are doing. You want to build Yourself in Christ into my being. I would like to make my being available to You. I would like to give You all the room in me. But Lord, this is not focused on me. All the saints are undergoing such a work, so I would like to pray for the churches. I would like to pray for the saints that You would strengthen them with power by Your Spirit into the inner man to make Your home in their hearts and fill them unto all the fullness of God." This is how Paul prayed on his knees in a prison cell while chained to a Roman centurion. He said, "I bow my knees unto the Father" (Eph. 3:14). He did not pray for something outward; he prayed that the Father would empower us so that Christ could make His home in our being (w. 16-17). This is the work of God.
Another expression of the ministry is that God's work is a matter of gaining persons. God's intention is to make God's chosen people a certain kind of being. That is His work. We need to learn in humility how to cooperate with the Lord and with one another to do such a work so that the effect of our contact with the saints is that more of God is worked into them, and the saints would want to contact us because when they do, they get a little more of God. Every day and month of our work should make a difference, and a year certainly should make a difference.
Another expression of the ministry is that what we are is what we do. If we are not an intrinsic constitution of the processed Triune God, our work cannot be that. If God's economy and even the high peak of the divine revelation of God's economy are objective theology to us, then all we can do is an objective work. Nothing intrinsic is gained. However, if God can gain our being, our being will become our work, and what we are when put into action will be what we do. The principle in God's work is to gain persons, and by gaining them we have a way to go on. Our view must be to gain persons thoroughly, intrinsically, that God would have a way to go on. Perhaps the Lord would spread His recovery to Iceland; perhaps there will be a church in Reykjavik. The Lord may also desire to gain persons for Bulgaria, Hungary, India, and so many other places. However, who will be the channel, the person that God can use to open up these countries?
The priority is not for us to work for God, but for God to work Himself into us. Early in the morning in the course of our fellowship we should pray a simple prayer, saying, "Lord, here is a new day. Work Yourself into me today. When I lie down on my bed at night sixteen hours from now, may there be more of You in my constitution than when I got up. Yet, I will not try to measure it, and I will not examine myself." God is waiting to hear this kind of prayer. How much ground will we give Him? Do we still have "No trespassing" signs over certain parts of our being? Do we have the confidence that we are absolutely open to the Lord, nothing guarded, nothing protected, and nothing reserved? How far can the Lord go? May the Lord grant us grace that we would say, "Lord, for the New Jerusalem as the goal, touch every part of my being. Gain every part of my being intrinsically for that goal."
The New Jerusalem is a reality. To become the New Jerusalem, we have to become it in the way of reality. The extent to which we co-labor with the Lord depends upon the extent to which He can work in us. It should be an obvious fact that we cannot bring the saints farther than we ourselves have gone. We should not suppose that because we are working or are in the lead, we are the most mature ones in the church. There may be saints suffering in our locality, not knowing why they are suffering, because we do not give them the room to go on and our being is a barrier to their progress. The church is what the leading brothers are. We need to be the New Jerusalem; then we can be used by God to expand the New Jerusalem. This is our work, and this is God's work.
We Should Regard the Local Churches Not as the Goal but as a Procedure to Reach God's Goal
We should regard the local churches not as the goal but as a procedure to reach God's goal (Rev. 22:16; 3:7, 12). Some of us are not very good at raising up churches. We note with much respect and appreciation in the Lord, however, that others have had a very good work of raising up churches. We truly and sincerely thank the Lord for this, and we have something to learn from brothers that obviously have a way to raise up assemblies in such numbers. Without detracting from the significance of this, however, we must ask what the intrinsic content of our churches is. We are not Brethren assemblies with a higher theology and more living meetings. The goal of a church must not be for a leading brother to be a "big frog in a small pond." The reason a church is raised up must never be a brother's personal goal. We must be able to say in faith with a pure heart that the church in our locality is part of the procedure to build up the Body of Christ to consummate the New Jerusalem.
According to God's goal in His economy, the churches are a procedure. When we migrated in the 1970s, we did not see this. The Lord may not hold us responsible for not seeing it at that time, but today we have to see it. What would God gain if there were 100,000 local churches on the earth but no reality of the Body of Christ? According to our view, the Lord would be ready to come back if there were 100,000 churches; we would have done a great work. Indeed, it would be a great work, but it is not the goal. The churches are a procedure to bring the reality of the Body,of Christ into being, which reality, at the Lord's coming, will be raptured to be the bride of Christ, and that bride will be the New Jerusalem in the millennial kingdom. If this matter is settled in the being of every leading brother, there will be much more blending in one accord among us, in which every church will have the same realization that we are part of the procedure.
May we not be deceived concerning the work of the New Jerusalem and meet the Lord only to find out that for decades we were not doing the work of God. We should not too quickly assume that our work is the work of the New Jerusalem. The work of the New Jerusalem is mainly an organic work, not a judicial work. Some brothers, by temperament or disposition, are judicial. They tend to pick up the judicial aspects of the ministry, and they have quite a judicial church life. However, the work of the New Jerusalem, although it is based upon a judicial work, is primarily organic, so our work must be an organic work. We must do an organic work, a vital and dynamic work of life.
The work of the New Jerusalem is intrinsic, not merely outward. We are in the old creation, the physical realm, so we have to have outward things. We meet in an outward hall and sit on outward chairs, but the outward is for something intrinsic. If we are not intrinsically one with this ministry, it is questionable whether we are one with it at all. We need to be intrinsically one and get into the intrinsic significance of the ministry and let it become our being. Brother Lee gave a charge to some brothers concerning the full ministry of Christ in its three stages. He said, "Get into these things until they become your intrinsic being. Then go and pour them out." This is what Brother Lee wanted his co-workers to do, and this is what God wants His co-workers to do. People will know whether our teaching is an objective teaching or an outpouring of our intrinsic constitution.
The work of the New Jerusalem is constitutional. It depends on what we are becoming in our being. The work of the New Jerusalem is organically progressive. We go on step by step and level by level. It should burden us if the saints in our locality remain the same year by year. Does anyone travail and labor for them? We must face the abnormality of our lack of growth, realizing that the work of the New Jerusalem is progressive. The saints should experience renewing, transformation, and all the other organic steps. This is the work of the New Jerusalem.
The work of the New Jerusalem is not physical, but divine and mystical. We do not minimize practical physical things, but they are for something invisible, divine, and mystical. Some receive the messages on the divine and mystical realm and then put them aside, saying, "Let us now deal with practical things; let us deal with people where they really are." Truly, we should deal with people where they really are, but we should labor to bring them to where God wants them to be, which is part of the divine and mystical realm.
The work of the New Jerusalem is a work in the stage of intensification. It is questionable whether many of us pray, "Lord, intensify me. I need to be intensified sevenfold for the New Jerusalem." Incarnation, inclusion, and intensification are not merely a new teaching. The Lord really means that we be intensified to overcome the degradation of the church and be produced as the overcomers for the building of the New Jerusalem. If there is no intensification, there is no overcoming; if there is no overcoming, there is no building up of the Body; and if there is no building up of the Body, there is no consummation of the New Jerusalem. We need to be intensified in actuality and do an intensified work for the consummation of the New Jerusalem.
The work of the New Jerusalem is a work of universal blending and is carried out in and through the universal blending. The work of the New Jerusalem ushers the believers into the reality of the Body of Christ, which consummates the New Jerusalem. Do we know what the reality of the Body of Christ is? We should kneel down and say, "Lord, show me the reality of the Body of Christ. Lord, grant me the experiences I need to be in this reality, and Lord, make me the kind of person who can usher others into this reality."
The work of the New Jerusalem requires that we engage in a struggle against Satan who opposes the building up of the Body of Christ, which consummates the New Jerusalem. On March 24, 1997, a brother told Brother Lee that his dream of a bright highway, like the dawning of the day, is our dream also. We are seeing the fulfillment of all the things that he has ministered throughout the years. The Lord has gained a blended group of co-workers to act as one man, and we are so grateful to see that we are beginning to enter into the reality of the things that he has spoken for many years. We can see the reality of the Body of Christ coming forth now. This is a fulfillment of the dream Brother Lee had, and we have the same dream. Brother Lee responded:
The real story started from that dream. I was never as burdened for the Lord's recovery as I have been since receiving that dream. I have been very much directed in my work by that dream. Now I feel strongly that God's enemy has only one thing to do: to destroy this work, a work firstly to build up the Body, then the Body becomes the means to accomplish something to produce the New Jerusalem. Here is a struggle between God and Satan. Who will get the goal? I am right away in the middle of this struggle. No Christian today on this earth knows what is to build the Body of Christ, but we have the secret. However, I do not know how much the Lord would have me in this struggle, but I do know your participation in this struggle means a lot. This is the only thing that will bring in the very thing that God's heart's desire wants to have.
God's enemy has only one thing to do, which is to destroy this work. He has one thing to do, and we have one work to do. The conflict is unavoidable. Here is a struggle between God and Satan. Our brother was right in the middle of this struggle. Our work is not only our living; our work is our warfare. There is an opposer; there is an enemy who is set to destroy the very thing God wants. Moses asked the two and a half tribes, "Shall your brethren go to war, and shall ye sit here?" (Num. 32:6). To "sit here" is to not realize that this work requires war and struggle. Our participation in this struggle means a lot. This struggle is the only thing that will bring in the very thing that God's heart's desire wants to have. In the years to come, the Lord will enlist many of us into this struggle.
The work of the New Jerusalem is unto the glory of God. The first description of the New Jerusalem is that it has the glory of God (Rev. 21:11). In this work there is no place for self-glory and no place for self-exaltation. — R. K.
GOVERNED BY THE VISION OF THE NEW JERUSALEM AS THE GREATEST AND ULTIMATE SIGN IN THE SCRIPTURES
The apostles' work is governed by the vision of the New Jerusalem as the greatest and ultimate sign in the Scriptures (Rev. 21:2, 9-10).
Knowing the Basic Truth concerning the New Jerusalem
The apostles' work is governed by the vision of the New Jerusalem through knowing the basic truth concerning the New Jerusalem:
1) The New Jerusalem is not a physical city but an organic constitution constituted with the redeeming processed and consummated Triune God and the redeemed, transformed, and glorified tripartite men as an organism of the consummated Triune God for His eternal enlargement and expression through the glorified tripartite men.
2) It is the union and mingling of all God's redeemed through all the four ages of the forefathers, the law, grace, and the kingdom with the processed and consummated Triune God.
3) It is the aggregate of the result of God's work of the new creation in all the dispensations within the old creation.
4)It is the spreading of the processed and consummated Triune God through His redeemed, transformed, and glorified people as His increase for His eternal purpose.
5) It is the Jerusalem above (Gal. 4:26), representing the new covenant, the mother of the believers, and the city of the living God, whose Architect and Builder is God (Heb. 11:10; 12:22).
6) It is the embodiment of God's complete salvation (Rev. 21:19-27; 22:1-2). God's complete salvation is a composition of God's righteousness as the base and God's life as the consummation (Rom. 5:18; 8:10). The foundation of the New Jerusalem is the righteousness of God with God's faithfulness, and the consummation is God's life with the divine nature. The entire New Jerusalem is a matter of life built on the foundation of righteousness.
Galatians 4:26 says, "But the Jerusalem above is free, which is our mother." We can know, enjoy, and experience the New Jerusalem as our mother. The New Jerusalem is a very fruitful mother, typified by Sarah, the proper wife of Abraham. Sarah typifies the new covenant, the new covenant is of grace, and grace produces sons. In brief, the Jerusalem above is our mother, this mother produces sons by grace, and these sons in turn become the composition of the New Jerusalem as the mother. The essential meaning here is that grace produces the New Jerusalem. Grace is the unique means for producing the New Jerusalem. The Triune God we know is a God of grace. Peter speaks of the God of all grace (1 Pet. 5:10), and Paul speaks of the grace of the Lord Jesus Christ (2 Cor. 13:14) and the Spirit of grace (Heb. 10:29). The last words of the ministries of the three principal apostles are words concerning grace. Peter said, "Grow in the grace" (2 Pet. 3:18). Paul told Timothy, "Grace be with you" (2 Tim. 4:22). The very last word of John, which is also the last word of the Bible, Revelation 22:21, says, "The grace of the Lord Jesus be with all the saints." Grace is God's last word. We need to become a constitution of grace. Paul said, "By the grace of God I am what I am" (1 Cor. 15:10). A God-man is a grace-man, a person constituted with the processed and consummated Triune God as grace. Such p. person has made a choice between the way of gift and the way of grace, and some of us may need to make this choice in the years to come. Gift cannot produce the New Jerusalem. Grace is the mother. Grace produces the city of God and the sons of God. Grace is the only source. God may have to do quite a work on us to convince us that we need grace, not just grace objectively to be saved, but grace to live, pray, speak, serve, endure, fight, and struggle, and grace to be blended, to be nothing, and to be a slave. Peter said a good word: "God resists the proud but gives grace to the humble" (1 Pet. 5:5). In our work to work out the New Jerusalem, the Lord does not need law givers; He needs grace dispensers. When Paul said his farewell to the elders of the church in Ephesus, he said, "And now I commit you to God and to the word of His grace, which is able to build you up and to give you the inheritance among all those who have been sanctified" (Acts 20:32). Brother Lee wrote a very precious chorus to Hymns, #312, which says, "All sufficient grace! / Never powerless! / It is Christ who lives in me, / In His exhaustlessness." Together in one accord and in the way of blending, let us become the embodiment of the grace of God, and let us minister this grace for the producing of the New Jerusalem.
Seeing the Vision of the New Jerusalem as a Mutual Dwelling Place and as a Universal Couple
The apostles' work is governed by their seeing the vision of the New Jerusalem as a mutual dwelling place and as a universal couple. This point and the preceding ones are taken from The Application of the Interpretation of the New Jerusalem to the Seeking Believers, Even though our purpose is not merely to have another exposition of the New Jerusalem, we should get into those messages so that we can come together to fellowship concerning how these matters affect our work as co-workers and as elders for the building up of the Body of Christ.
A Mutual Dwelling Place for God and His Redeemed People
According to Its Humanity, It Is the Tabernacle of God among Men—the Dwelling Place of God in His Humanity among Men on Earth
The New Jerusalem is the mutual dwelling place for God and His redeemed people. According to its humanity, it is the tabernacle of God among men—the dwelling place of God in His humanity among men on earth (Rev. 21:3). The tabernacle is a human dwelling place, so the New Jerusalem is a tabernacle because it is constituted with humanity. Our God can dwell in a tabernacle because He is a God with humanity. He is a God who has become man so that He can dwell in the tabernacle. John 1:14 says clearly that the Word became flesh and tabernacled among us. The God-man Jesus was an individual tabernacle who, through Acts and the Epistles, was enlarged to consummate the New Jerusalem. The New Jerusalem becomes an organic constitution, the tabernacle of God among men, in order that God with His humanity can dwell in it.
According to Its Divinity, It Is the Temple of God as the Dwelling Place of His Redeemed Elect
According to its divinity, it is the temple of God as the dwelling place of His redeemed elect. Revelation 21:22 says, "And I saw no temple in it, for the Lord God the Almighty and the Lamb are its temple." The Lord God and the Lamb as the temple are the dwelling place for God's redeemed elect. Although the temple is the dwelling place of God, we as His redeemed elect can dwell in it because we have been made God in life and nature but not in the Godhead. The high-peak truths are implied in these two points. If God had not become a man, He could not be the dweller in the tabernacle, and if man cannot become God, he cannot be the dweller in the temple of God. In saying that the New Jerusalem is a mutual dwelling place, we are saying that God has become man to dwell in man so that man can become God to dwell in God. This is the New Jerusalem as the mutual dwelling place of God and man, and this is the processed, consummated God with His humanity together with His redeemed elect who have the divine life and nature but not the Godhead. In the new terminology which the Lord has opened to us, this mutual dwelling place is a divine-human incorporation. The ministry in the Lord's recovery has labored to extract these crystals from the Word of God.
Our relationship with God can be described in several ways, and God's revelation in this matter is step by step, ascending level by level. When Brother Lee was in his ministry in mainland China, he used the word union to describe our relationship with God. Union is particularly a matter of life and is portrayed in the Word as grafting (Rom. 11:24). Our God is the processed God who became cut for us and also cut us to sever us from Adam that we may be grafted into the processed Triune God. Grafting is a matter of life. When Brother Lee came to Taiwan and the United States in his ministry, he began to use mingling to further describe our relationship with God in a more intrinsic way. Mingling is a matter of nature; we are partakers of the divine nature (2 Pet. 1:4). Mingling implies that two matters are combined together, each retaining its original nature. Our relationship with God is a mingling of divinity with humanity, but a third nature is not produced. Divinity is still there, and humanity is still there. This is a more intrinsic way of describing the relationship between the processed Triune God and His redeemed, regenerated elect. We are not only united to Him in life, but we are mingled with Him in nature.
Since the fall of 1996, Brother Lee began to receive further light from the Lord concerning the relationship between God and man, using the term incorporation. Incorporation is something even further than mingling because incorporation is a matter not merely of life and nature but of persons. In the human life, a father imparts his life and nature to his son, and the son has the life and nature of the father, but the father can never be in the son, and the son can never be in the father. They have the mingling, and they have the union, but they do not have the incorporation. Incorporation is a matter of persons coinhering, one being in the other and the other being in the one. Our relationship with our redeeming God is one of coinhering. We must see the New Jerusalem in the light of its being a divine-human incorporation. We are all burdened for the building up of the church that it may one day consummate the New Jerusalem, but what sort of building is it? We may still think of building somewhat as a physical matter. The New Jerusalem does have a practical aspect, but more intrinsically we must see the New Jerusalem as an incorporation. It is a coinhering dwelling place of God and man.
If we are to labor for the building of the New Jerusalem, we must labor toward this goal of incorporation. The work of the apostle Paul was one of incorporating the Triune God into the believers and incorporating the believers into the Triune God. He travailed in birth until Christ was formed in the believers (Gal. 4:19). For God to be formed in us is for God to dwell in us. He also bowed his knees to pray that the believers be strengthened into the inner man that Christ may make His home in their hearts (Eph. 3:14-17). For Christ to make His home in our hearts is for Him to coinhere with us as a mutual dwelling. This kind of work is the building of the New Jerusalem. Paul labored, struggling according to Christ's operation, admonishing every man and teaching every man in order to present every man full grown in Christ (Col. 1:28-29). He wanted to impart life to the believers until they were full grown in Christ.
Adding God to others, to bring God into man and man into God, to produce the coinhering, the incorporation of God and man, is the work that results in the building of the New Jerusalem.
We can testify that the ministry in the Lord's recovery always incorporates us into the Triune God and the Triune God into us, and we have been the beneficiaries of this ministry. In the early days of the church life, people from all backgrounds came into the church, from hippies to missionaries. Brother Lee repeatedly taught us not to adjust them but to impart Christ, that is, feed them with Christ. By incorporating these believers into the Triune God there was a transformation. To be sure, the work that our brother did resulted in the building of the New Jerusalem. Today we are still reaping the benefit and enjoying the solid foundation of the Triune God being incorporated into us. To incorporate man into the Triune God and the Triune God into man is the work that we need to do. May we be focused on the intrinsic things of the New Jerusalem, which is the incorporation of God and man, and not be disturbed or distracted by outward, superficial things. The New Jerusalem is nothing less than the enlarged, divine-human incorporation.
A Universal Couple, the Consummation of the Divine Romance
According to Its Humanity, It Is the Human Wife (with the Divine Life and Nature) of the Lamb— the Redeeming God
The New Jerusalem is a universal couple, the consummation of the divine romance. According to its humanity, it is the human wife of the Lamb—the redeeming God (Rev. 21:2, 9), but it has the divine life and nature. The New Jerusalem as the wife is human, yet it has the divine life and nature. Because it is human, it can be the human wife of the redeeming God, and because it has the divine life and nature, it can marry a divine person.
According to Its Divinity, It Is the Divine Husband (the Redeeming God in His Consummated Embodiment, Christ, with His Human Life and Nature) of God's Redeemed Elect
According to its divinity, it is the divine Husband, the redeeming God in His consummated embodiment, Christ, with His human life and nature, of God's redeemed elect. The New Jerusalem is constituted not merely with humanity but also with divinity. Because it has the divine constitution, it is the divine Husband to God's redeemed elect. The New Jerusalem, however, is not God in the "raw" form, but the redeeming God in His consummated embodiment, which is Christ, with His human life and nature. It is wonderful that we humans can have the divine life and nature and so become the human wife of the Lamb-God, and that the redeeming God, having the human life and. nature, can become the divine Husband to us, His redeemed elect. Because we have always viewed the New Jerusalem merely as the bride, we may be bewildered when we hear that the New Jerusalem is also the Husband. We must see that the New Jerusalem has both the divine and human natures. It is a divine-human constitution. In its divinity it is the Husband, and in its humanity it is the wife. What we see here is the wonderful, universal, enlarged, divine-human incorporation. At the end of the Bible we cannot distinguish God and man. Our new hymn declares, "The Triune God, tripartite man— / A loving pair eternally— / As man yet God they coinhere, / A mutual dwelling place to be." This is a very beautiful expression. The loving pair is one who is man yet God coinhering. This is the New Jerusalem.
We must apply this to our work. Our burden is that our work would be a work that results in the New Jerusalem. We certainly are blessed to have had the example, the pattern, of the ministry among us. Our conscience can testify that as Brother Lee labored in our midst for these many years, his living was always to live out the New Jerusalem, and his working was to work out the New Jerusalem. This was a struggle, and for all of us to participate in this struggle means a lot. It means much that there are some co-workers who are struggling for the same goal. God and Satan are struggling, and who will get the goal? Our brother was right in the middle of this struggle. We, and the co-workers and elders in particular, must ask the Lord for His enlightenment and mercy that we would be intrinsically one with our brother. Brother Samuel Chang was with Watchman Nee for many years. He once told us that of all the co-workers of Brother Nee in China, there were only two and a half of them who were really one with him, one of whom was Brother Lee. That is a sobering word.
By the Lord's mercy we all have seen that Brother Lee's ministry was the New Testament ministry. After all the storms through which the Lord has kept us, none of us is here opposing this ministry. Through all those storms the Lord did a work of purging, and those who opposed this ministry are no longer in our midst. Now we all purport to be one with this ministry, yet how intrinsic is our oneness with this ministry? The ministry that our brother carried out is for the producing of the New Jerusalem. We need to see this and be one with this ministry intrinsically. We can certainly be one with Brother Lee's ministry in an external way. We agree with it; we do not disagree with it. We may use his books, and we may even give his messages, yet if our being is not intrinsically one with this ministry to work out the New Jerusalem, we will never be the same, and we will never do the one work. The one work, the oneness, and the sameness are intrinsic, and they are in the divine and mystical realm. If we are not intrinsically in that divine and mystical realm to touch the intrinsic essence of this work, we will merely pick up the "chicken feathers" and the "garlic skins." We will carry on, even quoting Brother Lee, but our being and our work will never have the same effect on others to produce the New Jerusalem through the building up of the Body of Christ. With us there will not be the reality of the Body of Christ, and if there is no reality of the Body of Christ, there will not be the result of the New Jerusalem. This kind of work will one day pass through the fire and be burned. Only the work which results in and endures unto the New Jerusalem will remain. Brother Lee kept a large plaque on the wall in front of his desk. On the plaque is a picture of a bonfire with the words of 1 Corinthians 3:15: "If anyone's work is consumed, he will suffer loss, but he himself will be saved, yet so as through fire." This matter was always before our brother. If our work today does not result in the New Jerusalem, one day it will be consumed by fire. We will suffer loss, but we ourselves will be saved through fire.
May the Lord grant us mercy to delve into the ministry in such a way that the Lord brings us into the intrinsic things. We would not merely agree with Brother Lee's messages. Rather, something must happen to our being to make us intrinsically one with everything that his life and work were before us. Only in such a way can a group of blended co-workers be produced to carry the Lord's recovery forward. If we ourselves are not persons of the New Jerusalem, then merely quoting Brother Lee's words cannot vicariously produce the same result. Our work is nothing less than the operation of our being. Our being must be intrinsically one with the vision of the New Jerusalem as the incorporation of God with man, so that all that we do and our service to the saints and to the churches will be for the enlargement of this divine-human incorporation. Each one of us needs the incorporation of the apostolic ministry with the heavenly ministry of Christ, which is the intrinsic incorporation with the ministry of our brother, that the work we carry out in the churches will be one which results in the New Jerusalem.
Laboring for the Enlargement and Building Up of the Universal, Divine-human Incorporation
By Begetting the Believers to Be the Expansion of This Incorporation and by Ministering the Life Supply to the Believers for Their Growth in This Incorporation
The apostles labor for the enlargement and building up of the universal, divine-human incorporation by begetting the believers to be the expansion of this incorporation (1 Cor. 4:15; Philem. 10) and by ministering the life supply to the believers for their growth in this incorporation (1 Cor. 3:2, 6-7).
Betrothing the Believers to Christ to Be the Bride for the Bridegroom, the Wife of the Lamb
The apostles also betroth the believers to Christ to be the bride for the Bridegroom (John 3:29), the wife of the Lamb (2 Cor. 11:2; John 3:29; Rev. 19:7; 21:2, 9). —G. C.

第五篇　使徒们工作的最高点─作出新耶路撒冷（二）
The Highest Point of the Apostles' Work— To Work Out the New Jerusalem (2)
纲目|Outline|对照-目录
（本文为英文听抄扫描所得，未经校对）
God's central work is to build the New Jerusalem. The contents of this series of messages is not our own idea or our particular view of the Bible. If we were to spend many weeks to study the Bible in a careful and deep way, we could prove book by book and point by point that what God is doing is building the New Jerusalem. In the teaching of today's Christianity, the New Jerusalem, like heaven, is simply the destiny of the believers. Even the professors in the seminaries do not properly see the New Jerusalem. Among us in the Lord's recovery our understanding is much higher. Today God is actively building the New Jerusalem. This is our unique work. Everything must be brought into line with this. Everything in the Bible is for the New Jerusalem, so we should not teach anything that is not related to it in some way. In any message we give, we can show how a particular item in the Bible leads the believers into the New Jerusalem. We need to bring everything under the governing vision of the New Jerusalem. Everything we do in our locality and in the regions of the work needs to be brought in line with it. If we cannot bring our work in line with the New Jerusalem, we should immediately drop it. This is not a legal matter. Rather, it has to do with the fire through which our work will pass (1 Cor. 3:15). If our work does not pass through that fire, we will suffer loss. We must examine ourselves and consider whether our work is working out the New Jerusalem. This must be the view of our work. The New Jerusalem is the unique and central work that God is doing.
The way God does this work is by investing His own being into it. In the last three messages in The God-Men, Brother Lee shared that the New Jerusalem is the Father, the Son, and the Spirit invested into mankind to produce His expression. The work of the New Jerusalem is not a work which He does apart from Himself. He is pouring out His being to produce the New Jerusalem. Therefore, we should not pour out our being for anything else. If God Himself is investing His being in it, we certainly should do likewise.
It is a great honor for us to be brought into this work with God. If it were left to us, no doubt we would be carrying out another kind of ministry. It is a mercy that we have been given the secret of God's work—to build the Body of Christ to consummate the New Jerusalem—and it is a glory that we know this secret. There is no higher glory for a human being than to become God's co-worker (2 Cor. 6:1). It is unfathomable that God would make us co-workers with Him, but this is what has happened. We have been brought into the one work that God is doing. He has given us all the "trade secrets" and all the "capital" we need to carry out His enterprise to build what He is building.
The New Jerusalem is the highest point in a step by step, level by level progression. We must first work together with God to make God's chosen ones His regenerated ones by preaching the gospel and helping them to repent and call on the Lord. Then we work to make the new believers sanctified ones, not only positionally but dispositionally. Strictly speaking, if a believer does not become a sanctified one, he cannot be in the New Jerusalem, because the New Jerusalem is the holy city. Of course, the eternal life of God will work on the believers and bring them to the goal, but the elders and co-workers have been charged by the Lord to bring the believers in their locality into the New Jerusalem in a proper way, and they are accountable to the Lord for that. The sanctified ones must also become renewed ones. We need to study and fellowship concerning the way to help the believers to be renewed in their minds so that their goal is nothing other than the New Jerusalem. Then we must help them to be transformed. What does it mean to be transformed? If we are mostly the same as our natural parents, we are not transformed. Brother Lee once said that to be transformed means that we become flexible. If we are not flexible, we are still in our peculiarities, and this indicates that we are not transformed. After transformation, our work must help the believers to be conformed. To be conformed means that we are in the mold of the death of Christ. In the mold of His death, we no longer live out our own image, but we live the image of the firstborn Son of God. Ultimately we help the believers to be glorified until their full expression is Christ. We need to study and fellowship all these matters. We cannot have them merely as terms in the Lord's recovery. We have to have the reality, and we have to be in a work that is step by step, ascending level by level.
It is not enough to be impressed with these messages. As elders and co-workers we need to learn how to do this work. We need to be practical and fellowship, blend, and study everything we do, that we may help the saints and carry out a step-by-step, level-by-level work to work out the New Jerusalem. We are not merely working according to the New Jerusalem, but our very work produces the New Jerusalem. In our observation of Brother Lee we have seen one who worked out the New Jerusalem. Many of us are a testimony that he worked out the reality of the gold base, the pearl gates, and the wall and foundations of the New Jerusalem. It is needful to consider our work in this light. Is this the kind of work we are doing in our locality?
WORKING OUT THE REALITY OP THE GOLD BASE OF THE NEW JERUSALEM FOR ITS BUILDING
The apostles work out the reality of the gold base of the New Jerusalem for its building (Rev. 21:18b, 21b) by:
1) experiencing God the Father in His nature as the base upon which the New Jerusalem is built;
2) being directed by the divine nature as the way, signified by the golden street, which is part of the base;
3) having God's golden nature as their inward being;
4) helping the saints to partake of, to participate in and enjoy, the divine nature so that they may become a part of the New Jerusalem, which, as a mountain of gold, is the ultimate, unique, and eternal golden lampstand (2 Pet. 1:4; Rev. 21:18b); and
5) moving in the flow of the Spirit—the fellowship of the divine life—signified by the river of water of life (Rev. 22:1).
God the Father in His nature is the source of our work. When we work according to our own nature, we may feel that we are successful. However, we are merely building by our disposition. We may desire to be in the one work that God is doing, but in actuality we are building on our own "construction site." We may think we are doing a significant work, but if we are doing it by our own nature, it will not pass through the fire. Many times we deceive ourselves. We do things according to our disposition, our nature, and our natural way, and we feel it is acceptable and may even want to promote it. This is not working out the New Jerusalem. Often we need a word from the ministry to adjust us. Our work must be according to God the Father by our experiencing God the Father in His nature as the base.
As we work according to God's nature, we need to help the saints to participate in and enjoy the divine nature. At times the saints come to us with their concerns and problems. When we fellowship with them, we need to be moving and operating according to the divine nature, and we need to help them to be brought into a moving and a living that is according to the divine nature. Our goal is to have a divine, eternal, golden lampstand in our locality, not a lampstand composed of our own nature.
The apostles move in the flow of the Spirit, which is the fellowship of the divine life signified by the river of water of life. The apostle John was in the flow of the Spirit, the fellowship of the New Jerusalem (Rev. 22:1; 1 John 1:3). He was in the river of water of life, and he was striving to keep the saints in the fellowship, that is, in this flow of the Spirit. This kind of moving needs to be our work. We need to constantly refer to the New Jerusalem as the pattern, and we have to build according to the blueprint that we see in the Bible. Such a building needs to become a real structure in our locality. In our locality there should be a river, and the saints should move in the flow of the Spirit.
WORKING OUT THE REALITY OF THE PEARL GATES OF THE NEW JERUSALEM FOR ITS ENTRY
The apostles work out the reality of the pearl gates of the New Jerusalem for its entry (Rev. 21:12-13, 21a) by: 1) experiencing and ministering Christ in His life-essence through His life-releasing death and life-secreting resurrection to produce the pearls, which are the gates; 2) testifying that the three of the Triune God—signified by three gates on each side of the city—work together to bring people into the city (Matt. 28:19; Luke 15:1-32); 3) preaching regeneration through the death-overcoming and the life-secreting Christ as the entrance into the city (John 3:3, 5-6; 1 Pet. 1:3, 23; Titus 3:5); 4) proclaiming the availability of the entrance of the holy city toward mankind universally, signified by three gates on each of the four sides of the city (Rev. 21:12-13); and 5) being one with Christ in His life-releasing death and life-imparting resurrection to minister life to the saints for the building up of the Body (Col. 1:24; 2 Cor. 4:10-12).
One of the most attractive features of the New Jerusalem is that it has gates. This means that we can enter into it. We need to work out the reality of the pearl gates of the New Jerusalem in our locality and in our areas of the work. It is easy to build gates to attract people in a natural way, but these are not the pearl gates of the New Jerusalem. The entry we give to God's chosen ones needs to be through experiencing Christ in His life-essence through His life-releasing death and His life-secreting resurrection. The gates we build need to be gates that bring people in, and when they go in through the gates, they pass through the death of Christ, touching Christ in His life-essence through His life-releasing death and the life-secreting resurrection. Those are the kind of gates we must have in the Lord's recovery. The gospel implied by the gates is a gospel which includes the cross. God's chosen ones pass through the death and resurrection of Christ, and by doing so they are sanctified to be brought into the city. We must be one with Christ ourselves in His life-releasing death and life-imparting resurrection to minister life to the saints for the building up of the Body. We will have no problems in our work if we are always in the death and resurrection of Christ.
WORKING OUT THE REALITY OF THE WALL AND FOUNDATIONS OF THE NEW JERUSALEM
The apostles work out the reality of the wall and foundations of the New Jerusalem (Rev. 21:12, 14, 17, 19-20). The base of the New Jerusalem is related to God the Father; the pearl gates are related to God the Son; and the working out of the reality of the wall and the foundations is related to God the Spirit. This again indicates that the Triune God has invested His being into humanity to produce an expression of Himself in His Trinity.
Working Out the Reality of the Wall
The Wall Functioning to Express God and to Separate the City from the Nations
We cannot have the gold base and the pearl gates alone in our locality. We need to work out the reality of the wall. The New Jerusalem is not a city unless it has a wall. If we review church history in a brief way, we can see how believers have dealt with the building of the wall. As long as the church was persecuted in the first, second, and third centuries, there was a wall. The nations did not dare to enter in. There was a real separation and a real expression. The martyrs were expressing
God as they died. However, the subtle enemy found a way to tear down the wall. The Roman Empire stopped killing the believers and made Christianity the state religion. From that time onward there was no wall at all. The nations, under Constantine, came rushing in, and the whole nature of the church was diluted. Eventually, there was no distinction between the church of God and the emperor. The emperor called　councils, made decisions, and manipulated bishops.
There was no wall, and the nations were not kept out. Maintaining the wall, however, requires a balance. In the Lord's recovery we have been successful in keeping the nations out, but sometimes the wall may be too high and without any gates. Brother Lee told us that there is a pervasive attitude in the churches of being too strict with the new ones. There has to be a balance. We still want people to come into the city, but we do not want the nations to overrun the wall.
We must be careful in our localities that we do not take "the way of the nations." This is the term Brother Nee used to describe the Lord's Day morning message meeting. We have to move from a big meeting church life to a vital group church life. This is to keep the nations out of the city.
The Wall Functioning to Protect the City from the Destroyers of the Divine Building
Those Who Blow Divisive Winds of Teaching by Emphasizing Things Other Than the Central Teaching of God's Economy
The church has a tragic history related to the destroyers. The destroyers include those who blow divisive winds of teaching by emphasizing things other than the central teaching of God's economy (Eph. 4:14; 1 Tim. 1:4). Today in Christianity there is no room for the central teaching of God's economy. In this point the church historically has failed. This failure begins with a small deviation. Today we are in the center of God's economy, so we need to build the wall that nothing else can come in. We cannot let anything corrupt our resolve to stay on the central lane of God's economy. We may have other matters among us, but they must not be emphasized. Nothing can be emphasized but God's economy. History tells us that by A.D. 100 God's economy was mostly lost. If we read the second-century Christian writers, we cannot find the central line of God's economy. One early Christian writing, for example, says that there are two ways: the way of life and the way of death. It goes on to detail how not to be in the way of death, but it says little concerning the way of life. This indicates that the wall was torn down.
Those Who Preach and Teach Heresies
The wall protects the cit, from those who preach and teach heresies (2 Pet. 2:1; 2 John 7-11). This may be the only aspect of the wall that the church was able to build up. Many teachers, such as Athanasius, the Cappadocians, Gregory of Nyssa, Gregory of Nazianzus, and Basil of Caesarea were zealous to stop the heresies. Many heretics were even removed from the empire.
Those Who Are Sectarian and Who Make Divisions
The wall protects the city from those who are sectarian and who make divisions (Titus 3:10; Rom. 16:17). Many sects have been established throughout the history of the church. Long before Luther there were two wings, two main divisions, in the church: the west and the east. Moreover, both in the eastern wing and the western wing, there were many smaller groups. There was no wall against sectarianism. In this matter, the church became defenseless.
Those Who Are Ambitious for Position
The wall protects the city from those who are ambitious for position (3 John 9). The reason there is a papal system today is that in the fifth and sixth centuries there were some very ambitious persons. They made the papacy an institution to the extent that there was no longer a wall to keep out those who were ambitious for position; rather, the church was dominated by a group of ambitious people. This should cause us to fear, because in our natures we are not basically different from these men. Every one of us has the potential to be a "pope." We like people to follow us and honor us. If we are not in the divine nature of the Father, we may become like Catholicism or Greek Orthodoxy. The church did not merely decide to be this way. The Vatican came into being because the believers did not remain in the divine and mystical realm. Something similar can happen to us if we are not in the reality of working out the New Jerusalem.
The Wall of the City Being in Resurrection and the Apostles Ministering the Transforming and Building Life of Christ for the Believers' Transformation and Building
The wall of the city is also in resurrection (Rev. 21:17; 2 Cor. 1:9), and the apostles minister the transforming and building life of Christ for the believers' transformation and building (2 Cor. 3:18; Eph. 4:16).
We all want to work out the reality of the base, the pearl gates, and the wall and foundations. We do not want to fall into decline, but how can we check our work to know if it is the work of the New Jerusalem? How do we know we will not become a "pope" in our locality? In the past we only hoped and prayed that this would not happen, and the Lord has had mercy on us. However, there is a practical way to check our work. It is the blending of the churches. This is more than blending with the brothers in our own locality. Two brothers who have been together for a long time may know how to work with each other, but when a brother comes from another place, there is no blending with him. We cannot be blended only with the persons in our locality.
The real blending is to be "crossed out." It is not only shaking hands and looking into each others eyes. To be blended is to be willing to receive a frank word about our work. A group of brothers and sisters once had a strong feeling to stay in a certain city for the Lord's recovery as an "outpost" there. A co-worker warned them that if they stayed there apart from the neighboring churches, they would one day oppose the Lord's recovery. The saints received the fellowship and returned to the nearby churches. This is an example of blending. The only way we can check our work is to be blended. We have to take the way of blending; we have no other way to know if our work is right. The reason we do not blend is because we are afraid of what the other co-workers or elders will do when they come to our locality. We need to be changed so that we are more afraid of ourselves than of the ones who come to blend with us. We may be more dangerous for our locality than those who come to blend. Actually, the blending is to help us. Blending has to be our way.
We should get into these outlines and have a sober consideration. We should ask, "Am I living by God the Father as the base, by God the Son as the pearls, and by God the Spirit as the wall and foundations?" The only way to know the answer is to blend. When the Lord was on the earth, the main emphasis of His work was not healing or other matters. It was to produce a group of twelve blended co-workers. In time, they were so blended that when one of them stood up to speak, they all stood up to speak, when one of them prayed, they all prayed, and when the Spirit fell on one of them, they all received the Spirit. They were one group of blended co-workers. Eventually, those blended co-workers became the foundations of the New Jerusalem.
Brother Lee said, "I am right away in the middle of this struggle.... I do not know how much the Lord would have me in this struggle, but I do know your participation in this struggle means a lot." This struggle mainly refers to the spiritual warfare, but it also refers to the building of the Body. We need to struggle in prayer, but we also need to struggle to visit other churches to blend. It is inconvenient to do this, but if we want to preserve our localities and the saints who have been put under our care, and if we want to have a work that produces the New Jerusalem, a part of our laboring needs to be to visit the other churches, attend the conferences, fellowship with the brothers, and be willing to be terminated. We cannot only have blending in our locality. We must have blending in the Lord's recovery. This is the only way. If we do not blend, we will have different kinds of churches in a sectarian way. Blending is the real way to assure that we have the work of the New Jerusalem. — K. R.
The highest point of the apostles' living and work is the New Jerusalem. Since the New Jerusalem is unique, we can conclude that our work and living must also be one. We may wonder how we can have one work. The work of the New Jerusalem is not like a "nine-to-five job." This is a work that comes out of our very being. If our living is not the same as our being, it is merely a performance. An actor can be one person today and a different person tomorrow. The living of the New Jerusalem, however, comes out of our being, and it results in a work. Our being, our living, and our work should all be the same. To do one work requires us to have one being and one living. Apparently, every one of us has a different kind of living; you are you and I am I. Some of us come from the east, while some come from the west, Europe, and many other places. If our being is not the same, how can we do the one work? The only way we can do this is by denying ourselves and living according to the life that the Lord has given us. We praise the Lord that He has regenerated us and installed a life in us that can have one kind of living. If we walk according to this life, that is, according to the Spirit, then it is possible to do the one work.
WORKING UNDER THE ADMINISTRATION OF THE THRONE
Honoring God as the Source, Origin, Initiator, Planner, Designer, and Arranger of all Their Work
If we want to work out the New Jerusalem, we must work under the administration of the throne (Rev. 22:1-2). This means that we have to honor God as the Source, Origin, Initiator, Planner, Designer, and Arranger of all our work (Rom. 11:36; Heb. 4:16; Acts 13:2-4). Many times we may want to initiate a work for God, but we forget that God does not need us to be the Head; He Himself is the Head. God does not need us to be His council; He does not need, our advice. He is the very God, and all we have to do is follow Him. All our work should be from God as the Source.
Brother Nee told us that there are two kinds of sins. One is the sins that we commit before men, while the other is the sins we commit before God. The sins we commit before men are the ordinary sins in our daily life. In addition to our daily sins, however, we may also commit sins in God's work, which Numbers 18:1 calls "the iniquity of the sanctuary." Any time our flesh is manifested or we impose our self- will, speak rashly, or make suggestions out of our old, natural self, we are committing a sin in God's work.
In God's work we must take care of three considerations. First, the initiation of God's work must be according to God's will. It is not according to our will, purpose, thinking, inclination, or natural likes and dislikes. Second, the advance of God's work must be according to the power of God. A work may be initiated by God, but we may use our natural power to carry it out. This also is a sin against God's work. The proper way to carry out a work may be illustrated by the kind of currency which is usable in a country. If we go to a foreign country, we cannot use our own money there. We have to change it into the local currency before it will be accepted. Our natural power is like an unusable currency. In God's work, the only thing that God accepts is that which is done through His power. We cannot use our natural power to try to accomplish God's work. Lastly, the result of God's work must be for the glory of God. When we do something for the Lord, seemingly with His blessing, it is very easy for us to be proud. However, God resists the proud but gives grace to the humble (1 Pet. 5:5) because what God desires is something for His own glory. The result of all our work must be for the glory of our God. We are working out the New
Jerusalem, and the New Jerusalem is the enlargement of the Holy of Holies. In the Holy of Holies there* is only the ark with the cherubim above it, signifying the glory of God. For us to work out the New Jerusalem, we have to see that we are working out something which is for the glory of God.
In the Holy of Holies, everything is holy. This means that nothing is common. It is very easy for us to change the holy things into common things, but in the New Jerusalem, which is the enlarged Holy of Holies, we cannot handle things in a common and unholy way. When we first begin to work for the Lord, we are very careful before Him. We go to the Lord with much prayer, and we touch our spirit very much so that whatever we do in that work would be for the Lord. However, after doing a certain work a few times, we may become loose and more relaxed. We do not go to the Lord as much, and the matter becomes common to us. When we first baptize people, we are very much before the Lord, but after we baptize fifty or more people, we feel that we can do it "in our sleep," and we do not have to touch our spirit. In this way, the holy things become common. The first time we give a message, we may spend weeks to prepare for it. We may pray much with the brothers, and do many things to make sure we touch the holy things properly. As we speak more and more, however, the holy things may become common. In the same way, when we first learn about the Lord's table and all its significances, we are very much before the Lord to appreciate it. After years of attending the Lord's table, however, do we still have the same feeling, or has the Lord's table become something common and not holy?
May the Lord touch us that we may see that this is a holy work. We cannot offend our God in His holiness, glory, or sovereignty. We cannot do anything that is incompatible with His will. Otherwise, the Lord will judge us because we have sinned against His work. The priests were appointed to judge concerning the common, daily sins, but the Lord Himself judges the sins against His work. If we sin against God's work we will lose the power of life and become stale. Sometimes after speaking in the meetings we feel that we are stale. If we sin against God's work, we will also experience spiritual death. We have heard of certain brothers who, when they departed from God's work, immediately experienced spiritual death. All of us, especially the co-workers and elders, will be judged at the judgment seat of Christ. We must realize that God is only satisfied when His will, not ours, is fulfilled.
We can do a lot of things, and we may think they are very worthwhile things, but God's will is all that counts. We are not those doing our own work; we must be those doing God's work.
The result of God's work should be His glory. May the Lord really touch us about this. If someone comes after a meeting to commend us for giving a good message, it is too easy for us to feel glorious. We are here not working for our glory but for the glory of God. We do not want to be in a situation in which we are sinning against God, committing the iniquity of God's sanctuary, which is an infringement of God's glory and of God Himself.
Hebrews 4:16 says that we may come boldly to the throne of grace to receive mercy and find grace for timely help. After reading Brother Nee's message on the iniquity of the sanctuary, we may be in fear and trembling and question whether or not we should work for the Lord. Seemingly, if we do not serve as priests, we will not be in danger of sinning against the Lord in His work, but we have to serve because responsibility has been placed upon us. The very throne of grace is now installed in our spirit. The secret is in our spirit, which is the habitation and dwelling place of God. In John 1:51 there is the heavenly ladder which brings heaven, with the throne of God, to earth and joins earth to heaven. If we come to the throne which is now within us, we receive the love of God which is manifested in His mercy and His grace. Luke 15 tells us that our God loved us and sent the Savior Christ to seek for us. He also sent the Spirit to light a lamp and find us, and out of His compassion He receives us and gives us the best robe and the fattened calf. If we keep coming to this throne of grace that is within us, we will touch Him as mercy and grace, and this mercy and grace will make us the real workers that work out the New Jerusalem. We cannot do this work in ourselves. This one work has to be done out of the Spirit who has been joined to our spirit. Without working in this Spirit, we can never have the one work.
Submitting to Christ as the Head
We also must submit to Christ as the Head (Col. 1:18; Eph. 1:22-23; 4:15; 1 Cor. 11:3). Colossians 1:18 tells us Christ is the Head of the Body, and Ephesians 1:22-23 says that God subjected all things under His feet and gave Him to be Head over all things to the church. Ephesians 4:15 says that we grow up into Him in all things, who is the Head, Christ, and in 1 Corinthians 11:3 we see that Christ is the Head of all men, and God is the head of Christ. We have to recognize that we have one Head, and we have to submit to this Head. In another message concerning the prickly ministry, Brother Nee said that our service is related directly and solely to the Head. Sometimes we may think that we are serving a certain persons, but actually we are serving the Lord. Our service can be illustrated by the functioning of the members of our body. When we intend to lift a table, we put our hands to it. However, our hands do not serve each other or the table; they directly serve the head of our body.
As Vanquished Foes, Always Being Led in Triumph in Christ
The apostles are vanquished foes, always being led in triumph in Christ (2 Cor. 2:14-15; Eph. 4:8). Paul indicated that when we submit ourselves to the Head, we are the captives of Christ as vanquished foes being led in triumph in Christ (2, Cor. 2:14). Christ captured so many persons such as Paul, John, and Peter, and in time He also captured you and me. We are all captives. Previously, we were captives of Satan, but now we are captives of Christ. The captives of Christ are led in a triumphal procession which magnifies Christ in His victory.
Reigning in Life by the Abundance of Grace and of the Gift of Righteousness and Turning People from the Authority of Satan to God
The apostles reign in life by the abundance of grace and of the gift of righteousness (Rom. 5:17). If we are really captives of the Lord, we can reign in life. Then by being His captives, that is, by being under the authority of God, we can turn people from the authority of Satan to God (Acts 26:18).
WORKING TO BUILD UP THE TEMPLE OF GOD
Building with the Triune God as the Precious Materials and Not Marring or Destroying God's Temple by Building with Worthless Materials
Our work is to build up the temple of God, but we must build with only one thing, that is, with the Triune God as the precious materials (1 Cor. 3:12). We should not mar or destroy God's temple by building with worthless materials, that is, with our nature, disposition, old "I," old creation, self, inclination, and preference.
Carrying Out the Ministry of Reconciliation
The apostles carry out the ministry of reconciliation so that the believers who still live in the flesh may pass through the second veil, which signifies the flesh, to enter into the Holy of Holies to live with God in their spirit (1 Cor. 6:17) for the building up of God's dwelling place (2 Cor. 5:19-20). Second Corinthians 5:19 says that God in Christ was reconciling the world to Himself, but verse 20 says that He was reconciling the believers to Himself. In the tabernacle, sinners passed through the first veil to enter into the Holy Place. We should not stop here. We still have to pass through the second veil to enter into the Holy of Holies. To bring people through the two veils is the ministry of reconciliation. As the ambassadors of Christ, we are working with God in His commission and His ministry to accomplish His purpose by reconciling not only sinners into the Holy Place but also believers into the Holy of Holies, which is the New Jerusalem.
In the Organic Union with Christ in His Crucifixion
The apostles build in the organic union with Christ in His crucifixion, experiencing the breaking of the flesh that the believers may enter through the rent veil (the flesh) into the Holy of Holies for the building up of the New Jerusalem as the enlarged and consummate Holy of Holies (Matt. 27:51; Heb. 10:20; Rev. 21:16). We must be one with Christ in His crucifixion. We can only bring people to where we have been. If we are not one with Christ in His crucifixion, and if we have not dealt with the flesh, we cannot bring people into the Holy of Holies. We may feel that the other brothers and sisters are not going on and that they are still in their flesh, but the main reason they are not going on is because we are not going on. Because we have not dealt with our flesh, we cannot bring them into the same dealing with the flesh and the oneness with the crucifixion of Christ. To experience the breaking of the flesh is for us to enter through the second veil, which is the flesh, into the Holy of Holies.
MINISTERING THE PROCESSED AND CONSUMMATED TRIUNE GOD AS THE SUPPLY
Nourishing the Believers with Spiritual Food and Drink
The apostles minister the processed and consummated Triune God as the supply (Rev. 22:1-2, 14, 17; 21:6; 7:16-17) by nourishing the believers with spiritual food and drink (1 Cor. 3:2; 10:3-4; Heb. 5:12-14). In our work, we are nourishing the believers. Paul spoke a number of times concerning eating the same thing, drinking the same thing, and being nourished with spiritual food and drink.
Supplying the Divine Life, Imparting Grace to the Believers, and Blessing the Saints with the Processed and Consummated Triune God
The apostles minister by supplying the divine life (2 Cor. 4:12; 1 John 5:16a). First John 5:16a says, "If anyone sees his brother sinning a sin not unto death, he shall ask and he will give life to him." Here it is not that we ask of the Lord, and the Lord gives him life, but we give life to the sinning brother by the life that Christ has given to us. If we practice and exercise this life and live according to it, we can give people life. This is our work. Our work is to nourish people and supply them with the divine life. The apostles also impart grace to the believers (Rom. 1:7; Gal. 6:18; Heb. 4:16; 1 Pet. 5:10, 12; Rev. 22:21), blessing the saints with the processed and consummated Triune God (2 Cor. 13:14; Rom. 15:29).
WORKING TO DIFFUSE THE DIVINE LIGHT
Receiving the Commission to Turn People from Darkness to Light
The apostles work to diffuse the divine light (Rev. 21:23-24a, lib). God in Christ is the divine light, and this light is now in us. As we exercise our spirit and walk according to the spirit, we receive the commission to turn people from darkness to light (Acts 26:18).
Enlightening Others That They Might See the Economy of the Mystery
The apostles also enlighten others that they might see the economy of the mystery (Eph. 3:9). This mystery was hidden in God, but some brothers before us have been enlightened by the Lord that we also may be enlightened concerning the mystery of the economy.
Putting on the Weapons of Light
We also cast off the works of darkness and put on the weapons of light (Rom. 13:12). What the Lord is doing must be what we are doing, and this is to work out the New Jerusalem. This work is not according to our will but according to His will.
The three basic structures of the New Jerusalem are the gold base, the pearl gates, and the wall and its foundation. Besides these, there are three furnishings and a threefold supply. The three furnishings are the throne with the Lord's administration, the temple as the palace for the co-kings' dwelling, and the light. The threefold supply in the New Jerusalem is the river of water of life, the tree of life, and the street, which provides the fellowship from the throne.
The center of the New Jerusalem is the throne. To build up the New Jerusalem, which is the enlargement and expansion of God through the God-men, we must first see that we are under the throne of God and in His administration. We are no longer free; we are fully governed by our Lord. Any time that we get out of this administration we are not doing His work. At that time, we are doing our own work.
The temple is the house, the palace, of all the co-kings for them to dwell, to rule, and to reign with Christ. We have the life of the King in us, which makes us the co-kings living and reigning in the temple.
We also have God in Christ as the light. The light enables us to live and move in the city. In the New Jerusalem we do not need the natural light of the sun and moon or the artificial light of lamps because we have God in Christ as our light. This signifies that we do not need our natural understanding, realization, or education. We need God as our light. God is shining in us through His Word. The brothers who went before us could enlighten us because they spent so much time in the Word, which is a shining light, a light that gives life. We have been enlightened and brought into this ministry. To carry out God's work and bring it to all the believers, we also have to spend time to enter into the Word of God, which today is not just the written and translated Word but the interpreted Word. — A. L.

GENERAL SUBJECT
THE NEW JERUSALEM—THE HIGHEST POINT OF THE APOSTLES' LIVING AND WORK
Message Six
The Highest Point of the Apostles' Work— To Work Out the New Jerusalem (3)
纲目|对照-听抄-目录
X.　Cooperating in their ministry with Christ's heavenly ministry to shepherd God's flock for the ultimate consummation of the eternal economy of God—John 21:15-17:
A.　Christ's heavenly ministry and the apostles' ministry on the earth cooperate together to carry out God's economy.
B.　The heavenly ministry of Christ is mainly to shepherd the church of God as His flock, which issues in His Body:
1.　To shepherd is to take all-inclusive tender care of the flock.
2.　"God...brought up from the dead our Lord Jesus, the great Shepherd of the sheep, in the blood of an eternal covenant"-—Heb. 13:20:
a.　God raised up our Lord from the dead to be the great Shepherd to consummate the New Jerusalem according to God's eternal covenant.
b.　The eternal covenant is to consummate the New Jerusalem by shepherding.
c.　The eternal covenant is the covenant of the new testament to gain a flock, which is the church issuing in the Body and consummating in the New Jerusalem.
C.　The apostolic ministry has been incorporated with Christ's heavenly ministry to take care of God's flock, which is the church that issues in the Body of Christ—John 21:15-17; Acts 20:28-29; 1 Pet. 2:25; 5:1-4.
XI.　As ambassadors of Christ in representing Christ to accomplish God's purpose, the apostles reigning in life unto eternal life, unto the New Jerusalem—2 Cor. 5:20; Rom. 5:17, 21; John 4:14:
A.　Unto eternal life is a particular expression.
B.　John 4:14b says, "The water that I will give him will become in him a spring of water gushing up into eternal life":
1.　Into (unto in Rom. 5:21) speaks of destination.
2.　The eternal life is the destination of the flowing Triune God.
3.　The eternal life eventually will become the New Jerusalem.
4.　Into eternal life means into the New Jerusalem.
C.　The New Jerusalem is the totality of the divine life, the totality of the life of God.
D.　The issue of our reigning in life will be the New Jerusalem, the consummate incorporation of God and man:
1.　God wants to be united and mingled with man.
2.　The God who has been united and mingled with man wants to be incorporated with man to be an incorporation.
3.　This union, mingling, and incorporation will consummate as the New Jerusalem.
E.　The goal of our reigning in life should be uniquely and ultimately the goal of God's eternal economy—the New Jerusalem as the universal incorporation of the union and mingling of divinity with humanity.
XII.　Other aspects of the apostles' work to work out the New Jerusalem:
A.　Serving as slaves—Rev. 22:3; 1:1; 2 Cor. 4:5.
B.　Ministering as royal priests—Rev. 22:3; Rom. 15:16; 1 Pet. 2:9.
C.　Blending the churches together with the New Jerusalem in view—Rom. 16:1-16, 21-24.
D.　Ushering the believers into the divine and mystical realm of the consummated Spirit and the pneumatic Christ—Phil. 1:19; Rom. 8:9; 2 Cor. 3:17-18.
E.　Recognizing the need of overcomers and laboring to produce them—2 Tim. 4:7-8:
1.　To overcome the degradation of the church—2 Tim. 2:19-22; Rev. 2—3.
2.　To build up the Body of Christ, which will consummate the New Jerusalem—Eph. 4:16.
3.　To build up Zion, the reality of the Body of Christ, within Jerusalem, the local churches, so that ultimately the entire New Jerusalem may become Zion—Rev. 14:1.
Note: These messages reveal that the apostles live what they build. They follow the pattern of the Lord Jesus as the first God-man, whose work was His living and whose move was His being. His life was His move and His move was His work. With the Lord Jesus there was no difference between His life, His work, and His move. The apostles as the duplication of Christ are the same. Their living out the New Jerusalem is their working out, their building, the New Jerusalem.

The Highest Point of the Apostles' Work— To Work Out the New Jerusalem (3)

The highest point of the apostles' living and work is the New Jerusalem, Even in eternity future we will remember how Brother Lee opened up this vision to us.
COOPERATING IN THEIR MINISTRY WITH CHRIST'S HEAVENLY MINISTRY TO SHEPHERD GOD'S FLOCK FOR THE ULTIMATE CONSUMMATION OF THE ETERNAL ECONOMY OF GOD
The apostles cooperate in their ministry with Christ's heavenly ministry to shepherd Gods flock for the ultimate consummation of the eternal economy of God (John 21:15-17). Much of the content of this message is taken from the final message of the Crystallization-Study of John, entitled "The Apostolic Ministry in Cooperation with Christ's Heavenly Ministry," and message seven of The Vital Groups, entitled "Shepherding and Teaching—the Obligation of the Vital Groups, the Basic Way Ordained by God in the Building Up of the Body of Christ to Consummate His Eternal Goal—the New Jerusalem." In addition, we recommend The Elders' Training—Book 11, The Eldership and the God- ordained Way for further help and much light in the practice of shepherding. We may never have associated shepherding with the New Jerusalem. It may seem they are different matters, but the recent ministry has made it clear that they are intrinsically related.
Christ's Heavenly Ministry and the Apostles' Ministry on the Earth Cooperate Together to Carry Out God's Economy
Christ's heavenly ministry and the apostles' ministry on the earth cooperate together to carry out God's economy. The main purpose and goal of the apostolic ministry incorporated with Christ's ministry are to build up the Body of Christ which will consummate the New
Jerusalem for the accomplishment of the eternal economy of God. Whatever Christ does in His heavenly ministry, the apostles do on the earth to carry it out. This is the cooperation of the apostolic ministry with Christ's heavenly ministry. Christ is carrying out His heavenly ministry as the life-giving Spirit indwelling the believers and supplying them with life to work out His organic salvation for the building up of the Body, but He fulfills this ministry through the apostles. Before He ascended, He commissioned His apostles with the New Testament ministry of God's economy. Whatever Christ is doing in the heavens, we should be doing on the earth, and what we are doing on the earth should be what He is doing in the heavens. He and we are not doing two different works or carrying out two different ministries. The only difference is that He is in the heavens, and we are on the earth, although He is in us energizing us. The Head and the Body, the Sender and the sent ones, Christ and His apostles with all the members, are universally undertaking the same project, the same job, the same work, that is, to build up the Body of Christ which will consummate the New Jerusalem. This is the one unique ministry of all the apostles, in fact, of all the believers.
The Heavenly Ministry of Christ Is Mainly to Shepherd the Church of God as His Flock, Which Issues in His Body
The heavenly ministry of Christ is mainly to shepherd the church of God as His flock, which issues in His Body. Shepherding is vital for the goal of the New Jerusalem. Christ's earthly ministry in His humanity for thirty-three and a half years was a ministry of shepherding. That set the pattern and laid the ground work for His heavenly ministry. After passing through death, resurrection, and ascension, He entered into the heavens. In the heavens He is at the right hand of God carrying out His ministry of shepherding. First He accomplished His ministry in the stage of incarnation, and now in resurrection and ascension He is carrying out His ministry in the stage of inclusion. Not long after He ascended and established the churches, however, they became degraded, so Christ's ministry came into the stage of intensification, in which He as the seven Spirits supplies His believers that they may be produced as overcomers. It is these overcomers who build up His Body to consummate the New Jerusalem. Today we are in the stage of intensification.
What the Lord is doing today is a shepherding work. To shepherd is to take all-inclusive tender care of the flock. Hebrews 13:20 says, "God...brought up from the dead our Lord Jesus, the great Shepherd of the sheep, in the blood of an eternal covenant." God raised up our Lord from the dead to be th great Shepherd to consummate the New Jerusalem according to God s eternal covenant. The covenant of the New Testament is an eternal covenant. This indicates that it belongs to the New Jerusalem because only the New Jerusalem is eternal. Moreover, this covenant is enacted in His blood. The eternal covenant is to consummate the New Jerusalem by shepherding. It is the covenant of the new testament to gain a flock, which is the church issuing in the Body and consummating in the New Jerusalem. If we read and pray-read the outline of this message, we will have a much higher, more eternal, and more intrinsic vision concerning our work of shepherding. Our shepherding is not merely to solve problems and pass on teachings. The work of our shepherding is the work carried on by the great heavenly Shepherd. Even now the Lord is shepherding all the saints and all the churches in a tender, sweet, and marvelous way. He is busy on the throne carrying out His shepherding.
The Apostolic Ministry Has Been Incorporated with Christ's Heavenly Ministry to Take Care of God's Flock, Which Is the Church That Issues in the Body of Christ
The apostolic ministry has been incorporated with Christ's heavenly ministry to take care of God's flock, which is the church that issues in the Body of Christ (John 21:15-17; Acts 20:28-29; 1 Pet. 2:25; 5:1-4). Christ's heavenly ministry is a shepherding ministry. He is the Shepherd and Overseer of our souls (1 Pet. 2:25) from now until eternity. Revelation 7:17 says, "For the Lamb who is in the midst of the throne will shepherd them and guide them to springs of waters of life; and God will wipe away every tear from their eyes." This verse is fulfilled first in the millennium and then in the eternal age of the New Jerusalem, where we will be constantly drinking the water of life and eating the tree of life. We will constantly be under His throne and under His eternal shepherding. The New Jerusalem is a city of shepherding, and we will be the eternal flock of God with our Shepherd. Every day and every moment our heavenly Christ is doing His shepherding work so that the church will issue forth. His heavenly ministry is a shepherding ministry, and this shepherding ministry is to build up the Body, which will consummate the New Jerusalem. To see this uplifts our view of the work of shepherding.
The Lord is at the right hand of God interceding for us. Christ is shepherding us even through His interceding. This should cause us to pray, "Lord, give me an intercessor's spirit and a shepherd's heart, just like Yours. May I correspond to You, duplicate You, reflect You, be one with You, and cooperate with You in every way." Such shepherding is the New Testament ministry for God's economy, and it is the commission He gave to His apostles before He ascended to the heavens. John 20 tells us that Christ, the incarnated One, became the all-inclusive One to breathe the life-giving Spirit into His disciples. Following this, chapter twenty-one reveals Christ's commission to His disciples concerning shepherding. Chapter twenty-one may seem to be an unnecessary appendix in this Gospel. This chapter, however, is the conclusion, completion, and consummation of the Gospel of John. If we do not know what shepherding is, the entire Gospel of John will be in vain to us. It is only when we shepherd others that we can know John in an intrinsic way. Shepherding is the key to the Gospel of John. The entire book of John is practically realized through the genuine shepherding. Without shepherding, all the wonderful things in this book cannot be real to us. Without shepherding, this Gospel has no completion and no consummation, and without Christ's heavenly shepherding, the Body cannot be produced and the New Jerusalem will not issue forth.
Through these outlines and messages, we need to be carried up in our spirit to a great and high mountain to see our work in the light of the New Jerusalem. Not only should our being be incorporated with the Triune God; our work and our ministry should also be incorporated with the heavenly ministry of Christ. This means that we shepherd in His shepherding, and He shepherds in our shepherding. It is no wonder that Peter says we shepherd according to God (1 Pet. 5:2). We do not shepherd in a human, natural way like social workers. We shepherd according to what God is in His attributes. When we shepherd, we shepherd others in God and with God, ministering God to them. This is the real shepherding.
Feeding is part of the organic salvation of God, and feeding is carried out by shepherding. Likewise, building is part of our organic salvation, and it is carried out by blending. Without shepherding and blending, it is hard to carry out God's organic salvation in its fullest extent to gain the Body of Christ. In these days we must focus our being in a practical way on the shepherding and blending among us. If these two things are carried out in a prevailing way, there will be a great revival among us.
Shepherding is the all inclusive tender care of the flock. Preaching the gospel is the first task of shepherding. As the Lord laid down His soul-life on the cross, He dispensed His life into the chosen ones. This was part of His shepherding work. John 10:11 says, "I am the good Shepherd; the good Shepherd lays down His life for the sheep." Even in His judicial redemption He was carrying out a work of shepherding. This means that our gospel preaching is a shepherding work.
Our care for the saints is a further work of shepherding. We have seen that John speaks about shepherding in his Gospel, but Paul and Peter also speak concerning this. After the Lord spoke to Peter in John 21, he had a strong impression concerning shepherding, and in his first Epistle he had much to say about it. In 1 Peter 2:25 he tells us that the Lord is the Shepherd and Overseer of our soul, our inner being and real person. In 5:1-4 he specifically charged the elders to "shepherd the flock of God among you, overseeing not under compulsion but willingly, according to God; not seeking gain through base means but eagerly; nor as lording it over your allotments but by becoming patterns of the flock." If we read and muse upon these points, we may have the sensation that we are failures and even a shame to the Lord. How much of our work is in this kind of shepherding, and how much of it is just to build up a work? It is not that we do not work; it is that all our work must be a shepherding work because only the shepherding work builds up the Body. Verse 4 says, "And when the Chief Shepherd is manifested, you will receive the unfading crown of glory." Those who are faithful to shepherd in this way will receive a crown of glory. The crown does not belong to great spiritual giants who do many works. The crown belongs to the humble shepherd who faithfully shepherds God's flock.
Paul also speaks of shepherding. Acts 20 says that while Paul was on his way to Jerusalem, he sent word to Ephesus and called for the elders of the church. He told them that they should shepherd God's flock, which God purchased with His own blood (v. 28). He also charged them to protect this flock from the "fierce wolves" which would rise up among them (w. 29-30). That was his last charge in person to the church in Ephesus. Ephesians 4:11 says, "And He Himself gave some as apostles and some as prophets and some as evangelists and some as shepherds and teachers." Shepherds and teachers are the same category of persons. Among the four kinds of gifted persons, the ministry of the first three, that is, of the apostles, prophets, and evangelists, depends upon shepherding. Without shepherding, the apostles, prophets, and evangelists cannot function. Even the apostles' work of preaching the gospel, raising up churches, and defining the truth depends on shepherding. Likewise, the evangelists' work and the prophets' speaking for God depends on a shepherding spirit. This is why Brother Lee spent one whole term of the full-time training on the details of shepherding in the vital groups. We need to go back to those messages for our help in the churches. If the whole recovery will properly practice shepherding, there will be a great revival. The obligation of the vital groups is shepherding according to God's ordination. According to their function, our vital groups are shepherding groups. Our burden is that not only every elder but every saint would be raised up to be a shepherd to shepherd others. This work is a direct cooperation with Christ's heavenly ministry. From now on, we need to help all the churches and all the saints to do the work of shepherding in all of its aspects.
Many think that Paul was a great apostle doing a great work as a great career. But Paul considered what he did as shepherding the flock of God. We have to be revolutionized in our logic and consideration. We should not think that we are going to do a great work for Christ like certain spiritual giants. These so-called giants actually did not accomplish much for God's interests. Instead, they only made a name for themselves with little result for the building up of the Body of Christ.
There is no work more cumbersome or more taxing to our endurance than shepherding, and there is no other work that requires more patience or requires us to die to such an extent. In our experience, shepherding is unceasing; it is an instant call to duty. It is pouring out everything and not seeing much result. The problem among us related to the increase is not initially gaining new ones. It is our giving up on them too early, lacking the patience to bear remaining fruit. In the shepherding work we will never see immediate results. To spend three years to gain one person is worthwhile. Today the work in the Lord's recovery is not merely to gain "bodies." It is to gain the material who will grow, be saved organically, and mature for the building up of the Body. This is not the work of Christianity, and it cannot be done in the way of Christianity. It can only be done by the God-ordained way, which is the way of Christ's heavenly ministry, the way of shepherding. The work that is for the New Jerusalem must be a work of shepherding. May our work match the ministry of Christ for the consummation of the New Jerusalem.
AS AMBASSADORS OF CHRIST IN REPRESENTING CHRIST TO ACCOMPLISH GOD'S PURPOSE, THE APOSTLES REIGNING IN LIFE UNTO ETERNAL LIFE, UNTO THE NEW JERUSALEM
As ambassadors of Christ in representing Christ to accomplish God's purpose, the apostles reign in life unto eternal life, unto the New Jerusalem (2 Cor. 5:20; Rom. 5:17, 21; John 4:14). Eternal life is nothing less than the New Jerusalem.
Unto eternal life is a particular expression. John 4:14b says, "The water that I will give him will become in him a spring of water gushing up into eternal life." The Father is the source of the living water as the fountain, the Son is the spring, and the Spirit is the flow. This flow carries us into the New Jerusalem to become the New Jerusalem. Into {unto in Rom. 5:21) speaks of destination. The eternal life is the destination of the flowing Triune God. The eternal life eventually will become the New Jerusalem. Therefore, into eternal life means into the New Jerusalem. The New Jerusalem is the totality of the divine life, the life of God. The issue of our reigning in life will be the New Jerusalem, the consummate incorporation of God and man. The God who has been united and mingled with man wants to be incorporated with man to be an incorporation. This union, mingling, and incorporation will consummate as the New Jerusalem. The goal of our reigning in life should be uniquely and ultimately the goal of God's eternal economy—the New Jerusalem as the universal incorporation of divinity with humanity. May we all reign in life, and may we carry out the shepherding ministry to cooperate with Christ's heavenly ministry to build up His Body to consummate the New Jerusalem. — M. C.
OTHER ASPECTS OF THE APOSTLE'S WORK TO WORK OUT THE NEW JERUSALEM
Serving as Slaves
The apostles serve as slaves (Rev. 22:3; 1:1; 2 Cor. 4:5). Revelation 22:3 says that in eternity we will serve as slaves, but the apostles serve as slaves more than anyone else in this age. Brother Lee was a pattern of a slave. He served the work in Russia as a slave, being always available to supply fellowship as it was needed- Although many times this was an inconvenience to him, he never considered it as such. He served the co-workers there day after day and month after month. The co-workers did not know how to build up something in Russia, but he was always available to guide and help them. During the building of the hall in Anaheim, Brother Lee supervised the work, and at the same time he prepared outlines for weekly life-studies and for the semiannual trainings. He spent nearly thirty-four years in this country doing nothing but ministering Christ to us to build up the Body of Christ. This is the pattern of a slave. In his letter of fellowship with thanks written on March 22, 1997, he referred to himself not as a big brother or as a boss but as a fellow slave, "one of His small slaves," and "His and your slave." Apostles are slaves, and to work out the New Jerusalem requires slaves. May we all be the slaves of God.
Ministering as Royal Priests
The apostles minister as royal priests (Rev. 22:3; Rom. 15:16; 1 Pet. 2:9). Paul tells us how he labored as a priest in the gospel (Rom. 15:16), taking the gospel to cities, countries, and regions to make the Gentiles offerings to God. We need to do the same thing today. To follow the apostles to build up the New Jerusalem, the first thing needed is to be royal priests offering up sinners to God.
To be the New Testament priests of the gospel, we need to practice the new way, the God-ordained way. After begetting sinners, we should nourish and shepherd them that they would become further offerings to God. Paul said that he admonished every man and taught every man in order to present every man not only saved but full-grown in Christ (Col. 1:28) as a mature offering to God. Paul labored as a royal priest to offer up people to God and then perfect them until they were mature in Christ. All the saints should also be priests of the gospel, offering up sinners as living offerings to God and shepherding, teaching, and nourishing them unto maturity. This was the apostle's service, and this is our service as priests. May the Lord make us such royal priests.
To be royal priests, we should follow Paul and the example of Brother Lee. Our brother practiced the priesthood of the gospel among us for thirty-four years. He brought the complete gospel to us, and his ministry has caused us to offer ourselves to God (Rom. 12:2). Most among us are more mature today than they were when they first touched the Lord's recovery. This is because of the apostle's unrelenting, prevailing speaking in the ministry to dispense Christ into us that we could grow unto maturity. Most in the Lord's recovery are much different from most or believers in the typical gatherings in Christianity, where they discuss the family life or the way to receive material blessing. We are different because of this ministry and because of what this ministry has worked into us to make us the offerings to God. We believe that our brother will one day say, "Lord, I present all these brothers and sisters to You." It is glorious that there was one among us that produced mature offerings throughout the whole earth. We want to follow the apostles, and we want our work to be a work that produces the New Jerusalem.
Blending the Churches Together with the New Jerusalem in View
The apostles blend the churches together with the New Jerusalem in view (Rom. 16:1-16, 21-24). Blending is a great matter. We all must be involved in the blending for the building up of the Body of Christ. In the matter of blending, Brother Lee again is a real pattern to us. Wherever he went, there was always a group of co-workers who were blended together with him to carry out the ministry. This was the case in Taiwan, the Philippines, this country, and any other country he went to. The result of his ministry was that he gained a group of blended co-workers, and today the center of the work is these co-workers.
The Lord is sending out a call to all of us. After Brother Lee's departure, what we must have for the going on of the recovery is a group of blended co-workers. Our brother did not intend that there be ten, twenty, or even one hundred co-workers, but rather a group of blended co-workers over the whole earth. Brother Lee told us that such a group of blended co-workers will become the very center of the work. Through this center the Lord will have the way to continue to release His ministry on the earth today, and there will be the way to have the leadership to carry out the Lord's recovery. We are from many different countries and churches over the whole earth, but the Lord wants to blend us as co-workers so that He can have a way on this earth. This does not mean that others do not have their place in the work to carry on faithfully and to carry out everything related to the Lord's recovery today. All the saints and all the elders should be blended together. Our whole situation should become one of blending.
All the churches and all the regions on this earth should be blended together. There are close to 2,300 churches in the Lord's recovery, in addition to those in mainland China. All these churches have one source since they were brought forth through one ministry with one speaking, first through Brother Nee and then through Brother Lee. They all have been produced by this ministry, which has unveiled God's New Testament economy. Having the same source means that we all are the same. There should be no differences. Neither countries nor languages should constitute a difference. We all should be blended together. There should be a blending between the co-workers and elders that brings in and paves the way for the Lord's move in His recovery today.
The apostle Paul set up a pattern of living the church life for the living of the Body life by preaching the gospel to raise up local churches (Rom. 15:14-24) and by bringing the Gentile churches into the fellowship of the Body of Christ with the Jewish churches in the communication of the provision of the Gentile churches to meet the necessity of the Jewish churches (w. 25-33). Paul helped all the churches to have a consciousness of preaching the gospel for the Body life, and he blended them together without regard to nationalities, regions, and races. Paul put in them the consideration for all the churches on the whole earth. If there was a need in one church, then the other churches rose up to meet that need. It is significant that the apostle Paul bore a　gift fromtheGentile churches tothe
Jewish churches. This meant that Paul was　doing his best to blend all the churches together. He blended together many saints and many local churches under his ministry for the practical living of the Body of Christ in the universal fellowship of the Body by recommendations and greetings between the saints and the churches mutually through him (16:1-16, 21-24). Paul by his recommendations and greetings in 16:1-24 is blending together many saints and many churches under his ministry for the practical living of the Body of Christ in the universal fellowship of the Body.
In The Experience of God's Organic Salvation Equaling Reigning in Chrisfs Life, Brother Lee said:
The last three chapters of Romans show us that we need to reign in life in two crucial matters: in imitating the apostles to bring the local churches into the fellowship of the Body of Christ and in following the apostles' footsteps to bring all the saints into the life of the blending of the entire Body of Christ. I hope that we all can see that reigning in life is not a matter of position but of practical living. All the matters mentioned in Romans 14 through 16 are related to our reigning in life in our practical living.
Reigning in life also includes following in the apostles' footsteps to bring all the saints into the life of the blending of the entire Body of Christ. Brother Lee continues:
Paul gives us an excellent pattern in Romans 16. In such a short chapter, he greeted the saints one by one, mentioning at least twenty-eight names. This shows us his knowledge and understanding of their situation and his genuine concern for each of them. Moreover, he recommended Phoebe, a deaconess of the church in Cenchrea, and generally greeted the saints. Such recommendations and greetings reveal both the concern among the saints and the fellowship among the churches. It is by the churches' fellowship in the Body that the God of peace will crush Satan under our feet and we will be able to enjoy the rich grace of Christ.
There has never been a situation on this earth where all the churches have been blended together. There is a longing and a desire to see every church raised up through our brother's ministry blended with all the other churches. We all need a commitment to the Lord that the local church we are in will be blended with all the churches in the Lord's recovery. Blending means to be adjusted, tempered, harmonized, and mingled. When the elders, the saints, the churches, or the co-workers come together, there should be a mutual blending among them, and it should be a kind of blending that adjusts, harmonizes, mingles, and tempers us until we all are the same. The churches in one region should not be different than the churches in another region. In country after country, we should all be the same by blending. By blending we are all adjusted and tempered. When something hot and something cold are put together to be tempered, they become the same. We all need to become tempered to the point that we are altogether mingled as one to make a beautiful harmony on the whole earth to the Lord.
Brother Lee's ministry raised up churches all over the earth. Even concerning the churches in Russia, he received the leading from the Lord, and what happened there was under his fellowship. It was his heart's cry that all these churches be tempered together. It is also our endeavoring and longing to make the churches in Russia exactly the same as every other church on the earth. The work of the co-workers and the elders is to blend the whole Body to have the living of the Body life on the earth, and the living of the Body life is mainly in the blending. It is in the blending that we live the Body life together. Some churches may be only a few miles from each other, but each one only takes care of itself. This is not the blending of the Body, and this is not what the Lord wants to raise up. The Lord wants all the churches to be blended together, harmonized, tempered, mingled, and standing as one before the Lord.
When Brother Nee began his ministry, he helped the recovery and other Christians with the matters of salvation, life, and the ground of the church. The ground of the church and the local churches became very dear to His recovery, but in 1938 Brother Nee went forward another step to present the matter of the Body of Christ. At that time, he was "playing beautiful music to cows." Almost no one could understand what he was saying. It is the same in principle in this country. In the beginning of the ministry in this country we received the truth of the ground of the church. However, when the matters concerning the Body of Christ and living the Body life were presented to us, we were like the cows. We did not see how we should advance from being the local church to living the Body life. Paul's burden in Romans 15 and 16 was to bring the saints from merely living in the church life to living in the Body life.
Because of the problem of time and space, we have to meet in a certain locality. On the one hand, we have to be faithful to the Lord, until we go to be with Him, to live in the local churches to build up the church in the locality where we live. On the other hand, we desire that the Lord would work in us until we see the whole Body and live in it. Wherever we are, we would be lost in the Body, practicing the Body life. This does not mean that we are any less for the local church life, but it does mean that we see that the local church in our place is not the goal. The New Jerusalem is the goal. The local church is just the procedure to bring us to the goal, first for us to live in the Body and then to consummate the New Jerusalem, which springs out from all the saints' living in the Body. If the Lord does not gain the reality of the Body, there is no possibility to consummate the New Jerusalem. Today there is one ministry on the earth, and there is one Body, and the Lord has brought us into the one ministry and into the Body. We need to live the Body life and be blended through our fellowship. We all—the churches, the co-workers, the elders, and the saints—need to learn how to fellowship together and practice the fellowship. Blending is in the fellowship. All misunderstandings and problems are solved through fellowship because in the fellowship there is the blending, and in the blending everything is taken care of. We must learn how to fellowship everything with the ones we coordinate with and with all the other co-workers. We should not try to solve problems on our own; eventually our problem solving becomes a bigger problem. The elders and the co-workers need to fellowship, the more the better, because the more we fellowship, the more we are blended together.
All the churches and all the saints in the Lord's recovery should be blended together, but this does not mean that we merely have contact with one another. The reality of blending is that we all go through the cross, by the Spirit, to dispense Christ to one another for the building up of the Body of Christ. The more we blend together, the more we have to experience the cross. We may never feel that our opinion is wrong, but if the brothers feel that it is off, we have to go through the cross in blending, and to go through the cross we need the Spirit. It is only by the Spirit that the cross operates in us. Then, if we go through the cross by the Spirit, we have the way to dispense Christ to one another. This will build up the Body of Christ.
In the full-time training we share the marvelous truths, and all the trainees receive a great help, but one of the greatest benefits of the training is that the saints from many places are blended together. If all the trainees are blended together in such a way, the Lord's recovery has a glorious future. The churches are-already all over the earth, and by the Lord's move in His recovery there will be more. In Russia alone there are seeking ones in many cities where there is not yet a church, and some of these seeking saints will build up a church life in their locality. The Lord's recovery has a glorious, bright future, but to carry it out, we need to be blended together in fellowship. We need to be blended more and more, as much as possible.
One church among us sends out twenty or thirty young people to another district every summer. This is a great benefit to them and also to the churches. Another church may send its young people to Russia this coming summer. May we all blend together, sending our young people and all the saints everywhere to blend together in the Body to build up the Body of Christ. We should not merely live in our local church. We should not stay in the procedure but go on to the goal. The Body of Christ is the way to reach the goal. Our brother dreamed about all the churches being blended together as one, mutually adjusted, tempered, harmonized, and mingled together. May the Lord blend us all together.
Ushering the Believers into the Divine and Mystical Realm of the Consummated Spirit and the Pneumatic Christ
The ministry of the apostles and the real work of building the New Jerusalem is to usher the believers into the divine and mystical realm of the consummated Spirit and the pneumatic Christ (Phil. 1:19; Rom. 8:9; 2 Cor. 3:17-18). We are not living only in a physical realm. In one sense, the arrangements in the churches are physical, and we have to deal with the physical things, but this is not the realm the Lord wants to bring us into. The Lord Jesus said, "In that day you will know that I am in My Father, and you in Me, and I in you" (John 14:20). This is the divine and mystical realm, the realm that the Lord has brought us all into. The divine and mystical realm is the consummated Spirit of the Triune God, the pneumatic Christ. We have been brought into the Triune God, and He has been brought into us, not physically but mystically. In the Triune God, the Father is in the Son, and the Son comes as the Spirit. The Lord Jesus lived a mystical life, died a mystical death, and was resurrected in a mystical resurrection to become the life-giving Spirit so that He could enter into us and we could be brought into this realm. The ministry of the apostles is to usher us into this divine and mystical realm. We have to live here and we have to be on guard that we stay here for all of our days. Christ cannot abide in us physically, but He became the mystical Spirit so that He can dwell in us and we can dwell in Him in a divine and mystical way.
Recognizing the Need of Overcomers and Laboring to Produce Them
The apostles recognize the need of overcomers and labor to produce them (2 Tim. 4:7-8) in order to: 1) overcome the degradation of the church (2 Tim. 2:19-22; Rev. 2—3); 2) build up the Body of Christ, which will consummate the New Jerusalem (Eph. 4:16); and 3) build up Zion, the reality of the Body of Christ, within Jerusalem, the local churches, so that ultimately the entire New Jerusalem may become Zion (Rev. 14:1).
Producing the overcomers is directly related to the building up of the New Jerusalem. Today we the believers are the real Jerusalem, but within this Jerusalem, just as within the physical Jerusalem, there is a high peak, which is Zion. The overcomers are the real Zion. The apostles' ministry, which is the ministry of the New Testament, is to produce overcomers so that in all the local churches Zion will be raised up. In principle, the vital groups are the Zion in the local churches. Today we need to be in a vital group life that we may reach the high peak of Zion. Those who have become Zion will be the New Jerusalem in eternity, and all the other believers will also be brought through a process to become the New Jerusalem. The entire New Jerusalem in eternity will be the ultimate Zion as the high peak in the universe.
The reality of the Body of Christ is that all the saints are living in the resurrection life of Christ. This is to live in the spirit, not in the natural or fleshly life. The reality of the Body of Christ is a group of people that have become God-men and have been united and constituted with God. They do not live by the old "I," the natural life, or the self. Rather they live by the pneumatic Christ. They are crucified with Christ and it is no longer they that live, but it is Christ that lives in them. They are the ones in whom Christ is building His home. This is a corporate living, an aggregate of those who are the very expression of God's organic salvation. It is a mingled living, a mutual living with Christ in us by the bountiful supply of the life-giving Spirit. Today the Lord wants to have the reality of the Body of Christ among us. This is the high peak, this is Zion, and this is what the Lord is gaining today. The Lord's recovery is for the building up of Zion.
A CONCLUDING, WORD
These messages reveal that the apostles live what they build. They follow the pattern of the Lord Jesus as the first God-man, whose work was His living and whose move was His being. His life was His move and His move was His work. With the Lord Jesus there was no difference between His life, His work, and His move. The apostles as the duplication of Christ are the same. Their living out the New Jerusalem is their working out, their building, the New Jerusalem. — B. P.
