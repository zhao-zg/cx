第一篇　接枝生命的异象
大纲|Outline|对照-听抄-目录
壹　圣经启示，神所要与人的关系，乃是祂与人成为一：
一　神要神圣的生命与人的生命结合一起，成为一个生命。
二　这个一乃是一个生机的联结，就是一个在生命里的联结；这就是接枝的生命─罗十一17～24。
三　神的生命与人的生命接联为一的观念是奥秘的，是超乎人天然观念的，也是人思想中所未有的。
贰　接枝乃是两个类似的生命被接联，而生长在一起：
一　两个接枝的生命必须是类似的，接枝才能发生效力。
二　因为我们人的生命是照着神的形像并按着神的样式造的，所以人的生命能接于神的生命。
三　我们人的生命与神的生命是相像的；所以神的生命与人的生命能够接在一起，并生机的生长在一起。
参　我们要接枝到基督里面，基督必须经过成肉体、钉十字架并复活的过程：
一　基督成了肉体，成为大卫的后裔，大卫的枝子，使我们与祂接在一起─约一14，太一1，亚三8，耶二三5，三三15：
1　我们人乃是枝子，是木枝。
2　基督来成为大卫的枝子，与我们一样一式，好使祂与我们能接在一起。
二　基督在十字架上被“切割”，使我们能接到祂里面：
1　基督成为大卫的枝子，这事的本身并不是说祂就能与我们接在一起。
2　接枝需要切割；两根枝子都必须切割，才能接在一起：
ａ　基督死在十字架上时，就被“切割”。
ｂ　我们在悔改接受主时，也被“切割”。
3　切割发生之后，就有了结合与生机的联结；因此，在接枝中，有切割、结合与生机的联结。
三　基督在十字架上被“切割”之后，就复活成为赐生命的灵─林前十五45下，林后三17上：
1　基督虽然在肉体里死了，在灵里祂却活着─彼前三18。
2　借着死与复活，祂就从“木枝”变化为灵。
3　借着成为灵，基督就预备好可以接枝。
4　我们一悔改接受主，祂这赐生命的灵就进入我们的灵里，将神的生命带到我们里面：
ａ　这生命是死而复活的生命。
ｂ　基督作为赐生命的灵，将死与复活的门窍带到我们这相信基督的人里面，使我们与祂同死同复活。
ｃ　我们在这死与复活里，与基督接在一起。
肆　我们这些重生的人，应当活接枝的生命─在这生命里双方联结，而生机的生长在一起：
一　我们接到基督里之后，就不该再凭自己活着，乃该让是灵的基督在我们里面活着─加二20。
二　我们不该再凭我们的肉体或天然的所是活着，而该凭我们重生的灵，就是与基督接联的灵活着。
三　我们借着这样的接枝，与基督联结、调和并合并，成为在祂里面那扩大、宇宙、神人二性的合并，就是基督的身体，终极完成新耶路撒冷。
伍　我们都必须看见接枝生命的异象，这是极其重要的。我们若看见这异象，我们的生活必会改变。

GENERAL SUBJECT
THE GRAFTED LIFE
Message One
The Vision of the Grafted Life
纲目|对照-听抄-目录
Scripture Reading: none
I.　The Bible reveals that the relationship which God desires to have with man is that He and man become one:
A.　God desires that the divine life and the human life be joined together to become one life.
B.　This oneness is an organic union, a union in life—a grafted life—Rom. 11:17-24.
C.　The concept of the divine life and the human life being grafted into one is mysterious, beyond the natural concept, and foreign to human thought.
II.　In grafting, two similar lives are joined and then grow together organically:
A.　Grafting can be effective only if the lives to be grafted are similar.
B.　Because our human life was made in the image of God and according to the likeness of God, it can be joined to the divine life.
C.　Our human life resembles the divine life; therefore, the divine life and the human life can be grafted together and then grow together organically.
III.　In order for us to be grafted into Christ, He had to pass through the processes of incarnation, crucifixion, and resurrection:
A.　Christ became flesh to be the seed of David, the branch of David, that we may be grafted together with Him—John 1:14; Matt. 1:1; Zech. 3:8; Jer. 23:5; 33:15:
1.　As human beings we are branches, pieces of wood.
2.　Christ came as the branch of David to be the same as we are so that He and we could be grafted together.
B.　Christ was "cut" on the cross so that we could be grafted into Him:
1.　Christ's becoming the branch of David does not by itself mean that He could be grafted together with us.
2.　Grafting requires cutting; two branches cannot be grafted together unless both are cut:
a.　Christ was "cut" when He died on the cross.
b.　We were "cut" when we repented and received the Lord.
3.　After the cutting occurs, the joining and the organic union take place; therefore, in grafting we have the cutting, the joining, and the organic union.
C.　After Christ was "cut" on the cross, He was resurrected to become the life-giving Spirit—1 Cor. 15:45b; 2 Cor. 3:17a:
1.　Although Christ died in the flesh, He was resurrected in the Spirit—1 Pet. 3:18.
2.　Through death and resurrection He was transformed from "wood" into the Spirit.
3.　By becoming such a Spirit, Christ was ready for grafting.
4.　Once we repent and receive the Lord, He as the life-giving Spirit enters into our spirit, bringing the divine life into us:
a.　This life is a life of death and resurrection.
b.　As the life-giving Spirit, Christ brings the key to death and resurrection into us, the believers, that we may die and be raised with Christ.
c.　In this death and resurrection we are grafted together with Christ.
IV.　As regenerated ones we should live a grafted life—a life in which two parties are joined to grow organically:
A.　After we have been grafted into Christ, we should no longer live by ourselves; rather, we should allow the pneumatic Christ to live in us—Gal. 2:20.
B.　We should no longer live by our flesh or by our natural being; instead, we should live by our regenerated spirit, a spirit grafted with Christ.
C.　Through this grafting we are united with Him, mingled with Him, and incorporated with Him to become in Him an enlarged, universal, divine and human incorporation—the Body of Christ, which consummates the New Jerusalem.
V.　It is crucial that we all see the vision of the grafted life. If we see this vision, our living will be changed.

第二篇　接枝生命的定义
大纲|Outline|对照-听抄-目录
壹　接枝的生命不是一个交换的生命，乃是人生命与神生命的调和：
一　有些基督教的教师认为，基督徒的生命是一个交换的生命：
1　根据这个观念，主乃是要我们放弃我们卑贱的生命，来换取祂卓越的生命。
2　根据这个观念，我们乃是把我们人的生命交给主，而祂用祂神圣的生命来顶替。
二　基督徒的生命不是一个交换的生命，乃是一个接枝的生命─人生命与神生命的调和：
1　两个生命并没有交换或互易。
2　不是交换，而是神的生命分赐、灌注到人的生命里面，以及神的生命与人的生命相调和。
三　基督徒经历中最奇妙的实际，乃是在基督里的信徒以生命的方式与基督联结：
1　凡基督所是并所作的，都是为着一件事，就是祂与我们能生机的联结，而过一种接枝的生活。
2　主在祂的恢复中，正在恢复接枝的生命这件被忽略的事。
贰　在接枝的生命里，人的生命并没有被除掉，乃是被神圣的生命所加强、提高、并加上丰富：
一　在接枝的生命里，枝子仍然保有其同一素质上的特征，但其生命因着被接到更好的生命里，就被提高并得着变化：
1　较高的生命征服较低的生命。
2　较高的生命也使较低的生命得着丰富、提高、并变化。
二　在接枝的生命里，神的生命在我们里面作工，排除消极的元素：
1　神的生命渐渐的作工，将凡是天然的东西一概除掉。
2　神的生命吞灭我们的缺点和软弱。
3　我们性情中消极的元素被杀死，然而主不是丢弃我们的性情，乃是提高并使用这性情。
三　在接枝的生命里，神的生命使祂原初的创造复活过来：
1　神不是放弃祂所创造的，乃是借着祂神圣生命的复活大能，重新得回并复兴祂所创造的。
2　神的心意是要将我们所是的每一方面都带进复活里：
ａ　当神的生命将消极的东西排除时，神的生命就作工，使神原初的创造复活过来。
ｂ　这样，我们原初的功用─在创造时赐给我们的功用─就得着恢复、加强、丰富。
四　在接枝的生命里，我们的功能被提高：
1　当神的生命使我们的功能复活过来时，神的生命就将这些功能提高到最高程度。
2　这在信徒里面就产生了高度发展的性格。
五　在接枝的生命里，神圣的生命将基督的丰富供应到我们里面的各部分：
1　我们复活过来并被提高的功能，得着基督的丰富的供应。
2　借着这样的供应，我们的心思、情感和意志就得着更新。
六　在接枝的生命里，神圣的生命浸透我们的全人：
1　基督的丰富浸透我们，而使我们有真正新陈代谢的改变─变化─林后三18。
2　我们借这神圣生命的浸透，被模成基督的形像─罗八29。
七　神的生命在我们里面能作这样的工作，但我们需要操练自己来接触主，并为着祂所给我们看见的有祷告。

GENERAL SUBJECT
THE GRAFTED LIFE
Message Two
The Definition of the Grafted Life
纲目|对照-听抄-目录
Scripture Reading: none
I.　The grafted life is not an exchanged life but the mingling of the human life with the divine life:
A.　Some Christian teachers regard the Christian life as an exchanged life:
1.　According to this concept the Lord asks us to give up our poor life in exchange for His superior life.
2.　According to this concept we yield our human life to the Lord and He replaces it with His divine life.
B.　The Christian life is not an exchanged life but a grafted life— the mingling of the human life with the divine life:
1.　There is no exchange, or trade, of lives.
2.　Instead of exchange, there is the dispensing, the infusing, of the divine life into the human life and the mingling of the divine life with the human life.
C.　The most wonderful reality in the Christian experience is that the believers in Christ are united with Christ in the way of life:
1.　Whatever Christ is and whatever He has done are altogether for one thing: that He and we could be organically united and live a grafted life.
2.　In His recovery the Lord is recovering this neglected matter of the grafted life.
II.　In the grafted life the human life is not eliminated but is strengthened, uplifted, and enriched by the divine life:
A.　In the grafted life the branch still retains its same essential characteristics, but its life is uplifted and transformed by being grafted into a better life:
1.　The higher life subdues the lower life.
2.　The higher life enriches, uplifts, and transforms the lower life.
B.　In the grafted life the divine life works within us to discharge the negative elements:
1.　The divine life works in a gradual way to eliminate whatever is natural.
2.　The divine life swallows up our defects and infirmities.
3.　The negative element in our disposition is killed, and then, instead of casting away our disposition, the Lord uplifts it and uses it.
C.　In the grafted life the divine life resurrects God's original creation:
1.　Instead of giving up His creation, God will reclaim it and restore it by the resurrection power of His divine life.
2.　God intends to bring all the aspects of our being into resurrection:
a.　As the divine life discharges the negative things, it works to resurrect God's original creation.
b.　In this way our original functions—the functions given us at creation—are restored, strengthened, and enriched.
D.　In the grafted life our faculties are uplifted:
1.　As the divine life resurrects our faculties, it uplifts them to the highest level.
2.　This produces in the believers a highly developed character.
E.　In the grafted life the divine life supplies the riches of Christ to our inward parts:
1.　Our resurrected and uplifted faculties are supplied with the riches of Christ.
2.　Through such a supply we are renewed in our mind, emotion, and will.
F.　In the grafted life the divine life saturates our whole being:
1.　The riches of Christ saturate us and cause a genuine metabolic change—transformation—2 Cor. 3:18.
2.　By this saturation of the divine life we are conformed to the image of Christ—Rom. 8:29.
G.　The divine life is able to do such a work within us, but we need to exercise ourselves to contact the Lord and pray concerning what He has shown us.

2.　According to this concept we yield our human life to the Lord and He replaces it with His divine life.
二　基督徒的生命不是一个交换的生命，乃是一个接枝的生命─人生命与神生命的调和：
B.　The Christian life is not an exchanged life but a grafted life— the mingling of the human life with the divine life:
1　两个生命并没有交换或互易。
1.　There is no exchange, or trade, of lives.
2　不是交换，而是神的生命分赐、灌注到人的生命里面，以及神的生命与人的生命相调和。
2.　Instead of exchange, there is the dispensing, the infusing, of the divine life into the human life and the mingling of the divine life with the human life.
三　基督徒经历中最奇妙的实际，乃是在基督里的信徒以生命的方式与基督联结：
C.　The most wonderful reality in the Christian experience is that the believers in Christ are united with Christ in the way of life:
1　凡基督所是并所作的，都是为着一件事，就是祂与我们能生机的联结，而过一种接枝的生活。
1.　Whatever Christ is and whatever He has done are altogether for one thing: that He and we could be organically united and live a grafted life.
2　主在祂的恢复中，正在恢复接枝的生命这件被忽略的事。
2.　In His recovery the Lord is recovering this neglected matter of the grafted life.
贰　在接枝的生命里，人的生命并没有被除掉，乃是被神圣的生命所加强、提高、并加上丰富：
II.　In the grafted life the human life is not eliminated but is strengthened, uplifted, and enriched by the divine life:
一　在接枝的生命里，枝子仍然保有其同一素质上的特征，但其生命因着被接到更好的生命里，就被提高并得着变化：
A.　In the grafted life the branch still retains its same essential characteristics, but its life is uplifted and transformed by being grafted into a better life:
1　较高的生命征服较低的生命。
1.　The higher life subdues the lower life.
2　较高的生命也使较低的生命得着丰富、提高、并变化。
2.　The higher life enriches, uplifts, and transforms the lower life.

1　神的生命渐渐的作工，将凡是天然的东西一概除掉。
1.　The divine life works in a gradual way to eliminate whatever is natural.
2　神的生命吞灭我们的缺点和软弱。
2.　The divine life swallows up our defects and infirmities.
3　我们性情中消极的元素被杀死，然而主不是丢弃我们的性情，乃是提高并使用这性情。
3.　The negative element in our disposition is killed, and then, instead of casting away our disposition, the Lord uplifts it and uses it.

2.　God intends to bring all the aspects of our being into resurrection:
ａ　当神的生命将消极的东西排除时，神的生命就作工，使神原初的创造复活过来。
a.　As the divine life discharges the negative things, it works to resurrect God's original creation.
ｂ　这样，我们原初的功用─在创造时赐给我们的功用─就得着恢复、加强、丰富。
b.　In this way our original functions—the functions given us at creation—are restored, strengthened, and enriched.
四　在接枝的生命里，我们的功能被提高：
D.　In the grafted life our faculties are uplifted:
1　当神的生命使我们的功能复活过来时，神的生命就将这些功能提高到最高程度。
1.　As the divine life resurrects our faculties, it uplifts them to the highest level.
2　这在信徒里面就产生了高度发展的性格。
2.　This produces in the believers a highly developed character.

1　我们复活过来并被提高的功能，得着基督的丰富的供应。
1.　Our resurrected and uplifted faculties are supplied with the riches of Christ.
2　借着这样的供应，我们的心思、情感和意志就得着更新。
2.　Through such a supply we are renewed in our mind, emotion, and will.
六　在接枝的生命里，神圣的生命浸透我们的全人：
F.　In the grafted life the divine life saturates our whole being:
1　基督的丰富浸透我们，而使我们有真正新陈代谢的改变─变化─林后三18。

G.　The divine life is able to do such a work within us, but we need to exercise ourselves to contact the Lord and pray concerning what He has shown us.

GENERAL SUBJECT
THE GRAFTED LIFE
Message Three
The Principle of Incarnation Seen in the Grafted Life
纲目|对照-听抄-目录
Scripture Reading: none
I.　The principle of incarnation seen in the grafted life is that God enters into man and mingles Himself with man to make Himself one with man; thus, God is in man, and man is also in God:
A.　The incarnated Christ and the Father had a coinhering oneness with one life and one living—John 6:57a; 14:10.
B.　This coinhering oneness has been duplicated by Christ with His believers—John 15:4-5; 17:21, 23.
C.　Paul's speaking in 1 Corinthians 7 reveals the highest spirituality based on the principle of incarnation:
1.　The Old Testament principle of speaking for God (prophesying) is "Thus says the Lord"—Isa. 10:24; 50:1; Jer. 2:2; Ezek. 2:4.
2.　The New Testament principle of incarnation is that the speaker and the Lord are one:
a.　"I charge, not 1 but the Lord"—1 Cor. 7:10.
b.　"I say, I, not the Lord"—v. 12.
c.　"I have no commandment of the Lord, but I give my opinion"—v. 25.
d.　"...according to my opinion; but I think that I also have the Spirit of God"—v. 40.
e.　All the above words indicate the New Testament principle of incarnation (that is, that God and man, man and God, become one).
f.　In 1 Corinthians 7, the person who was Saul of Tarsus with the strong disposition had been dealt with, but the regenerated Paul remained as a sifted, uplifted, and purified person who was joined to the Lord as one spirit—1 Cor. 6:17.
g.　God mingled with man as one person with two natures, living together in one life and one living, is the experience of the grafted life in the principle of incarnation;
D.　In keeping with the principle of incarnation, Paul was one with Christ in His inward parts and lived a life in the inward parts of Christ—Phil. 1:8.
II.　The Christian life is a life in the principle of incarnation:
A.　This is a life that we, the God-chosen, redeemed, regenerated, and saved people, live together with the processed and dispensing Triune God, a life lived by two persons mingled together and living together as one.
B.　The Lord's recovery is to recover such a life.
C.　The experience of the grafted life in the principle of incarnation is seen in Galatians 2:20:
1.　On the one hand, Paul says, "It is no longer I who live"; on the other hand, he says, "Christ...lives in me" and "I live":
a.　In Galatians 2:20 we have both the old "I," which was without divinity and the new "I," which has God as life added to it.
b.　The "I" who has been terminated is the "I" who was without divinity (Rom. 6:6); the "I" who still lives is the "I" into which God has been added.
c.　The new "I" came into being when the old "I" was resurrected and God was added to it.
2.　It is only by being grafted into Christ that we can be one with Him in His death and resurrection:
a.　By the all-inclusive cutting of Christ's all-inclusive death, we are dead to everything other than God.
b.　On the positive side, we have been joined to Christ in His resurrection.
c.　Because we have been grafted into Christ, His experience has become our history.
3.　To live in the faith of the Son of God means to live in the organic union with the Son of God which comes through our believing in Him.
4.　The divine life, the spiritual life in our spirit, is lived by the exercise of faith, which is stimulated by the presence of the life-giving Spirit—2 Cor. 4:13:
a.　Faith is our appreciation of Christ as a reaction to His attraction—Heb. 12:2a; 11:6.
b.　When Paul referred to the Son of God as the One who "loved me and gave Himself up for me," he was filled with appreciation for the Lord Jesus.
c.　We need to turn to the Lord and receive more infusion from Him; as a result, we will have a greater love and appreciation for Him to strengthen our faith and our organic union with Christ.
d.　Whenever we say from the depths of our heart, "Lord Jesus, I love You," our faith and our organic union with Christ are strengthened.
e.　This carries out God's New Testament economy for the processed Triune God to be wrought into us to become our life and our very being.

THE GRAFTED LIFE

纲目|对照-听抄-目录

I.　In His desire to be one with man, God created man in His image and according to His likeness, and with a spirit to contact Him, receive Him, and contain Him:
A.　God created man in His image and according to His likeness; therefore, man was created not according to his own kind but according to God's kind—Gen. 1:24-27:
1.　The image of God is the expression of God in all that He is in His attributes and virtues—Col. 1:15; 2 Cor. 4:4.
2.　Whereas God's image is His inward being, His likeness is His outward form.
3.　There was no "mankind" created by God in His creation; rather, what God created was after His own kind, that is, God-kind.
B.　God created man with the breath of life for a spirit that man may contact Him and receive Him—Gen. 2:7:
1.　The breath of God breathed into man's body became man's spirit—Prov. 20:27.
2.　The breath of life is not the life of God or the Spirit of God but is very close to God's life and God's Spirit.
3.　The human life with the human spirit is very similar to God's life and God's Spirit because two,lives must be very similar for them to be grafted into one.
4.　Because our spirit was created with the breath of life, our spirit can become one spirit with the Lord—1 Cor. 6:17.
5.　Because the spirit pf man is the organ that can take in God to enjoy God's life and God's Spirit and be one with God's Spirit, it is so crucial that it ranks in importance with the heavens and the earth—Zech. 12:1.
II.　God placed man in front of the tree of life, indicating His desire to be one with man, that is, to be man's life, life supply, and everything:
A.　The tree of life signifies the crucified (implied in the tree as a piece of wood—1 Pet. 2:24) and resurrected (implied in the life of God—John 11:25) Christ as the embodiment of all the riches of God for our food.
B.　Eating the tree of life, that is, enjoying Christ as our life supply, should be the primary matter in the church life— Rev. 2:7:
1.　To receive Christ by eating Him is to have Him assimilated into our being organically and metabolically for Him to mingle Himself with us—John 6:57, 63:
2.　To enjoy Christ as the tree of life requires us to love Him with the first love—Rev. 2:4, 7.
3.　To eat Christ is to eat His words, to receive His words, which are the embodiment of the Spirit of life, by exercising our spirit—Jer. 15:16; Eph. 6:17-18; 1 Pet. 2:2; Heb. 5:13-14.
C.　We not only eat of the tree of life but also abide in this tree as the branches:
1.　In John 15 Christ is the vine tree, and in 14:6 He is the life; thus, He is the tree of life.
2.　We are the branches absorbing all that the tree is and has and we also abide in this tree, have our existence in this tree, by remaining in the fellowship of the divine life— John 15:5; 1 John 1:2-3.
D.　The tree of life was unique in Genesis 2:9, but today the tree of life grows in all of us, causing each of us to be a small tree of life.
1.　God has sown Himself into our spirit as the life-seed to grow a tree, a miniature tree of life—1 John 3:9; Mark 4:26.
2.　God's purpose is fulfilled by the growth of the tree of life, the growth of God, within us—Col. 2:19; Eph. 4:15-16.

二　基督，那住在我们里面荣耀的盼望，是救恩的元帅，凭祂在性情上的圣别，借着苦难，要领许多的儿子进荣耀里去─来二10～17，西一27，林后四17，罗八18，约十二23～26。
三　印涂之灵的印涂，在受印涂之信徒的一生中，用神荣耀的生命元素，从里面浸透他们，结果使祂们的身体得赎，改变形状─弗四30，腓三21。

七　这样的荣化，也就是我们身体的得赎，乃是我们对儿子名分完满的享受─罗八23。

The Grafted Life and God's Complete Salvation (5) God's Organic Salvation (3)

Scripture Reading: none
V.　Conformation:
A.　God has predestinated us to be conformed to the image of His Son, that He might be the Firstborn among many brothers—Rom. 8:29; 1:4; Eph. 1:5.
B.　As the divine life grows within us and transforms us, it spontaneously shapes us into the form, the image, of the firstborn Son of God—Rom. 8:2; Heb. 8:10.
C.　To be conformed to the image of the firstborn Son of God is to be saved in Christ's life from self-likeness, that is, from the expression, the appearance, of the self—Rom. 5:10b; Matt. 16:23-24.
D.　If we would be conformed to the image of the firstborn Son of God, we need to be conformed to Christ's death in all things through the power of His resurrection—Phil. 3:10; S.S. 2:8-14.
E.　The Spirit intercedes for us and all things work together for good to us that we may be conformed to the image of God's Son—-Rom. 8:26-29.
VI.　Glorification:
A.　Glorification is the step in God's complete salvation in which God will completely saturate our body with the glory of His life and nature—Rom. 8:30; 2 Thes. 1:10; Rev. 21:10-11; 4:3.
B.　Christ, our indwelling hope of glory, is the Captain of salvation leading many sons into glory through sufferings by His dispositional sanctification—Heb. 2:10-11; Col. 1:27; 2 Cor. 4:17; Rom. 8:18; John 12:23-26.
C.　The sealing of the sealing spirit saturates the sealed believers from within with the glorious life element of God throughout their life, resulting m the redemption and transfiguration of their body-—Eph. 4:30; Phil. 3:21.
D.　When our body is transfigured, the death in our mortal body will be swallowed up by the resurrection life—2 Cor. 5:4; 1 Cor. 15:54.
E.　We need to be one in the divine glory for the corporate expression of God—John 17:22; Eph. 3:21.
F.　We need to behold the glory of the Lord to be transformed from glory to glory until we are like Him at His manifestation for our glorification, when we see Him as He is—2 Cor. 3:18; 1 John 3:2.
G.　Such a glorification, the redemption of our body, is the full enjoyment of our sonship—Rom. 8:23.

GENERAL SUBJECT
THE GRAFTED LIFE
Message Ten
Knowing and Experiencing the Divine Life in the Grafted Life (1) The Sense of Life and the Law of Life
纲目|对照-听抄-目录
I.　The sense of life:
A.　The sense of life is the feeling, the consciousness, of the divine life within us—Rom. 8:6; Eph. 4:18-19.
B.　The source of the sense of life is the divine life, the law of life, the Holy Spirit, Christ abiding in us, and God operating in us—Eph. 4:18; Rom. 8:2; Heb. 8:10; Rom. 8:11; 1 John 2:27; John 15:4-5; Phil. 2:13.
C.　The sense of life involves both the feeling of death and the feeling of life and peace—Rom. 8:6; Isa. 26:3.
D.　The sense of life makes us know whether we are living in the natural life or in the divine life and whether we are living in the flesh or in the spirit—Rom. 8:6.
E.　In living a grafted life, we should live according to the principle of life, not according to the principle of right and wrong—Gen. 2:9b, 16-17; 2 Cor. 11:2-3.
F.　If we would follow the sense of life, we need to live a calm, steady, and unhurried way, living an ordinary life in the divine dispensing—John 14:27; 16:33; 1 Thes. 5:23; 2 Thes. 3:16; Isa. 30:15; 32:17.
G.　The sense of life guides us, governs us, controls us, and directs us—Luke 1:79; Rom. 3:17; Prov. 21:1.
H.　"Today there is the need for all the believers who have received the abundance of grace and of the gift of righteousness [Rom. 5:17] to practice the life restriction and limitation in the divine life" (Witness Lee, The Holy Word for Morning Revival: Crystallization-study of the Complete Salvation of God in Romans, p. 53)
II.　The law of life:
A.　The law of life, the law of the Spirit of life, is the processed Triune God as the life-giving Spirit dwelling in our spirit—Rom. 8:2-3, 11, 34.
B.　The law of the Spirit of life is the spontaneous power of the Spirit of life; it is the natural characteristic and the innate automatic function of the Spirit of life—Rom. 8:2.
C.　The function of the law of life is to make us God in life, nature, and expression (but not in the Godhead), shaping us to the image of the firstborn Son of God, and to constitute us the members of the Body of Christ with all kinds of functions—Rom. 8:2, 29; Eph. 4:16.
D.　We enjoy the dispensing of life into our being for the accomplishment of God's economy by the working of the law of the Spirit of life—Jer. 31:33; Heb. 8:10; Rom. 8:10, 6, 11.
E.　We need to cooperate with the installed and operating law of the Spirit of life by "switching on" this law:
1.　We need to exercise our spirit to contact the life of God—Rom. 8:4; Eph. 6:18; Isa. 12:3-6.
2.　We need to deal with our heart to allow the life of God to freely work in us—Luke 8:15.

纲目|对照-听抄-目录
I.　The fellowship of life:
A.　The fellowship of life is the flow of the eternal life within all the believers, who have received and possess the divine life—1 John 1:2-4; Acts 2:42; 1?Cor. 1:9; Rev. 22:1:
1.　The vertical aspect of fellowship refers to our fellowship with the Triune God; the horizontal aspect of fellowship refers to our fellowship with one another—1 John 1:2-3, 6-7; 1 Cor. 12:24.
2.　The means of fellowship of life is the Holy Spirit; the location of the fellowship of life is our spirit—2 Cor. 13:14; Phil. 2:1; 2 Cor. 6:6.
3.　The function of the fellowship of life is to supply us with all the riches of the divine life—Rev. 22:1; John 15:4-5.
B.　To have fellowship with the Triune God in the apostles' fellowship is to put aside our private interests and join with the apostles and the Triune God for the carrying out of God's purpose.
II.　The light of life:
A.　Life and light always go together; where light is, there is life, and where life is, there is light—Gen. 1:3, 14-18; Psa. 36:8-9; John 1:4; 1 John 1:1-2, 5, 7.
B.　In living a grafted life, we need to walk in the light, that is, live, move, act, behave, and have our being in the light—1 John 1:7; Eph. 5:8; Rev. 21:23.
C.　Within us we have two lamps, the Spirit of God and our human spirit; it is because of these two lamps that there is so much shining within us—Prov. 20:27; Rev. 4:5; 1:4; Luke 11:33-36.
D.　The kingdom is the shining of the Lord Jesus, and the kingdom is the spreading of the Lord Jesus by shining over us to deliver us from the authority of darkness—Matt. 16:28—17:3; Col. 1:12-13.
E.　When we walk in the light, the divine things are real to us; however, when we are in darkness, the divine things become vain and empty to us—1 John 1:5-7.
F.　When the Lord Jesus as the great I Am comes into us as life, He shines within us as light, which brings the divine element as reality into us—John 8:12, 32.

GENERAL SUBJECT
THE GRAFTED LIFE
Message Twelve
Knowing and Experiencing the Divine Life in the Grafted Life (3) The Growth in Life and Reigning in Life
纲目|对照-听抄-目录
I.　The growth in life:
A.　"Let us be brought on to maturity"—Heb. 6:1; Phil. 3:12-15; 1 Tim. 4:15.
B.　The growth in life is the increase of the Triune God as life within us, and this growth in life is the building up of the Body of Christ—Col. 2:19; Eph. 4:15-16.
C.　The way to grow in life for the organic building up of the Body of Christ—Eph. 4:15:
1.　Exercising our spirit—1 Tim. 4:7; Jude 19.
2.　Eating, drinking, and breathing Christ—1 Tim. 4:6; John 6:57; 7:38; 20:22.
3.　Allowing the Lord to measure us—Ezek. 47:1-5.
4.　Receiving the supply of the Body—Eph. 4:16.
5.　Ministering Christ as life to others—1 Tim. 5:1-2; Phil. 1:25.
II.　Reigning in life:
A.　We were regenerated with a divine, spiritual, heavenly, kingly, and royal life; therefore, we are now able to reign in life as kings—John 1:12-13; 3:3, 5; Rev. 5:10; Rom. 5:17, 21.
B.　The highest attainment of pursuing Christ is to reign with Christ in His divine life through His abounding grace—Phil. 3:13-14; Rom. 5:17b, 21b.
C.　God's complete salvation is for us to reign in life by the abundance of grace and of the gift of righteousness—Rom. 5:17, 21:
1.　The gift of righteousness is God's judicial redemption applied to us in a practical way.
2.　Grace is God Himself as our all-sufficient supply for our organic salvation.
D.　In practice, to reign in life is to be under the ruling of the divine life:
1.　All the believers who have received the abundance of grace and of the gift of righteousness need to practice the restriction and limitation in the divine life—Rom. 8:6, 2; Rev. 22:1; John 8:12; Prov. 4:18.
2.　As those who love the Lord Jesus, we have come under His pleasant rule, where we are restricted in the sweetness of love—Col. 1:12-13.
E.　If we want to reign in life, our spirit must be strong, living, active, positive, and diligent—2 Tim. 1:7; Rev. 1:10a.

GENERAL SUBJECT
THE GRAFTED LIFE
Message Thirteen
Bearing Fruit and Ministering Life in the Grafted Life
纲目|对照-听抄-目录
I.　Bearing fruit:
A.　The vine tree, the tree of life, with its branches is the organism of the Triune God in the divine dispensing to express the riches of the divine life—John 15:1, 5.
B.　Being branches of the divine vine and bearing fruit as the overflow of the riches of the inner life are matters of joy, and they issue in a joyful life.
C.　We must abide in the Lord, in His word and in His love, so that His instant words may abide in us—John 8:31; 15:7, 9-10; 1 John 1:2-3.
D.　As branches, when we are pruned, we are washed and cleansed through our confession according to God's enlightening to make us a clean, new, and fresh branch to bear fruit—John 15:2.
E.　The branches must love one another to express the divine life in fruit-bearing—John 15:12, 17.
F.　The God-ordained way to bear remaining fruit for the Father's glorification is for us to "go forth," to visit people:
1.　The Old Testament reveals God's pattern of visiting man—Gen. 3:8-9, 15, 21; Exo. 3:8.
2.　The New Testament reveals the example of the Lord Jesus in visiting man—1 Tim. 1:15; Luke 19:1-10; John 4:3-15; Luke 10:38-39; Matt. 26:6.
3.　The Lord sent His disciples to visit man—Matt. 10:5-8, 11-13; Luke 10:1-6; Mark 16:15; Matt. 28:19.
4.　The Lord has set us that we should go forth and bear fruit and that our fruit should remain—John 15:16; Rom. 15:16; 12:1; Col. 1:28.
II.　Ministering life:
A.　If we would minister life to others, we need to have genuine concern for them:
1.　The believers at Corinth were in Paul's heart both to live together and to die together—2 Cor. 7:2-3.
2.　Paul was one with Christ in his inward parts and lived a life in the inward parts of Christ—Phil. 1:8.
3.　Timothy was like-souled with Paul in genuinely caring for what concerned the saints—Phil. 2:19-21.
B.　If we would minister life to others, our word should convey grace to them:
1.　The word that proceeds out of our mouth should be good for building up and give grace to those who hear—Eph. 4:29.
2.　Our speech should be always with grace, seasoned with salt, that we may know how we ought to answer each other—Col. 4:6.
C.　If we would minister life to others, we need to lay down our lives on their behalf:
1.　Paul considered his life of no account as if precious to himself, and he risked his life for the Lord—Acts 20:24a; 15:26.
2.　Epaphroditus risked his life for the work of Christ, and Aquila and Prisca risked their necks for Paul—Phil. 2:30; Rom. 16:3-4.
3.　The Lord Jesus laid down His life on our behalf, and we ought to lay down our lives on behalf of the brothers—1 John 3:16.

一　新耶路撒冷是神终极的生机体─三一神与蒙祂救赎、重生、变化并荣化之人的生机大联结─启二一2，10，林前六17。
A.　The New Jerusalem is God's ultimate organism—a great organic union of the Triune God and His redeemed, regenerated, transformed, and glorified people—Rev. 21:2, 10; 1 Cor. 6:17.
二　在新耶路撒冷里，神要住在我们这帐幕里，我们要住在神这殿里─启二一9，22，三12：
B.　In the New Jerusalem God will dwell in us as the tabernacle, and we will dwell in God as the temple—Rev. 21:3, 22; 3:12:
1　神与我们要有相互的住处；神要住在我们里面，我们也要住在神里面─约十四23，十五4上。
1.　God and we will have a mutual abode; God will abide in us and we will abide in Him—John 14:23; 15:4a.
2　神与我们要互相内在，而神与我们的这个互相内在，乃是生机的联结─约壹四15，约十五5上。
2.　God and we will coinhere, and this coinhering of God and us is an organic union—1 John 4:15; John 15:5a.
三　新耶路撒冷是一个生机的组合，是神、人二性相调并调和在一起，成为一个实体─弗四4～6，林前六17：
C.　The New Jerusalem is an organic composition of divinity and humanity blended and mingled together as one entity—Eph. 4:4-6; 1 Cor. 6:17:
1　所有的组成分子都有同一个生命、性情和构成，因此是一个团体的人─约三15，彼后一4，西三10～11。
1.　All the components have the same life, nature, and constitution and thus are a corporate person—John 3:15; 2 Pet. 1:4; Col. 3:10-11.
2　这就是神成为人，人在生命和性情上成为神，但无分于神格─约一14，12～13，罗八3，一3～4。
2.　This is a matter of God becoming man and man becoming God in life and in nature but not in the Godhead—John 1:14, 12-13; Rom. 8:3; 1:3-4.
3　至终，三一神与蒙救赎的人，要调和、相调、并建造成为一个实体─弗三14～19：
3.　Eventually, the Triune God and redeemed humanity will be mingled, blended, and built up to be one entity—Eph. 3:14-19:
ａ　这建造乃是神圣元素加到人性元素里，人性元素加到神圣元素里的一个构成。
a.　This building is a constitution of the divine element into the human element and of the human element into the divine element.
ｂ　神在基督里在我们里面，要将祂的神性建造到我们人性里，并将我们的人性建造到祂的神性里，为要将祂的神性与我们的人性调成一个实体。
b.　God in Christ is within us to build His divinity into our humanity and to build our humanity into His divinity in order to blend His divinity with our humanity into one entity.
ｃ　这个实体就是新耶路撒冷─神建造的终极完成。
c.　This entity is the New Jerusalem—the ultimate consummation of God's building.

标　语
金灯台表征基督是三一神的具体化身，实化为那灵，作神圣的光，在复活的生命里照耀，为着召会的建造；
The golden lampstand signifies Christ, who as the embodiment of the Triune God realized as the Spirit is the divine light shining in the resurrection life for the building up of the church;
金灯台的七盏灯表征是灵的基督成了七倍加强的灵，在召会中照明并鉴察，为着三一神建造的恢复，并行动的加强。
The seven lamps of the golden lampstand signify the pneumatic Christ as the sevenfold intensified Spirit illuminating and searching in the church for the recovery of the building and the intensification of the move of the Triune God.
金灯台也表征召会是基督的复制并那灵的翻版，就是以三一神为源头、元素和素质，所建造之基督的身体；
The golden lampstand also signifies the church, which is the reproduction of Christ and the reprint of the Spirit as the Body of Christ built with the Triune God as its source, element, and essence;
金灯台是神建造之终极完成的新耶路撒冷，作神与人联调的透光体，永世里无限彰显三一神的荣耀。
The golden lampstand is the New Jerusalem, the consummation of God's building, as the diffuser of the union and mingling of God and man for the infinite expression of the glory of the Triune God in eternity.
