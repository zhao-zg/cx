第一篇　身体的异象
大纲|Outline|对照-听抄-目录
读经：弗三3～4，徒二十六19，九1～19，林前十二2，西三10～11。
壹　“照着启示使我知道这奥秘，正如我前面略略写过的，你们念了，就能借此明了我对基督的奥秘所有的领悟”─弗三3～4：
一　惟有从神得着启示的人，能看见基督的身体，也惟有他们能进入身体的实际里。
二　我们需要启示，好认识基督身体的实际，并引我们进入基督身体的范围里，惟有这样，基督的身体才成为我们的经历─弗一17，三5，太十一25，箴二九18上。
三　基督身体的异象要破碎我们；我们需要在身体的异象下被破碎，使我们能过一种照着神的心和意愿而有的生活─弗一9～11，三9～11。
贰　“我…没有违背那从天上来的异象”─徒二六19，九1～19，二二6～16：
一　大数的扫罗在往大马色的路上，天上的异象临到他，这异象使他有了极大的改变─徒九1～5：
１　他看见异象之后，眼睛就瞎了，不能看见什么，并且软弱无能，不能作什么─8节。
２　凡遇见从天上来的异象的人，就有一种蒙福的眼瞎临到他们：
a　这种眼瞎临到我们之后，就有一种内里的膏油涂抹，和内里的照耀，就是内里的光照。
b　这个内里的异象会越过越加增，并彻底改变我们事奉主的方式。
３　扫罗三天之久，不能看见，也不吃不渴；他所能作的就是祷告─徒九9，1：
a　在素质的灵的感动之下，他惟一的兴趣就是祷告，为要明白他所看见所听见之事的意义─徒二二14～15。
b　当扫罗祷告的时候，可能一个异象又一个异象，一个启示又一个启示的临到他，给他看见基督是神的具体化身，就是神的奥秘，以及召会是基督的身体，就是基督的奥秘─西二2，弗三4，五32。
c　行传九章所记载保罗异象的每一重要的点，都不该对我们只是教训而已；反之，每一点都该是我们在属天电视上所看见的异象。
二　我们读行传九章时，必须看见关于“我”(4)、“耶稣”(5)和“所拣选的器皿”(15)这三项的天上异象：─引用经文
１　“扫罗，扫罗，你为什么逼迫我？”─徒九4：
a　这是团体的“我”，包括主耶稣和他所有的信徒。
b　保罗开始看见，主耶稣和他的信徒是一个伟大的人─奇妙的“我”─林前十二12，西三10～11。
c　这必定使他印象深刻，影响他将来关于基督与召会是神极大奥秘的职事，（弗五32），并为他独特的职事立下了稳固的根基。─引用经文
d　保罗是新约里惟一使用“基督的身体”这词的作者─罗十二4～5，林前十二12～27，弗一22～23，二16，四4，12，16。
e　他非常强调基督的身体，因为在他悔改的时候，听到一篇论到团体的我的信息，就是一篇论到基督的身体的信息。
f　扫罗得救以后，主立刻开始以基督身体的事教育他─徒九6，17～18，24～25，二二14～16。
２　“他说，主阿，你是谁？主说，我就是你所逼迫的耶稣。”─徒九5：
a　“耶稣”这名具体包含了完备的福音信息。
b　扫罗看见耶稣就是耶和华救主，祂现今是在诸天之上的一位，曾经过成为肉体、人性生活、死、复活和升天的种种过程，为着产生并建造基督的身体。
c　保罗看见耶稣就是神，祂经过了种种过程，并终极完成为升天的主，身体的头，和赐生命的灵，分赐到祂所有的肢体里─罗十12～13，西一18上，林前十五45下。
３　“这人是我所拣选的器皿”─徒九15：
a　神拯救大数扫罗的心意，是要以祂自己将他充满，因而使他成为特出的器皿─西一25，弗三8～9。
b　我们在保罗的著作里，看见“器皿”一词属灵意义的发展：
㈠　保罗看见人是一个器皿，为要盛装基督作生命并被祂充满，好建造基督的身体─罗九21，23，林后四7，提后二20～21。
㈡　基督的身体是神极大的团体器皿，为要盛装祂并被祂充满，好作祂的彰显─弗三16～19。

GENERAL SUBJECT
KEEPING THE PRINCIPLES OF THE BODY
Message One
The Vision of the Body
纲目|对照-听抄-目录
Scripture Reading: Eph. 3:3-4; Acts 26:19; 9:1-19; 1 Cor. 12:12; Col. 3:10-11
I.　“By revelation the mystery was made known to me, as I have written previously in brief, by which, in reading it, you can perceive my understanding in the mystery of Christ”—Eph. 3:3-4:
A.　Only those who have received revelation from God will see the Body of Christ, and only they will enter into the reality of the one Body.
B.　We need revelation to know the reality of the Body of Christ, and to usher us into the realm of the Body, and only then will the Body of Christ become our experience—Eph. 1:17; 3:5; Matt. 11:25; Prov. 29:18a.
C.　The vision of the Body will break us; we need to be broken under the vision of the Body so that we may live a life according to God's heart and will—Eph. 1:9-11; 3:9-11.
II.　“I was not disobedient to the heavenly vision”—Acts 26:19; 9:1-19; 22:6-16:
A.　While Saul of Tarsus was on the way to Damascus, a heavenly vision came to him, and this vision revolutionized him—Acts 9:1-5:
1.　After he saw the vision, he became blind, unable to see anything, and impotent, unable to do anything—v. 8.
2.　A blessed blindness comes upon those who are met by the heavenly vision:
a.　After this blindness comes upon us, there will be the inner anointing, and the inner shining, the inner enlightening.
b.　The inner vision will increase more and more and will revolutionize the way we serve the Lord.
3.　For three days Saul did not see anything, and he did not eat or drink anything; all he could do was pray—Acts 9:9, 11:
a.　Under the inspiration of the essential Spirit, his only interest was to pray in order to know the significance of what he had seen and heard—Acts 22:14-15.
b.　It is likely that as Saul was praying, vision after vision and revelation after revelation came to him concerning Christ as the embodiment of God, the mystery of God, and the church as the Body of Christ, the mystery of Christ—Col. 2:2; Eph. 3:4; 5:32.
c.　Each crucial point of Paul's vision recorded in Acts 9 should not merely be a teaching to us; rather, every point should be a vision that we see on the heavenly “television.”
B.　In our reading of Acts 9 we need to see the heavenly vision concerning three items—“Me” (v. 4), “Jesus” (v. 5), and the “chosen vessel” (v. 15):
1.　“Saul, Saul, why are you persecuting Me?” (v. 4):
a.　This is a corporate “Me” comprising Jesus the Lord and all His believers.
b.　Saul began to see that the Lord Jesus and his believers are one great person—the wonderful “Me”—1 Cor. 12:12; Col. 3:10-11.
c.　This must have impressed and affected him for his future ministry concerning Christ and the church as the great mystery of God (Eph. 5:32) and laid a solid foundation for his unique ministry.
d.　Paul is the only writer of the New Testament to use the term the Body of Christ (Rom. 12:4-5; 1 Cor. 12:12-27; Eph. 1:22-23; 2:16; 4:4, 12, 16.
e.　He placed great emphasis on the Body because at the time of his conversion he heard a message concerning the corporate Me, a message concerning the Body of Christ.
f.　Immediately after Saul was saved, the Lord began to educate him concerning the Body of Christ—Acts 9:6, 17-18, 24-25; 22:14-16.
2.　“And he said, Who are You, Lord? And He said, I am Jesus, whom you persecute”—9:5:
a.　The same Jesus embodies the full message of the gospel.
b.　Saul saw that Jesus is Jehovah the Savior and that as the One who is now in the heavens, He has passed through the process of incarnation, human living, death, resurrection, and ascension for the producing and building up of the Body of Christ.
c.　Paul saw that Jesus is the very God who has been processed and consummated to be the ascended Lord, the Head of the Body and the life-giving Spirit to be dispensed into all His members—Rom. 10:12-13; Col. 1:18a; 1 Cor. 15:45b.
3.　“This man is a chosen vessel to Me”—Acts 9:15:
a.　God's intention in saving Saul of Tarsus was to fill him with Himself and thereby make him an outstanding vessel—Col. 1:25; Eph. 3:8-9.
b.　In Paul's writings we see the development of the spiritual significance of the word vessel:
(1)　Paul saw that man is a vessel to contain and be filled with Christ as life for the building up of the Body of Christ—Rom. 9:21, 23; 2 Cor. 4:7; 2 Tim. 2:20-21.
(2)　The Body of Christ is God's great corporate vessel to contain Him and be filled with Him for His expression—Eph. 3:16-19.

MESSAGE ONE
THE VISION OF THE BODY

（本文为英文听抄扫描所得，未经校对）
The general subject of this conference is "Keeping the Principles of the Body." Brother Lee first shared this very precious subject with a group of brothers on December 15, 1995. In that time of fellowship he asked the brothers to render to the work of the one ministry their highest cooperation. The meaning of the phrase to render the highest cooperation is that all of us have to be overcomers to keep every principle of the Body. This is a precious statement.
The Body of Christ is God's goal and also the target of God's enemy. God loves the Body of Christ. This is His very heart and will in the universe. But the enemy hates the Body of Christ. So we need to pray much, that is, to fight the battle, that we can enter into the reality of the Body and be the overcomers who keep the principles of the Body to become Christ's bride to bring Him back. There was a period of time in Brother Nee's ministry, from 1939 to 1942, when he ministered very much concerning the Body of Christ. Brother Lee said that what Brother Nee ministered during those years was the most strategic work of his entire life. He also said that such messages on the Body of Christ had not been released since the time of the early apostles; these messages were very solid, very deep, very profound, very rich, and very high. These messages were published in the book entitled The Mystery of Christ.
When Brother Nee ministered on this topic, he was at the peak of his ministry, even at the high peak of the divine revelation, and the enemy hated this. As a result, in 1942 there was a big turmoil in the church in Shanghai, and Brother Nee's ministry stopped for six years. However, in 1948, through Brother Lee's return to Shanghai and through his ministry in Shanghai, Brother Nee's ministry was recovered. In 1948 Brother Nee ministered again on the Body of Christ. This brought in a great revival to the churches in mainland China. But because the enemy hates this matter, he again rose up. In 1949 the Communists gained mainland China, and Brother Nee was imprisoned in 1952 until he died in martyrdom twenty years later in 1972. The Body of Christ is a matter of spiritual warfare. The enemy hates the Body of Christ because he knows that if we enter into the reality of the Body, we will bring Christ back! We will bring in His kingdom.
We need to be impressed with how crucial the Body of Christ is. In the last three years of Brother Lee's ministry, beginning in 1994, he reached the highest peak of the divine revelation. On May 9, 1994, Brother Lee wrote the following personal note:
I asked of the Lord after my illness what my ministry should stress. It seems that the Lord would burden me with:
(1) The Body of Christ and the life of the Body. (2) The reality of the life of the Body of Christ. May the Lord have mercy upon me that He would grace me to live the two stresses listed above and minister them to the Lord's recovery accordingly.
We need to pray that the Lord would burden us also with these items. We need to pray, "Lord, burden me with the Body of Christ and the life of the Body. Burden me with the reality of the life of the Body of Christ." We need to have the same burden as our brother and we need to pray that we would live these two stresses and minister them as our brother did. After writing this note, Brother Lee gave the Memorial Day conference messages entitled Practical Points concerning Blending. In those messages he pointed out that the local churches are not the goal of God's economy but the procedure to reach the goal of God's economy, and this goal is the reality of the Body of Christ. We praise the Lord for the procedure. Without the procedure we cannot reach the goal. But we should not make the procedure the goal. The procedure brings us to the goal. We should spend the rest of our lives in the goal of God's economy—the reality of the Body of Christ.
THE VISION CONCERNING THE BODY OF CHRIST
Saul of Tarsus received the vision concerning the Body at the time of his conversion in Acts 9. There has never been a conversion, nor perhaps will there ever be a conversion in the history of the church, like that of Saul of Tarsus. At his conversion he saw the Body of Christ. We should not be satisfied or content with our own situation. We need to pray, "Lord, show me what Paul saw! I want the same vision." Saul's conversion on the way to Damascus is recorded three times in the Bible (Acts 9:1-19; 22:1-16; 26:9-19), indicating how crucial it was. Acts 9:3 says, "And as he went, he drew near to Damascus, and suddenly a light from heaven flashed around him." We need this great light to flash out of heaven around us. We should say, "Lord, I pray that You as a great light would flash out of heaven around me and flash this tremendous, awesome, annihilating, earth-changing vision into my being."
In Acts 26:13 Paul tells King Agrippa, "At midday, on the way, I saw, O king, a light from heaven beyond the brightness of the sun, shining around me and those who journeyed with me." Midday is when the sun is at its highest. This is the kind of light we need. We may not have the exact same experience, but we need the same light because Paul is a pattern to us (1 Tim. 1:16). We need the same heavenly vision that he had. Ephesians 3:9 says, "And to enlighten all that they may see what the economy of the mystery is, which throughout the ages has been hidden in God, who created all things." Paul was given tremendous grace as a commission to enlighten all that they might see what the economy of the mystery is. Paul's commission was to enlighten us with the light that he had received. This is the same light that unveiled to him the very center of God's heart, the very meaning of the universe. Our eyes need to be opened so that we can be one with the Lord to go over the whole earth to open people's eyes with the same commission that Paul had.
THE MYSTERY BEING MADE KNOWN BY REVELATION
Ephesians 3:3-4 says, "By revelation the mystery was made known to me, as I have written previously in brief, by which, in reading it, you can perceive my understanding in the mystery of Christ." The mystery of Christ, which is the Body of Christ, was made known to us by revelation.
Receiving Revelation from God
Only those who have received revelation from God will see the Body of Christ, and only they will enter into the reality of the Body. We need to be impressed with how crucial it is that we receive revelation from God. This is not something of mere mental apprehension. We need revelation from God. When we see the Body, we enter into the reality of the Body. In the spiritual realm, to see is to enter into what we have seen. John 3 tells us clearly that unless someone is born anew he cannot see the kingdom of God (v. 3). To see the kingdom of God is to enter into the kingdom of God. To see the Body of Christ is to enter into the reality of the Body of Christ. One time when Brother Lee was ministering concerning the Body in one of the elders' trainings, one of the brothers stood up and asked Brother Lee a question: "How can we have what you just ministered concerning the Body of Christ?" Brother Lee responded by saying, "If you see it, you have it." This is a spiritual principle. In Genesis 13 when the Lord showed Abraham the land, He said, "All the land which thou seest, to thee will I give it, and to thy seed for ever" (v. 15). What you see is what God gives to you. In the spiritual realm, seeing is very crucial.
Needing Revelation to Know the Reality of the Body of Christ
We need revelation to know the reality of the Body of Christ and to usher us into the realm of the Body, and only then will the Body of Christ become our experience (Eph. 1:17; 3:5; Matt. 11:25; Prov. 29:18a). This is why we need to pray for a spirit of wisdom and revelation. This should be our daily prayer. Matthew 11:25 says, "At that time Jesus answered and said, I extol You, Father, Lord of heaven and of earth, because You have hidden these things from the wise and intelligent and have revealed them to infants." We never want to get to a place in our Christian life or in our church life where we become the wise and intelligent. We always want to be infants, children, who are able to receive new thoughts and new revelations from the heart of God. This is the way children are. They are not occupied with old concepts or old thoughts; they are always open to receive something new. This is also the way we must be.
Brother Nee pointed out that the smallest leaf can hide a star. The meaning of this is that trivial obstacles can prevent the light from shining into our being. We do not want that to happen. He also said that if we give the divine light the slightest chance in our being, even the smallest entrance, that light will shine into our being. If we want revelation, we need to drop our concepts, turn our hearts to the Lord, and pay attention to our spirit. Revelation is revealed to the holy apostles and prophets in spirit. We need to take care of these three things: drop our concepts, including our oldness and everything of the past, turn our heart to the Lord, and exercise our spirit. Then the Lord will give us the revelation that we need.
The Vision of, the Body Breaking Us
The vision of the Body will break us; we need to be broken under the vision of the Body so that we may live a life according to God's heart and will (Eph. 1:9-11;　3:9-11). Vision breaks us. Light, on the one hand, supplies us, heals us, and exposes us; but on the other hand, light kills us. The light that shined into Saul on the road to Damascus killed him; it annihilated him. The light that shined into Saul is what actually killed Saul and made him Paul. We need this kind of killing light. Revelation equals seeing plus killing. Being broken under the vision of the Body in this way is so that we may live a life according to God's heart and will. God's heart and will in the book of Ephesians is that God wants a Body for Christ to be His enlargement, expansion, and expression in this universe.
Romans 12:2, speaking of the will of God, says, "Be transformed by the renewing of the mind that you may prove what the will of God is, that which is good and well pleasing and perfect." The will of God is that we live the Body life. This is God's heart and will. We need to live a life according to God's heart and will under the heavenly vision.
NOT BEING DISOBEDIENT TO THE HEAVENLY VISION
In Acts 26:19 Paul said, "I was not disobedient to the heavenly vision" (9:1-19; 22:6-16).
A Revolutionizing Heavenly Vision Coming to Saul of Tarsus on His Way to Damascus
While Saul of Tarsus was on the way to Damascus, a heavenly vision came to him, and this vision revolutionized him (Acts 9:1-5).
Saul of Tarsus Becoming Blind and Impotent, Unable to See or Do Anything
After he saw the vision, he became blind, unable to see anything, and impotent, unable to do anything (v. 8). This was a great blessing to Saul. He was unable to see anything, and he was impotent, unable to do anything. Before this vision came to him, Saul considered himself marvelously knowledgeable in the things concerning God. He was a very confident person. However, when that vision came to him, he became ignorant. The light caused him to fall down. The light caused him to stop his activity. He did not know what to do, and he did not know where to go. To have this experience is marvelous! He needed someone to lead him into the Body.
Blessed Blindness
A blessed blindness comes upon those who are met by the heavenly vision. Brother Lee used this exclamatory statement, saying, "Oh, blessed blindness!" Have you ever considered that blindness is blessed? On the one hand, we are talking about vision, and on the other hand, we are talking about blessed blindness. We need such a blessed blindness. When Adam and Eve partook of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil, their eyes were opened. We do not want our eyes opened in that way. When Saul of Tarsus was blinded, he had a blessed blindness. This means that he became blind to everything but Christ. All he knew was Christ—Christ as the Head of the Body and Christ as the Body of the Head. Paul became a crazy lover of Christ.
After this blindness comes upon us, there will be the inner anointing and the inner shining, the inner enlightening. The inner vision will increase more and more and will revolutionize the way we serve the Lord. Paul saw this vision in Acts 9, but the seeing of the vision did not stop there. His vision continually expanded and was enlarged and increased until the day he went to the Lord. This is the way it needs to be with us.
Saul Praying for Three Days
For three days Saul did not see anything, and he did not eat or drink anything; all he could do was pray. Acts 9:9 tells us that he did not eat or drink for three days. In Acts-9:11 the Lord spoke to Ananias, saying, "Rise up and go to the lane called Straight, and seek in the house of Judas a man from Tarsus named Saul; for behold, he is praying." The only way to reach the high peak of the divine revelation is by prayer.
Under the inspiration of the essential Spirit, his only interest was to pray in order to know the significance of what he had seen and heard (Acts 22:14-15). It is likely that as Saul was praying, vision after vision and revelation after revelation came to him concerning Christ as the embodiment of God, the mystery of God, and the church as the Body of Christ, the mystery of Christ (Col. 2:2; Eph. 3:4; 5:32). This needs to be our experience. As we are praying, vision after vision and revelation after revelation will come to us to bring us more and more into the reality of the Body of Christ.
Each crucial point of Paul's vision recorded in Acts 9 should not merely be a teaching to us; rather, every point should be a vision that we see on the heavenly "television." May this be our experience. There is a big difference between doctrine and truth. The Lord did not say that He is full of doctrine and reality. Instead, He said that He is the truth, the reality. Truth is when the divine light shines on the facts of the Bible and conveys a living, heavenly vision of those facts into our being. We need this kind of heavenly "television" with all the points of Paul's vision in Acts 9.
Seeing the Heavenly Vision concerning Three Crucial Items
In our reading of Acts 9 we need to see the heavenly vision concerning three items—"Me" (v. 4), "Jesus" (v. 5), and the "chosen vessel" (v. 15). The Lord unveiled to Saul the entire contents of God's New Testament economy encapsulated in these three terms. Only God could do this. The intrinsic contents of Paul's entire ministry contained in his fourteen Epistles concerning the economy of God focused on the Body of Christ is encapsulated in these three terms. If we were going to save someone and make that person a particular vessel to carry out our heart's desire to the nations, we might have knocked him down, set him aside, and shared with him for six months until he was clear. But God just spoke three terms that contain His entire New Testament economy for the reality of the Body of Christ.
The Persecuted Disciples Being the Corporate "Me"
Acts 9:4 says, "Saul, Saul, why are you persecuting Me?" Acts 7:58 tells us that when Stephen was being martyred they laid down their garments at the feet of a young man called Saul. Acts 8:1 tells us that Saul approved of Stephen's killing, and in verse 3 we are told that Saul was devastating the church. The word devastating means that he was demolishing the church. This young man Saul was breathing out threatenings and murder against the disciples of the Lord (9:1). On his way to Damascus, Saul was knocked down. The light caused him to fall down, and a voice came to him and said, "Saul, Saul, why are you persecuting Me?" What a revelation this was to Saul! The voice said, "Me." Logically, the voice should have said, "Saul, Saul, why are you persecuting them?" Saul was persecuting the Lord's disciples, but the voice said, "Why are you persecuting Me?" The Me in this verse is them.
If someone were to take a hammer and hit my little finger, do you think that I would say, "Why are you persecuting my finger?" No, I would recoil and shout in pain. I would say, "Why are you persecuting me?" The reason for this is that my finger is me. Through regeneration we have become the members of Christ. Christ's members are Christ! To persecute a member of Christ is to persecute Christ. Consider how much revelation is behind the word "Me." This is why Paul is the only writer in the New Testament to use the term the Body of Christ. He received this message concerning the Body of Christ at his conversion.
This is a corporate "Me," comprising Jesus the Lord and all His believers. Saul began to see that the Lord Jesus and His believers are one great person—the wonderful "Me" (1 Cor. 12:12; Col. 3:10-11). In this universe there is a wonderful "Me." Eventually, this one great person who is Christ as the Head of the Body and Christ as the Body of the Head consummates in the New Jerusalem. The Body remains forever and ever. The New Jerusalem is the Body in its ultimate and consummate state. In 1 Corinthians 12:12 Paul said, "For even as the body is one and has many members, yet all the members of the body, being many, are one body, so also is the Christ." Here Paul shows us the "Body-Christ," the corporate Christ.
Mary McDonough wrote a book entitled God's Plan of Redemption. In this book she calls what Paul mentioned in 1 Corinthians 12:12 the "Composite Christ." This is the Christ who is composed of and completed by His many members. This is the "Body-Christ," the corporate Christ. In this corporate Christ, according to Colossians 3:10 and 11, the Body of Christ is revealed as the new man. In the new man there cannot be any natural person. There is no room for any natural person in the Body as the new man. In the Body as the new man, Christ is all of the members. In my physical body, all my members are me. In the Body as the new man, Christ is all the members and He is in all the members. There cannot be Greek and Jew, circumcision and uncircumcision, barbarian, Scythian, slave, free man, but Christ is all and in all. This must have impressed and affected Paul for his future ministry concerning Christ and the church as the great mystery of God (Eph. 5:32) and laid a solid foundation for his unique ministry. Paul is the only writer of the New Testament to use the term the Body of Christ (Rom. 12:4-5; 1 Cor. 12:12-27; Eph. 1:22-23; 2:16; 4:4, 12, 16; Col. 1:18).
Paul placed great emphasis on the Body because at the time of his conversion he heard a message concerning the corporate "Me," a message concerning the Body of Christ. Immediately after Saul was saved, the Lord began to educate him concerning the Body of Christ (Acts 9:6, 17-18, 24-25; 22:14-16). Immediately after Saul was saved he asked, "What shall I do, Lord?" The Lord spoke to him as the Head. Saul had touched Him in a negative way as the Body. But then the Lord seemed to say, "Go into the city and it will be told you what you must do. I am not going to speak to you directly as the Head; I am going to speak to you in and through a member of my Body. I am going to educate you concerning the Body of Christ."
Once I was in a dire circumstance. I was in my house with my children, and it was very difficult to get away. I really needed the Lord to speak to me concerning a certain situation. So I went into our bathroom and locked the door. This was the only place of solace I could find. I was desperate and I said to the Lord, "I am not coming out of this bathroom until I get an answer from You." Actually, the Lord was quick to respond. As I dealt with the Lord, what rose up in my being was, "'Go into the city and it will be told you what you must do.' You have to go to a member of the Body." I did not want to do that. Going to another person deals with our pride. I wanted a "Western Union telegram" to come from the third heavens, but that did not happen. I had to go to another member of the Body. I had to open up this situation to a member. I had to hear what the Lord had to say in and through a member of the Body, and then I would get clear. Although I struggled, by the Lord's mercy, I took His word, I got with a brother, and the light shined through that brother.
The Lord sent Ananias, a little brother, to Saul of Tarsus. Ananias's name is not recorded again in the divine record after this occasion. However, this brother was so precious. The record of his conversation with the Lord indicates that he was very intimate with the Lord. When the Lord told him to go to Saul, he said, "Lord, I have heard from many concerning this man, how many evil things he has done to Your saints in Jerusalem; and here he has authority from the chief priests to bind all who call upon Your name" (Acts 9:13-14). This shows his intimacy with the Lord. Many times the smallest member meets our need. Brother Lee has often used the illustration of the little finger rendering comfort to the itching ear when the other larger members cannot. Ananias was such a small member, yet he was able to meet Saul's need. Without Ananias, Saul of Tarsus had no way to go on. Without this member cooperating with the Lord to go to him, Saul of Tarsus would have remained blind.
The Lord educates us concerning the Body in this way. Without the brothers and sisters, many times we cannot see. It may take a brother giving you a phone call, knocking on your door, or coming into your office. Without Ananias, Saul had no way to see. Without Ananias, Saul had no way to confirm his salvation. Without Ananias, Saul of Tarsus had no way to be identified with the Body of Christ. Without the brothers and sisters, we have no way. To refuse the help of a member of the Body is to refuse the help of Christ. This is the marvelous way that Saul was educated.
The Lord spoke to Saul as the Head and then He spoke to him as the Body. He spoke to him as the Body, in the Body, and through a member of the Body. When Saul's eyes were opened, he saw the corporate Jesus! The first thing he saw was a member of Christ, and one of the first words he heard from this member was the word brother. Ananias said, "Saul, brother" (Acts 9:17). What a mercy and a glory that we can be a little brother! We are not the great Saul but Paul, which means little.
Acts 9 tells us that when Saul—later Paul—was preaching the gospel in Damascus, the Jews took counsel together to do away with him. They watched the gates closely both day and night. But his disciples took him by night and let him down through the wall, lowering him in a basket (vv. 23-25). In 2 Corinthians 11, Paul mentions this incident again, saying, "And in a basket I was lowered through a window, through the wall, and escaped his hands" (v. 33). This is his boasting to attest to the truth and verity of his apostleship. If you were going to boast and let people know that you are an apostle, would you tell them that you were let down through the wall in a basket? No. But Paul did this, indicating that his boast was not in his strength or in his individualism. His boast was in his weakness and in his dependence upon the Body. Saul in Acts 9 did not say, "I don't need your help." He depended on the Body. Many times the hardest place to be is "in the basket." We must be willing to be both those who receive the help from other members to lower us "in the basket" as well as those who are lowering others.
The Lord Being "Jesus, Whom You Persecute"
Acts 9:5 says, "And he said, Who are You, Lord? And He said, I am Jesus, whom you persecute." The name Jesus embodies the full message of the gospel. Saul saw that Jesus is Jehovah the Savior and that as the One who is now in the heavens, He has passed through the process of incarnation, human living, death, resurrection, and ascension for the producing and building up of the Body of Christ.
Saul said, "Who are You, Lord?" When Saul asked this, he knew that this was God, that this was Jehovah, that this was the God of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob in Exodus 3, the One about whom he had read and studied as a Pharisee. Then he heard from the heavens, "I am Jesus." This meant that Jesus is Jehovah, that Jesus is God. This is the Man-God, the God-man Jesus. Jesus is the very God of this universe. This voice came from the heavens. This meant that Jesus had ascended; this also implied that Jesus is God. As Saul prayed, he must have considered in the following manner: "Jesus is God; Jesus was a man, which means that God became a man and He is now in the heavens. This indicates that He ascended and that He passed through human living, crucifixion, and resurrection." All of God's process is in the word Jesus. As a result of this revelation, Paul tells us strongly in his Epistles that Jesus is God. In Romans 9:5 he says, "Christ, who is over all, God blessed forever," and in Hebrews 1:8 he says, "But of the Son, 'Your throne, O God.'"
In his Epistles, Paul speaks about the incarnation—one of the items of the process of God. Paul received the revelation of the entire process of God because it is embodied in the name Jesus. Jesus is the processed and consummated Triune God. He went through all of the processes—incarnation, human living, crucifixion, resurrection, and ascension. He was consummated to be the Lord of all, the Head of the Body, and the life-giving Spirit dispensed into all His members. This is what the name Jesus implies. Paul saw that Jesus is the very God who has been processed and consummated to be the ascended Lord, the Head of the Body, and the life-giving Spirit to be dispensed into all His members (Rom. 10:12-13; Col. 1:18a; 1 Cor. 15:45b).
In Romans Paul charges us to call, "Lord Jesus" (Rom. 10:9, 12-13). At one time Paul persecuted those who called on the Lord Jesus. When he heard them saying, "Lord Jesus," he would capture them. But now he became a person who called on the name of the Lord and one who declared that Jesus is Lord. He saw Him as the Head of the Body, Paul saw that this very Jesus as the processed and consummated God is the life-giving Spirit dispensed into all His members, to make His members God in life and nature but not in the Godhead.
Paul Being a Chosen Vessel
Paul was a chosen vessel. Acts 9:15 says, "This man is a chosen vessel to Me." The entire divine revelation contained in Paul's fourteen Epistles concerning the eternal economy of God is focused on the high peak of the divine revelation—the reality of the Body of Christ. This revelation is contained in three words: Me, Jesus, and vessel. When Ananias came to Saul, he must have told him that he was a chosen vessel. Paul's Epistles speak about man being a vessel more than other writings in the New Testament. God's intention in saving Saul of Tarsus was to fill him with Himself and thereby make him an outstanding vessel (Col. 1:25; Eph. 3:8-9).
In Paul's writings we see the development of the spiritual significance of the word vessel. To say that man is a vessel implies the entire tripartite man—spirit, soul, and body. Paul saw that man is a vessel to contain and be filled with Christ as life for the building up of the Body of Christ (Rom. 9:21, 23; 2 Cor. 4:7; 2 Tim. 2:20-21). We are vessels. This means that we are containers. According to Romans 9, we are all containers, and we were made to contain the God of mercy, the God of honor, and the God of glory. As vessels, if we do not have God as our content, we are a senseless contradiction. Every day we should pray, "Lord, be my content." This is a major point in Paul's Epistles, and he received this at the time of his conversion.
Paul's fourteen Epistles can be summed up in two words: open vessel. The Lord wants us to be an open vessel so that we say, "Lord, I open my entire being to You. Keep me open to You." During the day, we need to pray, "Lord, keep me open to You all day. I would like to always be receiving You. I want to continually be filled with You. I want You to be everything to me. I want You to do everything in me, through me, and for me."
The Body of Christ Being God's Great Corporate Vessel
The Body of Christ is God's great corporate vessel to contain Him and be filled with Him for His expression (Eph. 3:16-19). God does not want individual vessels; He desires a corporate vessel. All of us are individual vessels, but all of these vessels blended and mingled together in the Triune God become a great corporate vessel to be filled with the unsearchable riches of Christ for His expression, enlargement, and expansion in the universe.
THE THREE CRUCIAL POINTS IN PAUL'S EPISTLES
The first crucial point in Paul's Epistles is that God is our content. God needs to be our content. This is implied in the word vessel. The second crucial point in Paul's Epistles is Christ as the mystery of God, which is implied in the word Jesus. The third crucial point is the Body of Christ as the mystery of Christ. This is contained in the word Me. The summary of these three crucial points is the following: The name Jesus implies that He is the processed and consummated Triune God as the life-giving Spirit. As the life-giving Spirit He is dispensing Himself into the tripartite man—His corporate vessel—that this vessel might be filled with the unsearchable riches of Christ for the building up of the corporate "Me" to consummate in the New Jerusalem as the great God-man in the universe for God's eternal expression, glory, effulgence, and radiance to shine out to the whole universe forever and ever.—E. M.

GENERAL SUBJECT
KEEPING THE PRINCIPLES OF THE BODY
Message Two
The Principles of the Body (1)
纲目|对照-听抄-目录
Scripture Reading: Rom. 12:4-5; 1 Cor. 12:12-13; Eph. 4:3-6; Col. 3:4, 10-11
I.　The Body is one—Rom. 12:4-5; 1 Cor. 12:12-13; Eph. 4:4a; 2:16a:
A.　The Lord's recovery is the recovery of the oneness of the Body of Christ:
1.　The Body can exist and survive only in oneness.

Prayer: Lord, we are Your members, members of Your Body. We declare, Lord, by Your great mercy and wonderful salvation that You have incorporated us into Yourself and that You have mingled Yourself with us. Lord, we are here in this wonderful organic union. Lord, we treasure this oneness with You. We pray through the Spirit of wisdom and revelation that You would grant each one of us to see the Body—something that is beyond the comprehension and grasp of our natural mind and our human understanding. Lord, we need Your mercy and divine light to shine and penetrate into our being that we may see and know the Body subjectively. Lord, we pray that we would become what we see and, being broken by such a view, that we would be good for nothing but to live as Your Body on this earth. We believe that when we touch Your Body, we are truly touching the apple of Your eye. Hide and cover us under Your precious and prevailing blood. Anoint Your entire Body. We pray that You will be happy, satisfied, and uplifted, even though the number of people who see the Body and care for what they see is small. Lord, work into us the care for the Body of Christ. Lord Jesus, we look to You to bless this meeting. Shame the enemy and cover Your whole recovery. Amen.
The next five messages in this conference will cover the principles of the Body. The Body is a crucial matter. When we touch the matter of the Lord's Body, we are touching something very deep, high, broad, and in the center of the heart of God. Therefore, we must pray this vision and revelation into our spirit. We can only see the Body by a spirit of wisdom and revelation (Eph. 1:17), by a vision from the heavens, and by a revelation from God. When the Lord brought Peter and the other disciples to Caesarea Philippi, they saw who Christ was and heard about the church (Matt. 16:16, 18). "Jesus answered and said to him, Blessed are you, Simon Barjona, because flesh and blood has not revealed this to you, but My Father who is in the heavens" (v. 17). The Lord made it clear that Peter's seeing was not a result of blood and flesh. Peter saw Christ as a result of a revelation from the Father, and the disciples saw the Body, the church, by a revelation from the Son. Therefore, we must pray that we would not be satisfied with mere knowledge, teaching, or doctrine. We should pray, "Lord, remove the veils from my heart and grant true revelation to me."
THE GOVERNING PRINCIPLES OF THE BODY
We must see the principles of the Body. The word principle has mainly two meanings, both of which apply to our understanding of the principles of the Body. First, a principle is a general and fundamental law that governs something. Second, a principle is a certain rule of conduct that governs behavior. We need to see the governing principles and the fundamental laws when we practice the Body of Christ. God is most lawful, and everything He does is according to a law. He Himself is the very law.
We cannot touch, see, experience, or testify of the Body in a free and natural way. We must see the clear governing laws according to God so that we can have the real expression of the Body that is according to Him. These principles are a synthesis and crystallization of the laws that govern the Body of Christ. None of us should have the confidence that we know the Body of Christ. We ask the Lord to wash us that we could apply the eyesalve to see anew the governing principles of the Body of Christ.
These principles are also rules of conduct; the/ are not a legal code but certain guiding rules. If we take these principles, we will have a set of guiding rules, which are not legal but organic. These principles become a test and measuring stick for how much we have really seen and are experiencing the Body life. For example, consider the ecumenical movement, which is attempting a worldwide unity and states that it is for the Body. Using the principles revealed in the New Testament, we have to test to see whether or not such a movement is for the Body. To do so, we need a sober mind and a very exercised spirit. These five messages reveal twenty principles related to the life, function, order, authority, fellowship, and mutuality of the Body.
THE ONENESS OF THE BODY
The Body is one (Rom. 12:4-5; 1 Cor. 12:12-13; Eph. 4:4a; 2:16a). This principle is the most important. Satan's strategy throughout the centuries has been to divide the Body into millions of pieces. Some may consider that life is more important than oneness, but it is possible to talk about life in a way that is not for the Body. Some of the so-called inner-life believers have become individualistic, secluded, exclusive, and cliquish because they did not keep the oneness of the Body. The first and foremost principle in the matter of knowing the Body is that the Body is one. Romans 12:4-5 says, "For just as in one body we have many members, and all the members do not have the same function, so we who are many are one body in Christ, and individually members one of another." First Corinthians 12:12-13 says, "For even as the body is one and has many members, yet all the members of the body, being many, are one body, so also is the Christ. For also in one Spirit we were all baptized into one body, whether Jews or Greeks, whether slaves or free, and were all given to drink one Spirit." Ephesians also speaks of "one Body" (4:4a; 2:1.6a). We therefore cannot talk about the Body without also speaking about oneness. The Body is the oneness. The genuine oneness of the Christians is the Body. The oneness of the Body is everything!
There is only one Body. A head cannot have two bodies. This principle should kill and eliminate many things. We can conclude and automatically say that anything that is not one is not the Body and has nothing to do with the Body. There is one Body in this universe. The Jews and Gentiles are joined in one Body. We are baptized into one Body. We are all one Body in Christ. The Body is one because God is one. Everything that has to do with God is one. The Triune God is one.
The Lord's Recovery Being the Recovery of the Oneness of the Body of Christ
The Lord's recovery is the recovery of the oneness of the Body of Christ. The Lord's recovery is foremost for the purpose of testifying that the Body of Christ should be one. There are many things testified by the Lord's recovery, but the foremost, even the primary, purpose of the testimony of the Lord's recovery is that the Body is one.
The Body Existing and Surviving Only in Oneness
The Body can exist and survive only in oneness. If there is no oneness, the Body cannot exist, much less survive. For example, if the physical body is cut into pieces, it is no longer a body. The Body of Christ exists and survives in oneness.
Recovering the Oneness of the Body of Christ
The Lord is burdened to recover the oneness of the Body of Christ. The Lord's recovery has always been engaged in a spiritual battle. The battle is to recover the oneness of the Body. Many things have been recovered, but the oneness of the Body still needs to be recovered. We are standing apart from today's Christian religion to do something different, even suffering persecution for our stand, because we are for the oneness of the Body.
Brother Nee and Brother Lee both fought for the matter of oneness. For this reason they were not welcomed by the denominations. If they had shared Christ alone, they would have been warmly welcomed. However, the Lord's recovery is rejected by many because of our stand for the oneness of the Body. Oneness condemns the divisions, denominations, and any other separation among the Lord's children. Because we are for the oneness, we are engaged in a spiritual battle.
Recovering the Oneness by a Small Number of Saints Who Wish to Satisfy the Lord
The practice of this oneness has been lost. Many things have been lost among God's children, but the oneness is a particular matter that has been lost. Division is the first thing unbelievers see in Christianity. God is the one Husband, and He should have only one wife. We must be "Mrs. Christ," taking no other name. We are a relatively small number of saints, but we wish to satisfy the Lord's heart to recover the oneness.
The ecumenical movement believes that one day the entire earth will embrace oneness. However, the principle of the remnant applies in this age just as it did with Israel in the Old Testament. Only a small number will return back from a divisive situation to the proper ground to practice the oneness. When we touch the matter of oneness, we are touching the Lord's heart. John 17 records a long prayer of the Lord before He was crucified, in which He prayed not for victory or spirituality but for oneness. He prayed, "Holy Father, keep them in Your name, which You have given to Me, that they may be one even as We are" (v. 11). His prayer was for the oneness of the believers. This is something on the Lord's heart; to recover the oneness is to satisfy the Lord's heart. May we give ourselves to this, and may we live and die for the recovery of the oneness of the Lord's Body.
The Genuine Oneness of the Body Being the Processed and Consummated Triune God
The genuine oneness of the Body is the processed and consummated Triune God, who mingles Himself with the believers (Eph. 4:4-6). We need to be stripped of the natural understanding of a so-called "mathematical oneness" of people meeting together, agreeing to be one, and having a common cause. May the Lord open our eyes to see that the genuine oneness of the Body is actually the processed and consummated Triune God. The Triune God—the Father, the Son, and the Spirit—-has been processed (John 1:14; 1 Cor. 15:45b). According to Ephesians 4, there is one Father, one Lord, and one Spirit (vv. 4-6). The Triune God is the oneness in this universe. The Triune God, the Divine Trinity, is one.
The Mingling of the Processed and Consummated Triune God with His Chosen and Redeemed People Being the Genuine Oneness of the Body of Christ
The Triune God, who is the oneness, mingles Himself with His chosen, redeemed, tripartite believers. This mingling issues in the real oneness of the Body. The Divine Trinity is one and He is the real and genuine oneness in the universe. There is no oneness apart from God. God is the real oneness. Oneness is not a thing; oneness is God. Oneness is the Divine Trinity. This Divine Trinity went through a series of processes and became the life-giving Spirit (1 Cor. 15:45b). As such a processed and consummated Spirit, He mingles Himself with us. This mingling of Himself with us the believers is the genuine oneness.
In John 17 the Lord prayed "that they may be one, even as We are one" (v. 22). This is the intrinsic definition of oneness. Our oneness is the same as the oneness of the Triune God. The Divine Trinity mingled with us is our oneness today and the oneness of the Body of Christ. Oneness is God. Oneness is the Divine Trinity processed, consummated, and mingled with us. This mingling is the oneness. The Body does not have any other kind of oneness apart from this oneness. This is the real oneness of the Body of Christ.
The Body Itself Being the Oneness
The processed and consummated Triune God mingles Himself with His chosen and redeemed people in their humanity, and this mingling is the genuine oneness of the Body of Christ. Because the Body is such a mingling, the Body itself is the oneness. Because He has been mingled with us today, we are all in this wonderful God-man union, mingling, and divine-human incorporation. The three divine factors—the Father, the Son, and the Spirit—join with the one human factor—the spirit of the regenerated believers. This consummates in the oneness of the Body of Christ.
We can say reverently and without misunderstanding that there are "four in one." This does not mean that we become God in the Godhead or are absorbed into the Godhead. It means that in a very real sense the Divine Trinity has been mingled with us and we have been mingled with Him. We Christians are mingled with the Triune God. This mingling causes us to be joined to the divine oneness and even become the enlargement of the divine oneness. This is the oneness of the Body of Christ. We cannot join the oneness, sign up for it, create it, form it, or unify it. We just mingle into it. We become a part of this oneness by being mingled with the Triune God. We do not need to become one; rather, we just need to experience the oneness we already possess.
The Oneness of the Body of Christ Being the Enlarged Oneness of the Divine Trinity
The oneness of the Body of Christ is the oneness of the Divine Trinity; hence, the oneness of the Body of Christ is the enlarged oneness of the Divine Trinity (John 17:21,23). This enlargement happened at Pentecost. The Triune God was one in Himself, but after the Lord's resurrection the Spirit was breathed into the disciples (20:22), and at Pentecost He was poured out upon them (Acts 2:4, 17). They not only drank of the one Spirit, but were also immersed into the same Spirit (1 Cor. 12:13). By the drinking and immersion they became the one Body. For the first time in the universe the oneness of the Triune God was enlarged to include us, the Body of Christ. We are part of the enlargement, extension, and continuation of that oneness. Today there is an enlarged oneness, which includes millions of those who have believed throughout the centuries. This oneness is a corporate Body, the Body of Christ.
The Oneness of the Body of Christ Being Divine, Even Divinely Organic, Full of Life
The oneness of the Body of Christ is divine, and it is divinely organic, full of life. The oneness of the Body of Christ is not natural or human. We cannot fabricate it or manufacture it. It is a divine oneness; it is something of God, even pod Himself. It is not only divine but also divinely organic. It is not a mere mathematical oneness but an organic oneness, full of life. Our oneness is in life. When the organic aspect is gone, there is no light# God is absent, and there is no real oneness of the Body of Christ. We are not merely shouting that we are one; we are becoming divine and organic. This means that day after day we remain in the reality of the divine-human mingling, participating in the Triune God. We are thus in the actuality of this oneness. Day by day we must be living in the organic union with the Triune God. We must remain in, participate in, enjoy, and experience the divine life to be in the organic union with God. By remaining in this organic union, we are in the real oneness of the Body of Christ.
A few brothers, or even an entire congregation, could come together and say, "Let us be one. Let us drop our concepts to be one." This is not bad, but it is hardly attaining to the real oneness. We could drop all of our concepts and yet we could still be outside of the mingling and outside of life. Anything that causes death causes division. Anything that is not in the divine realm of God is automatically something in the way of division. If this light shines into us, it will govern our conduct. To really care for the oneness we have to be those who remain in this mingling and who experience this divine life all the time. Then we can have the genuine oneness.
Becoming the Body of Christ by Being Enlivened, Regenerated, and Made Alive in the Triune God
We are the church to be the Body of Christ not by being organized but by being regenerated and enlivened with the Triune God as the oneness within us. We cannot organize oneness. He is the oneness within us by regenerating and enlivening us with Himself.
The Organic Oneness of the Body of Christ Being the Oneness of the Spirit
The organic oneness of the Body of Christ is the oneness of the Spirit (Eph. 4:3). The seven "ones" in Ephesians 4:3-6, the oneness of the Divine Trinity, the oneness of the Spirit, and the Lord's prayer in John 17 reveal the organic oneness of the Body of Christ. This organic oneness is the oneness of the Spirit. We need to be diligent and endeavor to keep the oneness of the Spirit.
The Spirit Being the Essence of the Organic Body of Christ
The Spirit is the essence of the organic Body of Christ. The Father is the source, the Son is the element, and the Spirit is the essence. The Spirit is the essence of the Body of Christ in the same way that the blood is the essence of the physical body.
The Spirit Being the Reality of the Oneness of the Body of Christ
The Spirit is the reality of the oneness of the Body of Christ; this oneness is actually the Spirit Himself. There is only one Spirit in us. There is one Spirit and one Body. The Spirit is the very life and oneness of the Body. We need therefore to drink the Spirit (1 Cor. 12:13).
All the Local Churches Being the One Body Universally, Doctrinally, and Practically
All the local churches are and should be one Body universally, doctrinally, and practically. All the churches are the aggregate of the one Body. There is no such thing as a local, regional, or national Body. The situation among Christians today is a shame to the Lord. The Body is one, and all the local churches should be the one Body universally, doctrinally, and practically. We glory in oneness.
Keeping the Oneness of the Universal Body of Christ by Blending
Blending is the most helpful thing in keeping the oneness of the universal Body of Christ (1 Cor. 12:24b). We blend with one another to keep the oneness. If we remain distant from one another, we may become different; therefore, we need to blend. The blending helps us keep and maintain the oneness.
Keeping the Oneness Being the Primary Virtue of Our Christian Walk
The keeping of the oneness is the primary virtue of our Christian walk (Eph. 4:1-3). Paul says that we have to walk worthily of our calling. The Lord has called us to the one Body. We therefore need to walk according to the oneness of the Body. We have to care for the Body to the uttermost, even as the apostle Paul did. To walk worthily of the Lord's calling is to care for the Body of Christ. To maintain and keep the oneness at all cost is to care for the Lord's Body and to walk worthily of our calling.
In light of this matter we must hate division. Besides denying the person of Christ, nothing is so terrible as making a division in the Body. This is to dismember the Body of Christ. Many have felt free to start their own church and to say to other believers, "Since I do not like you, I am going away." This should not be heard among us, and we should not allow this thought to come up in our heart. We must fight for the oneness. We have to stay away from those who are sectarian (Rom. 16:17). Factiousness and division are works of the flesh, and those who practice such things will not enter into the kingdom of God (Gal. 5:20-21; Titus 3:10-11). We should be warned by this word to never get ourselves involved in anything that has to do with division. We should stay with the oneness.
THE CHURCH AS THE BODY OF CHRIST BEING ABSOLUTELY A MATTER OF LIFE
The church as the Body of Christ is absolutely a matter of life (John 17:2; 1 John 5:11-12; Col. 3:4). The Body of Christ is not an organization, society, or club. The Body of Christ is an organism. It is the unique organism of the Triune God and is totally, entirely, and absolutely a matter of life, the divine life.
The Body Formed by Christ as Life in Each of the Believers
The Body is formed by Christ as life in each of us, mingled with us (Col. 3:4; Rom. 8:10, 6, 11). We are joined together to be the Body of Christ by His life coming into each of us and mingling with us. His life joins us together as one Body. His life in you and me is the same one life.
Not a Member Life but a Body Life
The life in each of us is not a member life but a Body life (Rom. 12:5; Eph. 5:30). Sometimes we may consider that our life is unique and special as a member of Christ and that other saints also have their unique and special life of Christ. But the life in us is not a member life; it is a Body life. The same life in you is also in me. Our gifts and talents may be different, but our life is the same. Our life is Christ. He is our life (Col. 3:4). The life in us is a Body life. We share the same life with all the members of the Body. The life within us is the life of the entire Body. The life that is in the entire Body is the life in you and the life in me. This concept can revolutionize our whole Christian life. This fact means that wherever I am, the life in me is a Body life. I am therefore never alone and separated from the Body. My life is a Body life. This life is always conscious of the Body, always thinking about the Body, always needing fellowship with the Body, and always desiring to be with the Body. The life within us is not an individualistic life, nor even a member life, but a Body life.
Having Fellowship and Being Joined with All the Other Members
The divine life within us requires us to have fellowship with all the other members and be joined with them (Acts 2:42; 1 John 1:3). This is automatic. Since the life within us is a Body life, it always desires and requires us to have fellowship. It is hard to go through even one day without having fellowship with some of the saints. The life within us requires the fellowship and demands that we fellowship with the other members of the Body and be joined with them.
Brought into the Fellowship and Experience of Being Joined Together
The divine life brings us into fellowship and into the experience of being joined together. Our being joined together and our fellowshipping together are by the divine life. This is like the blood in our physical body and the electricity joining the lights in a building. The life, which is like the electricity and the blood, brings us into fellowship and joins us together.
The Expression of the Triune God
The oneness of the Body of Christ is the expression of the Triune God in and by His divine life. Our oneness is the expression of the Triune God among us. When we express and live out the Triune God in and by His divine life, that is the real oneness.
Caring for the Organic Oneness of the Divine Life
The oneness in the Body is an organic oneness in the divine life, and we must care for this life. We do not have a "robotic" church life or a "bionic" Body. If a brother is "out of it," we cannot just unplug him and throw him away and replace him with a living brother so that the Body can move again. We often experience in the church life that not all our "pistons" are firing. When one saint is burning, another is asleep; when one saint is up, another is down. We dream of a wonderful church life in which every "piston" is firing. We may wish that we could take away the "piston" that is not working and replace it with one that works. We may hope that all the parts of the Body could be interchangeable, well-oiled, always functioning, and working in an efficient way. If we were to have this, we;would not have the Body; we would have a robot that is dead and inorganic. Instead, the Body is living; therefore, it has weaknesses, illnesses, and particular situations. We always need to care for the Body and care for the life in the Body. We should always minister life to the Body.
An Organism Built Up by the Growth of the Divine Life
The Body of Christ is an organism built up by the growth of the divine life (Eph. 4:16; Col. 2:19). The Body is not built up with "Lego" building blocks. It is built by growth. Growth is the increase of God. God is life, so the growth in life is just the increase of God, the increase of the element of Christ. This is the building up of the Body. By this we can see that the Body is absolutely a matter of life. To build up the Body, we all need to grow.
BEING MADE CHRIST TO BE IN THE BODY
Since the Body of Christ is Christ, if we would be in the Body, we must be made Christ (Col. 3:10-11). That we are made Christ is the high peak of the divine revelation. First Corinthians 12:12 is very clear on this point: "For even as the body is one and has many members, yet all the members of the body, being many, are one body, so also is the Christ." For us to be the Body of Christ, we have to be Christ. For example, for the hand to be part of my body, it has to be me. The only way for us to be in the Body is to be made Christ.
One Body in Christ, Having an Organic Union with Him
We are one Body in Christ, having an organic union with Him (Rom. 12:5). We are in the one Body of Christ because we are in Christ. We are many members in Christ joined to Him in an organic union with Him. It is this organic union that makes us the many members and makes us part of the Body of Christ.
Christ Being All and in All in the Body
In the Body, Christ is all and in all (Col. 3:10-11). Christ is all the members and in all the members. In the Body of Christ there is no room for anyone other than Christ. Only Christ can go "through the door"; we all are kept out. There is no room for social class, race, or different cultures. In the Body there is only room for Christ. Only Christ fits and only He is allowed. We have to be in Christ and we have to be Christ ourselves.
The Church as the Body of Christ Coming out of Christ and Being One with Him
The church as the Body of Christ comes out of Christ and is one with Christ (Gen. 2:22-23; Eph. 5:23-32). In the picture presented in Genesis, Eve was built out of a rib taken out of the side of Adam. That woman who was built is a type of the church, the Body of Christ. By this we can see that the church, the Body of Christ, comes out of Christ and is one with Him. We are out of Christ and even we are one with Him as His Body.
Only Christ in the Believers Forming a Part of the Body of Christ
There is only one thing in a believer that forms a part of the Body of Christ—Christ. That part in us that is Christ is the Body of Christ. We therefore have to pray, "Lord, spread in me. Make my mind Christ. Make my emotion Christ. Make my will Christ. Make my soul Christ. O Lord, settle down in my heart and make my heart Christ." The more Christ spreads within us, the more parts of our being will be part of the Body of Christ.
The Body Being the Corporate Christ
The Body is the corporate Christ (1 Cor. 12:12). Christ and the church is one mysterious, corporate Christ—the Body-Christ. Individually, we are all particular members of the Body, but corporately we are part of the mystical Body of Christ. We are part of this mysterious, corporate Christ. The Head is Christ and the Body is Christ. Christ and the church, which is also Christ, form one mysterious, corporate Christ. The corporate Christ, the Body-Christ, includes not only Christ Himself but also the members of the Body, who are the members of Christ, parts of Christ, having Christ wrought into their being.
Becoming the Body of Christ in Reality and Practicality by Being Constituted with Christ
God is constituting us with Christ so that we can become the Body of Christ in reality and practicality. To become the Body of Christ in reality and in practice, we have to be constituted with Christ. Then we become Christ and live Him out. The only way to do this is to drink the Spirit, to receive Christ into us, to take in His word, and to be saturated with Him. If we do this day by day, we not only maintain our life, but we also are constituted to have the very reality and practicality of the Body of Christ.—M. C.

C.　We need to be transferred from the realm of the old creation into the realm of the new creation to be the new man to fulfill God's eternal purpose—to consummate the New Jerusalem—2 Cor. 5:17; Eph. 4:24; Col. 3:10; Rev. 21:2.

VIII.　The work of the cross consummates with the Body and ushers us into the Body—Eph. 2:16:

MESSAGE THREE
THE PRINCIPLES OF THE BODY (2)

Prayer: Lord, we praise You and thank You. You are the almighty God and the victorious Lord. You will obtain what You desire. Lord, we praise You that You desire to have the Body of Christ, and You will gain it. No one can frustrate Your desire. The more Your enemy tries to frustrate Your desire, the more he helps You to accomplish Your purpose. Lord, we thank You, and we praise You. We give You all the glory. We pray tonight that You will once again manifest Your glory.
In this message we will continue our fellowship concerning the principles of the Body. The last message covered the first three principles of the Body: the Body is one, the Body is altogether a matter of life, and the Body is Christ. These principles and the others that follow form a "yardstick" to manifest our true condition. When we are in Christ, abiding in Christ, and walking according to the Spirit, these principles concerning the Body should be manifested among us.
THE FUNCTION OF THE BODY BEING TO EXPRESS CHRIST
Christ Needing a Body to Be His Fullness, His Expression
The function of the Body is to express Christ. The universally great Christ needs a Body to be His fullness, His expression (Eph. 1:22-23). Our God is a God of purpose. His purpose is revealed from the very beginning of the Bible. Genesis 1:26 says, "And God said, Let us make man in our image, after our likeness: and let them have dominion over the fish of the sea, and over the foul of the air, and over the cattle, and over all the earth, and over every creeping thing that creeping upon the earth." When God created man, He created him according to His eternal purpose. This purpose has two aspects—image and dominion. The word image means expression. So when God created man, He had expression in mind. His heart's desire is to have man for His expression. To "make man in our image" means God wants man to express Him. Whether angels or other living creatures—God desires that whoever sees man would see God. This is His purpose.
Genesis 1 also reveals that God does not want His purpose to be accomplished by many individuals, but by a corporate man. Verse 27 says, "In the image of God created he him; male and female created he them." The word him in this verse indicates that God created one man. But immediately following the word him, it says, "Male and female created he them." God created him and He created them. The him is them. God wants a corporate man. God's purpose is fulfilled by a corporate man. In the New Testament this corporate man is the church, the Body of Christ. Ephesians 1:22-23 says, "And He subjected all things under His feet and gave Him to be Head over all things to the church, which is His Body, the fullness of the One who fills all in all." The Old Testament in Genesis 1 gives us a picture of God's purpose, but the New Testament in Ephesians 1 gives us the reality of God's purpose, which is the church, the Body of Christ. Ephesians 1 does not use the word expression; instead, it uses the word fullness (v. 23). Fullness actually means expression. The church is Christ's Body, and His Body is His fullness. The all-inclusive Christ is the One who fills all in all, and His Body is His fullness, His expression.
Many Christians are confused about two phrases in the Bible: the riches of Christ and the fullness of the One who fills all in all. Many consider that these two phrases refer to the same thing; that is, the riches of Christ are actually the fullness of Christ. But according to the Bible, they do not refer to the same thing. In Ephesians 3:8, Paul spoke of "the unsearchable riches of Christ as the gospel." rfere the term riches of Christ is used. The riches of Christ refer to all the items of what Christ is. Our Christ is so rich with many different aspects. He is life, He is resurrection, He is wisdom, He is power, He is strength, He is our spiritual food, He is our spiritual drink, and He is the spiritual air that we breathe. He is everything. All of these things are the riches of Christ.
What then is the fullness of Christ? The fullness of Christ is His Body. As an illustration, we may use a cup being filled with liquid. On the one hand, if the cup is not transparent you cannot know what kind of liquid the cup contains, but on the other hand, you cannot know what is inside the cup because it is not full. The way to know the contents of the cup is to fill the cup until it overflows. Th§ cup being filled illustrates the riches being added into the cup. The overflow illustrates what the fullness is. If the contents of the cup cannot be seen, more riches need to be added to it. As more and more riches are added, eventually the contents of the cup will overflow and be manifested. The overflow of the riches is the fullness, and the fullness expresses the riches. The Body is the fullness of Christ, and this fullness is the expression of Christ.
The Fullness of Christ Issuing from the Enjoyment of the Riches of Christ
The fullness of Christ issues from the enjoyment of the riches of Christ (Eph. 3:8). Through the enjoyment of Christ's riches, we become His fullness to express Him. This is a very important principle. The church as the Body of Christ fulfills its function to express Him by enjoying the riches of Christ. The more we receive the riches of Christ into us, the more we will be His fullness, and the more we will express Him. This is the reason that we often say, "Eating Jesus is the way." Eating Jesus is to receive His riches into us and to enjoy those riches. From our enjoyment of His riches comes the fullness. In this way the church fulfills its function to express Christ.
The Fullness of Christ Being Christ Experienced by Us and Constituted into Us
The fullness of Christ is Christ experienced by us and constituted into us. If we enjoy the riches of Christ, we will become the fullness of Christ. The Christ who has been experienced by us will be constituted within us. Only Christ Himself can express Christ. If we do not have Christ constituted within us, we can never express Him. Do not try to express Him by imitating Him in an outward way. The only way to express Christ is to receive Him, enjoy Him, and eat and drink of Him. Then the Christ who is being enjoyed and experienced by us can be constituted within us so that we may express Him.
The Objective Riches Becoming the Subjective Fullness
The objective riches become the subjective fullness, and this fullness is the Body of Christ. In the world, to be objective is very good and positive, but to be subjective is not so good. The word objective in scientific research means that we should not consider our personal feeling, personal opinion, or personal prejudice; we have to look at things in an objective manner. In the same context, subjectivity means that we have our own notions, preconceived ideas, feelings, and prejudices. So when we are very subjective persons, we cannot see things very clearly. To be objective means to be impersonal, setting aside one's own personal feeling in order to see the things in a clear way. But in the spiritual realm the meaning of these two words is completely different. In the spiritual realm, objective means something outside of us, something impersonal, and subjective means something inside of us, something very personal. In the spiritual realm, we desire to be subjective and to have subjective experience. We should not remain merely objective; we must also have subjective experience. All the spiritual things have to be our personal experience; they have to be something inside of us. We should not take things merely in an outward way. If we hear a truth and that truth remains outside of our personal experience, then that truth is merely a doctrine to us. It cannot benefit us very much. All the spiritual experiences and all the types that we have seen must become subjective and personal. If we talk about the all-inclusive Christ and these things are not our personal experience and enjoyment, then what we are talking about will be of little benefit to us; we run the risk of falling into dead religion. The objective riches of Christ must become the subjective fullness, and this fullness is the Body of Christ.
Our Christ is full of riches, but these riches have to be our subjective experience. God used both Brother Nee and Brother Lee to reveal so many riches of Christ to us. All of these riches are like a very delicious and nutritious plate of spiritual food. Before we eat this food, because it is outside of us, it is merely objective; it is not our personal experience. We can look at the food on our plate and say, "My, this food looks so good. I know this food is very nutritious." But if the food remains on our plate, it will not profit us. We have to take the food into us. In doing so, the food becomes our personal, subjective experience and enjoyment. Once we take the food into us, the food can be assimilated and digested to become a part of us. Christ is always rich, but we need to take in His riches so that they become our subjective experiences. This will produce the fullness of Christ, which is the Body of Christ, and this will fulfill the function of the Body.
The Body of Christ Being the Extension and Continuation of Christ on Earth
The Body of Christ is the extension and continuation of Christ on earth. First Timothy 3:16 says, "And confessedly, great is the mystery of godliness: / He who was manifested in the flesh, / Justified in the Spirit, / Seen by angels, / Preached among the nations, / Believed on in the world, / Taken up in glory," The mystery of godliness in this verse first refers to Christ the Head and then to the church His Body, the continuation of Christ on the earth. According to the actual historical sequence, Christ was taken up in glory (Acts I) before the preaching of Him began in Acts 2. Yet in this verse Paul mentions "taken up in glory" last; this may indicate not only the ascension of Christ but also the rapture of the church. Thus, there is a definite indication that 1 Timothy 3:16 refers not only to the Head as the manifestation of God in the flesh but also to the Body as the continuation of this manifestation (see Life-study of First Timothy, pp. 59-60). The Body of Christ is the extension and continuation of Christ on the earth as the mystery of godliness.
The Believers as Members of One Another Living Christ and Expressing Him Together
The purpose of us believers being members one of another in the Body of Christ is that we would live Christ and express Him together (Rom. 12:5). The function of the church is to express Christ. According to 1 Corinthians 14, if the church is in a normal condition, people should see Christ when they walk into a church meeting. They should be enlightened and convicted. They should fall on their face and worship God, declaring that indeed God is among you (vv. 24-25). This is the function of the church; therefore, if people today can walk into our meeting and not see Christ, then we must admit that we are not up to God's standard. If they only see a group of very zealous Christians or think we are a group of very religious people, then we have not yet fulfilled God's purpose. This principle is very important.
THE REALITY OF THE BODY OF CHRIST BEING A GROUP OF GOD'S REDEEMED WHO HAVE BEEN MADE GOD-MEN
The reality of the Body of Christ is a group of God's redeemed who have been made God, the God-men, by God and who live not by themselves but by another life, which is the processed and consummated Triune God (Gal. 2:20; Phil. l:19-21a; 3:10). The reality of the Body of Christ refers to what the Body of Christ really is. The reality of the Body of Christ is a group of God's redeemed people. This group of people has been made God, the God-men, by God. In other words, this group of people has been made God by God Himself. The reason that they can be made God is because they have believed into the Lord Jesus Christ and have been regenerated. They have received a new life into them, the divine life, the life of God. Because of this, they have God's life in them, and they can partake of God's divine nature. They have been made God in life and in nature. Of course, this does not mean that they are a part of the Godhead to be an object of worship.
We are God-men and have the life of God in us, yet we still have our old human life in us. The reality of the Body of Christ is not merely God's redeemed people. It is a group of God-men, who do not live according to their old life. They live by another life and not by themselves. They live by another life, which they received when they believed into the Lord and were regenerated. This life is the life of God, the processed and consummated Triune God. Hence, this life is a person.
There are many kinds of life in this universe. There is the vegetable life, the animal life, and the sea life. However, not all of these lives have personality. A tree can grow very tall and look very great. It definitely has life because it can grow and reproduce itself. But the tree has no personality; hence it is not a person. Regardless how big the tree grows, we do not fear it. If we kick it or rebuke it, the tree will not do anything in return; it is not a person. But our human life is not like this. Human life has personality. We have our mind, emotion, and will. The divine life is also a person. It is the processed, consummated Triune God. This divine life is not something apart from God. It is God Himself. Colossians 3:4 tells us clearly that Christ is our life. Today this Christ is the pneumatic Christ. He has gone through a process to become the life-giving Spirit (1 Cor. 15:45b; 2 Cor. 3:17). This Spirit is the processed, consummated Triune God. He is the One who is within us today. We need to live according to this life. If we do not do this, we do not have the reality of the Body.
The real meaning of the word church has been lost for a long time. Because of the degradation of Christianity, people do not know what the church is. In the past, many thought that the church was a physical building, such as a cathedral. Others thought that the church was a religious group. But at the beginning of the 1800s, the Lord raised up a group of brothers in England, and they saw many truths in the Bible. They saw that the Greek word for church is ekklesia, which means the assembly of the called-out ones. When we believed into the Lord, on one hand, we were regenerated, and on the other hand, the Lord called us out. We have been called out from this world. So we are all God's called-out ones. When all the called-out ones come together, that assembly of the called-out ones is the ekklesia, the church.
Seeing this was a great improvement and advancement over the past concept. But through Brother ]Lee's ministry, we have realized that the church as the assembly of God's called-out ones is only the outward structure, the physical frame. The church as the assembly does not touch the inward reality of the Body of Christ. The inward reality is the Triune God.
An egg with its shell is a very good illustration of the inward reality versus the physical frame. Suppose you extracted all of the egg white and egg yoke out of an egg through a small hole. The empty eggshell would be exactly the same as a real egg in appearance, but the empty eggshell would not have the organic substance inside. It would have no egg yolk and no egg white. If you were to wash the empty eggshell, it could even look better than the real egg. But if the eggshell were placed underneath a hen to hatch it, nothing would happen. The hen could sit on it for days, weeks, or even years, and nothing would happen. The real egg may not look as good as the empty eggshell; but if it were placed underneath a hen, after only a few days something would happen. There would be a change in life, and eventually a chick would be produced.
The egg cannot exist without the eggshell, yet the eggshell is not the reality of the egg. The organic substance—the egg white and the egg yolk—is the reality of the egg. The shell, the physical frame, is not the reality. It is the same with the church. Indeed, we are God's called-out ones, but to be God's called-out ones is only the outward structure, the physical frame. The inward reality of the Body of Christ is actually the Triune God—the Father, the Son, and the Spirit. The Bible tells us clearly that God the Father is in us, God the Son is in us, and God the Spirit is in us (Eph. 4:6; Rom. 8:9, 10). But when we check with our experience, we know that we do not have three Gods in us. We have only one God in us, who is the Triune God. This is the reality of the Body of Christ.
This matter of the reality of the Body of Christ is a heavy burden because many people still speak about the church according to its outward structure or physical frame. They may say, "Why do you say that only you are the church? Why don't you say that we are the church? Isn't it true that we are God's called-out ones?" Yes, we are all God's called-out ones, but by what life do we live? Do we live according to the divine life, or do we still live according to the human life? If we live according to the human life, we are still real believers, but we are like the empty eggshell without the inward reality.
We may love the Lord and gather as the church, but if we do not live according to the divine life, nothing will happen; we will remain the same even after five or ten years. But if we learn to turn to our spirit and live according to the divine life, things will be different. Today, when we look at our condition, we may feel that we have no hope. But if we live according to the divine life—the processed, consummated Triune God, who is the pneumatic Christ—we will be changed. This is our hope. Colossians 1:27 says, "Christ in you, the hope of glory." It is absolutely a matter of what life we live by. We may feel that as long as we live according to the divine life once in a while, everything is wonderful. But we need to realize what the reality of the Body is. The reality of the Body is the Triune God inside of us. Outside of this we have no hope. If we do not live according to the Triune God, many years will go by, and we will remain in the same condition. May the Lord be merciful to us.
A Corporate Living by the Perfected God-men
The reality of the Body of Christ is a corporate living by the perfected God-men, who are genuine men but who do not live by their own life but by the life of the processed and consummated Triune God, whose attributes are expressed through their virtues (Phil. 1:19-2la). The reality of the Body of Christ is a corporate living. This corporate living is not only by the God-men but by the perfected God-men. We have all been saved and now we all are God-men. Initially, we were just babes—immature, not having been perfected. But we cannot remain in such a state. We all have to press on. We need to eat and drink the Lord. We need to feed upon Him and learn of Him. As we learn the truth and pursue after Him, we will grow into maturity. As perfected God-men we can have the reality of the Body of Christ.
A -Living by All the God-men, United, Mingled, and Constituted Together with God
The reality of the Body of Christ is a living by all the God-men, united, mingled, and constituted together with God by mingling humanity with divinity and mingling divinity with humanity. The reality of the Body of Christ is not only a corporate living but also a mingled living. It is a living together with God by mingling humanity with divinity and mingling divinity with humanity. The word mingling is used because this indicates that the process has not yet been completed. As long as we are living on this earth, the mingling process will continue. We have to live this kind of life all the time. Do not consider that we are already fully mingled with the Triune God. No, this mingling of humanity with divinity and divinity with humanity is an ongoing process, and it will last our entire life.
A Corporate Living of the Conformity to the Death of Christ through the Power of Resurrection
The reality of the Body of Christ is a corporate living of the conformity to the death of Christ through the power of resurrection (Phil. 3:10). In order to live according to this divine life and not according to our old life, we have to be conformed to the death of Christ. We have to put ourselves into the mold of the death of Christ through the power of resurrection. We ourselves cannot be conformed to the death of Christ. It is possible only by the power of resurrection. If we really want to live this kind of life, then we have to have this kind of corporate living, that is, a living of the conformity to the death of Christ.
THE BODY OF CHRIST BEING IN THE RESURRECTION LIFE OF CHRIST
The Reality of the Body of Christ Requiring the Believers to Be Absolutely in the Resurrection Life of Christ
The Body of Christ is in the resurrection life of Christ (John 11:25; Acts 2:24; Rev. 1:18; 2:8; Phil. 3:10). The reality of the Body of Christ requires the believers to be absolutely in the resurrection life of Christ. Everything is in the resurrection life of Christ. If we are not in the resurrection life of Christ, then everything we have previously mentioned is mere theory, and it will not be real to us. The reality of the Body of Christ requires the believers to be absolutely in the resurrection life of Christ.
Resurrection Meaning That Our Natural Life Is Crucified and That the God-created Part of Our Being Is Uplifted
To be in resurrection means that our natural life is crucified and that the God-created part of our being is uplifted in resurrection to be one with Christ in resurrection (Rom. 6:4-6). When Christ was crucified on the cross, all of us were included in Him. When Christ was crucified on the cross, we all were crossed out; therefore, we cannot live according to our old life anymore. We all must live according to God's divine life.
Terminating Us by the Cross of Christ and Resurrecting Us by His Spirit of Life
God's way of salvation is to terminate us by the cross of Christ and to resurrect us by His Spirit of life. The experience of this is altogether in the Spirit. Many Christians think that the cross means suffering. Whenever the cross is mentioned, they think about suffering. Actually, the real meaning of the cross is not suffering but death. If someone were to put you on the cross, you would certainly know that his intention was to kill you. His intention would not be to make you suffer. The real meaning of the cross is death. Galatians 2:20 says, "I am crucified with Christ; and it is no longer I who live, but it is Christ who lives in me." This verse clearly shows us what it means to be crucified with Christ: "It is no longer I who live, but it is Christ who lives in me."
The Lord may allow some suffering in our life, or He may put us in a difficult situation. But to be in a suffering situation does not necessarily mean that we have the experience of the cross. It depends upon what life we are living. Can you say, "It is no longer I who live, but it is Christ who lives in me"? If in the suffering situation we let Christ live in us, then we definitely have the experience of the cross. However, if in the suffering situation we murmur, complain, fight back, and question the Lord, then we are still living according to our old life. All the suffering in that situation is in vain. Thus, we can only have the experience of the cross in the Spirit.
Experiencing Crucifixion, Burial, and Resurrection with Christ by Being in Him
We do not experience crucifixion, burial, and resurrection alone, but experience them with Christ by being in Him (Rom. 6:4-6; Gal. 2:20a; Eph. 2:6). Christ has already experienced crucifixion, burial, and resurrection. He has gone through the process. Therefore, all these matters are the ingredients of the all-inclusive Spirit. It is only when we live according to this Spirit and drink of this Spirit that we can have this experience. Do not try to crucify yourself. None of us can crucify ourselves, and we do not need to crucify ourselves. Christ has done everything. All we need to do is get into Christ, abide in Christ, and remain in Christ. Then all that He has experienced will become our experience.
The Body of Christ Being the Issue of Not Living by Our Natural Life but by the Divine Life
When we live not by our natural life but by the divine life within us, we are in resurrection; the issue of this is the Body of Christ (Gal. 2:20a; Rom. 8:10, 6, 11; 12:4-5). How can the Body of Christ grow into this reality? It depends upon, what kind of life we live. It is based upon whether or not we have the reality within, whether we live according to our old life or according to the divine life. The issue of living by the divine life is the Body of Christ.
THE BODY OF CHRIST BEING A NEW CREATION
God Desiring to Produce a New Creation out of the Old Creation
The Body of Christ is a new creation (Gal. 6:15; 2 Cor. 5:17; Eph. 2:15; 4:24). God in His economy desires to produce a new creation out of the old creation; therefore, as believers we must be thoroughly and absolutely renewed so that we may be the new creation of God and for God (Rom. 12:2; 2 Cor. 4:16; Eph. 4:23).
The Old Creation Being Our Old Man in Adam, and the New Creation Being the New Man in Christ
The old creation is our old man in Adam, our natural being by birth, without God's life and nature (Eph. 4:22). The new creation is the new man in Christ, our being that is regenerated by the Spirit, having God's life and nature wrought into it, having Christ as its constituent, and having become a new constitution (Eph. 4:24; John 3:6, 36; 2 Pet. 1:4; Col. 3:10-11). The Body of Christ is a new creation. The old creation does not have a place in it. In the Body of Christ nothing old can remain. The old creation cannot be a part of the Body of Christ. However, God will produce a new creation out of the old creation. The old creation is our old man in Adam. Adam fell because he did not receive the tree of life into him. As a result, the whole human race fell and became the old creation. But God did not give up man. He did not say, "Forget about it. You are rotten; therefore, you are finished. I will create something else. Let Me create another living creature, and let this creature help Me accomplish My purpose." God did not do this. On the one hand, the old creation is useless concerning God's economy and purpose, but on the other hand, God did not give up on the human race. Rather, He wants to redeem the old creation and produce a new creation out of the old creation.
Although the old creation in itself cannot help God's economy, He wants to produce a new creation out of the old creation. God will not produce a new creation out of nothing. He wants to take the redeemed people out of the old creation and add the divine element into them, making them the new creation. This means that regardless of man's fall, God still loves man. God did not give up on us. Many times I have given up on myself. When I looked at myself, it seemed that I was hopeless and of no use. But then I realized that God had not given up on me. He also has not given up on you. We might consider, "Lord, how could You have chosen me? If I were You, I would have given up on me a long time ago." We may look at the other saints and think, "My, how hopeless we are." But God does not give up. God has accomplished His judicial redemption, and He is now carrying out His organic salvation. He keeps adding and dispensing His divine element into us. He wants to save us. This is our hope.
The old creation in itself is of no use. In the past I have known some sisters who were very good at playing the piano. They were very skillful and could play very difficult pieces. When they came to the meeting they thought that playing the hymns would be no problem. But when they began to play, they were not able to do it. Their playing killed the meeting. However, they should not say, "Forget it. I am useless." God still wants to use them, but not in their old way, which is according to their natural life. They need to go to the Lord and say, "I cannot do it. I want to receive more of You." When they cry out to the Lord, God will dispense more of Himself into them. Then when they play the piano, they will not just play with their skill; they will play with their spirit and supply life to the meeting.
The New Creation Being Accomplished by the Old Creation Passing through Death and Resurrection
The new creation is accomplished by the old creation passing through death and resurrection. We have to pass through death and resurrection. When we put our skillful old man aside, turn to the Lord, and live according to the divine life, we experience death and resurrection. Then we can be in the new creation.
The Body of Christ Having Nothing to Do with the Old Creation
The Body of Christ has nothing to do with the old creation and has no use for anything from the old creation but takes only that which issues from Christ.
Being Transferred from the Realm of the Old Creation into the Realm of the New Creation
We need to be transferred from the realm of the old creation into the realm of the new creation to be the new man to fulfill God's eternal purpose—to consummate the New Jerusalem (2 Cor. 5:17; Eph. 4:24; Col. 3:10; Rev. 21:2).
THE WORK OF THE CROSS CONSUMMATING WITH THE BODY
The work of the cross consummates with the Body and ushers us into the Body (Eph. 2:16). The work of the cross does not consummate in spiritual giants. Some people want to experience the cross only because they think that afterwards they will become very spiritual. They desire to be a spiritual giant. We must forget about this. The more we experience the cross, the more we have the Body. The goal of the cross is to produce the Body, not to produce spiritual giants. The real work of the cross will usher us into the Body.
The Work of the Cross Going as Far as the Body of Christ and Consummating with the Body
The work of the cross goes as far as the Body of Christ and consummates with the Body. The knowledge of the cross brings us to the knowledge of the Body of Christ. Anything that is not a part of Christ frustrates our inward knowledge of the Body of Christ. If we have the real knowledge of the cross from our experience and not from our mental understanding only, then we can see the Body. But if we neglect this kind of experiential knowing of the cross, it will be very difficult to know the Body.
The Cross Leading Us to the Body, and Our Self-life Having to Be Utterly Dealt with by the Cross
The cross leads us to the Body, and the cross operates in the sphere of the Body. Only when our self-life has been utterly dealt with by the cross are we able to touch the reality of the life of the Body and come to know the Body (Matt. 16:24-25; Rom. 8:13; 12:4-5). To enter into the Body involves spiritual warfare; hence, we must continually contact the Lord by prayer in order that we may enter into and know the reality of the Body of Christ.—B. C.

Message Four
The Principles of the Body (3)

IX.　If we would understand and enter into the life of the Body, we must know the authority of the Head—Col. 2:19; Eph. 4:15; 5:23; 1:22, 10:
A.　Only Christ is the Head, and only He has the authority to direct the moves of the members of the Body:
1.　For Christ to be the Head means that He has the authority in the Body—Col. 1:18; 2:19.
2.　If we desire to live in the Body, we must learn to submit to the authority of Christ—Matt. 28:18-20.
B.　If we would have the Body life, whatever we think, feel, and do must be under the control of the Head:
3.　We should take the Head as our life and as the center of our whole being—Col. 3:4, 11.
4.　Like Paul, we should take care of the Body of Christ by taking the feeling of the Head as our own feeling—Phil. 1:8.
5.　If we as members have the feeling of the Head in everything and care for the Body, we will take the Body as the rule in our mind, thoughts, words, and actions—Col. 3:15-16.
X.　Spirituality is a Body matter—Eph. 4:16:
A.　According to the revelation of the New Testament, a person who truly touches God's heart and is an overcomer in the eyes of God is one who is living in the Body and practicing the Body—Rom. 12:4-5:
1.　He is not spiritual individually and is not overcoming individually.
2.　He understands clearly that he is a member of the Body, not a complete unit.
B.　God is not after a group of spiritual or sinless people—He is after a Body—Eph. 2:16:
1.　The most important thing is whether or not we are living in the Body.
2.　In order to seek after spirituality, we must be in the Body:
a.　God never intended that we would be individually spiritual.
b.　Individual spirituality spoils and damages the building of the church.
c.　If we see that God's ultimate intention is to have the Body as a corporate vessel to contain Christ, we will pray that the Lord will save us from our individual spirituality.

1.　If we have seen the Body, we will realize that we cannot live without the Body, that we cannot serve without the Body, and that we cannot have a spiritual life outside the Body.

a.　As soon as we see the Body, our life and work as individuals cease.
b.　Everything we have is in the Body, through the Body, and for the Body; nothing is for the individual.
XI.　We need to care for the feeling of the Body and the peace of the Body—Eph. 4:3; Col. 3:15; Eph. 2:14-16:
A.　Since we are members of the Body of Christ, we should have a feeling for the Body.
B.　In order for us to have the Body life, we must care for our fellow members and must be full of feeling for the Body.
C.　We need to realize that whatever we do involves the Body, and we should not do anything without taking care of the Body.
D.　The feeling for the Body is very precious, and it is closely related to one's frame of mind, that is, to one's perception of things—Rom. 12:3:
1.　If we do not have an adequate understanding of the Body of Christ, our frame of mind is not proper, and we will not be able to have the common recognition of the Body of Christ
2.　If we would be suitable for the building up of the Body of Christ and have the proper feeling for the Body of Christ, we need to be transformed by the renewing of the mind—Rom. 12:2, 5.
XII.　We need to have the consciousness of the Body, a Body- consciousness—1 Cor. 12:26-27; Rom. 12:5; Eph. 4:16; 2:21-22:
A.　In order to live and move with other members of the Body, we need to have the consciousness of the Body.

C.　For the Lord's move in His recovery both locally and universally, we all need to be Body-conscious in one accord—Acts 1:14; 2:46; 4:24; 15:25; Rom. 15:6.

MESSAGE FOUR
THE PRINCIPLES OF THE BODY (3)

Prayer: Lord, we need You, and we need every member of Your Body. Apart from You and Your Body, we can do nothing, and anything we try to do is nothing. Lord, we thank and praise You that we are in You and in the Body. We open to the flow of life in Your Body. Lord, we ask You to grant us the vision we need to see the Body, the experiences we need to know the Body, and the understanding we need to keep every principle of the Body. Lord, we ask You to grant us the grace we need to live in the Body, through the Body, for the Body, in the fellowship of the Body, under the authority of the Body, under the protection of the Body, and according to the limitation of the Body. Lord, we rest in Your Body, receiving the supply from the Head for the Body. Lord, remember Your enemy. Speak a word to expose and shame him. Destroy all of his pernicious work. Undo all the damage he has done to Your Body. Recover all the ground that he has usurped in us. Smite his head, and put him to an open shame. Lord, gain a living, functioning, organic Body on the earth today. O Lord, do this for Your glory. Amen.
SEEING AND KNOWING THE BODY
The mystical, organic Body of Christ is in the divine and mystical realm; hence, it is a deep and profound matter. Seeing the Body is a matter of having a vision. It is a matter of having veils removed, of having the light shine and penetrate our being, and of having our sight restored that we may see. Actually, we can see the Body in revelation early in our Christian life as indicated by Paul's experience on the road to Damascus. But to see the Body in a vision is one thing, and to know the Body intrinsically in life is another matter. Knowing the Body is a matter of the experience of life. It is not a matter in the elementary stage, but in the matured stage. We need many experiences under the operation of the cross, in the all-inclusive compound Spirit, and in the practical church life that we may gradually come to know the Body. The Body is essentially a subjective reality in life. The church is objective, an assembly; hence, it has a necessary physical and visible aspect. But the Body is invisible and mystical. Thus, to know the Body is to know something deep, intrinsic, and inward in life.
LIVING IN THE BODY—A MATTER OF LIFE, NOT OF BEHAVIOR
The principles of the Body are matters of life. The functions of our physical body, such as breathing and the circulation of the blood, work according to organic principles. Many things happen because the parts of our body honor all the organic principles of the body. There is no deliberate or self-conscious effort in our body to behave, yet all the parts function according to the relevant principles in the body. It is the same with the Body of Christ. To memorize all the principles being shared and then make a decision that we will live in the Body using these principles as ethical precepts for our behavior is inadequate. Instead, we simply need to live in the Body of Christ. We have to be brought to the point where we truly cease from our individual effort and live the life of the Body.
THE VISION OF THE BODY VERSUS THE VISION OF THE SELF
In regard to the divine reality, there are factors that oppose each other. The world is versus the Father; the devil, Satan, is versus Christ; the flesh is versus the spirit; and the individualistic self is versus the Body. Where the self is, the Body cannot be; and where the Body is, the self cannot be. The growth, the transformation, and the overcoming life should not be for the self. We need the light, the discipline, the dealing, and the growth to realize that nothing is for the self in God's economy; everything is for the Body, the Body is for Christ, and Christ is for God.
In the book entitled The Heavenly Vision Brother Lee speaks of the vision of the Body in relation to the vision of the self (pp. 25-47). Where there is no vision of the self, there is no vision of the Body. Where there is no vision of the Body, there is no exposure of the self. The self is the enemy of the Body and must be broken under the vision of the Body. The self must be abandoned, renounced, and denied for the Body. Throughout the centuries, the Body of Christ has not been in its manifest reality because the self in many of God's children has remained unbroken, undealt with, and undenied. We believe that both Brother Nee and Brother Lee had such a profound, inner knowing of the Body because in them the self had been broken, denied, repudiated, and lost.
OPENING AND HUMBLING OURSELVES TO SEE THE BODY
We are blessed if we are not self-deceived and do not pretend that we see more than we actually see. In this matter, it is better to be like Mary the mother of Jesus when the angel Gabriel came to her and said, "Behold, you will conceive in your womb and bear a son, and you shall call His name Jesus" (Luke 1:31). She responded by saying, "How will this be?" (v. 34). The angel said to her, "No word will be impossible with God" (v. 37). Mary then opened herself and humbled herself, saying, "Behold, the slave of the Lord. May it happen to me according to your word" (v. 38). She did not have the capacity to understand at that time what was happening in her. She could not understand that God, the very Triune God, was being begotten of the Spirit in her, that He would reside in her in a developmental stage for nine months, and that He would be born as a God-man. It is no wonder that she pondered these things in her heart (2:19). All she knew was that the speaking of the angel was from God.
Today, in a sense, we should be a "corporate Mary," not to produce the physical body of Jesus but to realize the growth, development, and building up of the organic Body of Christ. Many of us can pray, saying, "Lord, I am not sure if I know the Body. But the words that I read ring true in my being, and I open to them and receive them. I like to be poor in spirit and pure in heart. I like to be a learner, learning ofYou as the Head of the Body. I want to see what the Body is." We also can say, "Lord, may it be done unto us according to Your word. We desire that every principle of the Body be wrought into and worked out of us so that the Body can be manifested on the earth in our lifetime."
KNOWING THE AUTHORITY OF THE HEAD
If we would understand and enter into the life of the Body, we must know the authority of the Head (Col. 2:19; Eph. 4:15; 5:23; 1:22, 10). The word know implies much inward, subjective, and experiential realization. It is possible to have a natural, organizational, hierarchical, and even totalitarian concept of authority. We may confuse these concepts with what the Bible says concerning the headship of Christ and His authority. It is possible, therefore, to be in a situation where we obey but do not submit. We may dutifully and loyally obey the commands, but we may not know what it is to submit to the wish and desire proceeding out of the heart of God.
The headship and authority of Christ in the Body are a matter of life. They are inward and subjective. To know them requires an inward, intrinsic, and experiential knowing. There is a dispute in the universe. The universe is not a quiet place. This dispute is concerning authority, concerning who will exercise this authority, and concerning how God's authority will be secured. In the book Authority and Submission, Brother Nee says, "The center of dispute in the whole universe hinges on who has the authority. We have to contend with Satan by asserting that authority is with God. We have to set ourselves to submit to God's authority and to uphold God's authority. We must meet God's authority face-to-face and have a basic realization of it" (p. 9). The place to meet authority is the Body of Christ. Saul of Tarsus met this authority soon after he was saved. When he was converted, the Lord said, "Rise up and enter into the city, and it will be told to you what you must do" (Acts 9:6). Saul met the Head and the authority of the Head directly. He then had to meet God's authority through a member of the Body. The Head did not send Peter, John, or James to Saul of Tarsus; He sent Ananias, a small member of the Body. As Saul was praying and contacting the Head (v. II), Ananias also fellow-shipped with the Lord (vv. 13-17).
The word concerning a "chosen vessel" was spoken first to Ananias, not to Saul of Tarsus. This indicates that the Body may be clearer about you than you are about yourself. It is a great peril to insist on your own assessment of yourself and to repudiate the Body's assessment. The Body's understanding of what you are is the Head's awareness infused into the Body. The Lord told Ananias, "Go, for this man is a chosen vessel to Me, to bear My name before both the Gentiles and kings and the sons of Israel; for I will show him how many things he must suffer on behalf of My name" (vv. 15-16). Ananias laid his hands on Saul, and through this small member, he received his sight (vv. 17-18). Many today are blind like Saul, and they will stay blind until they humble themselves under the touch of an ordinary member of the Body.
Today, God is involved in a work to settle the dispute concerning authority. His work involves the heading up of all things in Christ. Ephesians 1 says, "Unto the economy of the fullness of the times, to head up all things in Christ, the things in the heavens and the things on the earth, in Him" (v. 10). Today, there is a heap of collapse, darkness, chaos, and insubordination. But in eternity, all these things will be eliminated. Everything in the universe will be headed up. God accomplishes this work of heading up all things in Christ in three steps. In the first step, God became a man named Jesus. This man Jesus took God as His Head, submitting to God the Father as His Head absolutely (1 Cor. 11:3). There was a man on this earth who never disobeyed, never had a feeling contrary to the Father, never had a thought or opinion at odds with God the Father, never had a motive or intention different from the Father's, and never said a word that was incompatible with the Father's feeling. What a wonderful God-man! This man lived on this rebellious earth in absolute submission to the Father. Before He died, He said, "For the ruler of the world is coming, and in Me he has nothing; but this is so that the world may know that I love the Father, and as the Father commanded Me, so I do" (John 14:30-31). He loved the Father, and the Father wanted Him to die. So He was willing to die out of love for Him. He willingly obeyed the Father and was subject to Him unto death.
In the second step of accomplishing His work of heading up all things in Christ, God highly exalted Christ and gave Him a name above every name (Phil. 2:9), making Him the Head over all things to the church (Eph. 1:22). He is now the Head of the Body, the church (Col. 1:18). The third step is that we, as His Body, must be headed up under Christ as the Head. The destiny of the universe hinges on the church being headed up organically under Christ as the Head. When the Lord comes back, the kingdom of the world will "become the kingdom of our Lord and of His Christ, and He will reign forever and ever" (Rev. 11:15). However, the church as the Body of Christ must be headed up in this age, before the universe and the kingdom of this world can be headed up, and before the great image in Daniel 2 can be smashed into pieces (v. 34). The problems in the universe concerning rebellion and chaos will be solved if we can be headed up, not according to organization but according to God's economy.
In the physical body, the head does not give commands in a cold, mechanical way. The head does not say to the right foot, "Move!" and the right foot does not say, "Yes sir! I will move!" Such a concept is a human notion of governing. The headship in the physical body is not in this way. Instead, the head has an inclination or a feeling, and the body responds effortlessly and spontaneously. We need to know the difference between obeying the Lord's command and submitting to His will. A command is a word spoken that elicits objective obedience and compliance. It is good to obey the Lord, and we should also obey the leading ones. But it is possible to obey day and night and still not be living in the Body. Brothers and sisters may be very obedient and compliant in the church, but inwardly, intrinsically, and organically they may not know the Head. The Lord may have a wish or a desire, and they may not even have the consciousness of it. However, little by little the Lord is gaining some of His members who detect the Lord's thought through His inclination or longing. They respond unconsciously, not as deliberate or calculated behavior, but as a life response. In the Body of Christ we have a beautiful, organic harmony of authority and submission. In the physical realm, the human body is the clearest picture of this authority and submission. The order in a family and a king with his subjects are incomplete pictures. The Body of Christ is an organism in which all the members respond immediately and organically to the Head. We need to know the authority of the Head in an organic way. It is so sweet, lovely, and harmonious to be under the Lord's leading.
Only Christ the Head Having the Authority to Direct the Moves of the Members of the Body
Only Christ is the Head, and only He has the authority to direct the moves of the members of the Body. This needs to be a settled matter. Colossians 2:19 speaks of holding the Head. This means that Christ is the unique authority in the Body and that we are absolutely under His headship. Only the Head has the authority to direct the moves of the members of His Body. In the physical body every member of the body is directly related to the head. There is no hierarchical relationship in which the toes have to consult with the feet, ankles, and legs before moving. On one hand, if Christ the Head wants the "toes" to move, He indicates that the "toes" should move. On the other hand, if Christ the Head tells a "leg" to move, the "toes" should not say, "We did not hear anything from the Lord. We are not moving until we hear from Him." As "toes" we should realize that we are attached to a moving member of the Body. This is the reality of the Body we desire to see on the earth—a Body that does whatever the Head wants to do. If I am a thumb and the Head tells me to move, I move. If the Head tells the arm to move, I move with the arm. This is so normal. In the central nervous system of the human body, every member is directly related to the head, and the head directs the moves of the members of the body.
The Head decides the pathway, the course, of every member of the Body of Christ, not the members. There is no ground for our choice. None of the members of our physical body has the standing to make a decision regarding its function or its course. This should be a reminder to us that although we are related to one another in the Body (and we will be for eternity), our ultimate relationship is to the Head. If our Head, perhaps for reasons known only to Him, would take a member to Him, we simply say amen, even though it may be with tears. The Head has the right to direct the pathway of every member. Brother Nee knew the Body to such an extent that he could testify that we do not have the right even to think or desire anything contrary to what the Lord wants.
In the Body of Christ there are no direct relationships among the members. A natural or special fondness or preference between two members will damage the Body. We need to consider all of our relationships and learn to relate to one another through the Head. On the one hand, if in my soul I want to be close to someone and the Head did not establish that relationship, the cross will work. On the other hand, if I do not like a person in a natural way, the Head may put me together with that person to labor. In such circumstances, I have no choice. Divisions, parties, and sects develop in the church when the members relate to each other directly apart from the Head.
The Body is a reality all of the time. Even the longest church meetings, such as the one that Paul had when he spoke all night until daybreak (Acts 20:7-11), are dismissed. The Body, however, is never dismissed; it exists twenty-four hours a day. Suppose the lungs would say, "We are tired. Give us a short break of seven minutes," or suppose the circulatory system would say, "I am tired as well. I would like to stop flowing for a few minutes." If this happened, the body would die. In like manner, the Body of Christ is a glorious "prison" for us. We cannot go anywhere or do anything at any time without affecting and being affected by the Body of which we are members. We should learn, for example, to relate to our husband or wife through the Head. Such a relationship will be wonderfully divine, mystical, human, and in resurrection. God Himself will be our love for our wives because our love will have passed through Christ the Head.
Christ as the Head Having the Authority in the Body
For Christ to be the Head means that He has the authority in the Body (Col. 1:18; 2:19). At this juncture, I would like to present a few practical matters concerning the authority of Christ in the Body. In interpreting the Bible we must learn to speak and teach the same thing. Brother Lee said, "We must enter into the intrinsic significance of the holy Scriptures to find out what the Lord says and what He wants. We have no right to invent anything. Rather, we discover things in the Bible" (The Problems Causing the Turmoils in the Church Life, p. 38). Ephesians 6 says that we need to put on the helmet of salvation before we take up the sword of the Spirit, which Spirit is the word of God (v. 17). We may have had the experience while reading the Bible of our mind being under assault, distracted, or having peculiar notions coming into it. We need to read and study the Bible under the headship of Christ. Brother Nee said, "If we hold the Head, we cannot have different interpretations of Scripture. Differences arise when someone is not holding the Head" (The Mystery of Christ, pp. 34-35). It is not a small thing to teach differently from the apostles' teaching that we have received. We may want to spin or rephrase the teaching in a different way. The source of our spin or teaching could be a "head" other than Christ, and such different speaking damages the Body of Christ. Paul told the Philippians to "think the same thing" (Phil. 2:2) and the Corinthians to "speak the same thing" (1 Cor. 1:10). He also told Timothy to "charge certain ones not to teach different things" (1 Tim. 1:3). This means that we must not have a different stress or a different emphasis from the New Testament economy of God. In 1984 Brother Lee seriously inquired of the Lord as to what stress his ministry should have (see The Ministry, Vol. 3, No. 3, p. 25). Today we are endeavoring to have the same stress as our brother.
The Members Learning to Submit to the Authority of Christ
If we desire to live in the Body, we must learn to submit to the authority of Christ (Matt. 28:18-20). We move not merely in obedience but in a subjective submission to the prompting of the Head. We must also be aware of another snare of the enemy. Some saints will mistake their psychological impulses for the prompting of the Head. This indicates that the self and its subjectivity must be crossed out. We are often full of our own thoughts and feelings. The Spirit may prompt us, but our emotion or mind may go off on its own way. We may think that we are being spontaneous, but it may be in a fleshy way. We may assume that something is the leading of the Head when it may simply be a psychological impulse. It is a fact that the Head directs the members of the Body, but we need to be delivered from our natural subjectivity. In the New Testament Paul tells us to be careful how we teach and speak (1 Tim. 1:3-4; Titus 2:1). In Mark 4:24, the Lord says, "Take heed what you hear," and in Luke 8:18, He says, "Take heed therefore how you hear." What we hear and How we hear may be a product of our subjectivity. Therefore, we need to learn and have the experiences of the self being broken and crossed out. Then we can tell the Lord, "I live for Your wish. I live for Your thought, Your idea. I do not ask merely for an outward objective direction. I desire to touch Your feeling." It is sweet when in such matters as migration, we touch the feeling, the longing, and the burden of the Head.
Whatever We Think, Feel, and Do Being under the Control of the Head
If we would have the Body life, whatever we think, feel, and do must be under the control of the Head. We cannot have a stable church life if the responsible brothers live according to their mood. To a subjective person, reality is determined by how he feels. But we must care about how the Lord feels and how the Body feels. Paul said, "I long after you all in the inward parts of Christ Jesus" (Phil. 1:8). Paul did not hold on to his own view or feelings; instead, the Lord's inward parts, feelings, and thoughts for the Body became his own tender feelings. The Body is a reality twenty-four hours a day. Today there may be pain or an attack of death somewhere in the Body. There may be no way to outwardly communicate it. The question is whether anyone will feel it, sense it, and respond to it. We may pray, "Lord, I have a certain feeling. What is it? I come to You, Lord." We may then have the burden to pray about a particular situation. The Body is intrinsic and subjective. The Body involves our inward parts being filled with the feelings and thoughts of Christ for His Body. Who among us is willing to be touched to the core of their being? We all are men of flesh with our own natural feelings about various things. But for the sake of His Body, the Lord must deal with everything that would hinder the expression of the Head toward His Body.
Taking the Head as Our Life and as the Center of Our Whole Being and Taking the Feeling of the Head as Our Feeling
We should take the Head as our life and as the center of our whole being (Col. 3:4,11). Like Paul, we should take care of the Body of Christ by taking the feeling of the Head as our own feeling (Phil. 1:8). Romans 12:15 says, "Rejoice with those who rejoice; weep with those who weep." We may have no particular reason to weep, but the Body is weeping. Will we be open for the feeling of the Body to become our feeling? Personally, we may be unhappy, but there may be a cause for rejoicing in the Body of Christ. Would we insist on remaining in our unhappy feeling? The Body has much to do with our feeling. First Corinthians 12:26 says that if "one member suffers, all the members suffer with it." This verse does not say that all the members should suffer as a matter of behavior. If a heavy object is dropped on my toe, my entire body feels the impact of the pain. In like manner, when some rebellious ones caused anguish in a certain church a number of years ago, the entire Body—not that locality alone—felt the pain. The reason for this is that the Body is one.
The Lord is going to gain a group of people who are really open to Him. If we are not willing be that people, He will find another people within the scope of His recovery who will abandon their inner being to Him. They will live this way and be willing to let go of their own subjective feelings, thoughts, and views, and allow Christ the Head to live in them and to infuse His feelings, thoughts, and views into them. We have been in the self, in "solitary confinement," too long. Outside of ourselves, there is a realm where, on one hand, we suffer more because we are conscious of the need, but on the other hand, we are supplied beyond our highest expectations because life flows into us from the Body. The Body is a wonderful realm. The realm of the Body changes our feeling and consciousness. We become burdened for things that seemingly make no sense in our own personal life or in our place, but make a lot of sense when we discover what is taking place in other localities.
Taking the Feeling of the Head in Everything and Taking the Body as the Rule in Our Mind, Thoughts, Words, and Actions
If we as members have the feeling of the Head in everything and care for the Body, we will take the Body as the rule in our mind, thoughts, words, and actions. Colossians 3:15-16 says, "Let the peace of Christ arbitrate in your hearts, to which also you were called in one Body; and be thankful. Let the word of Christ dwell in you richly in all wisdom, teaching and admonishing one another with psalms and hymns and spiritual songs, singing with grace in your hearts to God." We should let the peace of Christ, which is Christ Himself, arbitrate in our hearts for the Body life. For the proper Body life we need the peace of Christ to arbitrate, to adjust, and to decide all things in our heart in our relationship with the members of His Body.
SPIRITUALITY BEING A BODY MATTER
Spirituality is a Body matter (Eph. 4:16). If an individual member develops abnormally relative to the rest of the body, that is grotesque. We want to grow in life, overcome, and be spiritual in proportion to the Body. The day will come when, from the depths of our spirit and heart, we will pray, "Lord, I want to be a spiritual person for the Body of Christ. I do not want to overdevelop or be a cancer. I want to be a normal member of the Body."
Overcomers Being Those Who Live in the Body and Practice the Body
According to the revelation of the New Testament, a person who truly touches God's heart and is an overcomer in the eyes of God is one who is living in the Body and practicing the Body (Rom. 12:4-5). Overcomers are for the building up of the Body of Christ. Such people are not spiritual individually or overcoming individually. They understand clearly that they are members of the Body and not complete units in themselves. Seeing that we are believers, children of God, and sons of God may allow us to retain a notion of individual completeness. If we see the Body, we see that we are members and that by ourselves we are not organically and intrinsically complete units. We may not know which member of the Body we are, but seeing that we are members changes our life. We can no longer live without the Body or without its fellowship. A member that is beautiful as a part of the Body is ugly if it is detached from the Body. Oh, may the light break upon us to show us that we are members! Then we can really be at peace, because we do not have to be the whole Body.
The Desire of God for the Body
God is not after a group of spiritual or sinless people—He is after a Body (Eph. 2:16). We may be concerned about being sinless and spiritual, but God desires to have the Body. The most important thing is whether or not we are living in the Body. In order to seek after spirituality, we must be in the Body. God never intended that we should be individually spiritual. Individual spirituality spoils and damages the building of the church. The Lord needs to write on our inner being that spirituality is a matter of the Body. We need to read the Bible, consecrate ourselves, be broken, grow, be transformed, and overcome for the Body. We need to speak in faith, saying, "My decision is to live as a member of the Body for the Body. I want to grow in life for the Body and in the Body."
If we see that God's ultimate intention is to have the Body as a corporate vessel to contain Christ, we will pray that the Lord will save us from our individual spirituality. If we see this and are concerned for our inclination toward individual spirituality, we will pray for our protection, saying, "Lord, deliver me from individualistic spirituality."
The Christian Living Being Corporate and of the Body
The Christian living is corporate and of the Body; it is not individualistic (Eph. 3:6). If we have seen the Body, we will realize that we cannot live without the Body, that we cannot serve without the Body, and that we cannot have a spiritual life outside of the Body. To realize this is a conscious, inward matter. If we see the Body, our realization will change. Our answer to the following question is an indication of whether or not we have seen the Body: Do we fellowship because that is the "protocol" or proper "behavior" in the church, or do we fellowship because we realize that we cannot live without it? Sooner or later we will realize our need of the Body and say, "I am defeated by this circumstance because I am independent. I cannot handle this family matter on my owh. I cannot take care of this personal failure alone. I need the Body. I need someone to pray with me and for me. I cannot live alone anymore." We cannot live without the Body. Even if we go to a remote part of the earth, we must realize that we are still in the Body and that we cannot live without the Body. If we see the Body, we will be different, and our fellowship will not be a matter of behavior but a matter of life or death.
The strongest proof that we have seen the Body is that we can no longer be individualistic (1 Cor. 12:12,14, 21-22). As soon as we see the Body, our life and work as individuals cease. Everything we have is in the Body, through the Body, and for the Body; nothing is for the individual.
CARING FOR THE FEELING AND PEACE OF THE BODY
We need to care for the feeling of the Body and the peace of the Body (Eph. 4:3; Col. 3:15; Eph. 2:14-16). We may have peace, but this peace could be self-made or counterfeit. We need, therefore, to check the feeling of the Body. If we are not open to how the Body feels or we are afraid to have our feeling tested by the feeling of the Body, this is a sign that we are not one with the Lord in that matter. If it is genuinely of the Lord, we should be willing to let the Lord work it out through the Body. Sometimes the Body contradicts our personal feeling. The Lord may say, "It is not a matter of right or wrong. Just be one with the brothers." The Body's feeling is the Lord's feeling. We can never prosper if we disregard it.
Since we are members of the Body of Christ, we should have a feeling for the Body. In order for us to have the Body life, we must care for our fellow members and must be full of feeling for the Body. Paul's word about the inward parts (Phil. 1:8) is very crucial, because it is full of feeling for the Body. We need to realize that whatever we do involves the Body, and we should not do anything without taking care of the Body. Many times we are not aware of this principle, and we do or say things not caring for the Body. The person we marry, the job we have, the house we buy, what we do in our leisure time, what we do alone, or what we do on vacation when no one can see us are all Body matters. Whether we are near or far, we are still in the Body, and all these things affect the Body.
The Feeling for the Body Being Very Precious
The feeling for the Body is very precious, and it is closely related to one's frame of mind, that is, to one's perception of things (Rom. 12:3). We may be very subjective and have our own view and feeling about things. Yet the Body may have a different view. If we insist on our view, this may indicate that our frame of mind is warped. The Bible tells us that we need to be renewed in the spirit of our mind for the Body life (Eph. 4:23-24). We need the renewing of the mind to not think of ourselves more highly than we ought to think for the Body life (Rom. 12:2-3). If my left hand considered that it was an eye, a tongue, a thigh, a shoulder, and an ear, and if it attempted to function as all these parts, we would realize that there was some irrationality in the thinking of that member.
If we do not have an adequate understanding of the Body of Christ, our frame of mind is not proper, and we will not be able to have the common recognition of the Body of Christ (Eph. 4:22-24). Knowing the Body involves the way we think and perceive things. This is why we need fellowship. It is so good to have fellowship. In the fellowship we express our thought honestly and then let it go. Eventually, in the fellowship a common mind emerges and we take it. But when we are subjective, we never inwardly abandon our view, and practically we are not in the Body. If we would be suitable for the building up of the Body of Christ and have the proper feeling for the Body of Christ, we need to be transformed by the renewing of the mind (Rom. 12:2, 5).
THE CONSCIOUSNESS OF THE BODY
Living and Moving with Other Members of the Body by Having a Consciousness of the Body
We need to have the consciousness of the Body, a Body-consciousness (1 Cor. 12:26-27; Rom. 12:15; Eph. 4:16; 2:21-22). One definition of the word consciousness is "a special awareness or sensitivity." We should have a special awareness or sensitivity to the feeling of the Body. Another relevant definition of the word consciousness is "an alertness to or concern for a particular situation." This means that we are sensitive, aware, alert, and concerned. In The Mystery of Christ, Brother Nee uses the illustration of bees and butterflies (p. 18):
In the church life, we should learn to have the consciousness of the Body....Some Christians are like butterflies; they act independently. Others are like bees; they live and move together. The butterfly flies from flower to flower, going its own sweet way; but the bee works for the hive.
The butterfly lives and works individually, But the bee has a body-consciousness. We should all be like bees, having the consciousness of the Body so that we can live together with other members in the Body of Christ.
Are we beautiful butterflies or are we one of the worker bees who care, fight, and do everything for the hive? We should live and die in the Body, and we should live and die for the Body. In order to live and move with other members of the Body, we need to have the consciousness of the Body.
The Enjoyment of the All-inclusive Christ as the Head of the Body Making Us Conscious of the Body
The more we enjoy the all-inclusive Christ revealed in Colossians, the more we will realize experientially that the Christ whom we enjoy is the Head of the Body, and this realization will make us conscious of the Body and cause us to love all the members of the Body (Col. 1:18; 2:19; 3:12-14). We should not try to hold the Head or submit to the Head; instead, we should simply enjoy Christ. The Christ we enjoy is the Head. When we enjoy Him, we eventually realize that He is the Head and we submit to Him. The more we submit to Him, the more we will be conscious of the Body and love the Body. For the Lord's move in His recovery both locally and universally, we all need to be Body-conscious in one accord (Acts 1:14; 2:46; 4:24; 15:25; Rom. 15:6).—R. K.

拾叁　身体的供应乃是复合的膏油（圣膏油）所预表耶稣基督之灵全备的供应─腓一19～21上，出三十23～25：

三　我们是借着肢体的代祷和交通，接受那灵的供应：
１　当我们觉得枯干、碰壁的时候，我们需要弟兄姐妹为我们代祷，那种情形才可以过去─腓一19，帖前五25，伯四二8～10。
２　我们需要将自己祷告到神里面，以接受赐生生命的灵作我们的供应，好喂养我们自己，并喂养所有在我们照顾之下的人，使身体得着建造─路十一1～13。
３　我们若没有身体的供应，就不能活；因此，我们必须一直应用身体的交通─帖前三8，林前十16下，约壹一3。
４　无论我们的情形怎样，只要我们活在身体里，就能得着身体的供应。
５　人要看见光，就必须进入召会，就是圣所─诗七三16～17，太五14，启一20。
６　借着肢体间彼此的倚靠，整个身体就得着建造─林前十六18，弗四16。
拾肆　在身体里不能有独立或个人主义，因为我们是肢体，而肢体无法离开身体而生活─林前十二27，罗十二5，弗五30：
一　每一个信徒都是基督身体的肢体，而每一个肢体都是不可少的─林前十二15，21，罗十二3。
二　“你若真看见你在基督身体上的地位，你就会像是第二次得救一样”（基督的奥秘，一四页）─约一50～51，创二八17，提前三15。
三　那些看见自己是肢体的人，定规宝爱身体，看重其他的肢体─林前十二23～24，腓二29，林前十六18，士九9。
四　我们一有身体的启示，就有身体的感觉；一有身体的感觉，一切个人的想法和行动就除去了：
１　如果我们要认识身体，我们不但要蒙拯救脱离犯罪与天然的生命，更要蒙拯救脱离个人的生命。
２　父如何与世界相对，圣灵如何与肉体相对，主如何与魔鬼相对，照样，身体也与个人相对。
３　我们如何不能向头独立，照样也不能向身体独立。
４　个人主义在神眼中是可恨的：
a　身体的仇敌就己，那独立的“我”─太十六21～26。
b　我们若要在身体里被建造，己就必须被定罪、否认、拒绝并撇弃。
c　我们不仅该倚靠神，也该倚靠身体，倚靠弟兄姐妹─出十七11～13，徒九25，林后十一33。
５　我不知道的，身体里别的肢体知道；我不能看见的，身体里别的肢体能看见；我不能作的，身体里的别的肢体能作。
６　我们若拒绝在身体里同作肢体者的帮助，就是拒绝基督的帮助─林前十二12。
７　凡是单独的基督徒，迟早都要变得枯干。
拾伍　我们若活在身体里，就会经历身体的保护：
一　身体对于每一肢体都有保护─太十六18。
二　基督的身体乃是穿戴神全副的军装─弗六11。

To touch the principles of the Body of Christ is something very high, profound, and deep in God's heart. There is a battle whenever we come to the matter of the Body of Christ because this topic bothers God's enemy. Satan may not hinder us to preach the gospel, expound the Bible, or do some good works, but he does not want us to see the principles of the Body of Christ. Therefore, we need to pray that the Lord would give us a spirit of wisdom and revelation (Eph. 1:17). We need to stand together to fight against the enemy. The Lord said, "I will build My church, and the gates of Hades shall not prevail against it" (Matt. 16:18). We stand upon this fact.
We are burdened that all the Lord's children have a clear view concerning the precious principles of the Body. Though we may have heard these matters in the past, we should not assume that we know or see them entirely. In The Mystery of Christ Brother Nee says, "We cannot enter the realm of the Body by anything other than seeing" (p. 19). Some "pragmatic" saints may say, "We have heard and we know these things. Do not give us more teaching; simply tell us what to do. Tell us the steps to live the Body life." However, the spiritual realm does not work in this way. The entrance to every spiritual experience begins with our seeing. Our seeing is our entrance. Therefore, we need to humble and empty ourselves. We need to tell the Lord, "Despite all that I have heard and known, I want to be poor in spirit. I want to see something fresh and new. Lord, I still do not know much concerning Your Body." In The Mystery of Christ Brother Nee says further, "As soon as we see the Body, our life and work as individuals cease" (p. 19). We enter the realm of the Body by seeing it. We need much prayer for the Lord to open our eyes.
The principles of the Body of Christ are not in the physical realm of external rules for conduct, but in the divine and mystical realm. Our prayer is that these principles would become inward lights shining and illuminating our whole being. We need the light to make us uneasy and awkward when we do or say things which are individualistic or against the Body. When these principles have been transfused and infused into our inward being to such an extent, the Lord will have a way to build up His Body on the earth. We need to pray, "Lord, open my eyes, I want to be humble before You. I want to see and receive these principles not as external rules but as the divine light shining and illuminating my inward being to govern my walk and all my activities."
THE SUPPLY OF THE BODY BEING THE BOUNTIFUL SUPPLY OF THE SPIRIT OF JESUS CHRIST
The supply of the Body is the bountiful supply of the Spirit of Jesus Christ, typified by the compound ointment, the holy anointing oil (Phil. l:19-21a; Exo. 30:23-25). This is a precious principle—the supply is in the Body of Christ Many Christians struggle for survival or struggle to overcome besetting sins. They have a difficult time maintaining the Christian life because they do not know how to get the supply. It is a blessing to realize that our supply is in the Body. This supply is nothing less than the bountiful supply of the Spirit of Jesus Christ. A believer needs a constant supply to continue in a normal and healthy way. Some believers may say that they cannot make it without the Lord, and this is true. But those with more experience will also say that they cannot make it without the Body.
Most of the time the supply from Christ does not come to us directly, but through His Body. Sometimes the Lord speaks and supplies us directly, but many times the supply comes to us in the meetings, through a testimony, in a message, or through the visit of other brothers and sisters. Many times, We receive the supply of Christ through the members of His Body. It is futile for us to struggle to maintain our Christian life in an individualistic way. We need to attach ourselves to the other members and realize the supply of the Body.
When the apostle Paul wrote the Epistle to the , he was imprisoned in Rome. He expected that his imprisonment would turn out to salvation through the Philippians' petition and the bountiful supply of the Spirit of Jesus Christ (1:19). In this portion of the Word, Paul was not simply encouraging the saints to pray for him. We often ask others to pray for us when we have a need, and when others have a need, we pray for them. However, the thought here is deeper than this. Paul and the Philippian believers were in two different physical locations—Rome and Philippi—but in the mystical realm, in the Spirit, they were in one Body. Paul could experience the bountiful supply because he and the Philippians were in the Body. In verse 19 the petition of the saints is mentioned before the bountiful supply of the Spirit of Jesus Christ, indicating the crucial importance of the petition of the saints and the bountiful supply of the Spirit. This mention of the saints' petition by Paul is based on the fact that he and the believers in Philippi were in the Body. In the Body there is such a supply moving from member to member. This supply is the bountiful supply of the Spirit of Jesus Christ. If we merely pray for each other because of our needs, our mutual intercessions will not amount to much. The reality of the one Body causes our mutual prayers to work.
The Compound Spirit Being in and for the Body
The compound Spirit is in and for the Body and for the priestly service that builds up the Body (Exo. 30:26-31; Rom. 15:16). The compound ointment in Exodus 30 typifies the Spirit of Jesus Christ. God charged Moses to prepare an ointment made of one hin of oil mingled with four spices. According to God's ordination, this ointment was used to anoint the tabernacle with its utensils and all the serving priests.
This ointment is a type of the compound Spirit. The one hin of oil typifies the Spirit of God. The number four, corresponding to the four spices, signifies man or humanity. The four spices of myrrh, cinnamon, calamus, and cassia were in three complete units of measure—five hundred shekels each. Two of the spices—cinnamon and calamus—were two hundred fifty shekels each, but when put together, they formed one complete unit of five hundred shekels. The three units of five hundred shekels each, in four spices, signify the Triune God in resurrection mingled with humanity to bear the full responsibility. Myrrh typifies the death of Christ, cinnamon indicates the effectiveness of His death, calamus typifies His resurrection, and cassia signifies the power of His resurrection. These elements were all mingled together with the one hin of oil to produce a compound. From this picture we can see that the processed and consummated Triune God has become the compound Spirit containing divinity and humanity, God and man, and all the processes of Christ's death and resurrection. All these elements are included in the compound Spirit.
God charged Moses to use the compound ointment to anoint the tabernacle with its utensils and to anoint the serving priests. This signifies that the compound Spirit has been poured out upon the Body of Christ. The tabernacle is a type of the church, the house of God. The ointment is applied wherever there is the tabernacle, the house of God. The compound Spirit with the bountiful supply, typified by the compound ointment, is not for our individual "house," but for the church, the house of God. This compound Spirit is also applied to the priesthood (1 Pet. 2:5, 9), the serving priests.
In the church life, in the Body, there is a rich supply—the bountiful supply of the Spirit of Jesus Christ. This supply is applied wherever God's service is. Everything we need is in the Spirit. As long as we are in the right position—in and with the Body—we are in the place to receive such a supply. This corresponds to our experience. Before coming into the church life, we were individual Christians roaming around. We were thirsty and famishing until we came into the church life and touched the Body of Christ. When we came into the church life, we experienced a bountiful supply, the ointment of the compound Spirit, flowing in all the meetings. In the meetings we heard many crying out, "Hallelujah!" and we heard others calling on the Lord and pray-reading. Something within us Was bubbling. This is the bountiful supply, the compound Spirit as the ointment, applied to the Body. Similarly, when we participate in the divine and priestly service in the church life, serving together with other saints, there is a wonderful and precious ointment applied. In the past we may have been very zealous and diligent in doing the work of the Lord, serving God individually, but we were not in the proper place—the tabernacle and the priesthood. As a result, we did not sense the flowing of the compound ointment with its supply. In the early days of the church in Los Angeles, many pastors came into the church. They dropped their so-called ministry and became small brothers in the church life; they were happy to simply be members in the Body. When they entered into the practicality of the Body, they began to enjoy the rich supply in the Body.
Sometimes in our experience we may "squeak" or "grind" as if we are short of "oil." At such times, we need to check where we are. It is not a matter of zeal or of doing right or wrong. We simply must ask where we are with respect to the Body. If we are in the tabernacle serving with the other priests in the priesthood, the "oil," the anointing, the supply, and the ointment are with us. But if we are in and for ourselves as individuals, we will not experience the ointment, because the ointment is not for individuals. If we want the Spirit and the supply, we must be in the Body and for the Body.
The Inestimable Goodness and Pleasantness of Brothers Dwelling Together in Oneness
The inestimable goodness and pleasantness of brothers dwelling together in oneness is likened to the compound precious ointment (Eph. 4:3). Psalm 133 is a clear picture telling us that the ointment flows from the head to the skirts of the garment. Whenever there is a situation of oneness among God's people, the Spirit flows like the ointment flowed upon Aaron, from his head through his beard to the skirts of his garment. Many brothers and sisters have tasted such a rich supply. It is so good to dwell together in oneness with the brothers. This is the reality of the Body of Christ. There is such a sweet, harmonious, and pleasant oneness among us. Upon this oneness, this reality of the Body, the Spirit as the ointment is flowing as a rich supply.
Receiving the Supply of the Spirit by the Intercession and Fellowship of the Members
Needing the Intercession of the Saints in Dryness or When We Have No Way to Go On
We receive the supply of the Spirit by the intercession and fellowship of the members. We receive the supply by two means: intercession and fellowship. When we are dry and have no way to go on, we need other brothers and sisters to intercede for us before we can get through (Phil. 1:19; Job 42:8-10). We may come to a low point where we are dry and feel that the way is too hard or too demanding for us to continue. At such a time, we need the brothers and sisters to intercede for us. First Thessalonians says, "Brothers, pray also for us" (5:25). Are we willing to humble ourselves to ask others to pray for us? Sometimes we may be down and dry, but we still do not want to lose our face or our self by letting others know of our condition. Even the apostle Paul, who was graced, commissioned, and gifted by the Lord, asked the young Thessalonian believers in a new church to pray for him. He realized that though he was an apostle used by God, he was nothing more or less than a member of the Body of Christ. He needed the supply of the Body like anyone else.
Brother Lee was another example of this. He was matured, experienced, and very much used by the Lord, yet he often asked us to pray for him. He appreciated the prayers of all of the saints. One extreme is to consider that we can reach a point where we do not need the prayers of the other members. Another extreme is to consider that since we are so depressed and so hopeless, others should not pray for us. Both are lies from the enemy. If we are at a point where we feel very hopeless, we need to come to the Body and ask the brothers and sisters to pray for us. When we are weak and low, the enemy's lie and deception is to cause us to go away and hide from the Body. Instead, we should run to the Body. Such low times are when we need the Body the most.
Praying Ourselves into God to Receive the Life-giving Spirit as Our Supply to Feed Ourselves and Those under Our Care
We need to pray ourselves into God to receive the life-giving Spirit as our supply to feed ourselves and all those under our care for the building up of the Body. In Luke 11:1-13 the Lord taught His disciples how to pray and mentioned to them the case of someone who had a friend coming to visit him. Since this one who would receive his visiting friend did not have any food, he went to ask of another friend. Due to his persistent asking, the other friend opened the door, even though he and his family were already in bed. Verse 13 says, "How much more will the Father who is from heaven give the Holy Spirit to those who ask Him!" The Father will gladly give the Holy Spirit to those who ask Him. We need to ask, petition, and pray for one another. Due to our persistence, our Father will answer us by giving us the Holy Spirit. The answer to our prayer is the Holy Spirit, who is the rich supply given to us to meet the needs of our friends. We need this kind of prayer.
Not Living without the Supply and Constantly Availing Ourselves of the Fellowship of the Body
We cannot live without the supply of the Body; therefore, we must constantly avail ourselves of the fellowship of the Body (1 Thes. 3:8; 1 Cor. 10:16b; 1 John 1:3). The supply of the Body comes to us through the fellowship of the Body; therefore, we should not hide or keep ourselves away from the Body. We have seen that there is a rich supply in the Body, but often we do not avail ourselves of the fellowship of the Body to receive the supply. We may be afraid to lose our face or let others know that we need help. Conversely, we may look down on a certain brother, considering that he has nothing to give us. If we are living in the reality of the Body, we can receive the supply from every little brother and sister. If we are properly joined to the Body, there is not one saint from whom we cannot receive the supply. If we avail ourselves of the fellowship in the Body, we will find the supply everywhere in the Body of Christ.
I also noticed this of Brother Lee when he was with us. After giving a message, he paid careful attention to every word that came out of each brother and sister during the time of testimonies. As he shared and ministered, he was supplying. But he realized that he needed the supply, so he always left time for the saints to share. During the testimonies, he would draw his supply from the saints. Many times, if we do not get the supply, it is because we do not avail ourselves of the fellowship of the Body of Christ. If we would have the thought that this or that person is not worthy or rich enough to give us anything, we will be in poverty. If we are properly related to the Body of Christ, there is a supply for us every day and everywhere in the church life. We can receive the supply from every brother or sister.
Receiving the Supply of the Body, Regardless of Our Condition
As long as we live in the Body, we will receive the supply of the Body, no matter what our condition is. Some may feel that their condition is beyond hope. If you feel this way, you should fellowship about it with the other members. It is not your condition that matters, but where you are. If you are in the Body of Christ, you will receive the supply. In the physical body, all the members, whether fingers, legs, or hands, receive the supply because they are properly placed in the body. Some members may have been cut or wounded, but as long as they remain attached to the body they will receive the supply. If you are such a member, you will be healed and nourished. The worst thing to do when you have been bruised or wounded is to cut yourself off from the Body, because to cut yourself off is to cut yourself off from the supply of the Body.
Entering the Church, the Sanctuary, to Receive the Light
If a man wants to see light, he has to enter the church, the sanctuary (Matt. 5:14; Rev. 1:20). The light is in the church. When we are by ourselves we are often in darkness. Although God is omnipresent, He is particularly in the sanctuary, which is His dwelling place. The things that the psalmist could not understand troubled him, but when he entered the sanctuary, everything was made clear (Psa. 73:16-17). There is light in the Body. We can testify that many times we sought the Lord concerning a certain matter or looked for direction in a particular situation, yet we were not clear. Regardless of how much we prayed by ourselves, we still could not be clear. However, when we came to the Body and placed this matter in the Body, the light came. Often we sat in the meeting, and as the saints began to prophesy, the light came. Do not stay away from the meetings. You may be tired or busy, but do not forsake the meetings. Some may even be so foolish as to miss the meeting to study the Bible alone at home. The light we get by ourselves is quite limited at best. The light is in the sanctuary. If we would be willing to forsake ourselves and come to the sanctuary, that is, to the Body, we will receive light.
Building Up the Body through the Interdependence among the Members
The whole Body is built up through the interdependence among the members (Eph. 4:16). Referring to three brothers, Stephanas, Fortunatus, and Achaicus, who visited Paul, he said, "They refreshed my spirit and yours" (1 Cor. 16:18). The spirit of such a great apostle as Paul was refreshed by the visit of three little brothers who are not mentioned in any other book in the New Testament. We should not consider that the apostles were all-inclusive and self-sufficient. Paul recognized that he needed a visit from other brothers for his spirit to be refreshed. We do have the sensation and consciousness that we are interconnected with and interdependent upon one another. We are not all-inclusive and self-sufficient. We need each other!
Ephesians 4:16 tells us clearly that the Body of Christ is built up by the rich supply of the joints and by the operation in measure of each one part. We need every part of the Body to build up the Body. In the proper Body life, we do not depend upon one or two persons; rather, we depend upon one another. No one is so great that they are all-sufficient or all-inclusive. In the Body of Christ we all are just members needing one another and depending on one another.
MEMBERS IN THE BODY NOT LIVING DETACHED FROM THE BODY
In the Body there can be no independence or individualism, for we are members, and members cannot live in detachment from the Body (1 Cor. 12:27; Rom. 12:5; Eph. 5:30). We are just members. As Christians we should realize that we are not only forgiven sinners or disciples of Christ, but also members of the Body of Christ. Being a member means that we are part of a whole and are not complete in ourselves. Brother Nee made a distinction between a Christian and a member. He said that "being a Christian is something individualistic, whereas being a member is something corporate. Being a Christian is something one does for himself, whereas being a member is something for the Body" (The Mystery of Christ, pp. 16-17). A Christian can live on his own, but a member needs to live together with others. A member is not the whole Body, only a part of the Body. We need this kind of vision. We are not merely forgiven sinners or disciples of Christ individually; we are the many members of Christ needing one another. All the members are under the direction of the Head. At the same time, all the members are interconnected with one another. The thumb is not only directly related to the head but also directly related to the hand, arm, and shoulder.
We are not only members of Christ but also members of one another. Romans 12:5 says, "So we who are many are one body in Christ, and individually members one of another." It is a great revelation to see that we are not only members of Christ but also members one of another.
In 1 Corinthians 12, a chapter on the Body of Christ, Paul spoke much about the members. He spoke of the indispensability of the members (vv. 14-22), the blending together of the members (vv. 23-27), and the functioning of the members (vv. 28-31). On one hand, we may say that we are one Body in Christ, but on the other hand, practically speaking, we function and live as members. We have a Body life, but in our practical living we are members, needing one another and being dependent on each other. We are members in particular and cannot live detached from the Body.
Every Believer Being a Member of the Body of Christ, and Every Member Being Indispensable
Every believer is a member of the Body of Christ, and every member is indispensable (Rom. 12:3). First Corinthians 12:15 says, "If the foot should say, Because I am not a hand, I am not of the body, it is not that because of this it is not of the body." Verse 21 says, "And the eye cannot say to the hand, I have no need of you; nor again the head to the feet, I have no need of you." From these verses we can see that many Christians may fall into one of two extremes. One extreme is those who feel inferior to the other members. They feel that they cannot be like a particular brother who is strong, gifted, and full of supply. They say, "I am just a little member who is not qualified. I am not an eye so I am not of the Body." This is the "inferiority complex" of those who always feel that they are inferior. There is also the other extreme: those members who feel that they are superior. They say, "I do not need that brother or that sister. I can handle this myself. I have no need of them." This is the "superiority complex." Neither is right for the Body of Christ. In the Body every member is indispensable.
The so-called weaker or uncomely ones are more needed in the Body. We should not feel that because we are weaker and our portion is smaller, that we are not needed. We are indispensable. As members we must have the realization that we need every member, and that no one is dispensable.
Seeing Our Position in the Body
In The Mystery of Christ, page 16, Brother Nee says, "If we truly see our position in the Body, it will be as though we were saved a second time" (John 1:50-51; Gen. 28:17; 1 Tim. 3:15). Before we were saved, we were under the condemnation of sin and death; after we are saved, we are often under the constant condemnation of our self and natural life. We may be jealous of others, wishing we were like another brother or sister, or we may compare ourselves with others. The self is always giving us trouble. However, the day that we see we are simply members in the Body is a day of emancipation from condemnation. We will no longer try to be somebody we are not, and neither will we condemn others who seem to be weaker or inferior. Once we see that we are members of the Body and that our position is in the Body, we will sense that we are saved a second time. Our self is so troublesome, being the source of jealousy, striving, judgment, criticism, and all other such things that bring us under condemnation.
It is a great release when the Lord opens our eyes to see that we are members properly placed in the Body. Then we are very happy to simply be members. We do not have to be something God does not want us to be. If God wants us to be a thumb or a little finger, we are happy. We do not try to be an eye or a mouth. It is such a release when we the members find our place in the Body.
Treasuring the Body and Honoring the Other Members
Those who see that they are members of the Body treasure the Body and honor the other members (1 Cor. 16:18; Judg. 9:9). First Corinthians 12 says, "But much rather the members of the body which seem to be weaker are necessary. And those members of the body which we consider to be less honorable, these we clothe with more abundant honor; and our uncomely members come to have more abundant comeliness" (vv. 22-23). God has blended all the different members of Christ together into one Body (v. 24). For this we need much transformation (Rom. 12:2); that is, we need to be transformed from the natural life to the spiritual life by the same Spirit for the practical Body life. Paul encouraged the Philippians to receive Epaphroditus "in the Lord with all joy, and hold such in honor" (Phil. 2:29). He encouraged the Corinthian believers to acknowledge brothers such as Stephanas and Fortunatus. These brothers were probably ordinary brothers who through their visitation of Paul refreshed his spirit and the spirits of the Corinthians.
Body-consciousness Ruling Out Individualistic Thought and Action
Wherever there is Body-revelation, there is Body-consciousness, and wherever there is Body-consciousness, individualistic thought and action are ruled out. When we see the Body, we immediately have a consciousness of the Body. Whenever we see the Body, the individual self is eradicated. When the self is put aside, there will be the reality of the Body. These two things—the seeing of the Body and the individual self—cannot exist together. Therefore, we need to see the Body.
Being Delivered from Our Individualistic Life by Seeing the Body
The more we see the Body, the more we are delivered from the individualistic self. If we want to know the Body, we need deliverance not only from our sinful life and our natural life but also from our individualistic life. Regretfully, many of God's children do not have this consciousness. They may have a consciousness of their sinful and worldly life, and perhaps a slight consciousness of their natural life, but many do not see their individualistic life. They may repent for their sins and worldliness, but they may not repent for their individualistic life. If we remain individualistic, we are through with the Body.
The Body Being versus the Individual
Just as the Father is versus the world, the Spirit is versus the flesh, and the Lord is versus the devil, so also the Body is versus the individual. Ephesians 4:4-6 mentions four parties: the Father, the Lord, the Spirit, and the Body. These four parties are opposed by four other entities: the world, the devil, the flesh, and the individual self. The Body has not yet been built up after nearly two thousand years because we all have preserved our individual selves. The self is unbroken and untouched, and as a result the Body is not built. The enemy to the Body of Christ is our self.
Not Being Independent from the Body
Just as we cannot be independent from the Head, we cannot be independent from the Body. The Head is the Body. Christ is the Body (1 Cor. 12:12). A person needs both a head and a body. In like manner, we cannot be independent from either the Head or the Body.
The Self Being the Enemy of the Body
Individualism is hateful in the sight of God. The enemy of the Body is the self, the independent "I," the independent "me" (Matt. 16:21-26). If we would be built up in the Body, the self must be condemned, denied, rejected, and renounced. We should be dependent not only on God but also on the Body, on the brothers and sisters (Acts 9:25; 2 Cor. 11:33). Exodus 17 is a wonderful record of the Israelites fighting the battle with the Amalekites (vv. 11-13).-Moses prayed on the mountain for the fighting in the valley. Joshua led God's children to fight the battle, and Moses prayed for them. At a certain point Moses' hands became weary. Aaron and Hur came to lift up his hands to help Moses maintain and Continue his prayer until the battle was fully won. Even though Moses could pray and was burdened to pray, he needed Aaron and Hur to lift up his hands. In principle, he needed the Body. Do not think that you are so capable and all-sufficient in yourself. You need the Body to come and uphold your hands to pray. You have a portion and a gift to pray, but to continue to carry out your function, gift, and portion, you need the other members to uphold you.
The Interdependence of the Members
What I do not know, another member of the Body will know; what I cannot see, another member of the Body will see; what I cannot do, another member of the Body will do. It is a big relief to realize that we do not have to be all-inclusive and all-sufficient. We have all the members of the Body. If we cannot see, there are other brothers and sisters who can see. If we cannot hear, there are other members who can hear. If all the members function and operate according to the portion the Lord has allotted them, there is much that the Lord can do. Regretfully, instead of exercising their function and the measure allotted to them by the Lord, many of the believers spend their time fighting with one another, competing against one another, or being jealous of one another.
Refusing the Help of Christ by Refusing the Help of Our Fellow Members
If we refuse the help of our fellow members, we are refusing the help of Christ (1 Cor. 12:12). We must realize that we are not turning down the brothers and sisters; we are turning down Christ. The members are the corporate "Me" who is Christ Himself (Acts 9:4). If we refuse help from the other saints, we are turning Christ away.
The Dryness of Being an Individualistic Christian
Sooner or later all individualistic Christians will dry up. Individual Christians will eventually run out of the supply because they try to make it in themselves. They do not realize that they are only members. It is a blessed day when we realize that we are nothing more and nothing less than a member in the Body.
EXPERIENCING THE PROTECTION OF THE BODY
The Body Rendering Protection to Every Member
The Body renders protection to every member (Matt. 16:18). If we live in the Body, we will experience the protection of the Body. The Body not only supplies us; it also protects us. When we are apart from the Body, we are exposed to the enemy with his "germs" and "infections." When we stay within the Body we are protected. There is a mechanism in the Body that protects all the members.
The Body of Christ Putting on the Whole Armor of God
The Body of Christ puts on the whole armor of God (Eph. 6:11). In these days the Lord is propagating and spreading, but it is also a time of fighting. We should not fight the enemy by ourselves individualistically; we should fight in the Body by putting on the whole armor of God. The whole armor of God consists of the girdle of truth, the breastplate of righteousness, the shoes of the gospel of peace, the shield of faith, the helmet of salvation, and the sword of the Spirit, which is the word of God. All of these items of the armor are worn by different parts of the Body; therefore, the coming together of all these parts forms the whole armor of God. Today we are fighting the battle against the enemy under the whole armor of God.
One Chasing a Thousand and Two Putting Ten Thousand to Flight
Spiritual warfare is in this principle—one will chase a thousand and two will put ten thousand- to flight (Deut. 32:30; Rom. 16:20). According to our mathematics, if one can chase one thousand, two should be able to chase two thousand. But in heavenly mathematics, if one chases one thousand, two will chase ten thousand. This is the principle of the Body. Ecclesiastes 4 says, "Two are better than one, because they have a good reward for their labor; for if they fall, the one will lift up his companion. But woe to the one who falls and does not have another to lift him up! Again, if two lie together, they keep warm. But how can one be warm alone? And while a man may prevail against the one, the two will withstand him; and a threefold cord is not quickly broken" (vv. 9-12). When we are in the Body, we are protected. If we are part of the Body, another part of the Body will lift us up if we fall down.
An Isolated Individual Being Prone to Deception
An isolated individual is prone to be deceived, so we need the covering of the Body for this as well (Phil. 3:3; Acts 21:4,11-12). When we are by ourselves, we are subject to the deception of the enemy, but when we are in the Body, we have the covering. We all must see the reality of the Body's protection, hide under its protection, and accept its safeguard. The safest place is in the Body of Christ. We should not expose ourselves to the enemy, thinking that we are all-inclusive or fully capable. Instead, we should hide in the Body and be safeguarded by the Body.
BEING LIMITED BY OTHER MEMBERS, NOT GOING BEYOND OUR MEASURE
As members of the Body, we must allow ourselves to be limited by the other members, not going beyond our measure. This is another precious principle of living in the Body. We should always take the limitation of the Body. Our flesh and natural man like to be free and unrestricted, but the Body limits and restricts us. This limitation and restriction helps us to grow and develop.
Recognizing Our Measure and Not Going Beyond It
A basic requirement for the growth and development of the Body is that we recognize our measure and not go beyond it (Eph. 4:7,16). The Lord, as the Head of the Body, assigns a measure to each member. We all have to know our measure and not function beyond our measure. We should not try to overstretch, but put ourselves under the limitation of the Body. By the Lord's sovereignty we are often put together in coordination with someone who is the opposite of us. We may be fast, and the other saint slow. The slow one limits us. We may consider that this wastes time, but the Lord places this restriction on us so that He can gain His Body.
The Head Setting Us in Our Special Place and Pointing Us to Our Special Function
The Head sets us in our special place in the Body and points us to our special function (1 Cor. 12:18). It pleased the Lord to put us where we are. We should not try to strive or struggle for what we are not.
Staying within the Limits of God's Ruling, God's Measuring
The Lord has given us our measure. Like Paul we should move and act according to how much God has measured to us, staying within the limits of God's ruling and measuring (2 Cor. 10:13). Even Paul, a worker of the Lord, worked within God's measure. He submitted himself to God's measurement. Even in our serving and working for the Lord we should do so within our measure. Being limited by the Body will protect and safeguard us, and in this way the Body will be preserved.—J. L.

GENERAL SUBJECT
KEEPING THE PRINCIPLES OF THE BODY
Message Six
The Principles of the Body (5)
纲目|对照-听抄-目录
Scripture Reading: 1 Cor. 12:24-27; Rom. 12:2-8; 1 John 1:3; Eph. 4:16
XVII.　The Body has its proper order—1 Cor. 12:14-22:
A.　God has placed all the members of the Body even as He willed:
1.　Each one of us members has our own place in the Body of Christ; it is assigned by God and should be accepted by us— 1 Cor. 12:18.
2.　Since such as assignment is according to God's will, every member is necessary—vv. 21-22.
B.　Every member has a definite place, a definite assignment, and a definite position; every member has a particular portion with which he serves the Body of Christ:
1.　Each member has his own characteristics, and each has his own capability; these characteristics constitute the place, position, or ministry of each member—Rom. 12:4-8.
2.　No one can change the position of any member of the Body.
C.　We need to know the order of the Body, which is God's assigned pattern in the Body:
1.　We must be willing to be limited to our measure—Rom. 12:3, 6.
2.　As soon as we go beyond our measure, we go beyond the authority of the Body and move out from under the anointing; when we go beyond our measure, we interfere with the order of the Body.
3.　To think more highly of ourselves than we ought to think without a sober mind is to annul the proper order of the Body life—Rom. 12:3.
D.　The authority in the Body is the authority of the Head flowing throughout the Body—Col. 2:19; Eph. 5:23-24:

B.　The fellowship in the Body involves not only receiving help from other members but also giving help to other members—1 Cor. 16:18; 1 John 5:16.

A.　The divine fellowship is the flow of the divine life among and through all the members of the organic Body of Christ—1 John 1:3; Rev. 22:1.
B.　The Body in a practical way is in the fellowship.
C.　Just as the circulation of the blood is the fellowship of our body, the circulation of the Spirit is the fellowship of Christ's Body; the Spirit today is the “blood” in Christ's Body—Eph. 4:4; 2 Cor. 13:14.
D.　God has blended the Body together—1 Cor. 12:24:

2.　Blending means that we should always stop to fellowship with others.

4.　We should not do anything without fellowshipping with the other saints who are coordinating with us; fellowship requires us to stop when we are about to do something.
5.　To be blended means that you are touched by others and that you are touching others, but you should touch others in a blending way.
6.　Blending requires us to be crossed-out; blending requires us to be by the Spirit to dispense Christ and to do everything for the sake of His Body.

D.　We need to be Body-conscious, Body-centered, not self-centered.
E.　We need to be rescued from self-seeking back to a concern for the Body, that we might no longer be for our individual profit but for the building up of the Body.

MESSAGE SIX
THE PRINCIPLES OF THE BODY (5)
纲目|Outline|对照-目录
（本文为英文听抄扫描所得，未经校对）
In this message we will fellowship about the application of the principles presented in the foregoing messages. We certainly need to receive the supply, live in the resurrection life, become God in life and nature but not in the Godhead, and be in the reality of the Body. If we do not live in the Body according to the principles that the Lord has given us, we will eventually lose everything. Yet there are also many practical aspects of living in the Body. We must live in the Body in a practical way, and such a practical life will enhance our experience of the life aspects of the Body of Christ.
THE BODY HAVING ITS PROPER ORDER
God Having Placed All the Members of the Body Even as He Willed
The Body has its proper order (1 Cor. 12:14-22). God has placed all the members of the Body even as He willed. First Corinthians 12:13 says, "For also in one Spirit we were all baptized into one body," and verse 27 says that we are "members individually." We all have been baptized into one Body, and in this one Body we are members individually. The baptism in the Spirit into one Body has brought us into an organic union with the Triune God. Now we are living members. Just as the human body is something living, the Body of Christ is also living. The Body is not inorganic or robotic; it is a living organism. If we have an inorganic view of the Body, we will damage what the Lord wants to accomplish. We have not only been baptized into the Spirit into one Body, but each member has also been placed in the Body in a particular way. Furthermore, all the members have been blended together that there be no division in the Body (v. 25).
In 1 Corinthians 12:14-27 four laws that govern the Body of Christ are revealed. These laws are principles that govern the functioning of the members in the Body. Verses 15 and 16 say, "If the foot should say, Because I am not a hand, I am not of the body, it is not that because of this it is not of the body. And if the ear should say, Because I am not an eye, I am not of the body, it is not that because of this it is not of the body." The first law of functioning in the Body is that we must fulfill our responsibility. Sometimes, we do not fulfill our responsibility because we would like to be another member. Since we cannot be another member, we do not function. But we all have to rise up to fulfill our responsibility in the Body of Christ. Other times, we may genuinely feel that we are less than others and that our function is not compatible with the Body. Regardless, we all have to rise up and fulfill our responsibility. What a glorious thing it would be if all of us could put away our excuses and rise up to function in the Body! God has placed all of us in the Body in an organic way. We have an organic union with the Triune God, and we are the living members of the Body of Christ. As living members, we all should function in the Body.
God placed each of us in the Body according to His choice. We all should be satisfied with God's arrangement. We may think that if we cannot speak well, we do not have a way to function in the Body. This is not true. All God wants is that every member function according to where he has been placed in the Body. Since the Lord has placed us, we should not seek to be a different member. If we are a hand, we should not say, "Since I am not a foot, I am not in the Body." We are very much in the Body, we are living members of the Body, and God expects that we all would fulfill our responsibility.
The second law that governs the functioning in the Body is the matter of accepting limitations. First Corinthians 12:17-20 says, "If the whole body were an eye, where would the hearing be? If the whole were the hearing, where would the smelling be? But now God has placed the members, each one of them, in the body, even as He willed. And if all were one member, where would the body be? But now the members are many, but the body one." :
This law governs the functioning in the Body so that no one person is everything. Sometimes in the prayer meetings of the church, it seems we have only two or three members and that they are the only ones who pray. No one should be so large in the Body; no person should be the only one who functions while others remain dormant. Furthermore, no one can say that everyone should function as he does. The Body is not one member functioning; the Body is everyone functioning. We should not have a situation in any of the churches where only a few—and certainly not one—are the whole Body. In the denomination where I was saved, I always had the sense that it was a "one-man show." Yet the Body of Christ is not a "one-man show." If anyone has an outstanding or prominent function, that person must learn to restrict himself that all the other members may have the opportunity to function. On the one hand, no one should over-function, and on the other hand, no one should under-function. God has blended the whole Body together so that all the living members can function in an organic way.
We need to function according to what we are. It does not matter if we are a small member or a large member, a shoulder or a little finger. When we migrated to Russia in 1991, we had meetings where one brother would stand up to preach the gospel. On some nights as many as two hundred fifty people were baptized and hundreds of others prayed to receive the Lord. The one brother who preached the gospel did not do everything. One brother may have preached the gospel, but others were praying; still others brought people to the meetings. At the same time, the saints in the churches throughout the earth were praying for the preaching of the gospel in Russia. In the time of the kingdom, the Lord will not only reward the one who preached the gospel, but also all the saints who labored in contacting others for the gospel as well as those who prayed.
First Corinthians 12:21-22 says, "And the eye cannot say to the hand, I have no need of you; nor again the head to the feet, I have no need of you. But much rather the members of the body which seem to be weaker are necessary." This is a matter of esteeming or honoring the other members. A great temptation for all of us is to think that others are lower than we are. The fallen man thinks this way. We have to esteem and honor every member. God has blended the Body together. We cannot say to even one member, "I do not have any need of you." If we live the church life in this way, the Body will suffer great division. Eventually, the Lord may fill up the church with those whom we despise until we no longer despise them. The Lord may do this so that we learn to exercise our patience toward everyone. This is wonderful!
In some fellowship with the elders, Brother Lee once told us, "You have to learn to listen to every member in the church, from the smallest to the greatest." Soon after that, I went back to my locality, and a young brother made a suggestion. At first I did not esteem him or his suggestion, but Brother Lee's words echoed in me: "Learn to listen to every member." Eventually, through fellowship we older brothers realized that this young brother's suggestion was the mind of the Lord. Had we not taken his suggestion, the whole Body would have missed something. But by taking his feeling and bringing it to the Lord, we all felt that it was surely the Lord's speaking to His Body. Never despise any member. Esteem and honor every member, whether they are good or bad, refined or uncouth, weak or strong. Learn not to despise any member. Some members are very weak, and others are very strong. But do not love the strong ones only; learn to love all the weaker ones as well. We must honor and esteem them because God has blended the whole Body together.
The last principle governing the functioning in the Body is the keeping of the oneness. First Corinthians 12:23-25 says, "And those members of the body which we consider to be less honorable, these we clothe with more abundant honor; and our uncomely members come to have more abundant comeliness, but our comely members have no need. But God has blended the body together, giving more abundant honor to the member that lacked, that there would be no division in the body, but that the members would have the same care for one another." We may not give an uncomely member honor, but the Lord gives him honor. The reason for this is that there would be no division in the Body. We need to take care of all the uncomely members. By this, the whole Body is blended together and there is no division.
All the Members Having Their Own Place in the Body of Christ, and Every Member Being Necessary
Each one of us members has his own place in the Body of Christ; it is assigned by God and should be accepted by us (1 Cor. 12:18). We must learn to accept where we have been placed in the Body. We should be able to say that we are satisfied with where we have been placed in the Body. Since such an assignment is according to God's will, every member is necessary (vv. 21-22). If God has assigned us to a particular place, then the Body needs that assignment. Hence, every member is necessary.
Every Member Having a Definite Place, Assignment, and Position, and Every Member Having a Particular Portion
Every member has a definite place, a definite assignment, and a definite position; every member has a particular portion with which he serves the Body of Christ. First Corinthians 12:18 says, "But now God has placed the members, each one of them, in the body, even as He willed." In the Lord's Body we have a definite place, a definite assignment, and a definite position. If we consider our own body, we will realize that every member has been assigned a definite place and a definite position. Where the Lord has set us, where we have been positioned, is the place the Lord wants us to function.
Each Member Having His Own Characteristics and His Own Capability, and the Position of Any Member of the Body Being Unchangeable
Each member has his own characteristics, and each has his own capability; these characteristics constitute the place, position, or ministry of each member (Rom. 12:4-8). Our function, our ministry, our portion, is based on the characteristics that we possess and our own capabilities. All our characteristics and capabilities are different. Although education is a great benefit to the Lord's servants, we have not been placed in the Body of Christ according to education. Instead, our placement in the Body is according to our God-given characteristics and capabilities. These characteristics and capabilities give us the function that we possess, which becomes the ministry that we have toward others.
We must learn to rise up according to what the Lord has given us. We should not despise our function; we should not despise God's creation; we should not despise our background. All our characteristics and capabilities are not for ourselves; they are for the Body. We all have a definite place, or position, in the Body, and no one can change the position of any member.
Knowing the Order of the Body Being Willing to Be Limited to Our Measure
We need to know the order of the Body, which is God's assigned pattern in the Body. The order is the place where we function. We also must be willing to be limited to our measure (Rom. 12:3, 6). God has apportioned to each a measure of faith, and we are limited by that measure. We must not try to have a measure that is beyond what we possess. We all have been apportioned a certain measure of faith; we should not try to go beyond that measure. If we go beyond that measure, we will eventually be a problem to the entire Body and to ourselves.
We all have the same quality of faith, but we do not have the same quantity of faith. All of us have been apportioned a certain measure of faith, and that measure of faith can be increased. If we are under the supply day by day in the enjoyment of Christ, living Him and being one with Him, our measure of faith will increase more and more. Our function in the Body will be apportioned to us according to that measure of faith. When we were first born again, our measure of faith was quite small. But as we grew, our function began to be manifested. I enjoy watching my new granddaughter grow every day. The quality of all that she possesses from the time she was born is the same. She has everything. But day by day, the quantity is increasing. Today at her stage of growth, she makes unintelligible sounds, but eventually she will be speaking understandable words. This illustrates the increase of the measure of faith.
We all have faith, and we have all been apportioned a measure of faith, but that faith can grow. The gifts that are spoken of in Romans 12 are the gifts of grace. These gifts come out of life and are developed by all of us. The more we are under the bountiful supply of the Spirit of Jesus Christ, the more we grow in life, and the more we develop, the more the functions that we possess will come forth.
Going Beyond the Authority of the Head and Moving Out from under the Anointing
As soon as we go beyond our measure, we go beyond the authority of the Head and move out from under the anointing; when we go beyond our measure, we interfere with the order of the Body. My hand has been given a certain measure, but if my hand tries to over-function and do what my shoulder does, it will become a big problem in the body. Everyone has a measure of faith, but we should not try to go beyond that measure. To go beyond that measure always damages the Body of Christ. We must function according to the measure that has been apportioned to us. If we all function according to our measure in the Body, there will be a wonderful harmony in the Body. When we try to function beyond our measure, there is no anointing. Eventually, we will be frustrated and everyone around us will also be frustrated. We should simply function according to what we have been apportioned, and this will bring in a great harmony in the Body.
Annulling the Proper Order of the Body Life by Thinking More Highly of Ourselves Than We Ought to Think
To think more highly of ourselves than we ought to think without a sober mind is to annul the proper order of the Body life (Rom. 12:3). We must not think more highly of ourselves than we ought to think. Rather, we must think soberly. If we do not do this, we will annul the order in the Body. My hand can never think it is higher than the shoulder. It is not sober for my hand to think in this way. We must learn to have a sober mind and know where we are in the Body. Brother Nee illustrated this matter by pointing out the functions in a family. When one comes into a family, everyone knows who the grandfather is, everyone knows who the father is, and everyone knows who the children are. Everyone knows, and there are no questions. The order is established by the growth in life, by their position. They hold that position because that is what they are. No one doubts who the grandfather is, who the father is, or who the children are. There is no question. This is the order in the Body. It should be that when we are in the Body, we always keep the order established by the Lord. Do not fight against His order.
Many times someone is functioning in a particular way according to his placement in the Body, and we do not feel that he should be placed there. Most of the time this occurs when that particular one has been placed in the order above us. We realize that we should submit to that one; however, we would not submit. We may complain to the Lord and to others. But this will never change the order in the Body. We must accept that order. When we accept the order in the Body, we are fully at peace. But when we react and rebel against that order, we are without peace. God has placed us in the Body according to what He has willed. It is His choice, and we must submit to that order.
The Authority in the Body Being the Authority of the Head Flowing throughout the Body
The authority in the Body is the authority of the Head flowing throughout the Body (Col. 2:19; Eph. 5:23-24). This authority is flowing throughout the whole Body. Every member has a direct connection to the Head. Every member is directly under the authority of the Head. In our physical body all the nerves fan out from our brain and go to every member of our body. All the members have direct contact with the head. Our head becomes the authority in the body. When my head is directing the members of my body, my feet do not get crossed up. Everything is harmonious. It is innate. There is no competing, and there is no withdrawing. Everything flows freely as long as every member is under the head. As members in the Body we must give Christ the Head unreserved obedience. Without this unreserved obedience, chaos will be brought into the Body. But if we all live under the headship of Christ, this will bring a situation into the Body of Christ where the Body can exist in a harmonious way.
God Requiring Us to Come Directly under His Authority and Also under the Authority of Those Who Represent His Authority in the Body
God requires us to come directly under not only His authority but also the authority of those who represent His authority in the Body (1 Tim. 5:17a; 1 Cor. 3:10a; Eph. 5:24a; Heb. 13:17). We may say, "Praise the Lord, I should be under the authority of the Head. I will do it. I will give unreserved obedience to the Head." But in the Body there is also another kind of authority—the authority of those who represent the Head. We must also give obedience to this authority. I am so thankful that in the church there is the authority to which we should also submit. In the work the apostles are the authority. In the church the elders are the authority. However, it may not be so easy to submit to this authority. We must recognize that all the authorities raised up by the Lord are His representatives.
In the Old Testament when Korah rebelled against Moses, he did not say anything. God came in to vindicate the fact that Moses was His representative authority. In the Body there ar;e the Lord's representative authorities. Do not say, "I am living under the headship of Christ. I can function in the Body only in the way that the Lord directs me." This may seem good, but this can also bring in much damage to the Body. If the ones who rebelled in 1978 and 1987 had listened to the Body and had been under the authority of God's representatives in the Body, the rebellions would not have taken place. However, they did not remain under the representative authority.
Any Desire to Exercise Authority Being Contrary to the Life of the Body
Any desire on the part of a believer to exercise authority is contrary to the life of the Body (Matt. 20:26-28; 1 Pet. 5:3). This is seen in 3 John 9 with Diotrephes. He loved to have the preeminence. This is an ugly thing.
Submission Being One Outstanding Characteristic of Those Who Are Familiar with the Life of the Body
Submission is one outstanding characteristic of those who are familiar with the life of the Body (Eph. 5:21; 1 Pet. 5:5). We have to love the word submission.
MUTUALITY BEING THE CHARACTERISTIC OF THE BODY, AND THE FUNCTIONING OF THE BODY BEING MUTUAL
Mutuality is the characteristic of the Body, and the functioning of the Body is mutual; thus our relationship in the Body is one of mutuality and interdependence (1 Cor. 12:14-25).
All the Members of the Body Mutually Affecting One Another
All the members of the Body mutually affect one another (Phil. 1:8; 1 Cor. 12:26). Whatever we do affects others because the characteristic of the Body is mutuality. To function in mutuality in a positive way is a great benefit to the Lord's Body, but if our functioning in mutuality is in a negative way, it can be a great damage to the Body. Because the Body is one in mutuality, we affect one another, either positively or negatively.
The Fellowship in the Body Being a Matter of Giving and Receiving Help from One Another
The fellowship in the Body involves not only receiving help from other members but also giving help to other members (1 Cor. 16:18; 1 John 5:16). Mutuality is a "two-way street." While I am speaking, I do not consider such speaking a "one-way street." I do not consider that the audience is merely listening; instead, there is a reaction and a rebounding within the saints. This is mutuality. Even in sharing a message, there should be mutuality. Brother Watchman Nee told a story about his visit to a particular place where the whole situation was dead. As he shared with the saints, he had difficulty speaking to them. Then one sister in the congregation began to say amen as he spoke. Her face showed that she was receptive to his word. The more that sister said amen, the more Brother Nee was enlivened to speak. Later, he said that one sister saved the whole meeting because there was mutuality.
Never sit in a meeting without being a part of the meeting. It is better to speak in the meeting every time we come together. But if we do not speak, we should surely be a part of the meeting. We may not have the opportunity to pray, but we can take the opportunity to say amen after every prayer. If we will say amen after every prayer, we will become a great benefit to the meeting. If we listen to the praises at the table or to the prayers in the prayer meeting and never respond or say amen, then when we leave the meeting, our thought may be, "That was a terrible meeting. I wasted my time in coming." We should always be involved in the mutuality of the meeting. If we say amen and participate in the meeting, we will say, "What a glorious meeting!" Whether we are fellowshipping together or praying together, whatever we do in the Body life is mutual. Everything is mutual and in mutuality. The whole church life is in mutuality.
The Body Being Built Up by the Mutual Supply of the Members and through the Interdependence among the Members
Admonishing the Disorderly Ones, Consoling the Little-souled, Upholding the Weak, and Being Long-suffering toward All
The Body is built up by the mutual supply of the members and through the interdependence among the members (Eph. 4:16). In the Body life, we need to admonish the disorderly ones, console the little-souled, uphold the weak, and be long-suffering toward all. First Thessalonians 5:14, depicting the Body life, says, "Admonish the disorderly, console the fainthearted, sustain the weak, be long-suffering toward all." This is the normal church life in many cases. We need to admonish the disorderly ones. I wish that there were no disorderly ones in the church. But there are always the 'disorderly ones. Once Brother Lee said that the disorderly ones are given by the Lord to test our patience and to expose our genuine situation. We may have the disorderly ones, but how do we deal with them? If we go to them improperly, we will be fighting. The disorderly ones usually know how to fight. Eventually, we realize that we do not know how to deal with the disorderly ones. So we have to learn. If we do not learn how to deal with these disorderly ones and we despise the disorderly ones, the Lord will send more disorderly ones until we learn the lesson. We have to learn how to take care of the disorderly ones, how to pray for them, how to be before the Lord for them, how to approach them, and how to minister Christ to them until they are not disorderly.
We also have to learn how to console the little-souled. In the church life there are some who are little-souled, without much capacity, easily discouraged, needing consolation. If we go to them to rebuke them, they descend not just "six feet under", but "ten feet under." They give up and feel that they have no way to go on. In the Body we have to care for the little-souled ones. We must go to them and minister Christ to them. Brother Lee said that all these kinds of cases expose our shortage. We realize that we do not know the word, that we do not have the word, that we do not have the way, and that everything we touch causes the problem to be enlarged. We have to uphold the weak and be long-suffering toward all.
The Church Meeting Being a "One Another" Meeting, a Meeting of Mutuality
The church meeting is a "one another" meeting, a meeting of mutuality; a church meeting has the stamp of "one another" upon it (1 Cor. 14:26, 31; Acts 2:46). Hebrews 10:24-25 says, "And let us consider one another so as to incite one another to love and good works, not abandoning our own assembling together, as the custom with some is, but exhorting one another; and so much the more as you see the day drawing near." Every church meeting should be a meeting in mutuality, that is, a "one another" meeting. It may be more difficult to practice the way of mutuality in the big meetings, but for sure in the vital groups, we should practice this kind of church life—a church life where we are considering one another and exhorting one another. I exhort you and you exhort me. Everything should be done in mutuality.
THE DIVINE FELLOWSHIP BEING THE REALITY OF LIVING IN THE BODY OF CHRIST
The Divine Fellowship Being the Flow of the Divine Life among and through All the Members
The divine fellowship is the reality of living in the Body of Christ. The divine fellowship is the flow of the divine life among and through all the members of the organic Body of Christ (Rev. 22:1). First John 1:3 says, "That which we have seen and heard we report also to you that you also may have fellowship with us, and indeed our fellowship is with the Father and with His Son Jesus Christ." This fellowship was first in the Triune God. Then the Triune God flowed into the apostles, and it became the apostles' fellowship. This life next flowed into those to whom the apostles preached the gospel, and that eternal life entered into all those believers. This became their fellowship in the divine life. Eventually, all the members have entered into this marvelous fellowship. It was first in the Triune God, it was secondly in the apostles, and now it is in all the believers. In Acts 2:42 we are told to continue steadfastly in the apostles' teaching and fellowship. In the past I considered that the word fellowship in this verse meant speaking or teaching, but the apostles' fellowship is the eternal life that is now flowing in all the members of the Body of Christ. This fellowship is like the circulation of the blood within all the members of our physical body. Although in our physical body our nerves carry the direction from the head to every member, the circulation of the blood does not go from the head to every member; it flows from one member to another member. In all the churches we should enjoy this divine fellowship.
The fellowship that we enjoy is nothing other than the divine Spirit who has entered into the spirit of all the believers. This Spirit is not dormant; this Spirit is flowing. In Revelation 22:1-2, the river flows out of the throne of God, and it flows to reach the entire city, bringing the city into the fellowship of life. This life has flowed out of the Triune God and it has flowed into the apostles. Now it has flowed from the apostles to every member of the Body. We call this flow the apostles' fellowship, and this is the fellowship we enjoy in the Body.
The fellowship and the flow of the divine life must control us. We live in the Body by the fellowship of the divine life. In this fellowship of life—the consummated Spirit moving in every member—there is divinity, the humanity of Christ, crucifixion, resurrection, and ascension. When all the members are in this fellowship, it brings the living of the Body of Christ into existence. It is not that we establish certain rules and regulations. It is simply this life flowing in every member, making every member organic. Submission is in this fellowship; leadership is in this fellowship. Everything is in this fellowship. This fellowship makes us the living members of the Body of Christ.
How can you prove that you are a living member? It is because you have the fellowship of life. Is there something flowing in you? Brother Lee said that this flow is like something jumping within you. It is like Mary coming to see Elizabeth (Luke 1:36-45). Mary came to Elizabeth, and John the Baptist started jumping in Elizabeth's womb (vv. 41,44). Do you not have this same kind of jumping within you? This is the fellowship of the Body. Within all of us is this fellowship. When we contact another member of the Body, there should be this fellowship moving within all of us. In some parts of this country blacks and whites cannot even meet together. This is a shame. What can overcome this? What overcomes this is the fellowship of the divine life.
In 1968 approximately one hundred forty saints—new ones in the Lord's recovery in the United States- 115 visit the saints in Taiwan who had been in the Lord's recovery for a long time. The airport in Taipei had long ramps leading to the terminals inside, and when we arrived, all of the Chinese-speaking brothers and sisters lined up on both sides of these ramps. The number of saints lining these ramps seemed like thousands. They were all there to greet us. As we walked up the ramps, we began shaking the hands of the saints, and we learned that we had two words in common: amen and hallelujah. So we were shouting as we shook hands: "Amen, Hallelujah! Amen, Hallelujah!" The fellowship of life was so full within all the brothers and sisters. Although they did not know us and we did not know them, we all knew that we were in the same fellowship of life.
The Body in a Practical Way Being in the Fellowship, and the Circulation of the Spirit Being the Fellowship of Christ's Body
The Body in a practical way is in the fellowship. Just as the circulation of the blood is the fellowship of our body, the circulation of the Spirit is the fellowship of Christ's Body; the Spirit today is the "blood" in Christ's Body (Eph. 4:4; 2 Cor. 13:14).
God Having Blended the Body Together
The Meaning of Blending—to Be Adjusted, Harmonized, Tempered, and Mingled
God has blended the Body together (1 Cor. 12:24). The word blended means adjusted, harmonized, tempered, and mingled, and implies the losing of distinctions. To be blended means that we should be adjusted to fit, be harmonized, tempered, mingled, and lose all of our distinctions.
Stopping to Fellowship with Others
Blending means that we should always stop to fellowship with others. Fellowship tempers us; fellowship adjusts us; fellowship harmonizes us; and fellowship mingles us.
Fellowshipping with the Other Saints
We should not do anything without fellowshipping with the other saints who are coordinating with us; fellowship requires us to stop when we are about to do something. Learn to stop and fellowship.
Being Touched by Others and Touching Others
To be blended means that you are touched by others and that you are touching others, but you should touch others in a blending way. If you are not touched by others and you are not touching others, then you are not in the blending.
Being Crossed Out, Being by the Spirit to Dispense Christ, and Doing Everything for the Sake of Christ's Body
Blending requires us to be crossed out; blending requires us to be by the Spirit to dispense Christ and to do everything for the sake of His Body. In the blending we need the cross to operate all the time. In these last few days, many times I was ready to speak something, but the cross operated and would not allow me to speak. The cross has to operate in us; otherwise, we can never be blended. We are always either too much or not enough without the cross. Blending requires us to be by the Spirit to dispense Christ and do everything for the sake of the Body.
ALWAYS CONSIDERING THE BODY, CARING FOR THE BODY, HONORING THE BODY, AND DOING WHAT IS BEST FOR THE BODY
The Recovery Being for the Body
We should always consider the Body, care for the Body, honor the Body, and do what is best for the Body (1 Cor. 12:23-27). The recovery is for the Body, not for any individual or merely for any individual local church (1 Cor. 12:14; Rom. 12:2, 4-5). '
The Local Churches Being a Procedure to Reach God's Goal
The local churches are not the goal of God's economy, but a procedure to reach God's goal, which is the reality of the unique Body of Christ. Most of us in the past have considered that the local churches are God's goal. However, we should realize that the local churches are not the goal of God's economy. The goal is the Body of Christ. The Body of Christ is the reality. The local churches are the holding factor that holds the Body of Christ. The local churches are where the Body of Christ is developed and becomes the reality of the church. We must enter into the reality of the Body. Brother Lee said he loved the local churches for a purpose with a goal, and that purpose is the Body of Christ. We are for our local church for the goal that the Lord would gain the Body of Christ and that we would live in the Body of Christ in all its reality.
Brother Lee explained what he meant by the difference between the church and the Body of Christ by using the illustration of an apple tree. The apple tree is not only for the tree, but also for the organic essence of the tree. The organic essence of the apple tree is the fruit, the apples, that are produced. Do not treasure the tree; it is only the frame. You have to treasure the apple tree for the goal of the apple tree, the organic essence of the tree—the apples. Surely, you love the frame, because without the frame you could not have any apples. But you love the frame for a purpose: that apples may be produced. Love the local churches, but love them for a purpose. Love them for the Body of Christ.
Having a Proper Consideration for the Body
Whenever we do something, we must have a proper consideration for the Body; we need to consider how the Body would feel about what we are doing. In The Problems Causing the Turmoils in the Church Life, on page 35, Brother Lee said the following:
The recovery is for the Body, not for any individual or merely for any individual local church. If we are going to do something, we have to consider how the Body, the recovery, will react. The problems are all due to the lack of seeing the Body and of caring for the Body. We all need to come back to the truth, and to practice the truth is to take care of the Body. Sometimes the Body is strong, and sometimes the Body is weak, but it is still the Body. If we come back to the truth and take care of the proper order in the Body, the Body will immediately become stronger. All the problems are due to one thing: not seeing, not knowing, and not caring for the Body. We have to honor the Body.
Being Body-conscious, Body-centered, and Being Rescued from Self-seeking Back to a Concern for the Body
We need to be Body-conscious, Body-centered, not self-centered. We need to be rescued from self-seeking back to a concern for the Body, that we might no longer be for our individual profit but for the building up of the Body.—B. P.
