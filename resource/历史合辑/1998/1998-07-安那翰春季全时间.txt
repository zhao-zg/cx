第一篇　锡安的异象
大纲|Outline|对照-听抄-目录
读经：箴二十九18上，徒二十六19，启十四1～5，二7上，17，三12，20～21，十二11
壹　主的恢复乃是要建造锡安─启十四1：
一　耶路撒冷预表召会，锡安山预表召会中的得胜者─王上八1，诗一二二2～3，二6，八四7下：
１　锡安是召会的高峰、中心、拔高、加强、丰富和实际─诗二十2，四八2，11～12，五十2，五三6上，八七2。
２　耶路撒冷的特征、生命、祝福和建立，都来自于锡安─诗五一18，一O二21，一二八5，一三五21，赛四一27，珥三17。
３　锡安山是安全向主敞开，也是完全被主得着的─诗二四1，3，7～10。
二　在新约里，得胜者被比作锡安：
１　在启示录十四章一节，十四万四千得胜者不仅是在耶路撒冷，更是在锡安的高峰上。─引用经文
２　在启示录这卷书中，主所要的并所要建造的，乃是锡安，就是得胜者。
３　今天在召会时代，得了成全并成熟的神人乃是在召会里的锡安，就是得胜者。
三　今天在主的恢复里，我们必须一直往前，达到锡安山：
１　神经纶中的高峰乃是锡安，就是基督身体的实际，我们在召会生活中必须竭力达到这高峰。
２　在我们属灵的经历中，摩利亚山至终成了锡安山─创二二2，代下三1，撒下二四25。
贰　得胜者乃是为着建造基督的身体，以终极完成新耶路撒冷─启二7下，三12，21：
一　主需要得胜者来完成神的经纶，以得着基督的身体─提前一4，弗一10，三9，22～23。
二　得胜者看见身体，认识身体，顾到身体，并活在身体里，借着身体，也为着身体─罗十二4～5，林前十二12，18，24下─27，弗二16，四4，16，西二19。
三　得胜者顾到神的权益超过一切，包括他们的需要─太六33。
四　得胜者胜过一切反对基督或顶替基督的东西─约壹二18，22，腓三7～11。
五　得胜者征服了毁灭的、属撒但的混乱，而在建设的、神圣的经纶中夸胜─弗一10。
六　得胜者立定心志要成为有活力的人─士五15，启三19下。
七　得胜者决心要否认自己，并付主所要求的代价，为主、为恢复、并为基督的身体作得胜者─太十六24，启十一11，三18。

GENERAL SUBJECT
THE OVERCOMERS
Message One
The Vision of Zion
纲目|对照-听抄-目录
Scripture Reading: Prov. 29:18a; Acts 26:19; Rev. 14:1-5; 2:7a, 17; 3:12, 20-21; 12:11
I.　The Lord's recovery is to build up Zion—Rev. 14:1:
A.　Jerusalem typifies the church, and Mount Zion typifies the overcomers in the church—1 Kings 8:1; Psa. 122:2-3; 2:6; 84:7b:
1.　Zion is the high peak, the center, the uplifting, the strengthening, the enriching, and the reality of the church—Psa. 20:2; 48:2, 11-12; 50:2; 53:6a; 87:2.
2.　The characteristics, the life, the blessing, and the establishment of Jerusalem come from Zion—Psa. 51:18; 102:21; 128:5; 135:21; Isa. 41:27; Joel 3:17.
3.　The hill of zion is absolutely open to the Lord and is absolutely possessed by Him—Psa. 24:1, 3, 7-10.
B.　In the New Testament the overcomers are likened to Zion:
1.　In Revelation 14:1 the 144,000 overcomers are not just in Jerusalem; they are on the peak of Zion.
2.　In the book of Revelation what the Lord wants and what the Lord will build up is Zion, the overcomers.
3.　Today in the church age, the God-men who are perfected and matured are Zion, the overcomers, within the churches.
C.　Today in the Lord's recovery, we need to go on and on to reach Mount Zion:
1.　The highest peak in God's economy is Zion, the reality of the Body of Christ, and we need to endeavor in the church life to reach this high peak.
2.　In our spiritual experience Mount Moriah eventually becomes Mount Zion—Gen. 22:2; 2 Chron. 3:1; 2 Sam. 24:25.
II.　The overcomers are for the building up of the Body of Christ to consummate the New Jerusalem—Rev. 2:7b; 3:12, 21:
A.　The Lord needs the overcomers to carry out the economy of God to have the Body—1 Tim. 1:4; Eph. 1:10; 3:9; 1:22-23.
B.　The overcomers see the Body, know the Body, care for the Body, and live in the Body, through the Body, and for the Body—Rom. 12:4-5; 1 Cor. 12:12, 18, 24b-27; Eph. 2:16; 4:4, 16; Col. 2:19.
C.　The overcomers care for God's interests above everything, including their necessities—Matt. 6:33.
D.　The overcomers overcome anything that is against Christ or that replaces Christ—1 John 2:18, 22; Phil. 3:7-11.
E.　The overcomers conquer the destructive, satinic chaos and triumph in the constructive, divine eoncomy—Eph. 1:10.
F.　The overcomers make a resolution to be vitalized ones—Judg. 5:15; Rev. 3:19b.
G.　The overcomers resolve to deny themselves and to pay the price required by the Lord to be the overcomers for the Lord, for the recovery, and for the Body—Matt. 16:24; Rev. 11:11; 3:18.

第二篇　得胜者的工作和他们的蒙拣选

读经：林后四10～13，亚四12～14，太二十四45～47，罗十二1，约壹五21，约五41，44，七18，西一24
壹　得胜者的工作是站在死地（让他们的己留在死地），使别人能得生命─书三17：
一　得胜者不断操练拒绝己，并凭神圣的生命活着，以彰显并释放神圣的生命─林后四10～13，启十二11，林前十五31，提后三2，4，林后五14～15，罗八36～37。

贰　得胜者的蒙拣选，见于神拣选基甸，以及与他一同争战，击败米甸人的三百人─士六1～6,11～35,七1～8,19～25,八1～4:
一　基甸的故事给我们看见如何成为得胜者:
１　我们必须认识己,看见我们自己是至微小的─士六15，弗三8，太二十27～28。
２　我们必须看见从天上来的异象─士六12，徒二六18上，腓三8。
３　我们必须不违背那从天上来的异象，回应主的呼召，将祭物献给祂─士六21，24，徒二六19，彼前二5，罗十二1，启二7。
４　我们必须拆毁偶像，作为外在的见证─士六25～28，约壹五21，太十36～37。
二　得胜者如何蒙拣选，见于那三百人如何被选上：
１　第一次挑选的结果，有二万二千人离去；这些离去的人：
a　想要荣耀自己─士七1，路十七10，约五41，44，七18。
b　是惧怕胆怯的─士七3，太二五25。
２　第二次的挑选，由百姓怎样喝水而决定─士七4～6：
a　凡直接用嘴喝水的人，被神剔除。
b　凡用手捧水喝的人被神选上，因为他是否认己的人─士七7。
c　得胜者绝对为着神的荣耀，他们除了怕得罪主之外（赛十一2），什么都不畏惧，他们也让十字架对付他们的己。─引用经文
３　神给基甸三百人，使他们成为一体，他们同心合意的行动，表征在那灵里的一，以及在基督身体里的生活。
４　三百人争战劳苦，但全会众追赶仇敌，收获成果，表征我们得胜时，基督的全身体都要得复兴─士七22～25，八1～4，西一24，诗一二六5。

GENERAL SUBJECT
THE OVERCOMERS
Message Two
The Work of the Overcomers and Their Selection
纲目|对照-听抄-目录
Scripture Reading: 2 Cor. 4:10-13; Zech. 4:12-14; Matt. 24:45-47; Rom. 12:1; 1 John 5:21; John 5:41, 44; 7:18; Col. 1:24
I.　The work of the overcomers is to stand in the place of death (to allow their self to remain in the place of death) so that others may receive life—Josh. 3:17:
A.　The overcomers continually exercise to reject the self and live by the divine life for the manifestation and release of the divine life—2 Cor. 4:10-13; Rev. 12:11; 1 Cor. 15:31; 2 Tim. 3:2, 4; 2 Cor. 5:14-15; Rom. 8:36-37.
B.　The overcomers are channels of life to dispense life into others to meet God's need—Phil. 2:13; 1:21-25; Matt. 24:45-47; Zech. 4:12-14; Eph. 5:18; Matt. 25:9; Rev. 3:18.
II.　The selection of the overcomers is seen with God's selection of Gideon and the three hundred men to fight with him to defeat the Midianites—Judg. 6:1-6, 11-35; 7:1-8, 19-25; 8:1-4:
A.　The account of Gideon shows us how to be an overcomer:
1.　We must know the self, realizing ourselves to be the least—Judg. 6:15; Eph. 3:8; Matt. 20:27-28.
2.　We must see the heavenly vision—Judg. 6:12; Acts 26:18a; Phil. 3:8.
3.　We must not be disobedient to the heavenly vision, responding to the Lord's call by offering the sacrifices to Him—Judg. 6:21, 24; Acts 26:19; 1 Pet. 2:5; Rom. 12:1; Rev. 2:7.
4.　We must tear down the idols as an outward testimony—Judg. 6:25-28; 1 John 5:21; Matt. 10:36-37.
B.　How the overcomers are selected is seen with the selection of the three hundred:
1.　The first selection resulted in twenty-two thousand leaving; those who left:
a.　Wanted to glorify themselves—Judg. 7:1; Luke 17:10; John 5:41, 44; 7:18.
b.　Were fearful and afraid—Josh. 7:3; Matt. 25:25.
2.　The second selection was determined by how the people drank—Judg. 7:4-6:
a.　Those who drank directly with their mouths were eliminated by God.
b.　Those who drank by bringing water in their hand to their mouths were selected by God because they were self-denying persons—Judg. 7:7.
c.　The overcomers are absolute for God's glory, they are afraid of nothing except offending the Lord (Isa. 11:2), and they allow the cross to deal with the self.
3.　God gave Gideon three hundred men and made them one body, who moved and acted together in one accord, signifying the oneness in the Spirit and the living in the Body—Judg. 6:16.
4.　The three hundred men fought the battle and labored, yet the whole congregation chased the enemy and reaped the harvest, signifying that when we overcome, the whole Body is revived—Judg. 7:22-25; 8:1-4; Col. 1:24; Psa. 128:5.

C.　When we are rooted and grounded in Christ's love, we grow and are built up in His life and know His immeasurable, knowledge- surpassing love—3:17-19.
D.　The Body builds itself up in love—the love of God in Christ, which becomes the love of Christ in us, by which we love Christ and the fellow members of His Body—4:16.
E.　Love is the inner substance of God, and the goal of Ephesians is to bring us into God's inner substance that we may enjoy God as love and walk in love—5:2, 25.
F.　We need to love the Lord in incorruptibility, that is, in and through all the crucial things revealed and taught in the book of Ephesians—6:24.
II.　The first thing that we have to overcome is the leaving of the first love—Rev. 2:4:
A.　To love the Lord Jesus with the first love is to give Him the first place in all things—Col. 1:18b.

1.　Nothing but love can keep us in a proper relationship with the Lord—Mark 12:30.
2.　The leaving of the first love is the source of all the degradation in the succeeding stages of the church.
III.　If we would be overcomers, we must recover, maintain, and develop our first love toward the Lord:
A.　We need to give Christ the first place, the preeminence, in all things regarding Him as everything in our life—Col. 1:18b; 3:11.
B.　We must not love anyone or anything above the Lord, including our soul-life—Matt. 10:37-39; Rev. 12:11.
C.　Our love for the Lord is the issue of His forgiveness—Luke 7:40-47.
D.　We need to develop a personal and affectionate relationship with the Lord—S. S. 1:2-4.
E.　We love the Lord because He first loved us, infusing His loving essence into us and generating within us the love with which we love Him—1 John 4:19, 8, 16.
F.　We love the Lord according to the divine dispensing of the Divine Trinity as love—Rom. 5:5; 8:39, 35; 15:30.
G.　If we would maintain and develop our first love toward the Lord, we need to be vessels open to the Lord, loving Him, receiving Him, being filled with Him, and letting Him be everything to us and do everything in us, through us, and for us—2 Cor. 4:7; Rom. 9:21, 23.

4.　The church meeting in mutuality with the saints prophesying for the building up of the Body of Christ—1 Cor. 14:4b, 26.

1.　We must love the Lord personally and affectionately so that all our service would be a labor of love—Rev. 2:4; 1 Thes. 1:3; S. S. 1:2, 4; 1 Cor. 8:1; 14:4b.

D.　The overcomers suffer the chaos, but instead of being disappointed or discouraged, they are strengthened and enabled to stand for and live out the divine economy.
E.　God does not deliver us out of chaos; rather, He wants us to conquer the destructive satanic chaos and triumph in the constructive divine economy.

E.　To those who overcome the attack of death Christ will give the crown of life—Rev. 2:10b:
1.　The crown of life denotes the overcoming strength that is the power of the resurrection life—Phil. 3:10.
2.　The crown of life signifies that those who overcome the attack of death have attained to the out-resurrection from the dead—Phil. 3:11.

GENERAL SUBJECT
THE OVERCOMERS
Message Ten
The Overcomers in Sardis (1) Overcoming Deadness to Be Clothed in White Garments
纲目|对照-听抄-目录
Scripture Reading: Rev. 3:1-6; 19:7-8; Matt. 22:11-14; Phil. 1:20-21a; 3:9
I.　“To the messenger of the church in Sardis write”—Rev. 3:1a:
A.　The Greek word for Sardis means “the remains,” “the remainder,” or “the restoration.”
B.　As a sign, the church in Sardis prefigures the Protestant church from the time of the Reformation to the second coming of Christ.
II.　“These things says He who has the seven Spirits of God and the seven stars”—Rev. 3:1b:
A.　The seven Spirits enable the church to be intensely living.
B.　The seven stars enable the church to be intensely shining.
III.　“I know your works that you have a name that you are living, and yet you are dead”—Rev. 3:1c:
A.　The history of Protestantism is a history of being living in name but dead in reality.
B.　The dead, reformed church needs the sevenfold intensified Spirit and the shining leaders.
IV.　“Become watchful and establish the things which remain, which were about to die”—Rev. 3:2a:
A.　The things which remain refers to the things which had been lost and were restored by the Reformation.
B.　These things were about to die; thus, they needed to be revived.
V.　“For I have found none of your works completed before My God”—Rev. 3:2b:
A.　Nothing begun in the Reformation has been completed by the Protestant churches.
B.　“In the eyes of the Lord, He has not found anything complete—everything was a beginning without an end. The Lord is a Lord of completion; therefore, He requires completion” (Watchman Nee, The Orthodoxy of the Church, p. 54).
VI.　“Remember therefore how you have received and heard, and keep it and repent” —Rev. 3:3a:
A.　“God does not want us to invent; He just wants us to receive” (Watchman Nee, The Orthodoxy of the Church, p. 58).
B.　“We must examine what we have received, what we have heard, and hold fast and repent” (p. 58).
VII.　“If therefore you will not watch, I will come as a thief, and you shall by no means know at what hour I will come upon you”—Rev. 3:3b:
A.　Because the reformed Protestant churches are dead, they will be unaware of the Lord's coming as a thief in His secret appearing to His seekers.
B.　We need to be precious enough to be “stolen” by the Lord, and we need to be prepared, ready, and watchful for His coming.
VIII.　“But you have a few names in Sardis who have not defiled their garments, and they will walk with Me in white because they are worthy”—Rev. 3:4:
A.　The defilement in Sardis was not the defilement of sin but the defilement of death—Lev. 11:24-25; Num. 6:7, 9.
B.　Here white garments signify the walk and living which are unspotted by death and which will be approved by the Lord.
IX.　“He who overcomes will be clothed thus, in white garments”—Rev. 3:5a:
A.　Every Christian needs the garment of justification for our salvation, signifying the Christ whom we have received as our objective righteousness—Luke 15:22: 1 Cor. 1:30.
B.　Every Christian needs the garment of approvedness for our acceptance, signifying the Christ whom we live out as our subjective righteousness—Matt. 22:11-14; Phil. 1:20-21a; 3:9.
X.　“He who has an ear, let him hear what the Spirit says to the churches”—Rev. 3:6:
A.　The dead, reformed church needs the speaking of the living Spirit.
B.　The knowledge of the dead letters can never replace the speaking of the intensified Spirit—2 Cor. 3:5; John 6:63.

第十四篇　在老底嘉的得胜者（一）向主买金子、白衣和眼药，并得以在主宝座上与祂同坐

５　她是赤身的，因为她不活基督作她主观的义，作她在日常生活中的第二件衣服。
二　恢复后又堕落的召会需要买金子，叫她富足，买白衣遮盖她的赤身，买眼药医治她的瞎眼：
１　圣经把我们那运行作工的信比喻为金子，并且神圣的性情也是由金子表征的─加五6，彼前一7，彼后一4，出二五11。
２　白衣表征蒙主称许的行为，就是基督自己从召会活出来─启三4～5，太二二11～12，启十九8，14。
３　那擦眼睛所需要的眼药，乃是膏抹的灵，也就是基督自己这赐生命的灵─约壹二20，27，林前十五45下。
贰　得胜的，主要赐祂在主宝座上与主同坐─启三21上：

１　主耶稣成为人，并以人的身分登宝座─来一8，二8～9，八1，十二2。

１　在主的宝座上与主同坐，就是有分于主的权柄─启二16～17。
２　在主的宝座上与主同坐，就是在要来的千年国与主一同作王，管理全地─启十一15，二十4下，5，但二35，44。

２　在我们属灵经历中达到宝座那里，意思就是在凡事上我们完全服从主的权柄和行政─弗四15，一22，10，彼前五6。

神的心意是要在人身上作工，使人能在宝座上。我们若不在宝座上，神就不会满意。神的心意是要使我们登宝座。祂的心愿是要使我们成为属宝座的人。神的国惟有等到我们登宝座的时候，才能完全来临。不仅如此，神的仇敌也惟有等到我们登宝座时，才会被征服。因此，神的目标不仅要把我们从地狱拯救出来，更是要把我们带上宝座。神顾念人（来二6），祂要人彰显祂，并行使祂的权柄。神渴望借着人显明祂自己，并借着人掌权、管理。除非我们都被带到宝座，神无法完全得着荣耀。有一天我们要被带到宝座，然后神就能向撒但夸耀。祂要得胜的宣告，那些曾被撒但所掳，而蒙神所拣选的人，已经被带到宝座。主耶稣领头到达宝座。祂是开拓者，是先锋（来六20），开了到宝座的路（二10）。这指明祂不是惟一命定要到宝座的人。祂开了路，并带头使我们能跟随。祂是第一位登宝座的，我们都要跟着祂。如今我们迈向宝座，因为神的心意是要领我们进荣耀里去，并使我们坐在宝座上。（以西结生命读经，第十二篇）。

GENERAL SUBJECT
THE OVERCOMERS
Message Fourteen
The Overcomers in Laodicea (1) Buying from the Lord Gold, White Garments, and Eyesalve and Being Given to Sit with the Lord on His Throne
纲目|对照-听抄-目录
Scripture Reading: Rev. 3:14-22
I.　The Lord counsels us to buy from Him gold refined by fire that we may be rich, white garments that we may be clothed, and eyesalve to anoint our eyes that we may see—Rev. 3:18:
A.　The degraded recovered church is wretched, miserable, poor, blind, and naked—v. 17:
1.　She is wretched because she is proud of being rich in the vain knowledge of doctrine, but in reality she is sorely poor in the experience of the riches of Christ.
2.　She is miserable because she is naked, blind, and full of shame and darkness.
3.　She is poor because she is poor in the experience of Christ and in the spiritual reality of God's economy.
4.　She is blind because she lacks true spiritual insight in the genuine spiritual things.
5.　She is naked because she does not live Christ as her subjective righteousness, as the second garment in her daily walk.
B.　The degraded recovered church needs to buy gold to make herself rich, white garments to cover her nakedness, and eyesalve to cure. her blindness:
1.　In the Bible our operating, acting faith is likened to gold, and the divine nature is typified by gold—Gal. 5:6; 1 Pet. 1:7; 2 Pet. 1:4; Exo. 25:11.
2.　White garments signify conduct—Christ Himself lived out of the church—that can be approved by the Lord—Rev. 3:4-5; Matt. 22:11-12; Rev. 19:8, 14.
3.　The eyesalve for the anointing of the eyes is the anointing Spirit. who is Christ Himself as the life-giving Spirit—1 John 2:20. 27; 1 Cor. 15:45b.
II.　He who overcomes, to him the Lord will give to sit with Him on His throne—Rev. 3:21a:
A.　The Lord Himself overcame and sat with His Father on His throne—v. 21b:
1.　The Lord Jesus became a man, and as a man He went to the throne—Heb. 1:8; 2:8-9; 8:1; 12:2.
2.　Because the Lord Jesus obeyed God the Father and submitted to God's authority in an absolute way, after His resurrection God gave Him all authority in heaven and on earth and exalted Him to the throne—Phi1. 2:8-9; Matt. 28:18; Acts 2:33; 5:31; Eph. 1:20.
3.　Now the One sitting on the throne is not only God but also man, for this One is the mingling of God and man—Ezek 1:26-28.
B.　To sit with the Lord on His throne will be a prize to the overcomer:
1.　To sit with the Lord on His throne is to participate in the Lord's authority—Rev. 2:16-17.
2.　To sit with the Lord on His throne is to be a co-king with the Lord in ruling over the whole earth in the coming millennial kingdom—11:15; 20:4b, 6; Dan. 2:35, 44.
3.　God's intention is to work on us so that we can sit with the Lord on the throne—Gen. 1:26, 28; Rev. 21:10-11; 22:5.
C.　To have the throne, or to arrive at the throne, is to allow the Lord to have the highest and most prominent position in our Christian life—Ezek. 1:26; Rev. 5:5; 7:17; Col. 1:18:
1.　For the Lord to have the throne in us means that He has the position to reign in us—l Cor. 15:24-28; 11:3.
2.　To reach the throne in our spiritual experience means that in everything we are completely submissive to the Lord's authority and administrationmEph. 4:15; 1:22, 10; 1 Pet. 5:6.
3.　If we want God's purpose and plan to be carried out in us and with us, we must submit to the reign of God—Dan. 7:13-14; Luke 19:11-15, 27; Rev. 22:1; Heb. 4:16.
Additional Word: God's Intention to Bring Man to the Throne
God's intention is to work on man in order that man can be on the throne….God will not be satisfied until we are on the throne….God's intention is to take us to the throne. His desire is to make us people of the throne. God's kingdom cannot come in full until we are on the throne. Furthermore, God's enemy will not be subdued until we are on the throne. God's goal, therefore, is not merely to deliver us out of hell but to bring us to the throne….God's mind is on man (Heb. 2:6), and He wants man to express Him and to exercise His authority….God desires to manifest Himself through man, and He desires to reign, to administrate, through man….God cannot receive the full glory until we are brought to the throne. One day we will be brought to the throne, and then God will be able to boast to Satan. He will triumphantly declare that His chosen ones, who had been taken captive by Satan, have been brought to the throne….the Lord Jesus led the way to the throne. He was the Pioneer, the Forerunner (Heb. 6:20), cutting the path to the throne (2:10). This indicates that He is not the only man destined for the throne. He has cut the path and has taken the lead so that we may follow. He was the first one to the throne, and we will come after Him. Now we are marching to the throne, for God intends to bring us into glory and to set us on the throne. (Life-study of Ezekiel, msg. 12)

二　得胜者要在基督的审判台前得赏赐─林后五10，启十一18下。
A.　The overcomers will be rewarded at the judgment seat of Christ—2 Cor. 5:10; Rev. 11:18c.
三　得胜者成为新妇，与羔羊成为婚配─十九7～9。
B.　The overcomers will be the bride married to the Lamb—19:7-9.
四　得胜者成为羔羊的军队─十七14，十九14，19～21。
C.　The overcomers will be the army of the Lamb—17:14; 19:14, 19-21.
五　得胜者要带进神与基督的国─十一15，二十4，6。
D.　The overcomers will bring in the kingdom of God and of Christ—11:15; 20:4, 6.
六　得胜者要承受神与基督的国，更丰满的享受永远的生命─太五20，十九23～30。
E.　The overcomers will inherit the kingdom of God and of Christ in the fuller enjoyment of eternal life—Matt. 5:20; 19:23-30.
七　得胜者成为在开始和新鲜阶段中的新耶路撒冷，作基督的新妇一千年─启二7。
F.　The overcomers will be the New Jerusalem as the bride of Christ for one thousand years in its initial and fresh stage—Rev. 2:7.
八　得胜者要在千年国里小型的终极完成新耶路撒冷，然后在永世里新耶路撒冷要全型的终极完成，作神的帐幕和基督的妻子─二一1～3，8～10
G.　The overcomers will consummate the New Jerusalem on a small scale in the millennium, and then the New Jerusalem will be consummated on a full scale in eternity as the tabernacle of God and the wife of Christ—21:1-3, 9-10.
九　得胜者借着终极完成的那灵，最后终极完成三一神在基督里永远的经纶─二二17。
H.　The overcomers will consummate finally the Triune God's economy in Christ through the consummated Spirit—22:17.
十　得胜者要有分于对经过过程并终极完成之三一神最丰满的享受，与历代所有的圣徒有交通，成为新耶路撒冷，永远在祂的荣耀里作祂的彰显，使祂得着满足。
I.　The overcomers will participate in the fullest enjoyment of the processed and consummated Triune God in fellowship with all the saints throughout the ages as the New Jerusalem forever to be in His glory as His expression for His satisfaction.
叁　结语：
III.　A concluding word:
一　凡算为得胜者的，乃是比较软弱的，降卑自己的，自认为无有的，并且是倚靠主的─歌六13下。
A.　Those who are counted as overcomers are the weaker ones, those who humble themselves, realize that they are nothing, and depend on the Lord—S. S. 6:13b.
二　得胜者是基督在祂丰满职事的第三时期（加强时期）中所产生的─启三1，四5，五6。
B.　The overcomers are produced by Christ in the third stage of His full ministry—the stage of intensification—Rev. 3:1; 4:5; 5:6.
三　得胜者胜过了世界的吸引，并胜过了己、旧造、肉体、天然的人和旧人─歌六10上。
C.　The overcomers overcome the attraction of the world and overcome the self, the old creation, and the flesh, the natural man, the old man—S. S. 6:10a.
四　得胜者在生命、性情、彰显和功用上，成为与神和基督一式一样，但无分于神格─歌六13下。
D.　The overcomers become the same as God and Christ in life, nature, expression, and function but not in the Godhead—v. 13b.
五　得胜者看见身体，认识身体，并顾到身体─罗十二4～5，林前十二12。
E.　The overcomers see the Body, know the Body, and care for the Body—Rom. 12:4-5; 1 Cor. 12:12.
